## Question

What isolation precautions are needed for key pathogens? Use **"Contact (MDRO, *C.diff*), Droplet (Flu, COVID), Airborne (TB, Measles, VZV)"** framework.

## Answer

### **Standard Precautions (All Patients):**
- **Hand hygiene** (before/after patient contact)
- **Gloves** for contact with blood/body fluids
- **Gown/face protection** if splash/spray expected
- **Safe injection practices, sharps disposal**

### **Contact Precautions:**

**Indications:**
- **MDROs:** MRSA, VRE, ESBL, CRE, MDR *Acinetobacter*, MDR *Pseudomonas*
- ***C. difficile* infection**
- **Norovirus, rotavirus**
- **Scabies, lice**
- **Draining wounds/abscesses** (uncontained)
- **RSV, parainfluenza** (infants/young children)

**PPE:**
- **Gown + gloves** upon room entry
- **Hand hygiene:** Soap and water for *C. difficile* (alcohol gel does NOT kill spores)

**Room:**
- **Private room** preferred (or cohort)

### **Droplet Precautions:**

**Indications:**
- **Influenza**
- **COVID-19** (SARS-CoV-2)
- **Pertussis** (until 5 days of antibiotics)
- **Meningococcal meningitis** (until 24h of antibiotics)
- ***Mycoplasma pneumoniae, Adenovirus***
- **Mumps, rubella**

**PPE:**
- **Surgical mask** (patient and healthcare worker)
- **Eye protection** recommended

**Room:**
- **Private room** preferred (or cohort)
- **Can share air** (droplets travel <6 feet, NOT airborne)

### **Airborne Precautions:**

**Indications:**
- **Tuberculosis** (pulmonary or laryngeal)
- **Measles** (rubeola)
- **Varicella (chickenpox)** - disseminated or localized in immunocompromised
- **Disseminated herpes zoster** (shingles)

**PPE:**
- **N95 respirator** (fit-tested) OR PAPR (powered air-purifying respirator)

**Room:**
- **Airborne infection isolation room (AIIR):** Negative pressure, â‰¥12 air changes/hour, air exhausted outside or HEPA-filtered

**Special:**
- **TB:** Airborne precautions until **3 negative sputum AFB smears** (on different days) OR alternative diagnosis + clinical improvement

### **Special Situations:**

***C. difficile*:**
- **Contact precautions**
- **Hand hygiene:** **Soap and water** (alcohol does NOT kill spores)
- **Environmental cleaning:** **Bleach-based disinfectants** (sporicidal)

**COVID-19:**
- **Droplet + contact precautions**
- **Airborne precautions** for aerosol-generating procedures (intubation, bronchoscopy, nebulizers)
- **N95** for aerosol-generating procedures

**Varicella/Disseminated Zoster:**
- **Airborne + contact precautions**
- **Healthcare workers:** Must have evidence of immunity (history of chickenpox, vaccine, or positive serology)

**Localized Herpes Zoster (Shingles):**
- **Standard precautions** if immunocompetent + lesions covered
- **Airborne + contact** if immunocompromised OR disseminated

**Meningococcal Meningitis:**
- **Droplet precautions until 24 hours of effective antibiotics**
- **Post-exposure prophylaxis:** Close contacts (household, daycare, healthcare workers with unprotected exposure to secretions)

### **Duration of Isolation:**

| Pathogen | Duration |
|----------|----------|
| **MRSA/VRE** | Until cleared (3 negative cultures, 1 week apart) OR discharge |
| ***C. difficile*** | Until diarrhea resolves (some facilities: 48h after resolution) |
| **Influenza** | 5-7 days from symptom onset (longer if immunocompromised) |
| **COVID-19** | Per CDC guidance (typically 5-10 days) |
| **TB** | Until 3 negative AFB smears OR alternative diagnosis |
| **Pertussis** | Until 5 days of antibiotics |
| **Meningococcal** | Until 24h of antibiotics |

## Key Points

### **Hand Hygiene for *C. difficile*:**
- **Soap and water** (NOT alcohol gel)
- **Alcohol does NOT kill spores**
- **Bleach-based disinfectants** for environmental cleaning

### **N95 Required:**
- **TB, measles, varicella, disseminated zoster**
- **Must be fit-tested**

### **Droplet vs. Airborne:**
- **Droplet:** <6 feet, surgical mask (flu, COVID, pertussis, meningococcal)
- **Airborne:** >6 feet, N95 + negative pressure room (TB, measles, varicella)

### **Contact Precautions for MDROs:**
- **Gown + gloves** upon room entry
- **Controversial:** Some facilities discontinuing for endemic MRSA/VRE (horizontal strategies focus on hand hygiene, environmental cleaning)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Contact:** MDROs, *C. difficile* (soap and water), draining wounds
- **Droplet:** Flu, COVID, pertussis, meningococcal (until 24h antibiotics)
- **Airborne:** TB (until 3 negative AFB), measles, varicella, disseminated zoster (N95 + negative pressure room)
- ***C. difficile*:** Soap and water + bleach disinfection (spores)
- **Duration:** Varies by pathogen (see table)
