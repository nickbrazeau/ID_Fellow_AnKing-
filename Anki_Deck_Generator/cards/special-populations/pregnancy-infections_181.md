## Question

What are the key congenital infections and their features? Use **"TORCH-LS: Toxo (Triad), CMV (#1), Rubella (Deafness), HSV (Skin/Eye/Mouth), Syphilis, Listeria, Parvo B19"** framework.

## Answer

### **Toxoplasmosis:**
- **Transmission:** Primary maternal infection during pregnancy (highest risk if 3rd trimester, but more severe if 1st trimester)
- **Classic Triad:** Chorioretinitis, hydrocephalus, intracranial calcifications
- **Treatment (Maternal):** Spiramycin (reduces transmission), pyrimethamine + sulfadiazine + leucovorin (if fetal infection confirmed)

### **Cytomegalovirus (CMV) - MOST COMMON:**
- **#1 congenital infection** (0.5-1% of live births)
- **Features:** Microcephaly, periventricular calcifications, sensorineural hearing loss (most common sequela), hepatosplenomegaly, petechiae ("blueberry muffin baby")
- **Treatment (Neonatal):** Valganciclovir × 6 months (improves hearing outcomes)

### **Rubella:**
- **Highest risk:** 1st trimester (>80% risk of defects)
- **Classic Triad:** Cataracts, cardiac defects (PDA, pulmonary stenosis), deafness
- **Other:** "Blueberry muffin baby," microcephaly, intellectual disability
- **Prevention:** MMR vaccine (live virus, contraindicated in pregnancy - vaccinate before pregnancy or postpartum)

### **Herpes Simplex Virus (HSV):**
- **Transmission:** Peripartum (during delivery through infected birth canal) > intrauterine (rare)
- **Neonatal HSV:** Disseminated disease, CNS encephalitis, skin/eye/mouth (SEM) lesions
- **Management:** C-section if active genital lesions at delivery, maternal suppressive acyclovir from 36 weeks (if history of HSV)

### **Syphilis:**
- **Transmission:** Transplacental (all trimesters)
- **Early Congenital:** Hepatosplenomegaly, rash (palms/soles), rhinitis ("snuffles"), osteochondritis
- **Late Congenital:** Hutchinson teeth, mulberry molars, saddle nose, saber shins, Clutton joints, cranial nerve VIII deafness
- **Treatment:** Penicillin G (maternal during pregnancy prevents congenital syphilis)

### **Listeria monocytogenes:**
- **Transmission:** Foodborne (unpasteurized dairy, deli meats)
- **Maternal:** Flu-like illness (can be mild)
- **Fetal:** Miscarriage, stillbirth, "granulomatosis infantiseptica" (disseminated abscesses)
- **Neonatal:** Early-onset sepsis, late-onset meningitis
- **Treatment:** Ampicillin (+ gentamicin for serious infections)

### **Parvovirus B19:**
- **Transmission:** Respiratory droplets
- **Fetal:** Severe anemia → hydrops fetalis (ascites, pleural/pericardial effusions, skin edema)
- **Risk:** Highest 9-20 weeks gestation
- **Management:** Serial ultrasounds, intrauterine transfusion if hydrops

### **Zika Virus:**
- **Transmission:** Mosquito-borne (*Aedes*), sexual
- **Fetal:** Microcephaly, severe brain abnormalities, congenital Zika syndrome
- **Prevention:** Avoid travel to endemic areas during pregnancy

## Key Points

### **CMV = Most Common:**
- **0.5-1% of live births**
- **Sensorineural hearing loss** = most common long-term sequela
- **Treat neonates** with valganciclovir × 6 months

### **Rubella = Vaccine-Preventable:**
- **MMR before pregnancy** or postpartum
- **NOT during pregnancy** (live virus)
- **Highest risk 1st trimester**

### **Syphilis = Penicillin Prevents:**
- **Screen all pregnant women** (1st trimester, 3rd trimester, delivery if high-risk)
- **Penicillin during pregnancy** prevents congenital syphilis

### **HSV = Peripartum:**
- **C-section if active lesions** at delivery
- **Suppressive acyclovir** from 36 weeks (if history of genital HSV)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **CMV:** Most common, periventricular calcifications, hearing loss
- **Toxo:** Chorioretinitis, hydrocephalus, intracranial calcifications
- **Rubella:** Cataracts, cardiac defects, deafness (vaccine-preventable)
- **Syphilis:** Screen all pregnant women, penicillin prevents
- **Listeria:** Avoid unpasteurized dairy/deli meats
- **Parvovirus B19:** Hydrops fetalis (intrauterine transfusion)
