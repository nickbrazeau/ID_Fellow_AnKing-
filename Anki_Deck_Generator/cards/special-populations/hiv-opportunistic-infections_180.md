## Question

What are the key OIs by CD4 thresholds? Use the **"<200: PCP/Toxo, <100: Crypto/Histo, <50: MAC/CMV"** framework.

## Answer

### **CD4 <500:**
- **Tuberculosis** (reactivation risk increased)
- **Bacterial pneumonia** (recurrent)
- **Herpes zoster** (shingles)
- **Oral/vaginal candidiasis**

### **CD4 <200 (AIDS-Defining):**
- ***Pneumocystis jirovecii* pneumonia (PCP)** - most common
- **Toxoplasma encephalitis** (reactivation)
- **Progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy (PML)** - JC virus
- **Chronic *Cryptosporidium* diarrhea**
- **Chronic *Isospora* diarrhea**

### **CD4 <100:**
- **Disseminated histoplasmosis**
- **Cryptococcal meningitis**
- **Chronic *Cryptosporidium* (severe)**
- **Microsporidiosis**
- **Cerebral toxoplasmosis** (high risk)

### **CD4 <50:**
- **Disseminated *Mycobacterium avium* complex (MAC)**
- **CMV retinitis/colitis/esophagitis**
- **CNS lymphoma** (primary CNS lymphoma)

### **Primary Prophylaxis:**

**CD4 <200:**
- **TMP-SMX DS daily** OR **TMP-SMX SS daily** (PCP + toxo prophylaxis)
- Alternative: Dapsone 100mg PO daily (+ pyrimethamine/leucovorin for toxo), atovaquone, pentamidine inhaled

**CD4 <100 + Toxo IgG+:**
- **TMP-SMX DS daily** (covers PCP + toxo)
- Alternative (toxo only): Dapsone 50mg PO daily + pyrimethamine 50mg weekly + leucovorin 25mg weekly

**CD4 <50:**
- **Azithromycin 1200mg PO weekly** OR **azithromycin 600mg PO twice weekly** (MAC)
- Alternative: Clarithromycin 500mg PO BID, rifabutin 300mg PO daily

### **Stop Prophylaxis (Immune Reconstitution on ART):**

**PCP/Toxo:**
- **Stop when CD4 >200 × 3 months** on ART

**MAC:**
- **Stop when CD4 >100 × 3 months** on ART

### **Secondary Prophylaxis (After Treated OI):**

Continue suppressive therapy until immune reconstitution similar to primary prophylaxis thresholds.

## Key Points

### **PCP = Most Common OI (CD4 <200):**
- **TMP-SMX DS daily** (first-line prophylaxis)
- **Also prevents toxoplasmosis**

### **MAC (CD4 <50):**
- **Azithromycin weekly** (first-line prophylaxis)
- **Disseminated disease:** Fever, night sweats, anemia, ↑Alk phos

### **Toxoplasmosis (CD4 <100):**
- **Reactivation** (seropositivity required for risk)
- **TMP-SMX** covers (if already on for PCP)

### **CMV (CD4 <50):**
- **NO prophylaxis** recommended (valganciclovir toxicity > benefit)
- **Screen retinal exams** if CD4 <50 (every 3 months)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **CD4 <200:** Start TMP-SMX (PCP + toxo prophylaxis)
- **CD4 <50:** Add azithromycin (MAC prophylaxis), monitor for CMV retinitis
- **Stop prophylaxis** when CD4 >200 (PCP/toxo) or >100 (MAC) × 3 months on ART
- **Most common AIDS-defining OI:** PCP, then toxoplasmosis
