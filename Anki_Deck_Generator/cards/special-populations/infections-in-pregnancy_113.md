## Infections in Pregnancy

**Q:** What are the key congenital infections (TORCH), antibiotic safety categories in pregnancy, and approach to common infections during pregnancy?

**A:**

## TORCH INFECTIONS (Congenital Infections)

**Mnemonic: TORCH**
- **T**oxoplasmosis
- **O**ther (Syphilis, VZV, Parvovirus B19, Zika, Listeria)
- **R**ubella
- **C**ytomegalovirus (CMV)
- **H**erpes simplex virus (HSV)

---

## 1. TOXOPLASMOSIS

**Transmission:** Primary maternal infection → transplacental (30-40% overall risk)

**Risk by Trimester:**
- **1st trimester:** 10-15% transmission, **most severe** (microcephaly, hydrocephalus, chorioretinitis)
- **3rd trimester:** 60-80% transmission, **least severe** (subclinical)

**Diagnosis:**
- **Maternal:** Toxoplasma IgM + IgG (acute infection if IgM+ and low-avidity IgG)
- **Fetal:** Amniocentesis (Toxoplasma PCR after 18 weeks)

**Treatment:**
- **Before 18 weeks:** **Spiramycin** 1g PO TID (reduces transmission, does NOT treat fetal infection)
- **After 18 weeks (if fetal infection confirmed):** **Pyrimethamine + sulfadiazine + leucovorin**

**Prevention:** Avoid undercooked meat, unwashed produce, cat litter

---

## 2. CYTOMEGALOVIRUS (CMV)

**Transmission:** Primary maternal CMV → 30-40% transmission

**Congenital CMV Syndrome:**
- **Microcephaly, periventricular calcifications**
- **Sensorineural hearing loss** (most common sequela)
- **Hepatosplenomegaly, thrombocytopenia ("blueberry muffin" rash)

**Diagnosis:**
- Maternal: CMV IgM/IgG, **CMV PCR** (blood, urine)
- Fetal: Amniocentesis (CMV PCR)

**Treatment:** **Valganciclovir** (for neonates with symptomatic congenital CMV)

**Key Point:** **#1 cause of congenital infection** in US (0.5-2% live births)

---

## 3. RUBELLA

**Congenital Rubella Syndrome (CRS):**
- **Cardiac defects** (PDA, pulmonary stenosis)
- **Cataracts, glaucoma**
- **Sensorineural hearing loss**
- **"Blueberry muffin" rash**

**Risk:** **80-90% if infection in 1st trimester** (decreased to <20% after 16 weeks)

**Prevention:** **MMR vaccine BEFORE pregnancy** (live vaccine - contraindicated during pregnancy)

**Key Point:** Rare in US due to vaccination

---

## 4. HERPES SIMPLEX VIRUS (HSV)

**Neonatal HSV:**
- **Transmission:** Primarily **intrapartum** (passage through infected birth canal)
- **Risk:** 30-50% if **primary infection at delivery** vs <3% if recurrent

**Clinical Forms:**
- **Disseminated** (25%) - liver, lungs, CNS (high mortality)
- **CNS** (30%) - encephalitis
- **Skin/eye/mouth** (45%) - localized

**Prevention:**
- **Acyclovir prophylaxis** 400 mg PO TID (from 36 weeks if history of genital HSV)
- **C-section** if active lesions at delivery

**Treatment (Neonatal):** **Acyclovir** 60 mg/kg/day IV ÷ q8h x 14-21 days

---

## 5. SYPHILIS (Congenital Syphilis)

**Transmission:** Transplacental (any stage)

**Congenital Syphilis:**
- **Early (<2 years):** Hepatosplenomegaly, rash (palms/soles), snuffles (rhinitis), osteochondritis
- **Late (>2 years):** Hutchinson teeth, saddle nose, saber shins, CN VIII deafness

**Diagnosis:**
- **Maternal:** RPR/VDRL + confirmatory treponemal test (FTA-ABS, TP-PA)
- **Neonatal:** RPR (≥4-fold higher than maternal titer = congenital)

**Treatment:**
- **Maternal:** **Benzathine penicillin G** 2.4 MU IM x 1-3 doses (by stage)
- **Neonatal:** **Aqueous penicillin G** 50,000 units/kg IV q12h x 10 days

**Key Point:** **Screen ALL pregnant women** (1st trimester, 3rd trimester if high risk)

---

## 6. OTHER KEY INFECTIONS

**ZIKA:**
- **Congenital Zika syndrome:** Microcephaly, brain calcifications, arthrogryposis
- **Avoid travel to endemic areas during pregnancy**

**PARVOVIRUS B19:**
- **Hydrops fetalis** (severe fetal anemia from red cell aplasia)
- **Risk highest in 2nd trimester**

**LISTERIA MONOCYTOGENES:**
- **Maternal:** Flu-like illness, fever, gastroenteritis
- **Fetal:** Sepsis, meningitis, **granulomatosis infantiseptica**
- **Treatment:** **Ampicillin** 2g IV q4h

**VARICELLA (VZV):**
- **Congenital varicella syndrome:** Limb hypoplasia, microcephaly, chorioretinitis (if infection <20 weeks)
- **VZIG** if exposure + non-immune (within 10 days)

---

## ANTIBIOTIC SAFETY IN PREGNANCY

| **Antibiotic Class** | **Safety Category** | **Notes** |
|----------------------|---------------------|-----------|
| **Penicillins** | **SAFE** | Drug of choice for most infections |
| **Cephalosporins** | **SAFE** | All generations safe |
| **Macrolides** | **Azithromycin/Erythromycin SAFE** | Clarithromycin: **AVOID** (cardiovascular risk) |
| **Metronidazole** | **SAFE** (after 1st trimester) | Avoid 1st trimester if possible |
| **Nitrofurantoin** | **AVOID near term** | Risk of hemolytic anemia (G6PD) |
| **Fluoroquinolones** | **AVOID** | Cartilage toxicity (animal studies) |
| **Tetracyclines** | **AVOID** | Teeth discoloration, bone growth inhibition |
| **Aminoglycosides** | **Use with caution** | Ototoxicity, nephrotoxicity (monitor levels) |
| **TMP-SMX** | **AVOID 1st trimester, near term** | Neural tube defects (1st), kernicterus (term) |
| **Vancomycin** | **SAFE (with monitoring)** | Monitor levels (ototoxicity risk) |

**Key Point:** **Penicillins & cephalosporins = safest antibiotics in pregnancy**

---

## COMMON INFECTIONS IN PREGNANCY

**URINARY TRACT INFECTIONS:**
- **Asymptomatic bacteriuria:** Treat (prevents pyelonephritis, preterm labor)
- **Treatment:** **Nitrofurantoin** 100 mg PO BID x 5-7 days (avoid near term) OR **cephalexin** 500 mg PO QID x 5-7 days
- **Screen at 12-16 weeks** with urine culture

**GROUP B STREPTOCOCCUS (GBS):**
- **Screen at 35-37 weeks** (vaginal-rectal culture)
- **Intrapartum prophylaxis:** **Penicillin G** 5 MU IV load → 2.5-3 MU IV q4h until delivery

**INFLUENZA:**
- **Treatment:** **Oseltamivir** 75 mg PO BID x 5 days (SAFE in pregnancy)
- **Vaccination:** Inactivated influenza vaccine **recommended** in all trimesters

---

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **TORCH = Toxo, Other, Rubella, CMV, HSV**
- **CMV = #1 congenital infection** in US (sensorineural hearing loss)
- **Syphilis: Screen ALL pregnant women** (1st + 3rd trimester if high risk)
- **HSV: Acyclovir prophylaxis from 36 weeks** if history of genital HSV
- **GBS: Screen 35-37 weeks**, intrapartum penicillin prophylaxis
- **Penicillins & cephalosporins = safest antibiotics** in pregnancy
- **AVOID: Tetracyclines, fluoroquinolones, TMP-SMX (1st trimester/term)**
- **Treat asymptomatic bacteriuria** (prevents pyelonephritis, preterm labor)
