## Meningitis - Leptomeningeal Enhancement

**Q:** What are the characteristic MRI findings of bacterial meningitis including leptomeningeal enhancement patterns, and when is imaging indicated?

**A:**

## DEFINITION

**Leptomeningeal Enhancement:** **"Abnormal enhancement of the pia and arachnoid mater (leptomeninges) on post-contrast MRI"**

**Normal:** Leptomeninges do NOT enhance (blood-meninges barrier intact)

**Abnormal:** Enhancement indicates meningeal inflammation/infection

**Key Point:** **Leptomeningeal enhancement = meningitis** (infection/inflammation of pia-arachnoid)

---

## ANATOMY

**Meninges (3 Layers):**

1. **Dura mater** (outermost, pachymeninx)
2. **Arachnoid mater** (middle, leptomeninx)
3. **Pia mater** (innermost, leptomeninx)

**Leptomeninges = Pia + Arachnoid** (subarachnoid space between them)

**Key Point:** **Leptomeningeal enhancement = pia + arachnoid inflammation**

---

## MRI FINDINGS

**Hallmark: Leptomeningeal Enhancement on Post-Contrast T1**

### **Mnemonic: "ENHANCE-LEPTOMENINGES"**

**E**nhancement (abnormal, post-contrast T1)
**N**ormal meninges don't enhance
**H**igh signal (bright) on post-contrast images
**A**rachnoid + pia (leptomeninges)
**N**ot dura (pachymeninges - different pattern)
**C**isternsabnormal (basilar cistterns in TB meningitis)
**E**xtends into sulci (follows brain surface)

**L**inear enhancement (thin, smooth)
**E**arly finding (may appear before LP positive)
**P**ia-arachnoid (leptomeninges)
**T**hin layer (smooth, regular)
**O**ver convexity (cerebral hemispheres)
**M**ay be subtle (require fat-saturated T1)
**E**ntire brain surface (diffuse or focal)
**N**ormal variant minimal (vs pathologic)
**I**ntercommunicating (subarachnoid space)
**N**odular if fungal/TB (vs smooth bacterial)
**G**eneralized (diffuse) or localized
**E**xudates in cisterns (TB, fungal)
**S**ubtle early (may be normal initially)

**Key Point:** **Post-contrast T1 enhancement of brain surface = leptomeningeal enhancement**

---

## MRI SEQUENCES

### **FLAIR (Most Sensitive for Acute Meningitis):**
- **Hyperintense signal in sulci/cisterns** (inflammatory exudate)
- **Loss of normal CSF "black" signal**
- **Subarachnoid space bright** (protein, WBCs in CSF)
- **More sensitive than post-contrast T1** for acute bacterial meningitis

**Key Point:** **FLAIR hyperintensity in sulci = meningitis** (even without contrast)

---

### **Post-Contrast T1 Fat-Saturated:**
- **Leptomeningeal enhancement** (thin, smooth)
- **Extends into sulci** (follows gyri/sulci)
- **Diffuse or focal**
- **Basilar cisterns** (especially TB, fungal)

**Key Point:** **Fat-saturated T1 post-contrast = best for leptomeningeal enhancement**

---

### **DWI/ADC:**
- **May show restricted diffusion** in subarachnoid space (purulent exudate)
- **Suggests pyogenic bacterial meningitis**
- **Ventriculitis:** Intraventricular debris restricts

**Key Point:** **Restricted diffusion in subarachnoid space = purulent exudate**

---

## PATTERN BY ORGANISM

**Bacterial (Pyogenic):**
- **Diffuse leptomeningeal enhancement** (entire brain surface)
- **Smooth, linear** enhancement
- **Convexity** predominant
- **FLAIR hyperintensity** (prominent)

**Tuberculous:**
- **Basilar predominance** (basal cisterns - most characteristic)
- **Thick enhancement** (exudative)
- **Hydrocephalus** (common - communicating)
- **Infarcts** (arteritis, vascular involvement)
- **Tuberculomas** (ring-enhancing mass lesions)

**Fungal (Cryptococcus, Coccidioides):**
- **Basilar cisterns** (similar to TB)
- **Nodular enhancement** (vs smooth bacterial)
- **Gelatinous pseudocysts** (Cryptococcus - dilated perivascular spaces)
- **Hydrocephalus**

**Viral (Aseptic):**
- **Usually NO leptomeningeal enhancement** (mild if present)
- **More FLAIR hyperintensity** than enhancement

**Key Point:** **Basilar cisterns = TB/fungal | Diffuse convexity = bacterial pyogenic**

---

## WHEN IMAGING IS INDICATED

**CT/MRI Before Lumbar Puncture If:**

**Mnemonic: "MASS EFFECT"**

**M**ass effect suspected (papilledema, focal deficit)
**A**ltered mental status (GCS <11)
**S**eizure (new-onset, focal)
**S**uspicion of mass lesion (brain abscess, tumor)

**E**dema (cerebral edema, increased ICP)
**F**ocal neurologic deficit (hemiparesis, CN palsy)
**F**undoscopic changes (papilledema)
**E**levated ICP signs (headache, vomiting, decreased consciousness)
**C**oma (decreased level of consciousness)
**T**rauma (head injury with meningitis)

**Immunocompromised** (risk of focal lesions like Toxoplasma, abscess)

**Key Point:** **Focal signs or papilledema = image before LP** (herniation risk)

---

## WHEN IMAGING NOT NEEDED (Can Go Straight to LP)

**If ALL of the following:**
- **No focal neurologic deficit**
- **No papilledema**
- **Normal mental status** (GCS 15)
- **No seizures**
- **No immunocompromise**

**Key Point:** **Classic bacterial meningitis (fever, headache, stiff neck, normal mental status) = LP first** (imaging delays treatment)

---

## COMPLICATIONS VISIBLE ON IMAGING

**Mnemonic: "COMPLICATIONS"**

**C**erebral edema (diffuse swelling, sulcal effacement)
**O**bstructive hydrocephalus (ventriculomegaly)
**M**idline shift (if mass effect)
**P**yogenic ventriculitis (ependymal enhancement)
**L**eptomeningeal enhancement (persistent)
**I**nfarcts (arteritis, venous thrombosis)
**C**ommunicating hydrocephalus (TB, fungal)
**A**bscess formation (subdural empyema, brain abscess)
**T**hrombosis (venous sinus thrombosis)
**I**schemia (arterial infarcts)
**O**blitertive arteritis (TB, fungal)
**N**ecrosis (brain infarction)
**S**ubdural empyema (complication)

**Key Point:** **Complications: Edema, hydrocephalus, infarcts, empyema, abscess**

---

## DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS OF LEPTOMENINGEAL ENHANCEMENT

**Mnemonic: "MENINGITIS-CARCINOMATOSIS"**

**Infectious:**
- **M**eningitis (bacterial, TB, fungal)
- **E**ncephalitis (viral - minimal enhancement)
- **N**eurosyphilis (tertiary syphilis)
- **I**nfection (Lyme, brucellosis)
- **N**eurologic Lyme disease
- **G**ranulomatous (TB, sarcoid)
- **I**nfectious (bacterial, viral, fungal)
- **T**B meningitis (basilar)
- **I**nfection (any cause)
- **S**yphilis (neurosyphilis)

**Non-Infectious:**
- **C**arcinomatosis (leptomeningeal metastases - breast, lung, melanoma)
- **A**utoimmune (lupus, rheumatoid, Behçet's)
- **R**heumatologic (lupus meningitis)
- **C**hemical meningitis (post-LP, intrathecal drugs)
- **I**ntrathecal chemotherapy (chemical irritation)
- **N**eurosarcoidosis (granulomatous)
- **O**ther inflammatory (autoimmune)
- **M**etastases (leptomeningeal carcinomatosis)
- **A**utoimmune encephalitis
- **T**umor (primary CNS lymphoma, gliomatosis)
- **O**ther (Behçet's, sarcoid)
- **S**arcoidosis (neurosarcoidosis)
- **I**rritation (chemical, post-surgical)
- **S**ubarachnoid hemorrhage (blood irritation)

**Key Point:** **Infection #1, but must exclude carcinomatosis** (clinical context + LP)

---

## CSF FINDINGS BY ORGANISM

**Bacterial (Pyogenic):**
- **WBC:** >1000 (PMN predominant >80%)
- **Protein:** >200 mg/dL (elevated)
- **Glucose:** <40 mg/dL (<50% serum - low)
- **Gram stain:** Positive in 60-80%
- **Culture:** Positive in 70-85%

**Tuberculous:**
- **WBC:** 100-500 (lymphocyte predominant)
- **Protein:** >100 mg/dL (markedly elevated)
- **Glucose:** <45 mg/dL (low)
- **AFB smear:** Positive in 10-20% (insensitive)
- **Culture:** Takes 4-8 weeks
- **PCR:** More sensitive

**Viral (Aseptic):**
- **WBC:** 10-500 (lymphocyte predominant)
- **Protein:** Normal to mildly elevated
- **Glucose:** Normal
- **Viral PCR:** (HSV, enterovirus)

**Fungal (Cryptococcus):**
- **WBC:** 20-500 (lymphocyte predominant)
- **Protein:** Elevated
- **Glucose:** Low
- **India ink:** Positive in 50-80% (HIV patients)
- **Cryptococcal antigen:** >95% sensitive

**Key Point:** **Low glucose + high protein + high WBC = bacterial meningitis**

---

## TREATMENT IMPLICATIONS

**If Leptomeningeal Enhancement Seen:**

1. **Confirms meningitis** (supports diagnosis)
2. **Look for complications** (hydrocephalus, infarcts, abscess)
3. **Serial imaging** (monitor treatment response, complications)

**Empiric Antibiotics (Based on Age/Risk):**

**Neonates (<1 month):**
- **Ampicillin** + **Cefotaxime** (or gentamicin)

**Adults (Immunocompetent):**
- **Ceftriaxone** 2g IV q12h + **Vancomycin** 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h

**Adults (>50 years or immunocompromised):**
- **Ceftriaxone** + **Vancomycin** + **Ampicillin** (add Listeria coverage)

**Dexamethasone:** 10mg IV q6h x 4 days (if pneumococcal suspected)

**Key Point:** **Start antibiotics immediately** (don't delay for imaging if high suspicion)

---

## PROGNOSIS

**Imaging Predictors of Poor Outcome:**
- **Cerebral edema** (sulcal effacement, midline shift)
- **Hydrocephalus**
- **Infarcts** (arteritis, venous thrombosis)
- **Subdural empyema** or brain abscess
- **Basilar enhancement** (TB, fungal - worse prognosis)

**Overall Mortality:**
- **Bacterial meningitis:** 10-20% (despite treatment)
- **TB meningitis:** 20-50%
- **Fungal meningitis:** 30-50% (HIV patients)

**Key Point:** **Complications on imaging = worse prognosis**

---

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **Leptomeningeal enhancement = pia + arachnoid inflammation (meningitis)**
- **FLAIR hyperintensity in sulci = most sensitive** for acute bacterial meningitis
- **Post-contrast T1 fat-saturated = best for leptomeningeal enhancement**
- **Basilar cisterns = TB/fungal | Diffuse convexity = bacterial pyogenic**
- **Imaging before LP if: Focal signs, papilledema, altered mental status, seizures, immunocompromised**
- **Classic meningitis (no focal signs) = LP first** (imaging delays treatment)
- **Complications: Edema, hydrocephalus, infarcts, empyema, abscess**
- **Differential: Meningitis (infection), carcinomatosis (metastases), sarcoidosis**
- **Start antibiotics immediately** (don't delay for imaging)
- **Dexamethasone 10mg IV q6h x 4 days** if pneumococcal suspected
