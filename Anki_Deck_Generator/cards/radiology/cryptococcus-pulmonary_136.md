## Cryptococcus - Pulmonary Nodules & Reverse Halo Sign

**Q:** What are the characteristic CT findings of pulmonary cryptococcosis including nodule patterns, reverse halo sign, and associated features?

**A:**

## DEFINITION

**Pulmonary Cryptococcosis:** Fungal infection caused by ***Cryptococcus neoformans*** (pigeon droppings) or ***C. gattii*** (eucalyptus trees).

**Key Point:** ***C. neoformans* = immunocompromised (HIV CD4 <100)** | ***C. gattii* = immunocompetent (endemic areas)**

---

## CT FINDINGS

### **Nodules (Most Common Pattern):**

**Single or Multiple Pulmonary Nodules:**
- **Size:** 0.5-4cm (variable)
- **Shape:** Round, well-defined
- **Number:** Single (40%) or multiple (60%)
- **Location:** Subpleural or peribronchovascular
- **Enhancement:** May enhance peripherally
- **Cavitation:** 15-20% (thin-walled)

**Key Point:** **Subpleural nodules in HIV patient = consider cryptococcosis**

---

### **Reverse Halo Sign (Atoll Sign):**

**Definition:** **"Ground-glass opacity surrounded by ring of consolidation"**

**Significance in Cryptococcus:**
- **Organizing pneumonia pattern**
- **Seen in 10-15% of cases**
- **More common in immunocompromised**

**Differential of Reverse Halo:**
- **Organizing pneumonia** (COP) - most common
- **Cryptococcosis, mucormycosis** (fungal)
- **TB** (especially HIV patients)
- **Pulmonary infarction**

**Key Point:** **Reverse halo in HIV patient = fungal infection** (crypto, mucor)

---

## PATTERNS BY IMMUNE STATUS

| **Immune Status** | **CT Pattern** |
|-------------------|----------------|
| **Immunocompetent** | Single nodule, mass-like (mimics cancer) |
| **HIV/AIDS** | **Multiple nodules, consolidation, interstitial infiltrates** |
| **Transplant** | Disseminated nodules, cavitation |

**Key Point:** **HIV patients = multiple nodules + consolidation + CNS involvement**

---

## CONSOLIDATION PATTERN

**Mass-Like Consolidation:**
- **Wedge-shaped or peripheral**
- **Air bronchograms** (may be present)
- **Mimics bacterial pneumonia** or malignancy

**Key Point:** **Peripheral wedge consolidation = consider cryptococcus** (especially if post-transplant)

---

## ASSOCIATED FINDINGS

**Mnemonic: "CRYPTO"**

**C**NS involvement (meningoencephalitis - check brain MRI)
**R**everse halo sign (organizing pneumonia pattern)
**Y**east in CSF (India ink, antigen)
**P**leural effusion (10-20%)
**T**hin-walled cavities (15-20%)
**O**ften immunocompromised (HIV, transplant, steroids)

---

## CLINICAL PRESENTATION

**Pulmonary Symptoms:**
- **Asymptomatic** (40% - incidental nodule)
- **Cough, dyspnea** (subacute)
- **Fever, chest pain** (less common)

**CNS Involvement (Most Important):**
- **Meningoencephalitis** (headache, altered mental status)
- **Always check for CNS disease** (LP, brain MRI)

**Key Point:** **Always perform LP in HIV patient with pulmonary cryptococcosis** (rule out meningitis)

---

## DIAGNOSIS

**Serum/CSF Cryptococcal Antigen:**
- **Highly sensitive** (>95% in disseminated disease)
- **Positive in both pulmonary and CNS disease**

**Culture:** Sputum, BAL, blood, CSF
**Histopathology:** Mucicarmine stain (red capsule), GMS
**India ink:** CSF (visualizes encapsulated yeast)

**Key Point:** **Serum cryptococcal antigen = screening test** (check in all HIV patients with lung nodules)

---

**Clinical Pearls:**
- ***C. neoformans* = pigeon droppings, HIV CD4 <100**
- ***C. gattii* = eucalyptus trees, immunocompetent (Pacific Northwest, Australia)**
- **Pulmonary findings: Subpleural nodules (single or multiple)**
- **Reverse halo sign = organizing pneumonia pattern** (10-15% of cases)
- **HIV patients = multiple nodules + risk of CNS involvement**
- **Always perform LP** to rule out cryptococcal meningitis
- **Diagnosis: Serum cryptococcal antigen** (>95% sensitive)
- **Treatment: Amphotericin B + flucytosine (induction), fluconazole (consolidation/maintenance)**
