## Osteomyelitis - MRI Findings

**Q:** What are the comprehensive MRI findings of osteomyelitis across all sequences (T1, T2, STIR, DWI, post-contrast), and how do you distinguish infection from other marrow abnormalities?

**A:**

## MRI AS GOLD STANDARD

**Sensitivity:** 90-95%
**Specificity:** 80-90%

**Advantages:**
- **Detects early** (within 1-3 days of symptom onset)
- **X-ray takes 10-14 days** (30-50% bone loss required)
- **Best soft tissue detail** (abscess, sinus tracts, muscle involvement)
- **Multi-planar** (coronal, sagittal, axial)

**Key Point:** **MRI = gold standard** (90-95% sensitive, detects 10-14 days before X-ray)

## STIR (Short Tau Inversion Recovery)

**Advantages:**
- **Fat suppression** (nulls fat signal)
- **Sensitive for edema** (bright on STIR = edema)
- **Detects subtle marrow edema** (more sensitive than T2)

**Findings:**
- **Bright marrow** (edema)
- **Bright soft tissues** (periosteal, muscle edema)
- **Periosteal elevation** visible (fluid separating periosteum from cortex)

**Key Point:** **STIR most sensitive for marrow edema** (better than T2 for subtle changes)

---

## DIFFERENTIATING ABSCESS FROM PHLEGMON

| **Feature** | **Abscess** | **Phlegmon** |
|-------------|-------------|--------------|
| **Definition** | Loculated pus | Inflammatory edema (no pus) |
| **T2 signal** | Hyperintense | Hyperintense |
| **Enhancement** | **Rim enhancement** | **Diffuse enhancement** |
| **DWI** | **Bright (restricted)** | **Dark (facilitated)** |
| **ADC** | **Dark (low ADC)** | **Bright (high ADC)** |
| **Treatment** | **Drainage required** | Antibiotics alone |

**Key Point:** **Restricted diffusion (DWI bright, ADC dark) = abscess** (requires drainage)

---

## ASSOCIATED SOFT TISSUE FINDINGS

**Periosteum:**
- **Elevated periosteum** (fluid/pus separating from cortex)
- **Periosteal enhancement** (inflamed periosteum)
- **Fluid signal** (T2 bright) between periosteum and cortex

**Muscle:**
- **Edema** (T2 hyperintense striated pattern)
- **Myositis** (diffuse muscle enhancement)
- **Abscess** (rim-enhancing fluid collection in muscle)

**Soft Tissue Abscess:**
- **T1 hypointense** (fluid)
- **T2 hyperintense** (fluid)
- **Rim enhancement** (abscess wall)
- **Restricted diffusion** (DWI bright, ADC dark)

**Sinus Tract:**
- **T2 hyperintense track** (fluid-filled channel from bone to skin)
- **Enhancement** (inflamed tract)
- **Connects bone abscess to skin** (chronic osteomyelitis)

**Key Point:** **Periosteal fluid + muscle edema + soft tissue abscess = extensive infection**

---

## CORTICAL FINDINGS

**Cortical Breach:**
- **Interruption of cortical line** (normally dark on all sequences)
- **Hyperintense gap** (T2) where cortex disrupted
- **Enhancement through cortex** (infectious spread)

**Endosteal Scalloping:**
- **Inner cortex erosion** (medullary infection eroding cortex from inside)
- **Thinned cortex** (may be at risk of pathologic fracture)

**Key Point:** **Cortical breach visible on MRI** (indicates extension beyond bone marrow)

---

## ACUTE vs CHRONIC OSTEOMYELITIS (MRI)

**Acute Osteomyelitis:**
- **Bone marrow edema** (T1 dark, T2 bright)
- **Diffuse enhancement**
- **Soft tissue edema** (periosteal, muscle)
- **May have abscess** (rim-enhancing)
- **No sequestrum** (acute)

**Chronic Osteomyelitis:**
- **Sequestrum** (T1/T2 hypointense - dead bone, no signal)
- **Involucrum** (surrounding new bone)
- **Cloaca/sinus tracts** (T2 bright tracks)
- **Sclerotic bone** (low signal T1/T2)
- **Less marrow edema** (compared to acute)

**Key Point:** **Sequestrum on MRI = T1/T2 hypointense** (no signal - dead avascular bone)

---

## SEQUESTRUM ON MRI

**T1:** Hypointense (dark - no marrow, avascular)
**T2:** Hypointense (dark - dense sclerotic bone, no fluid)
**Post-contrast:** **NO enhancement** (avascular - key feature)

**Surrounding:**
- **Enhancing granulation tissue** (rim around sequestrum)
- **Involucrum enhances** (living bone with blood supply)

**Key Point:** **Sequestrum does NOT enhance** (avascular dead bone - no blood supply)

---

## VERTEBRAL OSTEOMYELITIS (Discitis)

**Classic Triad:**
1. **Vertebral body marrow edema** (T1 dark, T2 bright)
2. **Disc involvement** (T2 hyperintense - loss of normal dark disc)
3. **Endplate destruction** (irregular endplates)

**Additional Findings:**
- **Disc enhancement** (normally disc doesn't enhance - key sign of infection)
- **Paravertebral abscess** (rim-enhancing fluid collection)
- **Epidural abscess** (cord compression - surgical emergency)
- **Psoas abscess** (if lumbar)

**Key Point:** **Disc hyperintense T2 + disc enhancement = discitis** (infection)

---

## DIABETIC FOOT OSTEOMYELITIS

**Challenges:**
- **Neuropathic arthropathy (Charcot)** mimics infection
- **Soft tissue ulcer** often present

**MRI Findings Favoring Osteomyelitis:**
- **Bone marrow edema** (T1 dark, T2 bright)
- **Enhancement** of bone marrow
- **Soft tissue ulcer** tract to bone
- **Sinus tract** (T2 hyperintense track)
- **Cortical disruption** (break in cortex adjacent to ulcer)

**Differentiating from Charcot:**
- **Charcot:** Subchondral cysts, fragmentation, preserved marrow fat signal
- **Osteomyelitis:** Marrow edema (T1 dark, T2 bright), enhancement

**Key Point:** **Cortical disruption + sinus tract + marrow edema = diabetic foot osteomyelitis**

---

## LIMITATIONS OF MRI

**False Positives:**
- **Fracture edema** (marrow edema, enhancement without infection)
- **Neuropathic arthropathy** (Charcot - mimics infection)
- **Tumor** (marrow replacement, enhancement)
- **Post-surgical changes** (edema, enhancement can persist months)

**False Negatives:**
- **Very early infection** (<24 hours - may not see changes)
- **Chronic sclerotic osteomyelitis** (minimal edema, low signal)

**Cannot Image:**
- **Metallic hardware** (artifacts obscure detail)
- **Pacemakers** (contraindication to MRI)

**Key Point:** **MRI non-specific** (marrow edema + enhancement can be fracture, tumor, post-op)

---

## DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS OF MRI MARROW ABNORMALITIES

**Mnemonic: "INFECTION-FRACTURE-TUMOR-INFARCT"**

**Infection (Osteomyelitis):**
- **T1 dark, T2 bright** + enhancement + soft tissue changes
- **Clinical context** (pain, fever, elevated CRP)

**Fracture (Bone Contusion, Stress Fracture):**
- **T1 dark, T2 bright** (marrow edema)
- **Linear low signal** (fracture line on T1/T2)
- **History of trauma** (or repetitive stress)
- **No soft tissue abscess** (key differentiator)

**Tumor (Metastases, Myeloma, Primary Bone Tumor):**
- **T1 dark** (marrow replacement)
- **T2 variable** (depending on tumor type)
- **Enhancement** (variable)
- **Mass effect** (expansile, cortical destruction)
- **No periosteal fluid** (unlike infection)

**Infarct (Bone Infarction):**
- **Serpentine low signal rim** (T1/T2 - necrotic marrow)
- **Central fatty marrow preserved** (unlike infection replaces fat)
- **No enhancement** of infarcted bone (avascular)
- **Sickle cell disease** or vaso-occlusive crisis

**Post-Operative Changes:**
- **Marrow edema + enhancement** (can persist 6-12 months)
- **History of surgery** (key)
- **Follows surgical site**

**Key Point:** **Soft tissue abscess + periosteal fluid + clinical context = infection** (vs other causes)

---

## MRI FOLLOW-UP

**When to Repeat MRI:**
- **Assess treatment response** (persistent symptoms despite antibiotics)
- **Evaluate complications** (abscess formation, sinus tract)
- **Rule out sequestrum** (if chronic course)
- **Plan surgery** (extent of debridement needed)

**Timing:**
- **Not immediately** (edema/enhancement persists weeks to months despite treatment)
- **If clinical failure** (persistent fever, pain after 2-4 weeks antibiotics)
- **Pre-operative planning** (if surgery planned)

**Key Point:** **MRI abnormalities lag clinical improvement** (edema/enhancement persist months)

---

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **MRI = gold standard** (90-95% sensitive, detects 1-3 days after symptom onset)
- **T1 hypointense marrow = infection** (replaces normal bright fatty marrow)
- **T2/STIR hyperintense marrow + soft tissue edema = osteomyelitis**
- **Rim enhancement + central non-enhancing = abscess** (requires drainage)
- **DWI bright + ADC dark = purulent abscess** (restricted diffusion, 80-100% specific)
- **Sequestrum does NOT enhance** (avascular dead bone, T1/T2 hypointense)
- **Involucrum ENHANCES** (living bone with blood supply)
- **Disc hyperintense T2 + disc enhancement = discitis** (vertebral osteomyelitis)
- **Cortical breach + periosteal fluid = extension beyond marrow**
- **Soft tissue abscess + periosteal fluid + clinical context = infection** (vs fracture/tumor)
- **MRI abnormalities lag clinical improvement** (edema/enhancement persist months)
