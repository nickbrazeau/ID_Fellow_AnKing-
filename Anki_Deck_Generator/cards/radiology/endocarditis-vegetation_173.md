## Endocarditis - Vegetation

**Q:** What are the characteristic echocardiographic findings of endocarditis including vegetation appearance on TTE vs TEE, and what other imaging modalities detect complications?

**A:**

## DEFINITION

**Vegetation:** **"Infected mass of fibrin, platelets, inflammatory cells, and microorganisms adherent to valve leaflet or endocardial surface"**

**Echocardiography Hallmark:** **Mobile, oscillating mass attached to valve leaflet**

**Key Point:** **Vegetation = mobile echogenic mass** on valve leaflet (hallmark of endocarditis)

---

## DUKE CRITERIA (Modified)

**Diagnosis of Endocarditis:**

**Major Criteria:**
1. **Positive blood cultures** (2 separate, typical organisms - S. aureus, Viridans strep, Enterococcus, HACEK, S. lugdunensis)
2. **Endocardial involvement** (echocardiographic evidence):
   - **Vegetation** (mobile mass on valve)
   - **Abscess** (perivalvular)
   - **New valvular regurgitation** (new murmur)
   - **Prosthetic valve dehiscence**

**Minor Criteria:**
- **Predisposing condition** (IVDU, prosthetic valve, prior endocarditis)
- **Fever** (>38Â°C)
- **Vascular phenomena** (emboli, septic pulmonary infarcts, mycotic aneurysm, Janeway lesions)
- **Immunologic phenomena** (Osler nodes, Roth spots, glomerulonephritis, positive rheumatoid factor)
- **Microbiologic evidence** (positive blood culture not meeting major criteria, serologic evidence)

**Diagnosis:**
- **Definite:** 2 major OR 1 major + 3 minor OR 5 minor
- **Possible:** 1 major + 1 minor OR 3 minor

**Key Point:** **Duke criteria: 2 major OR 1 major + 3 minor = definite endocarditis**

---

## ECHOCARDIOGRAPHY FINDINGS

**TTE (Transthoracic Echocardiography) vs TEE (Transesophageal Echocardiography):**

| **Feature** | **TTE** | **TEE** |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| **Sensitivity** | **40-60%** (limited) | **90-95%** (gold standard) |
| **Specificity** | 90-95% | 90-95% |
| **Best for** | Screening | Definitive diagnosis, prosthetic valves, complications |
| **Limitations** | Obesity, COPD, poor windows | Invasive (requires sedation) |
| **Prosthetic valve** | **Poor** (metallic artifact) | **Excellent** |
| **Abscess detection** | **20-30%** | **90-95%** |

**Key Point:** **TEE = gold standard** (90-95% sensitive) | **TTE = screening** (40-60% sensitive)

---

### **LOCATION OF VEGETATION:**

**Native Valve Endocarditis:**
- **Mitral valve** (40-50% - most common, anterior leaflet)
- **Aortic valve** (30-40%)
- **Tricuspid valve** (10-20%, IVDU - 50% if IVDU)
- **Pulmonic valve** (<5%, rare)

**Prosthetic Valve Endocarditis:**
- **Aortic prosthesis > mitral prosthesis**
- **Vegetation on sewing ring** or valve leaflets

**IVDU:**
- **Tricuspid valve** (50% - most common in IVDU)
- **Right-sided endocarditis** (tricuspid, pulmonic)

**Key Point:** **Native valve: Mitral = most common** | **IVDU: Tricuspid = most common**

---

## CARDIAC CT FINDINGS

**CT Advantages:**
- **Detects perivalvular abscess** (90% sensitive)
- **Evaluates coronary arteries** (pre-operative planning)
- **Detects mycotic aneurysm** (Valsalva sinus, coronary)

**Findings:**
- **Vegetation** (soft tissue mass on valve)
- **Abscess** (perivalvular low-attenuation area with rim enhancement)
- **Pseudoaneurysm** (outpouching from annulus)
- **Mycotic aneurysm** (aortic root, coronary arteries)

**Key Point:** **CT excellent for abscess** (90% sensitive, alternative to TEE)

---

## CARDIAC MRI FINDINGS

**MRI Advantages:**
- **No radiation**
- **Excellent soft tissue characterization**
- **Detects abscess, fistula**

**Findings:**
- **Vegetation** (T1 intermediate, T2 hyperintense)
- **Abscess** (perivalvular - rim enhancement)
- **Valvular regurgitation** (phase-contrast imaging quantifies)

**Limitations:**
- **Long scan time** (not suitable for unstable patients)
- **Metallic prosthetic valves** (artifact)

**Key Point:** **MRI less commonly used** (echocardiography and CT preferred)

---

## PET-CT FINDINGS (18F-FDG PET-CT)

**PET-CT Advantages:**
- **Detects prosthetic valve endocarditis** (high sensitivity)
- **Detects extracardiac emboli** (spleen, kidney, brain)
- **Guides diagnosis if echo non-diagnostic**

**Findings:**
- **Increased FDG uptake** around prosthetic valve (infection)
- **Embolic lesions** (spleen, kidney - septic infarcts)

**Utility:**
- **Prosthetic valve endocarditis** (when TEE non-diagnostic)
- **Evaluating extracardiac complications**

**Key Point:** **PET-CT useful for prosthetic valve endocarditis** (when TEE inconclusive)

---

## CLINICAL PRESENTATION

**Symptoms:**
- **Fever** (80-90%)
- **New murmur** (70-85%, regurgitant)
- **Fatigue, malaise** (60-70%)
- **Weight loss** (30-40%, chronic)
- **Night sweats** (30-40%)

**Physical Exam:**
- **New regurgitant murmur** (70-85%)
- **Splenomegaly** (30-40%, chronic)
- **Peripheral stigmata:**
  - **Osler nodes** (painful nodules on fingers/toes - 10-20%)
  - **Janeway lesions** (painless palmar/plantar macules - 10-15%)
  - **Splinter hemorrhages** (nailbeds - 10-20%)
  - **Roth spots** (retinal hemorrhages with white center - 5-10%)

**Key Point:** **Fever + new murmur = endocarditis** (until proven otherwise)

---

## ORGANISMS

**Native Valve:**
- ***Staphylococcus aureus*** (30-40% - most common)
- **Viridans Streptococcus** (20-30%)
- ***Enterococcus*** (10-20%)
- ***Streptococcus gallolyticus* (S. bovis)** (colon cancer association)
- **HACEK** (Haemophilus, Aggregatibacter, Cardiobacterium, Eikenella, Kingella - 5%)

**Prosthetic Valve:**
- **Early (<12 months):** Coagulase-negative Staph (S. epidermidis), S. aureus
- **Late (>12 months):** Similar to native valve (S. aureus, Viridans strep)

**IVDU:**
- ***S. aureus*** (70-80% - most common in IVDU)
- ***Pseudomonas aeruginosa*** (5-10%)
- ***Candida*** (fungal - 5%)

**Culture-Negative (5-10%):**
- **HACEK organisms** (fastidious, require prolonged culture)
- ***Coxiella burnetii*** (Q fever)
- ***Bartonella*** species
- ***Tropheryma whipplei*** (Whipple disease)
- **Prior antibiotics** (most common cause of culture-negative)

**Key Point:** **S. aureus = most common overall** (30-40%) | **IVDU = S. aureus (70-80%)**

---

## PROGNOSIS

**In-Hospital Mortality:** 15-20%

**Factors Predicting Poor Outcome:**
1. ***S. aureus*** infection (worse than Strep)
2. **Prosthetic valve endocarditis** (worse than native)
3. **Perivalvular abscess** (30-50% mortality)
4. **Heart failure** (acute severe regurgitation)
5. **CNS emboli** (stroke, abscess)
6. **Delayed diagnosis** (>1 week)
7. **Immunosuppression** (HIV, diabetes)

**Key Point:** **S. aureus = worst prognosis** (highest mortality) | **Abscess = 30-50% mortality**

---

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **Vegetation = mobile oscillating echogenic mass on valve** (hallmark)
- **TEE = gold standard** (90-95% sensitive) | **TTE = screening** (40-60% sensitive)
- **Native valve: Mitral = most common** | **IVDU: Tricuspid = most common**
- **Duke criteria: 2 major OR 1 major + 3 minor = definite endocarditis**
- **Fever + new murmur = endocarditis** (until proven otherwise)
- **S. aureus = most common overall** (30-40%) | **IVDU = S. aureus (70-80%)**
- **Abscess (10-30%) = most severe complication** | **TEE superior** for abscess (90-95%)
- **Surgical indications: Abscess, acute severe regurgitation, recurrent emboli, heart failure**
- **S. aureus = worst prognosis** | **Abscess = 30-50% mortality**
- **PET-CT useful for prosthetic valve endocarditis** (when TEE inconclusive)
- **CT excellent for abscess detection** (90% sensitive, alternative to TEE)
