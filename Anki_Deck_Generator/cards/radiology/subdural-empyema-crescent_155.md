## Subdural Empyema - Crescent-Shaped Collection

**Q:** What are the characteristic imaging features of subdural empyema, and why is it considered a neurosurgical emergency?

**A:**

## DEFINITION

**Subdural Empyema:** **"Collection of pus in the subdural space (between dura mater and arachnoid mater)"**

**Imaging Hallmark:** **Crescent-shaped extra-axial fluid collection with restricted diffusion**

**Key Point:** **Subdural empyema = neurosurgical emergency** (high mortality if untreated)

---

## PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

**Location:** **Subdural space** (potential space between dura and arachnoid)

**Sources:**

**Mnemonic: "SINUS-TRAUMA-SURGERY"**

**S**inusitis (paranasal - most common, 40-60%)
**I**ntracranial extension from meningitis
**N**eurosurgery (post-craniotomy, 10-20%)
**U**pper respiratory infection (frontal sinusitis most common)
**S**kull osteomyelitis

**T**rauma (penetrating head injury, compound skull fracture)
**R**upture of brain abscess into subdural space
**A**djacent infection (mastoiditis, otitis media)
**U**nderlying meningitis (bacterial)
**M**iddle ear infection (otogenic)
**A**erobic and anaerobic mix

**S**pread from adjacent sinusitis (frontal > ethmoid > maxillary)
**U**ncommon hematogenous spread
**R**arely spontaneous
**G**rowing infection (rapid progression)
**E**xtradural abscess extension
**R**upture from brain parenchymal abscess
**Y**outh (children and young adults predominantly)

**Key Point:** **Frontal sinusitis = #1 cause** (40-60%)

---

## MRI CHARACTERISTICS

**Hallmark: Crescent-Shaped Extra-Axial Collection**

### **Mnemonic: "CRESCENT DWI"**

**C**rescent-shaped (follows contour of brain surface)
**R**estricted diffusion (**DWI bright, ADC dark** - purulent)
**E**xtra-axial (outside brain parenchyma, subdural space)
**S**ubdural space (between dura and arachnoid)
**C**onvexity (over cerebral hemisphere - most common)
**E**nhancement (meningeal enhancement adjacent to empyema)
**N**arrow (thin layer, follows sulci and gyri)
**T**apers at edges (vs epidural lens-shaped)

**D**WI hyperintense (bright - confirms purulent nature)
**W**all enhancement (adjacent meningeal inflammation)
**I**nterhemispheric fissure (common location - 20-30%)

**Key Point:** **Crescent-shaped + restricted diffusion + extra-axial = subdural empyema**

---

## LOCATION PATTERNS

**Most Common Sites:**

1. **Cerebral convexity** (70-80%) - over frontal/parietal lobes
2. **Interhemispheric fissure** (20-30%) - between hemispheres (falcine)
3. **Tentorium cerebelli** (10-15%) - infratentorial

**Extent:**
- **Unilateral** (60-70%)
- **Bilateral** (30-40%, worse prognosis)

**Key Point:** **Convexity most common** (from frontal sinusitis spreading over hemisphere)

---

## IMAGING FINDINGS BY MODALITY

### **CT (Less Sensitive):**
- **Crescentic hypodense or isodense collection**
- **Contrast enhancement** (meningeal adjacent to empyema)
- **Mass effect** (midline shift, sulcal effacement)
- **May be subtle** (CT insensitive for subdural empyema)

**Key Point:** **CT may miss subdural empyema** (MRI superior)

---

### **MRI (Preferred):**

**T1 (Pre-Contrast):**
- **Hypointense to isointense** (relative to CSF)
- **Crescent-shaped** extra-axial

**T2/FLAIR:**
- **Hyperintense** (pus, inflammatory fluid)
- **Follows sulci and gyri** (conforms to brain surface)
- **Does NOT cross falx or tentorium** (vs abscess can cross)

**Post-Contrast T1:**
- **Meningeal enhancement** (adjacent to empyema - inflamed arachnoid/pia)
- **Empyema itself may enhance** at margins

**DWI/ADC (Most Important):**
- **DWI hyperintense** (bright)
- **ADC hypointense** (dark - restricted diffusion)
- **Confirms purulent nature** (distinguishes from sterile subdural effusion)

**Key Point:** **MRI with DWI mandatory** (confirms purulent empyema vs sterile effusion)

---

## SUBDURAL EMPYEMA vs SUBDURAL EFFUSION

| **Feature** | **Subdural Empyema** | **Subdural Effusion (Sterile)** |
|-------------|----------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Contents** | **Pus (infected)** | **Sterile inflammatory fluid** |
| **DWI** | **Hyperintense (restricted)** | Hypointense (facilitated) |
| **ADC** | **Hypointense (low ADC)** | Hyperintense (high ADC) |
| **Enhancement** | **Meningeal enhancement** | Minimal or absent |
| **Clinical** | **Fever, toxic appearance** | Fever (less toxic) |
| **Treatment** | **Urgent surgery + antibiotics** | Observation or aspiration |
| **Prognosis** | **High mortality (15-30%)** | Good (resolves with treatment) |

**Key Point:** **Restricted diffusion distinguishes empyema from sterile effusion** (DWI/ADC critical)

---

## SUBDURAL EMPYEMA vs EPIDURAL ABSCESS

| **Feature** | **Subdural Empyema** | **Epidural Abscess** |
|-------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Location** | **Subdural space** (between dura and arachnoid) | **Epidural space** (between skull and dura) |
| **Shape** | **Crescent** (follows brain contour) | **Lens-shaped** (biconvex) |
| **Crosses midline** | **Yes** (can cross falx) | **No** (stops at suture lines) |
| **Follows sulci** | **Yes** (conforms to brain) | **No** (displaces brain) |
| **Speed** | **Rapid progression** (hours to days) | **Slower** (days) |
| **Source** | **Sinusitis, meningitis** | **Osteomyelitis, trauma** |
| **Treatment urgency** | **Extreme emergency** | **Urgent** |
| **Mortality** | **15-30%** | **<10%** |

**Key Point:** **Crescent = subdural (crosses midline) | Lens = epidural (stops at sutures)**

---

## CLINICAL PRESENTATION

**Classic Triad:**
1. **Fever** (80-90%)
2. **Headache** (70-90%)
3. **Altered mental status** (50-80%)

**Neurologic Signs:**
- **Seizures** (40-60%, focal or generalized)
- **Focal deficit** (hemiparesis, cranial nerve palsies - 50-70%)
- **Meningismus** (nuchal rigidity - 60-80%)
- **Papilledema** (increased ICP - 30-40%)

**Progression:**
- **Rapid** (hours to days from symptom onset to coma/death)
- **Fulminant course** if untreated

**Key Point:** **Rapid progression = distinguishing feature** (neurosurgical emergency)

---

## COMPLICATIONS

**Mnemonic: "BRAIN DAMAGE"**

**B**rain herniation (transtentorial, subfalcine - mass effect)
**R**apid deterioration (hours)
**A**ltered mental status â†’ coma
**I**ncreased ICP (intracranial pressure)
**N**eurologic deficit (permanent)

**D**eath (15-30% mortality despite treatment)
**A**bscess formation (brain parenchymal involvement)
**M**eningitis (spread to meninges)
**A**rterial/venous thrombosis (septic thrombophlebitis)
**G**rowing collection (rapidly enlarging)
**E**pilepsy (chronic seizures - 20-30%)

**Key Point:** **Mortality 15-30%** even with treatment (50-90% if untreated)

---

## DIAGNOSIS

**Imaging:**
- **MRI with DWI** (diagnostic - 95-100% sensitive)
- **CT with contrast** (if MRI unavailable, but less sensitive)

**Labs:**
- **WBC elevated** (leukocytosis)
- **ESR/CRP elevated** (inflammatory markers)
- **Blood cultures** (positive in 20-40%)

**CSF:**
- **NOT recommended** (risk of herniation with mass effect)
- **If done:** Elevated WBC, protein, low glucose (like meningitis)

**Surgical Drainage:**
- **Gram stain + culture** (from drained pus)
- **Organisms:** Strep (most common), Staph, anaerobes

**Key Point:** **MRI with DWI = diagnostic** (do NOT delay for LP if mass effect)

---

## ORGANISMS

**Mnemonic: "STREP-STAPH-ANAEROBES"**

**Sinusitis-Related (Most Common):**
- ***Streptococcus*** species (aerobic strep, *S. milleri* group)
- **Anaerobes** (Bacteroides, Fusobacterium, Prevotella)
- ***Staphylococcus aureus***

**Post-Neurosurgery:**
- ***Staphylococcus aureus*** (MRSA)
- **Gram-negatives** (Pseudomonas, Enterobacter)

**Post-Trauma:**
- ***Staphylococcus aureus***
- **Mixed flora**

**Key Point:** **Strep + anaerobes = most common** (polymicrobial in 40-60%)

---

## TREATMENT

**EMERGENCY:** **Urgent surgical drainage + IV antibiotics**

### **Surgical:**
- **Craniotomy with drainage** (preferred - allows complete evacuation)
- **Burr holes with irrigation** (if craniotomy not feasible)
- **Remove all pus** (incomplete drainage = high failure rate)
- **Source control** (drain sinusitis, treat mastoiditis)

### **Medical:**

**Empiric Antibiotics (Until cultures):**
- **Vancomycin** 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h + **Ceftriaxone** 2g IV q12h + **Metronidazole** 500mg IV q6h
- **Covers:** MRSA, Strep, anaerobes

**Duration:** 4-6 weeks IV

**Anti-Edema:**
- **Dexamethasone** 4-10mg IV q6h (if mass effect)
- **Osmotic therapy** (mannitol, hypertonic saline if herniation risk)

**Anticonvulsants:**
- **Levetiracetam** or **phenytoin** (if seizures)

**Key Point:** **Surgery + antibiotics = both mandatory** (antibiotics alone = 90% mortality)

---

## PROGNOSIS

**Mortality:** 15-30% (even with treatment), 50-90% (if untreated or delayed)

**Factors Predicting Poor Outcome:**
- **Delayed diagnosis** (>48 hours from symptom onset)
- **Bilateral empyema**
- **Coma at presentation** (GCS <8)
- **Seizures**
- **Inadequate drainage**
- **Age extremes** (<5 years, >60 years)

**Survivors:**
- **Full recovery:** 30-50%
- **Neurologic sequelae:** 30-50% (hemiparesis, seizures, cognitive deficits)
- **Chronic epilepsy:** 20-30%

**Key Point:** **Early surgery + antibiotics = improved survival** (delay = death)

---

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **Subdural empyema = neurosurgical emergency** (high mortality 15-30%)
- **Crescent-shaped extra-axial collection + restricted diffusion = subdural empyema**
- **MRI with DWI mandatory** (confirms purulent vs sterile effusion)
- **Frontal sinusitis = #1 cause** (40-60%)
- **Rapid progression** (hours to days - distinguishing feature)
- **Crescent shape crosses midline** (vs epidural lens-shaped, stops at sutures)
- **Restricted diffusion (DWI bright, ADC dark) = empyema** (vs sterile effusion facilitated)
- **Treatment: Urgent craniotomy + drainage + IV antibiotics** (both mandatory)
- **Antibiotics alone = 90% mortality** (surgery essential)
- **Mortality 15-30% despite treatment** (50-90% if untreated)
- **Complications: Herniation, seizures, death, permanent neurologic deficit**
