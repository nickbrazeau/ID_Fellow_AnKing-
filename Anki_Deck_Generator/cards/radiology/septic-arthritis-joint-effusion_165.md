## Septic Arthritis - Joint Effusion

**Q:** What are the characteristic imaging findings of septic arthritis on X-ray, ultrasound, and MRI, and how do you distinguish infectious from inflammatory arthritis?

**A:**

## DEFINITION

**Septic Arthritis:** **"Bacterial infection of joint space (synovial fluid)"**

**Imaging Hallmark:** **Joint effusion + synovial enhancement + adjacent bone marrow edema**

**Key Point:** **Septic arthritis = orthopedic emergency** (irreversible cartilage destruction within 24-48 hours)

---

## PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

**Routes of Infection:**

**Mnemonic: "HEMATOGENOUS-CONTIGUOUS-IATROGENIC"**

**Hematogenous (Most Common - 80-90%):**
- **Bacteremia** seeds synovium (highly vascularized)
- **S. aureus** most common (80%)
- **Septic emboli** (endocarditis)
- **Synovium is target** (rich blood supply)

**Contiguous Spread (10-15%):**
- **Adjacent osteomyelitis** (infection spreads from bone into joint)
- **Soft tissue abscess** (penetrates joint capsule)
- **Penetrating trauma** (open injury)

**Iatrogenic (5-10%):**
- **Intra-articular injection** (steroid injection)
- **Arthroscopy** (post-surgical)
- **Prosthetic joint infection** (late hematogenous or early post-op)

**Key Point:** **Hematogenous = #1 route** (80-90%, bacteremia seeds highly vascularized synovium)

---

## PATHOPHYSIOLOGY OF CARTILAGE DESTRUCTION

**Why Urgent:**

**Mnemonic: "ENZYMES-DESTROY-CARTILAGE-HOURS"**

**E**nzymes (bacterial + WBC proteases)
**N**eutrophil release (elastase, collagenase)
**Z**one of damage (cartilage)
**Y**ielding degradation
**M**etal loproteinases (MMPs)
**E**rosion of cartilage
**S**ynovial fluid (purulent, high WBC)

**D**estruction rapid (within 24-48 hours)
**E**nzymes from bacteria
**S**ynovial enzymes (inflammatory)
**T**otal cartilage loss (irreversible)
**R**apid degradation
**O**steoclast activation (bone erosion)
**Y**ielding permanent damage

**C**artilage destruction (proteoglycans degraded)
**A**vascular (cartilage - no repair)
**R**apid (24-48 hours)
**T**otal loss (if delayed treatment)
**I**rreversible damage
**L**oss of joint space
**A**rthritis (permanent)
**G**lobal cartilage destruction
**E**rosion of subchondral bone

**H**ours matter (24-48 hours to irreversible damage)
**O**rthopedic emergency
**U**rgent drainage required
**R**isk of joint destruction
**S**urgical washout mandatory

**Key Point:** **Cartilage destruction within 24-48 hours** (orthopedic emergency - drain immediately)

---

## CLINICAL PRESENTATION

**Classic Triad (in Children):**
1. **Fever** (60-80%)
2. **Joint pain** (100%)
3. **Refusal to bear weight/use joint** (90-100%)

**Adults:**
- **Joint pain** (90-100%, severe, monoarticular)
- **Swelling** (80-90%)
- **Warmth, erythema** (70-80%)
- **Fever** (60-80% - may be absent in adults)
- **Limited range of motion** (pain with passive/active motion)

**Most Commonly Affected Joints:**
1. **Knee** (50%)
2. **Hip** (20%)
3. **Shoulder** (10%)
4. **Ankle** (10%)
5. **Wrist** (5%)

**Key Point:** **Monoarticular joint pain + fever + effusion = septic arthritis** (until proven otherwise)

---

## X-RAY FINDINGS (Insensitive Early)

**Early (0-2 weeks):**
- **Soft tissue swelling** (earliest finding, within hours)
- **Joint space widening** (effusion distends joint - may be subtle)
- **Fat pad displacement** (knee - anterior/posterior fat pads displaced)
- **Loss of normal fat planes** (muscle planes obliterated)

**Late (>2-4 weeks):**
- **Joint space narrowing** (cartilage loss)
- **Subchondral erosions** (bone destruction)
- **Periarticular osteopenia** (bone demineralization)
- **Ankylosis** (if untreated - joint fusion)

**Key Point:** **X-ray insensitive early** (soft tissue swelling only - MRI/US superior)

---

## ULTRASOUND FINDINGS (Excellent for Effusion Detection)

**Advantages:**
- **Fast, bedside** (no radiation)
- **Highly sensitive for effusion** (>90%)
- **Guides arthrocentesis** (real-time needle placement)

**Findings:**

**Mnemonic: "EFFUSION-SYNOVITIS-DOPPLER"**

**E**ffusion (anechoic fluid distending joint)
**F**luid collection (joint space)
**F**at pad displacement (if anterior joint)
**U**ncommon to be normal (usually effusion present)
**S**ynovial thickening (hypoechoic)
**I**ncreased fluid (compared to contralateral)
**O**ther findings (soft tissue edema)
**N**ormal cartilage (thin hypoechoic line) may be obscured

**S**ynovial thickening (hypoechoic irregular synovium)
**Y**ielding inflammation
**N**odular or shaggy appearance
**O**utgrowth of pannus
**V**illi (synovial proliferation)
**I**rregular synovial surface
**T**hickened (>2-3mm)
**I**nflamed synovium
**S**urrounding joint

**D**oppler flow (hyperemia in inflamed synovium)
**O**vular increased vascularity
**P**ower Doppler (sensitive for synovitis)
**P**oweful hyperemia
**L**ow-resistance flow
**E**nhanced vascularity
**R**ed = inflammation (Doppler signal)

**Key Point:** **US excellent for detecting effusion** (>90% sensitive, guides arthrocentesis)

---

## MRI FINDINGS (Most Comprehensive)

**MRI = Gold Standard for Septic Arthritis**

**Sensitivity:** 90-100%
**Specificity:** 70-90%

**Hallmark: Effusion + Synovial Enhancement + Bone Marrow Edema**

---

### **T1-WEIGHTED FINDINGS:**

**Joint Effusion:**
- **Hypointense fluid** (low signal - like water)
- **Distends joint capsule**

**Synovium:**
- **Intermediate signal** (thickened synovium)
- **May be difficult to see** without contrast

**Bone Marrow:**
- **Hypointense signal** if adjacent osteomyelitis (marrow edema)

**Key Point:** **T1 shows effusion as dark fluid** distending joint

---

### **T2/STIR FINDINGS:**

**Joint Effusion:**
- **Hyperintense fluid** (very bright - high T2 signal)
- **Distends joint** (expanded joint space)

**Synovium:**
- **Thickened** (>2-3mm)
- **Intermediate to hyperintense** signal (inflamed)
- **Nodular or irregular** (pannus formation)

**Bone Marrow Edema:**
- **Hyperintense signal** adjacent to joint (reactive edema or osteomyelitis)
- **Metaphyseal involvement** (infection spreading to bone)

**Soft Tissue:**
- **Edema** (hyperintense) in periarticular tissues (muscle, fat)
- **Soft tissue abscess** (if infection extends beyond joint)

**Key Point:** **T2 shows effusion as very bright** + adjacent bone marrow edema

---

### **POST-CONTRAST T1 FAT-SATURATED FINDINGS:**

**Synovial Enhancement (Hallmark):**
- **Thick enhancement** of synovium (inflamed synovium is highly vascular)
- **Irregular, nodular** enhancement
- **Differentiates synovitis from simple effusion**

**Effusion:**
- **Central fluid does NOT enhance** (purulent fluid)
- **Synovial lining enhances** (vascularized)

**Bone Marrow:**
- **Enhancement** if adjacent osteomyelitis (inflamed marrow)
- **No enhancement** if reactive edema only

**Soft Tissue:**
- **Periarticular soft tissue enhancement** (phlegmon)
- **Rim-enhancing abscess** (if soft tissue abscess formed)

**Key Point:** **Thick synovial enhancement = septic arthritis** (inflamed vascularized synovium)

---

### **DWI/ADC FINDINGS:**

**Purulent Effusion:**
- **DWI hyperintense** (restricted diffusion if very purulent)
- **ADC variable** (may be low if thick pus)

**Utility:**
- **Less useful than in abscess** (effusion is liquid, may not restrict)
- **Very purulent effusion** may restrict (high protein, cellular debris)

**Key Point:** **DWI less reliable** for septic joint (liquid effusion, vs solid abscess)

---

## KOCHER CRITERIA (Pediatric Hip Septic Arthritis)

**Probability of Septic Arthritis:**

**Mnemonic: "FEWER-ESR-BEAR-WBC"**

**F**ever >38.5°C (1 point)
**E**SR >40 mm/hr (1 point)
**W**eight-bearing inability (refusal to bear weight - 1 point)
**E**levated WBC >12,000 (1 point)
**R**isk assessment

**0 points:** <0.2% (unlikely)
**1 point:** 3%
**2 points:** 40%
**3 points:** 93%
**4 points:** 99.6% (almost certain)

**Key Point:** **≥3 Kocher criteria = 93% probability** of septic arthritis (urgent arthrocentesis)

---

## ARTHROCENTESIS (SYNOVIAL FLUID ANALYSIS)

**Gold Standard for Diagnosis:**

**Septic Arthritis (Bacterial):**
- **WBC:** >50,000 cells/μL (often >100,000)
- **PMN:** >90% (neutrophil predominant)
- **Gram stain:** Positive in 50-70%
- **Culture:** Positive in 70-90%
- **Glucose:** Low (<40 mg/dL, <50% serum)
- **Protein:** Elevated (>3 g/dL)

**Inflammatory (Non-Infectious):**
- **WBC:** 2,000-50,000 (lower than septic)
- **PMN:** 50-90%
- **Gram stain/culture:** Negative
- **Glucose:** Normal or slightly low

**Key Point:** **WBC >50,000 + PMN >90% = septic arthritis** (until proven otherwise)

---

## ORGANISMS

**Mnemonic: "STAPH-GONORRHEA-STREP-GRAM-NEG"**

**Adults:**
- ***Staphylococcus aureus*** (40-60% - **most common overall**)
- **MRSA** (increasing, especially USA300)
- ***Neisseria gonorrhoeae*** (10-20% in sexually active young adults)
- **Streptococcus** species (15-20% - S. pyogenes, S. agalactiae, S. pneumoniae)
- **Gram-negatives** (10-15% - E. coli, Pseudomonas - elderly, IVDU)

**Children:**
- ***Staphylococcus aureus*** (50-70%)
- ***Streptococcus pyogenes*** (Group A Strep - 10-20%)
- ***Kingella kingae*** (6 months - 4 years - 10-30%)
- **Streptococcus pneumoniae** (<5%)
- **Haemophilus influenzae** (rare post-vaccine era)

**Neonates (<1 month):**
- **Group B Streptococcus** (GBS)
- ***S. aureus***
- **Gram-negatives** (E. coli, Klebsiella)

**IV Drug Users:**
- ***S. aureus*** (MRSA)
- ***Pseudomonas aeruginosa*** (axial skeleton - SI joint, sternoclavicular)
- ***Candida*** species (fungal)

**Prosthetic Joint:**
- **Coagulase-negative Staph** (S. epidermidis - most common)
- ***S. aureus***
- **Gram-negatives** (Pseudomonas, Enterobacter)

**Key Point:** **S. aureus = #1 overall** (40-60%) | **N. gonorrhoeae = young sexually active adults**

---

## TREATMENT

**URGENT SURGICAL DRAINAGE:**

**Indications (ALL Septic Joints):**
- **ALL septic joints require drainage** (arthroscopic lavage or open arthrotomy)
- **Hip septic arthritis = surgical emergency** (closed space, high pressure → avascular necrosis)

**Surgical Options:**
1. **Arthroscopic lavage** (preferred if accessible - knee, shoulder, ankle)
2. **Open arthrotomy** (hip, extensive infection)
3. **Repeat drainage** (if clinical failure after 48-72 hours)

**Goals:**
- **Remove purulent fluid** (decrease bacterial load)
- **Remove inflammatory debris** (enzymes, WBC)
- **Decompress joint** (reduce pressure, protect cartilage)

**Key Point:** **Drain ALL septic joints urgently** (within 6-12 hours, cartilage destruction within 24-48 hours)

---

**ANTIBIOTICS:**

**Empiric (Until Cultures):**
- **Vancomycin** 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h + **Ceftriaxone** 2g IV q12h
- **Add cefepime** if Gram-negative risk (elderly, immunocompromised, IVDU)

**Targeted (Based on culture):**
- **MSSA:** Cefazolin 2g IV q8h OR nafcillin 2g IV q4h
- **MRSA:** Vancomycin OR daptomycin 8-10 mg/kg IV daily
- **N. gonorrhoeae:** Ceftriaxone 1-2g IV daily
- **Pseudomonas:** Cefepime OR meropenem + aminoglycoside (consider)

**Duration:**
- **Non-gonococcal:** 3-4 weeks IV (minimum)
- **Gonococcal:** 7-14 days (shorter course)
- **Prosthetic joint:** 4-6 weeks IV (or longer)

**Key Point:** **Drain + IV antibiotics 3-4 weeks** (both mandatory)

---

## PROGNOSIS

**With Prompt Treatment (<24-48 hours):**
- **Good functional outcome:** 70-80%
- **Mortality:** 5-15% (higher if elderly, immunocompromised)

**With Delayed Treatment (>48 hours):**
- **Permanent joint damage:** 40-60%
- **Cartilage loss** (irreversible)
- **Ankylosis** (joint fusion)
- **Chronic arthritis**

**Factors Predicting Poor Outcome:**
1. **Delayed diagnosis** (>48 hours from symptom onset)
2. **Hip joint** (closed space, high pressure)
3. **Age extremes** (<1 year, >60 years)
4. **Immunosuppression** (diabetes, HIV, steroids)
5. **Polyarticular** (multiple joints - worse prognosis)

**Key Point:** **Early drainage (<24 hours) = best outcome** (delay >48 hours = permanent damage)

---

## COMPLICATIONS

**Mnemonic: "CARTILAGE-DESTRUCTION-CHRONIC"**

**C**artilage loss (irreversible if delayed)
**A**nkylosis (joint fusion)
**R**ecurrent infection (5-10%)
**T**otal joint destruction
**I**rreversible damage (if delayed >48 hours)
**L**imitation of motion (stiffness, contracture)
**A**vascular necrosis (hip - if pressure → ischemia)
**G**rowth disturbance (children - physeal damage)
**E**rosive arthritis

**D**eath (5-15% mortality)
**E**xtensive bone destruction (osteomyelitis)
**S**epsis (bacteremia)
**T**hrombosis (septic, DVT)
**R**efractory infection (antibiotic failure)
**U**ntreated (devastating outcome)
**C**hronic osteomyelitis
**T**otal joint replacement (may be needed)
**I**nfection spread (osteomyelitis)
**O**utcome poor if delayed
**N**eeds long-term antibiotics

**C**hronic arthritis (permanent)
**H**ip dislocation (if extensive destruction)
**R**esidual pain (chronic)
**O**steonecrosis (hip)
**N**eurologic deficit (if spinal)
**I**rreversible joint damage
**C**ontracture (loss of ROM)

**Key Point:** **Permanent cartilage loss if delayed >48 hours** (irreversible)

---

## SEPTIC ARTHRITIS vs TRANSIENT SYNOVITIS (Pediatric Hip)

**Transient Synovitis (Toxic Synovitis):**
- **Post-viral** (benign, self-limited)
- **Fever absent or low-grade** (<38.5°C)
- **WBC normal** (<12,000)
- **ESR normal or mildly elevated** (<40 mm/hr)
- **Can bear weight** (with discomfort)
- **Kocher criteria:** 0-1 points (<3% risk)
- **Ultrasound:** Small effusion

**Septic Arthritis:**
- **Bacterial infection** (orthopedic emergency)
- **Fever >38.5°C**
- **WBC elevated** (>12,000)
- **ESR elevated** (>40 mm/hr)
- **Refusal to bear weight**
- **Kocher criteria:** ≥3 points (>90% risk)
- **Ultrasound:** Moderate-large effusion

**Key Point:** **≥3 Kocher criteria = septic arthritis** (urgent arthrocentesis, cannot wait)

---

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **Septic arthritis = orthopedic emergency** (cartilage destruction within 24-48 hours)
- **Joint effusion + synovial enhancement + bone marrow edema = septic arthritis**
- **Monoarticular joint pain + fever + effusion = septic arthritis** (until proven otherwise)
- **Knee = most common joint** (50%)
- **S. aureus = #1 overall** (40-60%)
- **N. gonorrhoeae = young sexually active adults** (10-20%)
- **WBC >50,000 + PMN >90% = septic arthritis** (synovial fluid analysis)
- **≥3 Kocher criteria = 93% probability** of septic arthritis (pediatric hip)
- **Urgent drainage mandatory** (within 6-12 hours, ALL septic joints)
- **Drain + IV antibiotics 3-4 weeks** (both mandatory)
- **Early drainage (<24 hours) = best outcome** (70-80% good functional outcome)
- **Delayed treatment (>48 hours) = permanent damage** (40-60% joint destruction)
- **Ultrasound excellent for effusion detection** (>90% sensitive, guides arthrocentesis)
- **MRI = gold standard** (90-100% sensitive, shows synovial enhancement + bone marrow edema)
