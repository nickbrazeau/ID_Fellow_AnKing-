## Liver Abscess - Rim-Enhancing

**Q:** What are the characteristic CT and MRI findings of liver abscess, how do you distinguish pyogenic from amebic abscesses, and when is drainage indicated?

**A:**

## DEFINITION

**Liver Abscess:** **"Localized collection of pus within liver parenchyma"**

**CT/MRI Hallmark:** **Rim-enhancing hypodense/T2 hyperintense lesion**

**Key Point:** **Liver abscess = pyogenic (bacterial) or amebic**

---

## PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

**Routes of Infection:**

**Mnemonic: "PORTAL-BILIARY-ARTERIAL-CONTIGUOUS-TRAUMA"**

**Portal Vein (Most Common - 40-50%):**
- **Diverticulitis** (#1 source in developed countries)
- **Appendicitis**
- **IBD** (Crohn's disease)
- **Bowel perforation** (any cause)
- **Portal phlebitis** (pylephlebitis)

**Biliary Tract (20-30%):**
- **Cholangitis** (ascending infection)
- **Biliary obstruction** (stones, malignancy, stricture)
- **ERCP** (post-procedural)
- **Biliary-enteric anastomosis**

**Arterial (Hematogenous - 10-20%):**
- **Bacteremia** (endocarditis, catheter infection)
- **Septic emboli**

**Contiguous Spread (<5%):**
- **Perforated viscus** (stomach, duodenum, colon)
- **Perinephric abscess**
- **Subphrenic abscess**

**Trauma (<5%):**
- **Penetrating trauma** (gunshot, stab)
- **Blunt trauma** (liver laceration → superinfection)

**Cryptogenic (10-20%):**
- **No identifiable source**

**Key Point:** **Portal vein = most common route** (40-50%, from diverticulitis/appendicitis in developed countries)

---

## PYOGENIC vs AMEBIC LIVER ABSCESS

**Pyogenic (Bacterial - 80% in Developed Countries):**
- **Etiology:** Bacterial (polymicrobial or monomicrobial)
- **Source:** Biliary tract, portal vein (GI), hematogenous
- **Location:** **Right lobe > left** (70-80% right)
- **Number:** **Multiple (40-60%)** or solitary
- **Organisms:** E. coli, Klebsiella, Enterococcus, anaerobes
- **Appearance:** Thick irregular rim

**Amebic (Parasitic - Rare in US, Endemic Developing Countries):**
- **Etiology:** *Entamoeba histolytica*
- **Source:** Amebic colitis (colon → portal vein → liver)
- **Location:** **Right lobe** (90%, superior posterior segment)
- **Number:** **Solitary (90%)**
- **Appearance:** Thin smooth rim, "anchovy paste" pus
- **Serology:** Positive *E. histolytica* antibodies (>90%)

**Key Point:** **Pyogenic = multiple, irregular rim** | **Amebic = solitary right lobe, smooth rim**

---

## CT FINDINGS (Preferred Modality)

**CT = Gold Standard for Liver Abscess**

**Advantages:**
- **Fast, widely available**
- **Excellent lesion detection**
- **Guides percutaneous drainage**
- **Evaluates adjacent pathology** (biliary tree, bowel)

**Hallmark: Rim-Enhancing Hypodense Lesion**

---

### **CT WITH CONTRAST FINDINGS:**

**Mnemonic: "RIM-ENHANCE-HYPODENSE-CLUSTER"**

**Abscess:**
- **Hypodense lesion** (10-40 HU - pus, necrosis)
- **Rim enhancement** (abscess wall - vascularized granulation tissue, 2-15mm thick)
- **Central non-enhancing** (purulent fluid)
- **Double target sign** (inner hypodense rim + outer enhancing rim - pyogenic)
- **Cluster sign** (multiple small abscesses coalesce - pyogenic)
- **Gas bubbles** (if gas-forming organism - 20-30%, pyogenic)

**I**rregular rim (pyogenic) vs smooth (amebic)
**M**arked peripheral enhancement

**H**ypodense collection (10-40 HU)
**A**bscess cavity
**N**ecrotic center
**C**avity fluid
**E**nlargement (mass effect)

**Y**ielding to fluid

**Additional Findings:**
- **Perilesional edema** (surrounding liver hyperemic - "halo sign")
- **Hepatomegaly** (liver enlargement)
- **Ascites** (if severe infection)
- **Portal vein thrombosis** (if pylephlebitis)
- **Biliary dilatation** (if biliary source)
- **Adjacent bowel pathology** (diverticulitis, appendicitis)

**Key Point:** **Rim-enhancing hypodense lesion = liver abscess** (CT with contrast)

---

### **UNENHANCED CT:**

- **Hypodense mass** (fluid attenuation)
- **Gas bubbles** (if gas-forming organism)
- **Cannot distinguish abscess from necrotic tumor** without contrast

**Key Point:** **Contrast essential** to confirm abscess (rim enhancement)

---

## MRI FINDINGS

**MRI Advantages:**
- **Superior lesion characterization**
- **Better for small abscesses** (<1 cm)
- **No radiation**
- **DWI confirms purulent nature**

**Hallmark: T2 Hyperintense with Rim Enhancement**

---

### **T1-WEIGHTED FINDINGS:**

- **Hypointense lesion** (pus - low signal)
- **Rim:** Variable signal (granulation tissue, hemorrhage)
- **Surrounding liver:** Hypointense (edema)

**Key Point:** **T1 shows abscess as dark** fluid collection

---

### **T2-WEIGHTED FINDINGS:**

- **Hyperintense lesion** (very bright - pus, edema)
- **Rim:** Hypointense (granulation tissue, fibrous capsule)
- **Double target sign:** Inner hyperintense (edema) + outer hypointense (capsule)
- **Perilesional edema** (hyperintense halo around abscess)

**Key Point:** **T2 shows abscess as very bright** with hypointense rim

---

### **POST-CONTRAST T1 FAT-SATURATED:**

- **Rim enhancement** (abscess wall)
- **Central non-enhancing** (purulent fluid)
- **Perilesional enhancement** (hyperemic liver)
- **Septations enhance** (if multiloculated)

**Key Point:** **Rim enhancement + central non-enhancing = abscess**

---

### **DWI/ADC:**

- **DWI hyperintense** (restricted diffusion - purulent abscess)
- **ADC hypointense** (low ADC values <1.0 x 10^-3 mm²/s)
- **Confirms purulent nature** (80-100% specific for abscess)
- **Differentiates from cyst or necrotic tumor**

**Key Point:** **DWI bright + ADC dark = purulent abscess** (confirms diagnosis)

---

## PYOGENIC LIVER ABSCESS FEATURES

**Imaging:**
- **Multiple abscesses** (40-60%)
- **Right lobe predominance** (70-80%)
- **Thick irregular rim** (2-15mm)
- **Cluster sign** (small abscesses coalesce)
- **Gas bubbles** (20-30%)
- **Biliary dilatation** (if biliary source)

**Organisms:**
- ***E. coli*** (30-40% - most common)
- ***Klebsiella pneumoniae*** (10-30%, K1/K2 serotypes - metastatic infection syndrome)
- **Enterococcus** (10-15%)
- **Anaerobes** (Bacteroides, Fusobacterium - 20-30%)
- **Streptococcus** (5-10%)
- **Polymicrobial** (40-60%)

**Klebsiella Liver Abscess Syndrome:**
- **K. pneumoniae** (K1/K2 hypermucoviscous strains)
- **Metastatic infection** (endophthalmitis, meningitis, lung abscess - 10-20%)
- **Diabetes mellitus** (80% have diabetes)
- **Asian ethnicity** (more common)
- **Single abscess** (usually)

**Key Point:** **Pyogenic = multiple, irregular rim** | **E. coli most common, but Klebsiella → metastatic infection**

---

## AMEBIC LIVER ABSCESS FEATURES

**Imaging:**
- **Solitary abscess** (90%)
- **Right lobe** (90%, superior posterior segment - near diaphragm)
- **Thin smooth rim** (<5mm)
- **Round/ovoid shape**
- **No gas** (unless superinfected)
- **Large size** (5-15 cm average)

**Clinical:**
- **History of travel** to endemic area (Mexico, Central/South America, Africa, Asia)
- **Amebic colitis** (may precede or concurrent - bloody diarrhea)
- **"Anchovy paste" pus** (reddish-brown, from lysed RBCs and necrotic hepatocytes)
- **Tender hepatomegaly** (right upper quadrant pain)

**Serology:**
- **Anti-*E. histolytica* IgG** (>90% sensitive)
- **Stool PCR** for *E. histolytica* (if concurrent colitis)

**Aspiration (Rarely Needed):**
- **Anchovy paste pus** (reddish-brown, odorless)
- **Trophozoites** on microscopy (rare - organisms in abscess wall, not pus)

**Key Point:** **Amebic = solitary right lobe, thin smooth rim, travel history, serology positive**

---

## CLINICAL PRESENTATION

**Symptoms:**
- **Fever** (90-95%, spiking)
- **Right upper quadrant pain** (70-90%)
- **Nausea/vomiting** (40-60%)
- **Anorexia, weight loss** (chronic)
- **Jaundice** (15-25%, if biliary source)

**Physical Exam:**
- **Tender hepatomegaly** (70-80%)
- **Right pleural effusion** (20-30%, reactive - sympathetic effusion)

**Key Point:** **Fever + RUQ pain + tender hepatomegaly = liver abscess**

---

## LABS

**Inflammatory Markers:**
- **WBC elevated** (leukocytosis 70-90%)
- **CRP/ESR elevated** (90-95%)
- **Alkaline phosphatase elevated** (80-90%)
- **Bilirubin elevated** (if biliary obstruction)
- **Transaminases mildly elevated** (50-70%)
- **Albumin low** (chronic inflammation)

**Blood Cultures:**
- **Positive 40-60%** (pyogenic)
- **Negative** (amebic)

**Serology:**
- **Anti-*E. histolytica* IgG** (if amebic suspected)

**Key Point:** **Blood cultures positive 40-60%** (pyogenic), **serology for amebic**

---

## DIAGNOSIS

**Imaging:**
- **CT with contrast** (preferred - 90-95% sensitive)
- **MRI with DWI** (if CT contraindicated)
- **Ultrasound** (bedside, guides drainage)

**Aspiration/Drainage with Culture:**
- **CT-guided aspiration** or **surgical drainage**
- **Gram stain + culture (aerobic + anaerobic)**
- **AFB stain** (if immunocompromised)
- **Fungal stain** (if immunocompromised)
- **PCR for *E. histolytica*** (if amebic suspected)

**Key Point:** **CT with contrast + drainage with culture** (diagnostic and therapeutic)

---

## TREATMENT

**Pyogenic Liver Abscess:**

**Drainage (First-Line for Most):**
- **Percutaneous CT-guided drainage** (preferred - 80-90% success)
- **Indications:**
  - **Abscess >3-5 cm** (most require drainage)
  - **Failed medical therapy** (persistent fever after 48-72 hours antibiotics)
  - **Diagnostic uncertainty** (need culture)
- **Surgical drainage** (laparoscopic or open) if:
  - **Failed percutaneous** (persistent after CT-guided attempt)
  - **Multiloculated** (complex septations)
  - **Ruptured abscess** (peritonitis)
  - **Source control needed** (e.g., bowel perforation, need to resect gangrenous bowel)

**Medical Therapy Alone (Small Abscesses):**
- **Criteria:** Abscess <3 cm + no gas + not loculated
- **Success rate:** 60-80%

**Antibiotics:**

**Empiric (Until cultures):**
- **Metronidazole** 500mg IV q6h + **Ceftriaxone** 2g IV q12h OR **Cefepime** 2g IV q8h
- **OR:** Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5g IV q6h
- **Covers:** Gram-negatives, anaerobes, Enterococcus

**Targeted (Based on culture):**
- **E. coli/Enterobacteriaceae:** Ceftriaxone OR ertapenem
- **Klebsiella:** Ceftriaxone (if susceptible) OR carbapenem (if ESBL)
- **Enterococcus:** Ampicillin (if susceptible) OR vancomycin
- **Anaerobes:** Metronidazole OR carbapenems

**Duration:** 4-6 weeks IV (or until abscess resolved on imaging + CRP normalized)

**Key Point:** **Drainage + IV antibiotics 4-6 weeks** (abscess >3-5 cm requires drainage)

---

**Amebic Liver Abscess:**

**Medical Therapy (First-Line):**
- **Metronidazole** 750mg PO/IV tid x 7-10 days
- **OR Tinidazole** 2g PO daily x 3-5 days (better tolerated)
- **THEN:** Luminal agent (to eradicate intestinal colonization):
  - **Paromomycin** 25-35 mg/kg/day divided tid x 7 days
  - **OR Iodoquinol** 650mg PO tid x 20 days

**Drainage (Rarely Needed):**
- **Indications:**
  - **No response to metronidazole** after 3-5 days
  - **Large abscess** (>10 cm - risk of rupture)
  - **Left lobe abscess** (risk of peritonitis if ruptures)
  - **Impending rupture** (thin capsule, symptoms of leakage)
  - **Superinfection** (bacterial superinfection of amebic abscess)

**Success Rate (Medical Therapy Alone):** 85-95% (drainage rarely needed)

**Key Point:** **Amebic = metronidazole + luminal agent** (drainage rarely needed, 85-95% cure with meds alone)

---

## PROGNOSIS

**Pyogenic:**
- **Mortality:** 10-30% (higher if septic shock, immunocompromised)
- **Cure rate:** 80-90% (with drainage + antibiotics)

**Amebic:**
- **Mortality:** <1% (with appropriate treatment)
- **Cure rate:** 95-98% (medical therapy alone)

**Factors Predicting Poor Outcome:**
1. **Septic shock at presentation**
2. **Multiple abscesses** (worse than solitary)
3. **Immunosuppression** (diabetes, cirrhosis, steroids)
4. **Delayed diagnosis** (>2 weeks)
5. **Ruptured abscess** (peritonitis)
6. **Klebsiella** (metastatic infection syndrome)

**Key Point:** **Amebic has excellent prognosis** (<1% mortality) | **Pyogenic 10-30% mortality**

---

## COMPLICATIONS

**Mnemonic: "RUPTURE-SEPSIS-METASTATIC"**

**R**upture (into peritoneum, pleura, pericardium - peritonitis, empyema)
**U**ncontrolled sepsis (bacteremia, septic shock)
**P**leural effusion (reactive, sympathetic)
**T**hrombophlebitis (portal vein thrombosis - pylephlebitis)
**U**ntreated (leads to death)
**R**ecurrence (5-10% if inadequate drainage/antibiotics)
**E**mpyema (if ruptures into pleura)

**S**epsis (bacteremia, septic shock - 20-30%)
**E**xtension (to adjacent organs)
**P**eritonitis (if ruptures into peritoneum - 5-10%)
**S**ubphrenic abscess
**I**nadequate drainage (multiloculated)
**S**inus tract (chronic draining)

**M**etastatic infection (Klebsiella - endophthalmitis, meningitis, lung abscess - 10-20%)
**E**ndophthalmitis (Klebsiella)
**T**hrombosis (portal vein)
**A**bscess recurrence
**S**hock (septic)
**T**issue destruction (liver parenchyma)
**A**djacent organ involvement
**T**oxicity (sepsis)
**I**nfection spread
**C**hronic abscess

**Key Point:** **Rupture (5-10%) → peritonitis, empyema** | **Klebsiella → metastatic infection (endophthalmitis, meningitis)**

---

## DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

**Other Rim-Enhancing Liver Lesions:**

**Mnemonic: "ABSCESS-TUMOR-CYST-INFARCT"**

**Abscess (Pyogenic, Amebic, Fungal):**
- **Restricted diffusion** (DWI bright, ADC dark)
- **Gas bubbles** (if pyogenic, gas-forming)
- **Fever, leukocytosis**

**Necrotic Tumor (HCC, Metastases):**
- **Irregular thick rim** (vs smooth abscess)
- **No diffusion restriction** (necrotic tumor - facilitated diffusion)
- **Underlying cirrhosis or primary malignancy**

**Cyst (Simple, Hydatid):**
- **Thin smooth wall** (vs thick abscess)
- **No enhancement** (simple cyst)
- **Hydatid:** Daughter cysts, calcified wall, serology positive
- **No diffusion restriction**

**Hematoma (Trauma):**
- **History of trauma**
- **Blood products** (high T1 signal if subacute)
- **Evolving appearance** (acute → subacute → chronic)

**Infarct:**
- **Wedge-shaped** (peripheral, follows vascular territory)
- **Arterial occlusion** (if arterial infarct)
- **No rim enhancement** (vs abscess enhances)

**Key Point:** **Restricted diffusion + fever + leukocytosis = abscess** (vs tumor/cyst)

---

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **Liver abscess = rim-enhancing hypodense lesion on CT**
- **Pyogenic = multiple, irregular rim** | **Amebic = solitary right lobe, smooth rim**
- **Portal vein = most common route** (diverticulitis/appendicitis in developed countries)
- **E. coli most common** overall | **Klebsiella → metastatic infection** (endophthalmitis, meningitis)
- **Fever + RUQ pain + tender hepatomegaly = liver abscess**
- **CT with contrast = gold standard** (90-95% sensitive)
- **DWI bright + ADC dark = purulent abscess** (confirms diagnosis)
- **Pyogenic: Drainage + IV antibiotics 4-6 weeks** (abscess >3-5 cm requires drainage)
- **Amebic: Metronidazole + luminal agent** (85-95% cure, drainage rarely needed)
- **Amebic has excellent prognosis** (<1% mortality vs pyogenic 10-30%)
- **Klebsiella → metastatic infection** (endophthalmitis, meningitis, lung abscess - 10-20%)
- **Rupture (5-10%) → peritonitis or empyema**
