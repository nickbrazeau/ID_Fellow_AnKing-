## Empyema - Loculated Pleural Effusion

**Q:** What are the CT and ultrasound findings of loculated empyema, and how does loculation affect management?

**A:**

## DEFINITION

**Loculated Empyema:** **"Infected pleural fluid trapped in fibrin septations, forming multiple compartments"**

**Formation:** Fibrin deposition → septations → compartmentalized fluid → loculations

**Key Point:** **Loculated empyema requires drainage** (antibiotics alone insufficient)

---

## PATHOPHYSIOLOGY - STAGES OF EMPYEMA

**Mnemonic: "EFO" (Exudative → Fibrinopurulent → Organizing)**

### **Stage 1: Exudative (Day 1-3):**
- **Sterile or early infection**
- **Thin, free-flowing fluid**
- **No septations**
- **pH >7.20, glucose >60**
- **Treatment:** Antibiotics ± thoracentesis

### **Stage 2: Fibrinopurulent (Day 3-7):**
- **Frank pus**
- **Fibrin deposition** → septations form
- **Loculations develop**
- **pH <7.0, glucose <40, LDH >1000**
- **Treatment:** Drainage required (chest tube, VATS)

### **Stage 3: Organizing (>7-14 days):**
- **Thick pleural peel** (fibrosis)
- **Lung trapped** (cannot expand)
- **Dense adhesions**
- **Treatment:** Surgical decortication

**Key Point:** **Loculations = Stage 2** (fibrinopurulent) → drainage mandatory

---

## CT FINDINGS

**Hallmark: Septations Dividing Pleural Fluid**

### **Mnemonic: "SPLIT-LENTICULAR"**

**S**plit pleura sign (enhanced visceral + parietal pleura)
**P**leural thickening (>2mm, enhanced)
**L**enticular shape (lens-shaped, obtuse angle with chest wall)
**I**nternal septations (fibrin strands)
**T**hick pleural layers (>4mm)

**L**oculations (multiple compartments)
**E**nhancement of pleura (inflammation)
**N**on-dependent pleural fluid (trapped by septations)
**T**hick pus (high attenuation 10-20 HU)
**I**rregular margins
**C**ompressed lung (unable to expand)
**U**ltrasound shows septations (echogenic strands)
**L**ow attenuation centers (pus)
**A**ir-fluid levels (if bronchopleural fistula)
**R**im enhancement (pleural layers)

---

## CT CHARACTERISTICS

**Fluid Characteristics:**
- **Lenticular (lens-shaped)** collection
- **Obtuse angle with chest wall** (>90°)
- **High attenuation** (10-20 HU, vs simple effusion 0-10 HU)
- **Internal septations** (fibrin strands dividing fluid)
- **Non-dependent distribution** (fluid trapped, doesn't layer)

**Pleural Changes:**
- **Split pleura sign** (visceral + parietal pleura thickened, enhanced)
- **Pleural thickening** (>2mm each layer, total >4mm)
- **Enhancement on contrast** (inflammation)

**Adjacent Lung:**
- **Compressed** (passive atelectasis)
- **Consolidation** (adjacent pneumonia)

**Key Point:** **Septations + split pleura sign = empyema** (loculated, requires drainage)

---

## ULTRASOUND FINDINGS

**Advantages of Ultrasound:**
- **Real-time imaging**
- **Portable** (bedside)
- **Identifies septations** (better than CXR)
- **Guides thoracentesis/drain placement**
- **No radiation**

**Ultrasound Characteristics:**

**Simple Effusion:**
- **Anechoic** (black, echo-free)
- **Moves with respiration**

**Complicated Effusion/Empyema:**
- **Echogenic** (internal echoes from fibrin, debris)
- **Septations visible** (echogenic strands)
- **Multiple loculations** (compartments)
- **Thick pleural layers**
- **Does not move freely** (loculated)

**Key Point:** **Ultrasound superior to CXR for detecting septations** (guides drainage)

---

## LOCULATION PATTERNS

**Types:**

1. **Single loculation:**
   - **One compartment**
   - **May respond to single chest tube**

2. **Multiple loculations:**
   - **Many compartments** (honeycomb appearance)
   - **Requires multiple drains or VATS**

3. **Non-dependent loculation:**
   - **Fluid trapped in non-gravity dependent area**
   - **Will not drain with standard chest tube placement**

**Key Point:** **Multiple loculations = VATS or intrapleural fibrinolysis** (chest tube alone insufficient)

---

## CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE

**Why Loculations Matter:**

1. **Antibiotics cannot penetrate** loculated pus effectively
2. **Chest tube may drain only one compartment** (others remain infected)
3. **Prolonged illness** if inadequately drained
4. **Risk of organizing** → pleural peel → trapped lung
5. **Requires aggressive drainage** (multiple tubes, fibrinolysis, or VATS)

**Key Point:** **Loculated empyema = more aggressive drainage required**

---

## TREATMENT OPTIONS BY LOCULATION

### **Free-Flowing Effusion (No Loculations):**
- **Single chest tube** (28-32 Fr)
- **Antibiotics**
- **Usually sufficient**

### **Few Loculations (1-2):**
- **Chest tube** (may need repositioning or second tube)
- **Intrapleural fibrinolysis** (tPA/DNase)
- **Antibiotics**

### **Multiple Loculations (>2):**
- **VATS** (video-assisted thoracoscopic surgery) - **preferred**
  - Break down septations
  - Evacuate pus
  - Decortication if needed
- **Intrapleural fibrinolysis** (tPA + DNase) - alternative if not surgical candidate

### **Organized (Stage 3):**
- **Open thoracotomy + decortication**
- **Remove pleural peel**
- **Allow lung re-expansion**

**Key Point:** **Multiple loculations = VATS preferred** (break down septations)

---

## INTRAPLEURAL FIBRINOLYSIS

**MIST-2 Trial Protocol:**

**Agents:**
- **tPA** (alteplase) 10mg + **DNase** (dornase alfa) 5mg
- **Administered via chest tube** (twice daily x 3 days = 6 doses)

**Mechanism:**
- **tPA:** Breaks down fibrin septations
- **DNase:** Degrades DNA in pus (reduces viscosity)

**Outcomes:**
- **Improved drainage** (reduces loculations)
- **Decreased need for surgery** (by 50%)
- **Shorter hospital stay**

**Contraindications:**
- **Bronchopleural fistula** (active air leak)
- **Bleeding risk** (recent surgery, coagulopathy)

**Key Point:** **tPA + DNase intrapleural = alternative to VATS** (if not surgical candidate)

---

## DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

**Loculated Pleural Collection:**

**Mnemonic: "HEMATO-CHYLO-EMPYEMA"**

**H**emothorax (trauma, post-surgical)
**E**mpyema (infected, septated)
**M**alignant effusion (lung cancer, mesothelioma)
**A**bscess (lung abscess rupture into pleura)
**T**B (tuberculous empyema - high lymphocytes, positive AFB)
**O**rganized hematoma (old blood)

**C**hylothorax (lymphatic leak, milky fluid)
**H**epatic hydrothorax (cirrhosis, transdiaphragmatic)
**Y**ellow nail syndrome (lymphatic disorder)
**L**upus pleuritis (autoimmune)
**O**sler-Weber-Rendu (hereditary hemorrhagic telangiectasia)

**E**osinophilic effusion (drugs, parasites)
**M**esothelioma (asbestos exposure)
**P**ost-cardiac injury syndrome (Dressler's)
**Y**ellow nail syndrome
**E**sophageal rupture (Boerhaave's)
**M**alignancy
**A**sbestos (mesothelioma)

**Key Point:** **Thoracentesis differentiates** (Gram stain, pH, glucose, LDH, cytology)

---

## DIAGNOSTIC APPROACH

**Thoracentesis Mandatory:**

**Send Pleural Fluid for:**
- **pH** (<7.20 = complicated)
- **Glucose** (<60 = complicated)
- **LDH** (>1000 = complicated)
- **Gram stain + culture**
- **Cell count + differential**
- **Protein** (exudate vs transudate)

**Drainage Criteria (Any of the Following):**
- **Frank pus** (cloudy, turbid)
- **Positive Gram stain or culture**
- **pH <7.20**
- **Glucose <60 mg/dL**
- **Loculated on imaging**

**Key Point:** **pH <7.20 or glucose <60 = drainage mandatory**

---

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **Loculated empyema = fibrinopurulent stage** (Stage 2) → drainage required
- **Septations visible on CT and ultrasound** (echogenic strands dividing fluid)
- **Split pleura sign = empyema** (enhanced visceral + parietal pleura)
- **Ultrasound superior for detecting septations** (guides drainage)
- **Multiple loculations = VATS preferred** (break down septations)
- **Intrapleural fibrinolysis (tPA + DNase)** = alternative if not surgical candidate
- **pH <7.20 or glucose <60 = drainage indicated**
- **Antibiotics alone insufficient** for loculated empyema
- **Organized (Stage 3) empyema = decortication** (remove pleural peel)
- **MIST-2 protocol: tPA 10mg + DNase 5mg** (twice daily x 3 days)
