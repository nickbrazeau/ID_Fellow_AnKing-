## Empyema - Split Pleura Sign

**Q:** What is the split pleura sign, what is its pathophysiology, and what is its diagnostic significance for empyema?

**A:**

## DEFINITION

**Split Pleura Sign:** **"Thickened and enhanced visceral and parietal pleural layers separated by pleural fluid on contrast-enhanced CT"**

**Appearance:** **"Two distinct enhanced lines (pleural layers) with hypodense fluid between"**

**Key Point:** **Split pleura sign = empyema** (>90% specific)

---

## PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

**Normal Pleura:**
- **Visceral pleura** (covers lung surface) - normally thin (<1mm)
- **Pleural space** (potential space, <20mL fluid)
- **Parietal pleura** (lines chest wall) - normally thin (<1mm)

**In Empyema:**
1. **Infection/inflammation** → pleural layers thicken
2. **Vascular congestion** → pleural enhancement on contrast CT
3. **Fibrin deposition** → thickening progresses
4. **Fluid accumulation** → pleural layers separate ("split")
5. **Result:** Two distinct enhanced pleural layers visible

**Key Point:** **Inflammation → pleural thickening + enhancement + separation = split pleura sign**

---

## CT FINDINGS

**Hallmark: Two Enhanced Pleural Layers Separated by Fluid**

### **Mnemonic: "SPLIT"**

**S**eparated pleural layers (visceral + parietal)
**P**arietal pleura enhanced (outer layer, against chest wall)
**L**ayers thickened (>2mm each, >4mm total)
**I**nner visceral pleura enhanced (against lung surface)
**T**wo distinct lines (with fluid between)

---

## DETAILED CT CHARACTERISTICS

**Visceral Pleura (Inner Layer):**
- **Covers lung surface**
- **Thickness:** >2mm (vs <1mm normal)
- **Enhancement:** Marked (inflammation)
- **Separates from parietal pleura** (fluid between)

**Pleural Fluid:**
- **Between the two layers**
- **Hypodense** (low attenuation)
- **High protein** (exudative)
- **May be septated** (loculated)

**Parietal Pleura (Outer Layer):**
- **Lines chest wall/diaphragm/mediastinum**
- **Thickness:** >2mm (vs <1mm normal)
- **Enhancement:** Marked (inflammation)

**Total Pleural Thickness:** **>4mm** (both layers combined)

**Key Point:** **Both layers enhanced >2mm each = split pleura sign**

---

## DIAGNOSTIC SIGNIFICANCE

**Specificity:** **>90%** for empyema (highly specific)

**Sensitivity:** **70-80%** (may be absent in early or treated empyema)

**Clinical Utility:**
- **Distinguishes empyema from simple parapneumonic effusion**
- **Indicates need for drainage** (antibiotics alone insufficient)
- **Predicts loculation** (often associated with septations)

**Key Point:** **Split pleura sign = empyema requiring drainage** (not just antibiotics)

---

## SPLIT PLEURA SIGN vs OTHER PLEURAL PATHOLOGY

| **Condition** | **Pleural Enhancement** | **Split Pleura Sign** |
|---------------|-------------------------|----------------------|
| **Empyema** | **Both layers enhanced** | **Present** |
| **Simple parapneumonic effusion** | **Minimal or absent** | **Absent** |
| **Malignant effusion** | **Nodular, irregular** | **Absent** (diffuse thickening, not split) |
| **Tuberculosis** | **Variable** | **May be present** (TB empyema) |
| **Transudative effusion** | **Absent** | **Absent** |
| **Hemothorax** | **Absent** | **Absent** |

**Key Point:** **Split pleura sign specific for empyema** (vs other effusion causes)

---

## CONTRAST ENHANCEMENT PATTERN

**Why Pleura Enhances:**
- **Increased vascularity** (inflammation)
- **Vascular congestion**
- **Capillary leak** (exudative process)

**Enhancement Characteristics:**
- **Homogeneous** (smooth, uniform)
- **Both layers** (visceral + parietal)
- **Symmetric** (equal enhancement both sides)

**Key Point:** **Contrast-enhanced CT required** to visualize split pleura sign

---

## ASSOCIATED FINDINGS

**Mnemonic: "EMPYEMA SIGNS"**

**E**nhanced pleural layers (split pleura)
**M**ultiple septations (loculated)
**P**leural thickening (>4mm total)
**Y**ellow-green pus (on drainage)
**E**ffusion (hypodense fluid between pleural layers)
**M**ediastinal shift (away from effusion if large)
**A**djacent consolidation (pneumonia)

**S**eptations visible (fibrin strands)
**I**nternal loculations (compartmentalized)
**G**lucose <40 (pleural fluid chemistry)
**N**on-dependent fluid (trapped by loculations)
**S**ubphrenic extension (may extend below diaphragm)

**Key Point:** **Split pleura sign + septations = loculated empyema**

---

## DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS OF PLEURAL THICKENING

**Mnemonic: "THICKENED PLEURA"**

**T**B (tuberculous empyema, pleural TB)
**H**emorrhage (hemothorax - may thicken over time)
**I**nfection (empyema - split pleura sign)
**C**ancer (mesothelioma, metastatic disease - nodular)
**K**ollagen vascular disease (lupus pleuritis, RA)
**E**mpyema (split pleura sign)
**N**odular thickening (malignancy)
**E**sophageal rupture (Boerhaave's - mediastinal/pleural involvement)
**D**ressler's syndrome (post-MI pericarditis/pleuritis)

**P**ost-inflammatory (organized empyema, pleural peel)
**L**upus (autoimmune pleuritis)
**E**osinophilic pleuritis (drugs, parasites)
**U**remia (uremic pleuritis)
**R**adiation (post-RT fibrosis)
**A**sbestos (pleural plaques, mesothelioma)

**Key Point:** **Split pleura sign distinguishes empyema** from other causes of pleural thickening

---

## TEMPORAL EVOLUTION

**Stage 1 (Exudative) - Day 1-3:**
- **Thin pleural layers** (may not be visible)
- **No enhancement** or minimal
- **No split pleura sign**

**Stage 2 (Fibrinopurulent) - Day 3-7:**
- **Pleural thickening begins**
- **Split pleura sign appears**
- **Enhancement prominent**
- **Septations develop**

**Stage 3 (Organizing) - >7-14 days:**
- **Thick pleural peel** (fibrosis)
- **Split pleura sign may persist**
- **Trapped lung** (cannot expand)

**Key Point:** **Split pleura sign appears in Stage 2** (fibrinopurulent)

---

## CLINICAL CORRELATION

**When Split Pleura Sign Present:**

**Diagnosis:** Empyema confirmed (>90% specificity)

**Treatment Required:**
- **Drainage mandatory** (chest tube, VATS, surgery)
- **Antibiotics alone insufficient**
- **Delay worsens prognosis** (progression to organizing stage)

**Thoracentesis Findings Expected:**
- **Frank pus** (cloudy, turbid)
- **pH <7.0**
- **Glucose <40 mg/dL**
- **LDH >1000 U/L**
- **Positive Gram stain/culture** (in 60-70%)

**Key Point:** **Split pleura sign = proceed directly to drainage** (don't delay)

---

## IMAGING MODALITY COMPARISON

| **Modality** | **Visualizes Split Pleura?** | **Advantages** |
|--------------|------------------------------|----------------|
| **CXR** | **No** | Quick, low cost, but limited |
| **Ultrasound** | **No** (but shows septations) | Bedside, real-time, no radiation |
| **CT without contrast** | **No** | Shows fluid, loculations, but no enhancement |
| **CT with contrast** | **Yes** | **Best for split pleura sign** |

**Key Point:** **Contrast-enhanced CT = gold standard** for visualizing split pleura sign

---

## MANAGEMENT IMPLICATIONS

**If Split Pleura Sign Present:**

1. **Confirm with thoracentesis** (pH, glucose, Gram stain, culture)
2. **Immediate drainage:**
   - **Chest tube** (28-32 Fr) if few loculations
   - **Intrapleural fibrinolysis** (tPA + DNase) if loculated
   - **VATS** if multiple loculations or failed medical therapy
3. **Broad-spectrum antibiotics** (cover anaerobes, Strep, Staph)
4. **Monitor response:**
   - **Clinical improvement** (fever resolves)
   - **Drainage output** (decreases over days)
   - **Repeat imaging** (CT or ultrasound)

**Key Point:** **Split pleura sign = empyema = drainage required**

---

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **Split pleura sign = thickened enhanced visceral + parietal pleura separated by fluid**
- **>90% specific for empyema** (highly reliable sign)
- **Requires contrast-enhanced CT** (non-contrast CT cannot visualize)
- **Indicates fibrinopurulent stage** (Stage 2) → drainage mandatory
- **Antibiotics alone insufficient** if split pleura sign present
- **Often associated with septations** (loculated empyema)
- **Both pleural layers >2mm each** (>4mm total)
- **Distinguishes empyema from simple parapneumonic effusion**
- **Early recognition improves outcomes** (prevents progression to organizing stage)
- **May be absent in early empyema** (Stage 1, exudative)
