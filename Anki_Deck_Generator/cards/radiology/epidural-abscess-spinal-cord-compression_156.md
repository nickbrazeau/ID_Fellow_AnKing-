## Spinal Epidural Abscess - Cord Compression

**Q:** What are the characteristic MRI findings of spinal epidural abscess, and why is it considered a surgical emergency?

**A:**

## DEFINITION

**Spinal Epidural Abscess:** **"Collection of pus in the epidural space (between dura and vertebral periosteum) causing spinal cord or nerve root compression"**

**MRI Hallmark:** **T2 hyperintense epidural collection with rim enhancement + cord compression**

**Key Point:** **Spinal epidural abscess = surgical emergency** (irreversible paralysis if delayed)

---

## PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

**Location:** **Epidural space** (potential space between dura mater and vertebral column)

**Most Common Location:**
- **Posterior** > anterior (60-80% posterior)
- **Thoracic** > lumbar > cervical (thoracic 50-70%)
- **Dorsal** epidural space (posterior to cord)

**Sources:**

**Mnemonic: "HEMATOGENOUS-CONTIGUOUS-IATRO"**

**Hematogenous (50-60% - most common):**
- **H**ematogenous spread (bacteremia, endocarditis)
- **E**ndocarditis (septic emboli)
- **M**RSA bacteremia (IVDU, catheter)
- **A**bscess elsewhere (skin, dental, UTI)
- **T**ransient bacteremia (dental procedures)
- **O**steomyelitis (vertebral)
- **G**enous spread from distant source
- **E**mbolic (septic)
- **N**eedle stick (IVDU)
- **O**rigin distant
- **U**terine/pelvic infection
- **S**ource infection (skin, lung, abdomen)

**Contiguous Spread (20-30%):**
- **C**ontiguous vertebral osteomyelitis (most common contiguous)
- **O**steomyelitis/discitis extension
- **N**eighboring infection
- **T**rauma (penetrating spinal injury)
- **I**atrogenic (post-spinal procedure)
- **G**rowing from adjacent bone/disc
- **U**nderlying osteomyelitis
- **O**ther adjacent source
- **U**lcer (decubitus with vertebral osteomyelitis)
- **S**urgical site infection

**Iatrogenic (10-20%):**
- **I**atrogenic (epidural anesthesia/steroid injection)
- **A**nesthesia complication (epidural catheter)
- **T**ransforaminal injection
- **R**ecent spinal procedure
- **O**peration (spinal surgery)

**Key Point:** **Hematogenous = #1 cause** (50-60%, bacteremia/endocarditis)

---

## MRI CHARACTERISTICS

**Hallmark: Epidural Collection with Cord Compression**

### **Mnemonic: "EPIDURAL-CORD"**

**E**pidural space (between dura and bone)
**P**osterior more common (60-80%)
**I**ntense T2 signal (hyperintense fluid)
**D**isplaces thecal sac (compresses cord)
**U**sually thoracic (50-70%)
**R**im enhancement (post-contrast - abscess wall)
**A**bscess extending multiple levels (2-4 vertebral levels)
**L**ongitudinal extent (craniocaudal)

**C**ord compression (mass effect)
**O**steomyelitis (adjacent vertebral body involvement - 50%)
**R**estricted diffusion (DWI bright if purulent)
**D**iscitis (adjacent disc involvement - 50%)

**Key Point:** **Posterior epidural collection + cord compression + rim enhancement = spinal epidural abscess**

---

## MRI FINDINGS BY SEQUENCE

**T1 (Pre-Contrast):**
- **Hypointense to isointense** (relative to cord)
- **Epidural collection** (displaces dura inward)
- **Vertebral signal changes** if osteomyelitis (hypointense marrow)

**T2/STIR:**
- **Hyperintense collection** (fluid/pus)
- **Cord compression** (flattened, displaced)
- **Cord edema** (T2 hyperintense if myelopathy)
- **Vertebral edema** if osteomyelitis (T2 hyperintense marrow)
- **Disc hyperintensity** if discitis

**Post-Contrast T1 (Fat-Saturated):**
- **Rim enhancement** (abscess wall)
- **Phlegmon enhancement** (if early, pre-abscess)
- **Vertebral enhancement** (if osteomyelitis)
- **Disc enhancement** (if discitis)
- **Meningeal enhancement** (if adjacent meningitis)

**DWI/ADC:**
- **DWI hyperintense** (restricted diffusion if purulent)
- **Confirms abscess** (vs phlegmon or granulation tissue)

**Key Point:** **T2 hyperintense + rim enhancement + cord compression = abscess**

---

## EXTENT & DISTRIBUTION

**Longitudinal Extent:**
- **2-4 vertebral levels** (most common)
- **May extend entire spine** (holocord)
- **Posterior >> anterior** (60-80% posterior)

**Circumferential:**
- **Dorsal** (posterior) - 60-80%
- **Ventral** (anterior) - 20-30%
- **Circumferential** - 10-20% (worst prognosis)

**Key Point:** **Posterior epidural abscess = most common pattern**

---

## ASSOCIATED FINDINGS

**Vertebral Osteomyelitis (50%):**
- **T1 hypointense** marrow signal
- **T2 hyperintense** marrow edema
- **Enhancement** of vertebral body
- **Endplate destruction** (irregularity)

**Discitis (50%):**
- **T2 hyperintense** disc signal (loss of intranuclear cleft)
- **Disc enhancement** (normally disc doesn't enhance)
- **Disc space narrowing** (endplate destruction)

**Paraspinal Abscess (30-40%):**
- **Phlegmon or abscess** extending into paraspinal soft tissues
- **Psoas abscess** (if lumbar)

**Cord Changes:**
- **Cord edema** (T2 hyperintense intramedullary signal - myelopathy)
- **Cord enhancement** (myelitis if cord infected)

**Key Point:** **50% have vertebral osteomyelitis + discitis** (contiguous spread)

---

## CLINICAL PRESENTATION

**Classic Triad (15% have all three):**
1. **Fever** (50-70%)
2. **Back pain** (70-90%)
3. **Neurologic deficit** (35-50%)

**Progression (Heusner Stages):**

**Stage 1: Back Pain**
- **Localized back pain** (days to weeks)
- **Tenderness to percussion**

**Stage 2: Radicular Pain**
- **Nerve root pain** (radiculopathy)
- **Radiating pain** (dermatomal)

**Stage 3: Motor/Sensory Deficit**
- **Weakness** (paraparesis, quadriparesis)
- **Sensory loss** (level)
- **Bladder/bowel dysfunction**

**Stage 4: Paralysis**
- **Complete paralysis** (paraplegia, quadriplegia)
- **Irreversible if >48 hours**

**Key Point:** **Progression over days to weeks** (Stage 1 → 4, irreversible paralysis if delayed)

---

## RISK FACTORS

**Mnemonic: "IVDU-DM-IMMUNE"**

**I**VDU (intravenous drug use - **#1 risk factor in adults**)
**V**ascular catheters (central lines, hemodialysis)
**D**iabetes mellitus
**U**remia (chronic kidney disease, dialysis)

**D**iabetes mellitus
**M**alignancy (immunocompromised)

**I**mmunosuppression (steroids, transplant, HIV)
**M**etabolic (DM, liver disease, CKD)
**M**edical procedures (epidural injections, spinal surgery)
**U**terine/pelvic infection
**N**osocomial (hospital-acquired bacteremia)
**E**nd-stage renal disease (dialysis)

**Key Point:** **IVDU = #1 risk factor** (MRSA bacteremia)

---

## ORGANISMS

**Mnemonic: "STAPH-STREP-GRAM-NEG"**

**Hematogenous:**
- ***Staphylococcus aureus*** (60-80% - **most common**, especially MRSA in IVDU)
- ***Streptococcus*** species (10-15%)
- **Gram-negatives** (E. coli, Pseudomonas - 10-15%, especially if UTI source)

**Post-Surgical:**
- ***Staphylococcus aureus*** (MRSA)
- **Coagulase-negative staph** (S. epidermidis)
- **Gram-negatives** (Pseudomonas)

**Tuberculosis:**
- ***Mycobacterium tuberculosis*** (Pott's disease - more common in endemic areas)
- **Anterior epidural** (from vertebral osteomyelitis)

**Fungi:**
- ***Aspergillus*, *Candida*** (immunocompromised)

**Key Point:** **S. aureus = 60-80%** (MRSA if IVDU)

---

## DIAGNOSIS

**Imaging:**
- **MRI with contrast + DWI** (diagnostic - 90-95% sensitive)
- **Entire spine** (abscess may extend beyond symptomatic area)

**Labs:**
- **WBC elevated** (leukocytosis in 60-70%)
- **ESR/CRP markedly elevated** (almost always, >100 mm/hr)
- **Blood cultures** (positive in 50-60%)

**Surgical Specimen:**
- **Gram stain + culture** (from surgical drainage or CT-guided aspiration)
- **Acid-fast stain** (if TB suspected)
- **Fungal stains** (if immunocompromised)

**Key Point:** **MRI entire spine mandatory** (extent assessment for surgical planning)

---

## TREATMENT

**SURGICAL EMERGENCY:** **Laminectomy + decompression + drainage within 24 hours**

### **Indications for Surgery (Most Patients):**
- **Neurologic deficit** (weakness, sensory loss, bowel/bladder dysfunction)
- **Cord compression** on MRI
- **Sepsis** (hemodynamically unstable)
- **Progression despite antibiotics**
- **Diagnostic uncertainty** (obtain tissue for culture)

**Surgical Approach:**
- **Laminectomy** + **abscess evacuation** + **decompression**
- **Entire extent** (drain all levels involved)
- **Debridement** of osteomyelitis/discitis if present

**Key Point:** **Surgery within 24 hours** (delay >48 hours = irreversible paralysis)

---

### **Medical (Antibiotics):**

**Empiric (Until cultures):**
- **Vancomycin** 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h + **Ceftriaxone** 2g IV q12h OR **Cefepime** 2g IV q8h
- **Add metronidazole** 500mg IV q6h (if post-surgical or anaerobes suspected)

**MRSA Coverage Essential** (vancomycin or daptomycin)

**Duration:** 4-8 weeks IV (longer if osteomyelitis/discitis)

**Medical Management Alone (Rare):**
- **No neurologic deficit** AND
- **No spinal instability** AND
- **Small abscess (<2 vertebral levels)** AND
- **Close monitoring** (serial MRI, neurologic exams)

**Key Point:** **Most require surgery + antibiotics** (antibiotics alone = high failure rate)

---

## PROGNOSIS

**Mortality:** 5-15% (despite treatment)

**Neurologic Outcome Depends on:**

1. **Pre-operative neurologic status** (most important factor)
   - **No deficit:** 90% full recovery
   - **Mild deficit:** 70% full recovery
   - **Severe deficit:** 30% full recovery
   - **Complete paralysis >48 hours:** <10% recovery

2. **Time to surgery**
   - **<24 hours:** Good outcome
   - **24-48 hours:** Moderate outcome
   - **>48 hours:** Poor outcome (irreversible paralysis)

3. **Extent of abscess**
   - **Localized:** Better prognosis
   - **Circumferential:** Worse prognosis

**Key Point:** **Pre-op neurologic status = most important prognostic factor** (paralysis >48 hours = irreversible)

---

## COMPLICATIONS

**Mnemonic: "PARALYZED"**

**P**aralysis (permanent if delayed treatment)
**A**bscess recurrence (10-20% if inadequate drainage)
**R**espiratory failure (if cervical/high thoracic)
**A**rachno iditis (meningeal inflammation)
**L**ong-term disability (50% have residual deficits)
**Y**ielding to sepsis (septic shock)
**Z**one of myelopathy (cord infarction)
**E**nduring pain (chronic)
**D**eath (5-15%)

**Key Point:** **Permanent paralysis if treatment delayed >48 hours**

---

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **Spinal epidural abscess = surgical emergency** (irreversible paralysis if delayed)
- **Posterior thoracic epidural space = most common location**
- **T2 hyperintense epidural collection + rim enhancement + cord compression = abscess**
- **IVDU = #1 risk factor** (MRSA bacteremia)
- **S. aureus = 60-80%** (MRSA if IVDU)
- **Hematogenous spread = most common source** (50-60%)
- **50% have vertebral osteomyelitis + discitis** (contiguous spread)
- **Progression: Back pain → radiculopathy → weakness → paralysis** (over days to weeks)
- **Treatment: Urgent laminectomy + drainage + IV antibiotics** (within 24 hours)
- **Pre-op neurologic status = most important prognostic factor**
- **Paralysis >48 hours = irreversible** (<10% recovery)
- **MRI entire spine mandatory** (abscess may extend beyond symptomatic area)
