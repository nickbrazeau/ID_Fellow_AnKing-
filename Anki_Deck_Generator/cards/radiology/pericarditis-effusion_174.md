## Pericarditis & Pericardial Effusion

**Q:** What are the characteristic imaging findings of pericarditis and pericardial effusion on CT and echocardiography, and when does it become a surgical emergency (tamponade)?

**A:**

## DEFINITION

**Pericarditis:** **"Inflammation of pericardium (visceral + parietal layers)"**

**Pericardial Effusion:** **"Abnormal fluid accumulation in pericardial space (between visceral and parietal pericardium)"**

**Cardiac Tamponade:** **"Hemodynamic compromise from pericardial effusion compressing heart chambers"**

**Key Point:** **Pericarditis = inflamed pericardium** | **Effusion = fluid in pericardial space** | **Tamponade = hemodynamic compromise**

---

## ANATOMY

**Pericardium Layers:**

1. **Visceral pericardium** (epicardium - adherent to myocardium)
2. **Pericardial space** (potential space, normally 15-50 mL fluid)
3. **Parietal pericardium** (fibrous sac)

**Normal Pericardial Fluid:** 15-50 mL (lubricates heart motion)

**Key Point:** **Pericardial space = between visceral and parietal pericardium** (normally 15-50 mL fluid)

---

## ETIOLOGY

**Infectious (20-30%):**

**Viral (Most Common Infectious):**
- **Coxsackievirus** (most common viral)
- **Echovirus**
- **Adenovirus**
- **HIV**
- **Influenza**

**Bacterial (Purulent - 5-10%):**
- ***Staphylococcus aureus*** (most common bacterial)
- ***Streptococcus pneumoniae***
- ***Mycobacterium tuberculosis*** (TB pericarditis - 5-10% globally, up to 70% in endemic areas/HIV)
- **Anaerobes** (if esophageal perforation)

**Fungal (<5%, Immunocompromised):**
- ***Histoplasma*** (most common fungal)
- ***Coccidioides***
- ***Candida***

**Non-Infectious (70-80%):**
- **Idiopathic** (presumed viral - 30-40%)
- **Post-MI** (Dressler syndrome - 1-3%)
- **Uremia** (renal failure)
- **Autoimmune** (SLE, rheumatoid arthritis)
- **Malignancy** (lung, breast, lymphoma)
- **Post-cardiac surgery** (post-pericardiotomy syndrome)
- **Radiation** (post-RT)
- **Trauma** (blunt, penetrating)
- **Medications** (hydralazine, procainamide)

**Key Point:** **Viral = most common infectious** (Coxsackie) | **TB = important in endemic areas/HIV** (5-70%)

---

## ECHOCARDIOGRAPHY FINDINGS

**Echo = First-Line Imaging (Bedside, Fast)**

**Advantages:**
- **Bedside** (critical care, ED)
- **Real-time** (assesses hemodynamics)
- **Quantifies effusion**
- **Detects tamponade**

---

### **PERICARDIAL EFFUSION:**

**Hallmark: Echo-Free Space Between Visceral and Parietal Pericardium**

**Appearance:**
- **Anechoic (echo-free)** space surrounding heart
- **Circumferential** (surrounds entire heart) OR **loculated** (localized)

**Quantification (Size):**

**Small:**
- **<10mm** separation (diastole)
- **<100 mL**

**Moderate:**
- **10-20mm** separation
- **100-500 mL**

**Large:**
- **>20mm** separation
- **>500 mL**

**Massive:**
- **>20mm** with **swinging heart** (heart moves within fluid)

**Key Point:** **Anechoic space around heart = pericardial effusion** (size by diastolic separation)

---

### **PERICARDIAL THICKENING (Pericarditis):**

**Normal Pericardium:**
- **<2mm thick** (may not be visible on echo)

**Pericarditis:**
- **Thickened pericardium** (>2-3mm)
- **Echogenic strands** (fibrinous exudate)
- **May have small effusion**

**Chronic Constrictive Pericarditis:**
- **Thickened pericardium** (>4mm)
- **Calcification** (bright echogenic foci)
- **Restricted ventricular filling**

**Key Point:** **Thickened pericardium >2-3mm = pericarditis**

---

### **CARDIAC TAMPONADE (Hemodynamic Compromise):**

**Echocardiographic Signs:**

**Mnemonic: "COLLAPSE-RA-RV-SWINGING-RESPIRATORY"**

**C**ollapse (chamber)
**O**ne or more chambers compressed
**L**ate diastolic RA collapse (early sign)
**L**ow blood pressure (clinical)
**A**trial collapse (RA - early sign, sensitive)
**P**ericardial effusion (large)
**S**hocked patient
**E**arly diastolic RV collapse (more specific)

**RA Collapse:**
- **Right atrial collapse** (in diastole - early, sensitive sign)
- **RA diastolic collapse >1/3 of cardiac cycle** (high specificity)

**RV Collapse:**
- **Right ventricular collapse** (in early diastole - more specific)
- **Indicates tamponade**

**S**winging heart (heart moves within large effusion)
**W**obbling motion (electrical alternans on ECG)
**I**nferior vena cava (IVC) plethora (dilated, no respiratory collapse)
**N**o respiratory IVC collapse
**G**eneral hemodynamic compromise
**I**VC >2cm diameter without collapse
**N**o variation with respiration
**G**ross distention

**R**espiratory variation (exaggerated)
**E**xaggerated respiratory variation in mitral/tricuspid inflow velocities
**S**winging heart
**P**ulsus paradoxus (>10mmHg drop in systolic BP with inspiration)
**I**nflow velocities vary (>25% mitral, >40% tricuspid)
**R**espiratory changes exaggerated
**A**lternans (electrical - swinging heart)
**T**amponade physiology
**O**utflow velocities vary
**R**educed cardiac output
**Y**ielding to shock

**Clinical Tamponade (Beck Triad):**
1. **Hypotension** (systolic BP <90mmHg)
2. **Elevated JVP** (jugular venous distention)
3. **Muffled heart sounds**

**Key Point:** **RA/RV collapse + IVC plethora + respiratory variation = tamponade** (surgical emergency)

---

## CT FINDINGS

**CT Advantages:**
- **Quantifies effusion** (volume measurement)
- **Characterizes fluid** (density - simple vs complex)
- **Evaluates pericardium** (thickening, calcification)
- **Detects complications** (abscess, fistula)

---

### **PERICARDIAL EFFUSION:**

**Hallmark: Hypodense Fluid in Pericardial Space**

**Appearance:**
- **Hypodense fluid** (0-20 HU - simple transudate)
- **Circumferential** or **loculated**
- **Surrounds heart** (between visceral and parietal pericardium)

**Characterization by Density:**

**Simple Transudate (0-20 HU):**
- **Viral pericarditis**
- **Uremic pericarditis**
- **Idiopathic**

**Exudate (20-40 HU):**
- **Bacterial pericarditis** (purulent)
- **Malignancy**
- **TB pericarditis**

**Hemorrhagic (40-60 HU):**
- **Trauma** (hemopericardium)
- **Post-MI** (myocardial rupture)
- **Aortic dissection**
- **Post-cardiac surgery**

**Key Point:** **Hypodense fluid around heart = pericardial effusion** | **Density characterizes fluid type**

---

### **PERICARDIAL THICKENING (Pericarditis):**

**Normal Pericardium:**
- **<2mm thick** (may not be visible)

**Acute Pericarditis:**
- **Thickened pericardium** (>2-3mm)
- **Pericardial enhancement** (inflamed pericardium enhances)
- **Pericardial effusion** (small to moderate)

**Chronic Constrictive Pericarditis:**
- **Thickened pericardium** (>4mm, often >6mm)
- **Calcification** (high attenuation foci)
- **Encases heart** (restricts filling)

**Key Point:** **Thickened enhancing pericardium = acute pericarditis** | **Thickened calcified = chronic constrictive**

---

### **COMPLICATIONS:**

**Pericardial Abscess:**
- **Loculated collection** (rim-enhancing)
- **Gas bubbles** (if anaerobes)
- **Purulent pericarditis** (S. aureus, S. pneumoniae)

**Fistula:**
- **Esophageal-pericardial fistula** (if esophageal perforation - Boerhaave syndrome)
- **Coronary-pericardial fistula** (post-MI)

**Tamponade:**
- **Large effusion** (>500 mL)
- **Compressed chambers** (RA, RV)
- **IVC dilatation**

**Key Point:** **CT excellent for abscess, fistula detection** (complications)

---

## CARDIAC MRI FINDINGS

**MRI Advantages:**
- **Superior tissue characterization** (pericardium, effusion, myocardium)
- **No radiation**
- **Detects pericardial inflammation** (enhancement)

**Findings:**

**Pericardial Effusion:**
- **T1:** Hypointense (simple fluid) OR hyperintense (hemorrhage, protein-rich)
- **T2:** Hyperintense (bright - fluid)

**Pericarditis:**
- **Thickened pericardium** (>2-3mm)
- **Post-contrast enhancement** (inflamed pericardium enhances - HALLMARK)
- **Adjacent myocardial enhancement** (if myopericarditis)

**Constrictive Pericarditis:**
- **Thickened pericardium** (>4mm)
- **Calcification** (low signal)
- **Septal bounce** (ventricular interdependence - septal shift with respiration)

**Key Point:** **MRI excellent for pericardial enhancement** (confirms inflammation)

---

## CLINICAL PRESENTATION

**Acute Pericarditis (Classic Triad):**

1. **Chest pain** (90-95%):
   - **Pleuritic** (sharp, worse with inspiration)
   - **Positional** (worse lying flat, relieved sitting forward)
   - **Radiates to trapezius ridge** (phrenic nerve - pathognomonic)

2. **Pericardial friction rub** (30-50%):
   - **Triphasic** (atrial systole, ventricular systole, ventricular diastole)
   - **Scratchy, grating** sound
   - **Best heard at left sternal border** (leaning forward)

3. **ECG changes:**
   - **Diffuse ST elevation** (concave upward - all leads except aVR, V1)
   - **PR depression** (pathognomonic)
   - **No reciprocal ST depression** (vs STEMI)

**Pericardial Effusion:**
- **Asymptomatic** (if small, slow accumulation)
- **Dyspnea** (if large)
- **Chest discomfort**

**Cardiac Tamponade (Beck Triad):**
1. **Hypotension** (systolic <90mmHg)
2. **Elevated JVP** (jugular venous distention)
3. **Muffled heart sounds**
- **Pulsus paradoxus** (>10mmHg drop in systolic BP with inspiration - sensitive)
- **Tachycardia**
- **Dyspnea**

**Key Point:** **Pleuritic chest pain + friction rub + diffuse ST elevation = pericarditis** | **Beck triad = tamponade**

---

## DIAGNOSIS

**Imaging:**
- **Echocardiography** (first-line - bedside, quantifies effusion, detects tamponade)
- **CT with contrast** (if echo non-diagnostic, evaluates complications)
- **MRI** (if chronic, constrictive pericarditis)

**ECG:**
- **Diffuse ST elevation** (concave upward)
- **PR depression** (pathognomonic)
- **Electrical alternans** (if large effusion - QRS axis shifts with heart movement)

**Labs:**
- **Troponin elevated** (if myopericarditis - 30-50%)
- **CRP/ESR elevated** (90-95%)
- **WBC elevated** (if bacterial)

**Pericardiocentesis (If Effusion):**
- **Indications:** Tamponade, suspected bacterial/TB pericarditis, diagnostic uncertainty
- **Send:** Cell count, Gram stain, culture (aerobic, anaerobic, AFB, fungal), cytology, glucose, protein, ADA (if TB suspected)

**Key Point:** **Echo = first-line** | **Pericardiocentesis if tamponade or suspected bacterial/TB**

---

## TREATMENT

**Acute Viral/Idiopathic Pericarditis:**

**First-Line:**
- **NSAIDs** + **Colchicine**
  - **Ibuprofen** 600-800mg PO tid OR **Aspirin** 750-1000mg PO tid x 2-4 weeks (taper over 2-4 weeks)
  - **Colchicine** 0.5-0.6mg PO bid x 3 months (reduces recurrence by 50%)
- **Gastroprotection:** PPI (omeprazole)

**Refractory:**
- **Corticosteroids** (prednisone 0.5-1 mg/kg/day - if NSAIDs contraindicated or refractory)

**Key Point:** **NSAIDs + colchicine = first-line** (3 months colchicine reduces recurrence)

---

**Bacterial (Purulent) Pericarditis:**

**Empiric Antibiotics:**
- **Vancomycin** 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h + **Ceftriaxone** 2g IV q12h OR **Cefepime** 2g IV q8h

**Targeted (Based on culture):**
- **S. aureus (MSSA):** Cefazolin or nafcillin
- **S. aureus (MRSA):** Vancomycin or daptomycin
- **S. pneumoniae:** Ceftriaxone or penicillin G
- **Anaerobes:** Metronidazole or carbapenems

**Duration:** 4-6 weeks IV

**Drainage:**
- **Pericardiocentesis** (diagnostic + therapeutic)
- **Surgical drainage** (pericardial window, pericardiectomy - if loculated, thick pus)

**Key Point:** **Bacterial pericarditis = drainage + IV antibiotics** (both mandatory)

---

**TB Pericarditis:**

**Treatment:**
- **RIPE therapy** (rifampin, isoniazid, pyrazinamide, ethambutol) x 6-12 months
- **Corticosteroids:** Consider (prednisone 1 mg/kg/day x 4 weeks, taper over 6-12 weeks)
  - **Reduces mortality** in TB pericarditis (especially if HIV+)

**Pericardiocentesis:**
- **If large effusion** or **tamponade**
- **Send for AFB stain, culture, PCR, ADA** (>40 U/L suggestive)

**Key Point:** **TB pericarditis = RIPE + steroids** (reduces mortality, especially HIV+)

---

**Cardiac Tamponade:**

**EMERGENCY: Urgent Pericardiocentesis**

**Procedure:**
- **Subxiphoid approach** (most common)
- **Echo-guided** (safer, real-time visualization)
- **Remove fluid** (decompress heart)

**Temporary Measures (Before Pericardiocentesis):**
- **IV fluids** (increase preload)
- **Avoid positive pressure ventilation** (worsens tamponade)
- **Avoid vasodilators** (hypotension)

**Surgical Drainage:**
- **Pericardial window** (if recurrent, loculated)
- **Pericardiectomy** (if constrictive pericarditis)

**Key Point:** **Tamponade = urgent pericardiocentesis** (life-saving, cannot wait)

---

## PROGNOSIS

**Acute Viral/Idiopathic Pericarditis:**
- **Cure rate:** 90-95%
- **Recurrence:** 15-30% (reduced to 10-15% with colchicine)

**Bacterial (Purulent) Pericarditis:**
- **Mortality:** 30-50% (even with treatment)
- **Worse if delayed diagnosis**

**TB Pericarditis:**
- **Mortality:** 20-40% (without steroids)
- **Mortality:** 10-20% (with RIPE + steroids)
- **Constrictive pericarditis:** 20-30% develop (chronic complication)

**Cardiac Tamponade:**
- **Mortality:** 100% if untreated
- **Mortality:** 5-10% if treated promptly

**Key Point:** **Viral pericarditis = excellent prognosis** (90-95% cure) | **Bacterial/TB = high mortality** (30-50%)

---

## COMPLICATIONS

**Mnemonic: "TAMPONADE-CONSTRICTIVE-RECURRENT"**

**T**amponade (hemodynamic collapse - EMERGENCY)
**A**bnormal rhythm (atrial fibrillation - 5-10%)
**M**yopericarditis (troponin elevated, myocardial involvement)
**P**ersistent effusion (chronic)
**O**utcome poor (if bacterial)
**N**ew recurrence (15-30% recurrence)
**A**bscess (if bacterial)
**D**eath (if untreated tamponade)
**E**xtensive inflammation

**C**onstrictive pericarditis (chronic - 1-2% after acute, 20-30% after TB)
**O**bliteration (pericardial space fibrosis)
**N**ormal pericardium thickens (>4mm)
**S**clerosis (fibrous thickening)
**T**hickened calcified pericardium
**R**estriction (ventricular filling)
**I**mpaired diastolic filling
**C**alcification (chronic)
**T**otal pericardial thickening
**I**ncreased JVP (chronic)
**V**entricular interdependence (septal bounce)
**E**ventual heart failure

**R**ecurrence (15-30% - reduced with colchicine)
**E**ffusion (persistent)
**C**hronic pericarditis
**U**nresponding (refractory)
**R**elapse (recurrent)
**R**epeat episodes
**E**xacerbations
**N**eed for colchicine
**T**amponade (if large effusion)

**Key Point:** **Tamponade = life-threatening** | **Constrictive pericarditis = chronic complication** (1-2% acute, 20-30% TB)

---

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **Pericarditis = pleuritic chest pain + friction rub + diffuse ST elevation** (concave upward)
- **Pericardial effusion = anechoic space around heart** (echo)
- **Tamponade = RA/RV collapse + IVC plethora + pulsus paradoxus** (emergency pericardiocentesis)
- **Beck triad (tamponade): Hypotension + elevated JVP + muffled heart sounds**
- **Viral = most common infectious** (Coxsackie) | **TB = important in endemic areas/HIV** (5-70%)
- **Echo = first-line imaging** (bedside, quantifies effusion, detects tamponade)
- **CT excellent for complications** (abscess, fistula, calcification)
- **Viral pericarditis = NSAIDs + colchicine x 3 months** (reduces recurrence)
- **Bacterial pericarditis = drainage + IV antibiotics 4-6 weeks** (mortality 30-50%)
- **TB pericarditis = RIPE + steroids** (reduces mortality, especially HIV+)
- **Tamponade = urgent pericardiocentesis** (100% mortality if untreated)
- **Constrictive pericarditis = chronic complication** (1-2% after acute, 20-30% after TB)
