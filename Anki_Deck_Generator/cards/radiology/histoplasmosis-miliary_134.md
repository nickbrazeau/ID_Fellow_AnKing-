## Histoplasmosis - Miliary Nodules & Calcified Granulomas

**Q:** What are the characteristic CT findings of histoplasmosis including miliary nodules, calcified granulomas, and mediastinal lymphadenopathy?

**A:**

## DEFINITION

**Histoplasmosis:** Endemic fungal infection caused by ***Histoplasma capsulatum***, dimorphic fungus in soil with bird/bat droppings.

**Geographic Distribution:** **Ohio and Mississippi River Valleys** ("Histo Belt")

**Key Point:** **Histoplasmosis = cave exploration, bird roosts, endemic to Midwest**

---

## CT FINDINGS IN DISSEMINATED HISTOPLASMOSIS

**Hallmark: Small Random Nodules (Miliary Pattern)**

**Acute Disseminated:**
- **Small random nodules** (1-3mm, miliary pattern)
- **Bilateral, diffuse distribution**
- **Interlobular septal thickening**
- **Ground-glass opacities**
- **Hepatosplenomegaly** (multiorgan)

**Key Point:** **Disseminated histo = miliary nodules + hepatosplenomegaly + immunocompromised**

---

## CALCIFIED GRANULOMAS (Healed Histoplasmosis)

**"Buckshot" Calcifications:**
- **Multiple small calcified nodules** (2-5mm)
- **Random distribution** (throughout lungs + spleen)
- **Popcorn or laminated calcification**
- **Hilar/mediastinal lymph node calcification**

**Key Point:** **Multiple calcified lung + splenic nodules = old histoplasmosis**

---

## MEDIASTINAL LYMPHADENOPATHY

**Acute Infection:**
- **Hilar and mediastinal LAD** (bilateral)
- **Low-density centers** (caseating necrosis)
- **Rim enhancement** on contrast CT
- **May compress airways** (especially in children)

**Chronic/Healed:**
- **Calcified lymph nodes** ("eggshell" or diffuse)
- **Fibrosing mediastinitis** (late complication)

**Key Point:** **Mediastinal LAD + rim enhancement = active granulomatous infection** (histo, TB, sarcoid)

---

## CLINICAL PRESENTATIONS BY IMAGING PATTERN

| **Pattern** | **Clinical Context** | **CT Findings** |
|-------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Acute pulmonary** | Immunocompetent, cave exposure | Patchy consolidation, hilar LAD |
| **Chronic cavitary** | COPD, upper lobe disease | Thick-walled cavities (mimics TB) |
| **Disseminated** | AIDS (CD4 <150), immunosuppressed | **Miliary nodules, hepatosplenomegaly** |
| **Mediastinal granuloma** | Healed infection | Calcified mass, airway compression |
| **Fibrosing mediastinitis** | Late complication | Mediastinal fibrosis, vascular compression |

**Key Point:** **AIDS + miliary nodules = disseminated histoplasmosis**

---

## DIAGNOSIS

**Serology:** Antibody testing (complement fixation, immunodiffusion)
**Antigen:** Urine/serum *Histoplasma* antigen (sensitive in disseminated disease)
**Culture:** Blood, bone marrow, BAL (slow - takes 2-6 weeks)
**Histopathology:** GMS or PAS stain (small intracellular yeasts)

**Key Point:** **Urine *Histoplasma* antigen = best test for disseminated disease** (90% sensitive)

---

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **Ohio/Mississippi River Valleys = "Histo Belt"**
- **Disseminated histo = miliary nodules (1-3mm random) + hepatosplenomegaly**
- **Calcified "buckshot" nodules in lung + spleen = old healed histoplasmosis**
- **Mediastinal LAD with rim enhancement = active infection**
- **Fibrosing mediastinitis = late complication** (vascular/airway compression)
- **AIDS CD4 <150 at highest risk for disseminated disease**
- **Diagnosis: Urine *Histoplasma* antigen** (90% sensitive in disseminated)
- **Treatment: Itraconazole (mild-moderate), amphotericin B (severe)**
