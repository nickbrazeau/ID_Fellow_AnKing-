## Burn Wound and Trauma-Related Infections

**Q:** What are the key organisms, topical agents for burn wounds, and antibiotic prophylaxis for open fractures and traumatic wounds (2024)?

**A:**

**Burn Wound Infections:**

**Common Organisms:**

| Time Post-Burn | Predominant Organisms |
|----------------|----------------------|
| **First 5 days** | Gram-positive: ***S. aureus*** (MSSA/MRSA), β-hemolytic strep |
| **After 5 days** | Gram-negative: ***Pseudomonas aeruginosa*** (most common), *Acinetobacter*, *Klebsiella*, *E. coli* |
| **Late/invasive** | **Fungi**: *Candida*, *Aspergillus*, mucormycosis (eschar invasion) |

**Topical Antimicrobials (2024 Guidelines):**

| Agent | Coverage | Application | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|-------|----------|-------------|------------|---------------|
| **Silver sulfadiazine (SSD) 1%** | Broad (gram-pos, gram-neg, *Pseudomonas*) | BID to burn areas | **Most commonly used**; affordable, easy to use, painless | Impairs epithelialization (cytotoxic to fibroblasts); resistance possible |
| **Mafenide acetate 5%** | Broad (esp. gram-neg); **penetrates eschar** | BID; painful | Deep burn penetration; cartilage infection | Painful; carbonic anhydrase inhibitor (metabolic acidosis) |
| **Silver-coated dressings** | Broad antimicrobial | Per dressing instructions | **Recommended over SSD** in guidelines; less frequent changes | More expensive |
| **Bacitracin/polymyxin B/neomycin compounded** | Broad | BID-TID | 100% effective against isolates in recent study | Availability variable |

**2024 Guideline Recommendations:**
- **Silver-containing dressings** preferred over antiseptics or antibiotics (regardless of burn depth)
- **Silver sulfadiazine**: Most common in low-resource settings; use for deep 2nd/3rd degree burns pre-excision
- **Systemic antibiotics**: NOT routinely indicated for prophylaxis; use only for proven infection

---

**Trauma-Related Antibiotic Prophylaxis (2024 GAIS/AAST/WSES Guidelines):**

**Open Fractures:**

| Fracture Type | Antibiotic Regimen | Duration | Key Points |
|---------------|-------------------|----------|------------|
| **Type I** (clean, <1 cm laceration) | **Cefazolin 2 g IV** | **≤24 hours** | NOT 7-10 days |
| **Type II-III** (>1 cm laceration, contamination) | **Cefazolin 2 g IV** PLUS<br>**Gentamicin 5 mg/kg IV** (if severe contamination) | **≤24 hours** | Add gram-neg coverage if farm/aquatic contamination |
| **Gunshot-related fractures** | **Cefazolin 2 g IV** | **Single dose to 24h** | NO prolonged antibiotics |
| **Marine/freshwater contamination** | Add **ciprofloxacin** or **doxycycline** (for *Aeromonas, Vibrio*) | ≤24h | Source control (irrigation) most important |

**Key Principles (2024):**
- **Timing**: Antibiotics within **1 hour of injury** (preferably in ED)
- **Duration**: **≤24 hours** (NOT 7-10 days)
- **Source control**: Irrigation, debridement, wound closure more important than prolonged antibiotics
- **No benefit**: Prolonged antibiotics do NOT reduce infection rate

**Soft Tissue Lacerations:**
- **Simple lacerations**: **NO prophylactic antibiotics**
- **Exceptions**: Specific wound concerns (contamination, devitalized tissue) OR patient risk factors (immunocompromised) → 24h antibiotics

**Mammalian Bites:**
- **Prophylaxis**: **Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg PO BID × 3-5 days**
- Alternatives: Doxycycline + metronidazole; moxifloxacin
- Source control: Irrigation, wound care

**Tetanus Prophylaxis:**
- **Clean minor wounds**: Td booster if >10 years since last dose
- **Contaminated/deep wounds**: Td booster if >5 years; **TIG** (tetanus immune globulin) if <3 lifetime doses or unknown

**Key Points:**
- **Burn infections**: Early = *S. aureus*, Late = *Pseudomonas*; topical silver-containing agents preferred
- **Open fractures**: Cefazolin ≤24h (NOT 7-10 days); timing <1 hour from injury
- **No prolonged antibiotics**: ≤24h sufficient for most open fractures
- **Tetanus**: Always assess and update immunization status

**Mnemonic - "Burns = Silver for Pseudomonas, Trauma = ≤24h antibiotics (NOT 7-10 days)"**

**Pearl:** Burn wound infections: early *S. aureus*, late *Pseudomonas aeruginosa*. Silver-containing dressings preferred over silver sulfadiazine (2024 guidelines). Open fracture prophylaxis (2024): cefazolin ≤24 hours (NOT 7-10 days), administer within 1 hour of injury. Simple lacerations do NOT require prophylactic antibiotics. Always check tetanus status (Td booster if >5 years for contaminated wounds; TIG if <3 lifetime doses).
