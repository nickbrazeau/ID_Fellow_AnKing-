## Diverticulitis and Neutropenic Enterocolitis: Management

**Q:** How does antibiotic management differ for uncomplicated vs. complicated diverticulitis (Hinchey classification), and what is the management of neutropenic enterocolitis?

**A:**

**DIVERTICULITIS - Hinchey Classification:**

| Stage | Description | Antibiotics | Management |
|-------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| **0** | Clinical only | **NOT needed** | Outpatient, conservative |
| **Ia** | Pericolonic inflammation/phlegmon | **Selective use** (can omit) | Outpatient possible |
| **Ib** | Pericolic abscess | IV antibiotics | Inpatient; drainage if >4 cm |
| **II** | Pelvic/distant abscess | IV antibiotics | Percutaneous drainage >4 cm (80% success) |
| **III** | Purulent peritonitis | IV antibiotics | **Surgery** (Hartmann's if unstable) |
| **IV** | Feculent peritonitis | IV antibiotics | **Urgent surgery** |

**Antibiotic Regimen (complicated):**
- **Piperacillin-tazobactam 3.375 g IV q6h** OR
- **Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q24h + metronidazole 500 mg IV q8h**
- **Carbapenem** if high resistance risk

**NEUTROPENIC ENTEROCOLITIS (Typhlitis):**

**Diagnostic Criteria:**
- ANC <500 cells/μL
- Fever >38.3°C
- Abdominal pain (often RLQ)
- **CT: Bowel wall thickening >4 mm** (>3 cm length) + fat stranding

**Management:**
1. **Broad-spectrum IV antibiotics** (↓ mortality by 40%)
   - Cover Gram-negatives, Gram-positives, anaerobes
   - **Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 g IV q6h** + **vancomycin** OR **meropenem 1 g IV q8h** + vancomycin
2. **Bowel rest**, fluid resuscitation, NPO
3. **Surgery only** if perforation, uncontrolled bleeding, clinical deterioration

**Pearl:** Uncomplicated diverticulitis often does NOT require antibiotics; typhlitis requires immediate broad-spectrum coverage.
