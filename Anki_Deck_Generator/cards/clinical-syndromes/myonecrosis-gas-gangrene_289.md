## Myonecrosis and Gas Gangrene: Treatment

**Q:** What are the key differences between gas gangrene and necrotizing fasciitis, and what is the treatment approach?

**A:**

**Clinical Distinctions:**

| Feature | Gas Gangrene (Clostridial Myonecrosis) | Necrotizing Fasciitis |
|---------|----------------------------------------|-----------------------|
| **Pathogen** | *Clostridium perfringens* (most common), *C. septicum* | Group A Strep (*S. pyogenes*), polymicrobial, *S. aureus* |
| **Tissue affected** | **Muscle** (deep) | **Fascia + subcutaneous tissue** (spares muscle initially) |
| **Gas production** | Prominent crepitus, "bronze" skin discoloration | Variable gas |
| **Onset** | **Rapid** (hours) - most rapidly fatal human infection | Rapid (days) |
| **Risk factors** | Trauma, surgery, bowel perforation, spontaneous (*C. septicum* in colon cancer) | Trauma, surgery, IVDU, varicella (children) |

**Treatment (URGENT):**

**1. Surgical Debridement:**
- **IMMEDIATE + aggressive** removal of all necrotic tissue
- May require **amputation** to control infection
- **Repeat surgeries** often needed
- **DO NOT delay** for imaging/cultures

**2. Antibiotics:**

**Empiric (before culture):**
- **Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h** (MRSA coverage)
- \+ **Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 g IV q6h** OR **carbapenem**
- \+ **Clindamycin 900 mg IV q8h** (toxin suppression - ESSENTIAL)

**Targeted (after identification):**
- **Clostridial**: **Penicillin G 4 million units IV q4h + clindamycin 900 mg IV q8h**
- **Group A Strep**: **Penicillin G 4 million units IV q4h + clindamycin 900 mg IV q8h**

**3. Supportive Care:**
- ICU admission, aggressive fluid resuscitation
- **Hyperbaric oxygen**: Controversial (may delay surgery - NOT recommended)

**Pearl:** Clindamycin ESSENTIAL for toxin suppression in both gas gangrene and GAS necrotizing fasciitis; surgery is life-saving and cannot be delayed.
