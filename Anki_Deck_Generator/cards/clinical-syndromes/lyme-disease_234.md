## Question

How is Lyme disease managed? Use **"Erythema Migrans → Doxycycline 100mg BID × 10d, Neurologic/Cardiac → Ceftriaxone IV × 14-28d"** framework.

## Answer

### **Etiology:**

***Borrelia burgdorferi*** (US) - spirochete
- **Vector:** *Ixodes scapularis* (deer tick) - Northeast/Upper Midwest US
- **Requires ≥36-48h attachment** for transmission

### **Clinical Stages:**

**Early Localized (3-30 Days Post-Bite):**
- **Erythema migrans (EM)** - 70-80% of cases
  - **"Bull's-eye" rash** (central clearing, but often uniformly erythematous)
  - **Expanding annular lesion** (≥5 cm diameter)
  - **Single or multiple lesions**
  - **Minimally symptomatic** (non-pruritic, non-painful)
- **Flu-like symptoms:** Fever, fatigue, headache, myalgias, arthralgias

**Early Disseminated (Days to Weeks):**
- **Multiple erythema migrans lesions** (hematogenous spread)
- **Neurologic (15%):**
  - **Lymphocytic meningitis**
  - **Cranial neuropathy** (facial nerve palsy - bilateral in 25%)
  - **Radiculopathy**
- **Cardiac (1-2%):**
  - **AV block** (1st, 2nd, 3rd degree)
  - **Myopericarditis**
- **Arthritis:** Migratory arthralgias

**Late Disseminated (Months to Years):**
- **Lyme arthritis** (60% if untreated)
  - **Oligoarticular** (knee most common)
  - **Intermittent, recurrent**
- **Neurologic:**
  - **Encephalopathy** (memory, concentration deficits)
  - **Peripheral neuropathy**

### **Diagnosis:**

**Clinical Diagnosis (EM):**
- **Erythema migrans = CLINICAL diagnosis** (do NOT need serology)
- **Treat empirically** if classic EM in endemic area

**Serology (Two-Tier Testing):**

**Indications for Testing:**
- **Suspected early disseminated or late Lyme** (NOT for isolated EM)
- **Neurologic, cardiac, arthritic manifestations**

**Two-Tier Approach:**
1. **ELISA (screening):**
   - **Negative → stop** (no Lyme)
   - **Positive/equivocal → confirmatory test**
2. **Western blot (confirmatory):**
   - **IgM:** ≥2 of 3 bands (OspC, 39 kDa, 41 kDa)
   - **IgG:** ≥5 of 10 bands

**Timing:**
- **IgM:** Positive 2-4 weeks post-infection
- **IgG:** Positive 4-6 weeks post-infection
- **Serology often NEGATIVE** in early EM (<4 weeks)
- **IgG remains positive lifelong** (does NOT indicate active infection)

**CSF (If Neurologic Lyme):**
- **Lymphocytic pleocytosis** (10-500 cells/μL)
- **↑Protein, normal glucose**
- **CSF *Borrelia* antibody index** (intrathecal antibody production)

**Do NOT Test:**
- **Asymptomatic tick bite** (very low yield)
- **Non-specific symptoms** (fatigue, myalgias) without objective findings

### **Treatment:**

**Early Localized (Erythema Migrans):**

**First-Line:**
- **Doxycycline 100mg PO BID × 10 days** (preferred, also covers *Anaplasma*)
- **Alternatives:**
  - **Amoxicillin 500mg PO TID × 14 days**
  - **Cefuroxime 500mg PO BID × 14 days**
  - **Azithromycin 500mg PO daily × 7-10 days** (less effective, only if cannot take others)

**Early Disseminated (Neurologic - Cranial Neuropathy, Meningitis, Radiculopathy):**

**Meningitis/Radiculopathy:**
- **Ceftriaxone 2g IV daily × 14-28 days** (first-line)
- **Alternative:** Cefotaxime 2g IV q8h OR penicillin G 18-24 million units/day IV

**Isolated Facial Nerve Palsy (Without Meningitis):**
- **Doxycycline 100mg PO BID × 14-28 days** (oral adequate if no CSF involvement)

**Early Disseminated (Cardiac - AV Block):**

**1st Degree AV Block:**
- **Doxycycline 100mg PO BID × 14-21 days**

**≥2nd Degree AV Block:**
- **Ceftriaxone 2g IV daily × 14-21 days** (hospitalize, telemetry)
- **Temporary pacemaker** (if symptomatic, high-degree block)
- **Usually resolves** within 1-6 weeks

**Late Disseminated (Lyme Arthritis):**

**Oral Therapy:**
- **Doxycycline 100mg PO BID × 28 days**
- **Alternative:** Amoxicillin 500mg PO TID × 28 days

**If Persistent Arthritis After Oral:**
- **Repeat oral course × 28 days** OR **ceftriaxone 2g IV daily × 28 days**

**Antibiotic-Refractory Arthritis:**
- **Synovectomy, NSAIDs, DMARDs** (immune-mediated, NOT active infection)

**Late Disseminated (Encephalopathy, Peripheral Neuropathy):**
- **Ceftriaxone 2g IV daily × 28 days**

### **Post-Treatment Lyme Disease Syndrome (PTLDS):**

**Definition:**
- **Persistent symptoms** (fatigue, pain, cognitive deficits) **>6 months after treatment**
- **10-20%** of treated patients

**Management:**
- **Supportive care** (prolonged antibiotics NOT effective, per multiple RCTs)
- **Symptomatic treatment** (NSAIDs, physical therapy, cognitive therapy)

### **Prophylaxis (Tick Bite):**

**Single-Dose Doxycycline:**
- **Indications (ALL must be met):**
  1. **Attached tick identified as *I. scapularis***
  2. **≥36h attachment** (tick engorged)
  3. **≥20% local tick infection rate** (endemic area)
  4. **Doxycycline not contraindicated**
  5. **Can give within 72h of tick removal**
- **Dose:** Doxycycline 200mg PO × 1

**If Criteria NOT Met:**
- **Observe** (watchful waiting)
- **Educate:** Return if EM develops (within 30 days)

### **Special Situations:**

**Pregnancy:**
- **Amoxicillin 500mg PO TID** (avoid doxycycline)
- **If IV needed:** Ceftriaxone 2g IV daily

**Children <8 Years:**
- **Amoxicillin 50 mg/kg/day divided TID** (avoid doxycycline)

**Jarisch-Herxheimer Reaction:**
- **Fever, chills, myalgias** within 24h of first antibiotic dose
- **Self-limited** (supportive care)

## Key Points

### **Erythema Migrans = Clinical Diagnosis:**
- **Do NOT need serology** (often negative early)
- **Treat empirically:** Doxycycline 100mg BID × 10 days

### **Serology Two-Tier (ELISA → Western Blot):**
- **Only if disseminated/late disease** (NOT for isolated EM)
- **IgG remains positive lifelong** (does NOT indicate active infection)

### **Neurologic/Cardiac = Ceftriaxone IV:**
- **Ceftriaxone 2g IV daily × 14-28 days**
- **Isolated facial palsy:** Oral doxycycline adequate

### **Lyme Arthritis = Oral × 28 Days:**
- **Doxycycline 100mg BID × 28 days** (first-line)
- **If persistent:** Repeat oral OR ceftriaxone IV

### **Tick Bite Prophylaxis:**
- **Doxycycline 200mg × 1** (if *I. scapularis*, ≥36h attached, endemic area, within 72h removal)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **EM:** Doxycycline 100mg BID × **10 days** (do NOT need serology)
- **Neurologic/cardiac:** Ceftriaxone 2g IV daily × 14-28 days
- **Lyme arthritis:** Doxycycline 100mg BID × **28 days**
- **Serology two-tier:** ELISA → Western blot (only if disseminated/late disease)
- **Tick prophylaxis:** Doxycycline 200mg × 1 (if ≥36h attached, endemic area, within 72h)
- **Facial palsy:** Can treat with oral doxycycline (if no meningitis)
