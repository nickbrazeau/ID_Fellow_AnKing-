## Question

How is acute bacterial sinusitis managed? Use **"Symptoms >10d OR Severe OR Worsening After Improvement → Amoxicillin-Clavulanate × 5-7d"** framework.

## Answer

### **Definition:**

**Acute Rhinosinusitis:**
- **Inflammation of nasal cavity + paranasal sinuses** (<4 weeks duration)

**Viral (90-98%):**
- **Part of common cold** (self-limited)

**Bacterial (2-10%):**
- **Bacterial superinfection**

### **When to Suspect BACTERIAL Sinusitis:**

**≥1 of the following:**

1. **Persistent symptoms** (≥10 days) WITHOUT improvement
2. **Severe symptoms** (≥3-4 days of high fever ≥39°C + purulent nasal discharge)
3. **"Double-sickening"** (worsening symptoms after initial improvement)

**If NONE of above:** Likely VIRAL → supportive care only

### **Clinical Presentation:**

**Symptoms:**
- **Purulent nasal discharge** (anterior or posterior)
- **Nasal obstruction**
- **Facial pain/pressure** (worse with bending forward)
- **Hyposmia/anosmia** (decreased smell)
- **Fever** (if bacterial)

**Physical Exam:**
- **Purulent drainage** from middle meatus
- **Tenderness** over sinuses (frontal, maxillary)

### **Microbiology:**

**Most Common:**
- ***Streptococcus pneumoniae*** (30-40%)
- ***Haemophilus influenzae*** (20-30%)
- ***Moraxella catarrhalis*** (10-20%)

**Less Common:**
- ***Streptococcus pyogenes***
- ***Staphylococcus aureus*** (chronic sinusitis, post-surgical)
- **Anaerobes** (dental source, chronic)

### **Diagnosis:**

**Clinical Diagnosis:**
- **Based on criteria above** (≥10 days OR severe OR worsening)
- **Imaging NOT routinely needed**

**Imaging (CT Sinus):**
- **Only if:** Suspected complications (orbital cellulitis, intracranial extension), recurrent/chronic sinusitis, immunocompromised

**Do NOT obtain cultures** (sinus aspiration rarely performed)

### **Treatment:**

**1. Observation (Watchful Waiting):**

**Indications:**
- **Uncomplicated bacterial sinusitis**
- **No severe symptoms**
- **Reliable follow-up**

**Symptomatic treatment × 7 days:**
- **Intranasal saline irrigation** (improves mucociliary clearance)
- **Intranasal corticosteroids** (mometasone, fluticasone) - reduces inflammation
- **Oral decongestants** (pseudoephedrine) - SHORT-TERM only (<3-5 days)
- **Analgesics** (NSAIDs, acetaminophen)

**Reassess in 7 days:** If no improvement → antibiotics

**2. Antibiotic Therapy:**

**Indications for Immediate Antibiotics:**
- **Severe symptoms** (high fever ≥39°C, purulent discharge ≥3-4 days)
- **"Double-sickening"** (worsening after initial improvement)
- **Failed watchful waiting** (no improvement after 7 days)
- **Immunocompromised**

**First-Line:**
- **Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875mg PO BID × 5-7 days** (covers β-lactamase-producing *H. influenzae*, *M. catarrhalis*)
- **High-dose amoxicillin-clavulanate 2000mg PO BID × 5-7 days** (if high-dose needed: recent antibiotics, daycare, geographic resistance)

**Alternative (PCN Allergy):**
- **Doxycycline 100mg PO BID × 5-7 days**
- **Levofloxacin 500mg PO daily × 5-7 days** (if severe PCN allergy)
- **Moxifloxacin 400mg PO daily × 5-7 days**

**NOT Recommended (Poor Coverage):**
- **Amoxicillin alone** (does NOT cover β-lactamase producers)
- **Azithromycin** (high *S. pneumoniae* resistance, 30-40%)
- **TMP-SMX** (high *S. pneumoniae* resistance)

**Duration:** **5-7 days** (adequate for uncomplicated acute sinusitis)

### **Complications (Rare, <1%):**

**Orbital:**
- **Preseptal cellulitis** (eyelid swelling, no vision change)
- **Orbital cellulitis** (proptosis, ophthalmoplegia, vision loss) → urgent ENT/ophthalmology consult, IV antibiotics

**Intracranial:**
- **Meningitis, epidural abscess, subdural empyema, brain abscess, cavernous sinus thrombosis**
- **Signs:** Severe headache, altered mental status, focal neurologic deficits
- **Urgent imaging (CT/MRI)**, neurosurgery consult, IV antibiotics

### **Follow-Up:**

**Re-evaluate if:**
- **No improvement after 3-5 days** of antibiotics
- **Worsening symptoms** at any time

**Consider:**
- **Different antibiotic** (if treatment failure)
- **CT sinus** (if recurrent, complicated)
- **ENT referral** (if chronic, recurrent, complications)

### **Chronic Rhinosinusitis (≥12 Weeks):**
- **Different entity** (often inflammatory, not infectious)
- **ENT referral** for evaluation
- **Prolonged antibiotics** (3-6 weeks) + intranasal steroids

## Key Points

### **Most Sinusitis is VIRAL:**
- **Do NOT routinely treat with antibiotics**
- **Supportive care** (saline irrigation, intranasal steroids)

### **Antibiotics Only if ≥10d OR Severe OR Worsening:**
- **≥10 days** persistent symptoms
- **Severe** (high fever ≥39°C, purulent discharge ≥3-4 days)
- **"Double-sickening"** (worsening after improvement)

### **Amoxicillin-Clavulanate = First-Line:**
- **875mg PO BID × 5-7 days**
- **Covers β-lactamase producers** (*H. influenzae*, *M. catarrhalis*)

### **5-7 Days Duration:**
- **Adequate** for uncomplicated acute sinusitis
- **No benefit** from longer courses

### **Do NOT Use:**
- **Amoxicillin alone** (poor coverage)
- **Azithromycin** (high *S. pneumoniae* resistance)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Most sinusitis is VIRAL** → supportive care (saline, intranasal steroids)
- **Antibiotics only if:** ≥10 days OR severe (high fever, purulent discharge ≥3-4d) OR worsening after improvement
- **Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875mg BID × 5-7 days** (first-line)
- **5-7 days adequate** (no benefit from longer courses)
- **Do NOT use:** Amoxicillin alone, azithromycin (resistance)
- **Complications rare** (<1%): Orbital/intracranial (urgent imaging, IV antibiotics)
