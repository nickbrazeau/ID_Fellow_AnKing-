## Question

How is acute otitis media managed? Use **"High-Dose Amoxicillin (80-90 mg/kg/day) × 5-10d, Observation if >2y + Non-Severe"** framework.

## Answer

### **Definition:**

**Acute Otitis Media (AOM):**
- **Acute middle ear infection** (behind tympanic membrane)

**Diagnosis Requires ALL 3:**
1. **Acute onset** of symptoms (ear pain, fever, irritability)
2. **Middle ear effusion** (MEE) - bulging TM, limited mobility, otorrhea
3. **Inflammation** - erythema of TM (with MEE)

### **Clinical Presentation:**

**Symptoms:**
- **Ear pain** (otalgia)
- **Fever** (70%)
- **Irritability, tugging at ear** (infants)
- **Hearing loss** (temporary)
- **Otorrhea** (if TM perforation)

**Otoscopic Findings:**
- **Bulging tympanic membrane** (most specific)
- **Erythema, opacification**
- **Decreased TM mobility** (pneumatic otoscopy)
- **Air-fluid level, bubbles**

### **Microbiology:**

**Most Common:**
- ***Streptococcus pneumoniae*** (30-40%)
- ***Haemophilus influenzae*** (non-typeable, 20-30%)
- ***Moraxella catarrhalis*** (10-15%)

**Less Common:**
- ***Streptococcus pyogenes*** (5-10%)
- **Viruses** (RSV, rhinovirus, influenza) - often co-infection

### **Treatment:**

**1. Observation (Watchful Waiting):**

**Indications (ALL must be met):**
- **Age ≥2 years** (or 6-23 months with unilateral, non-severe)
- **Non-severe illness:**
  - Mild otalgia (<48h)
  - Fever <39°C
- **Certain follow-up** (reassess in 48-72h)

**If symptoms worsen or do not improve in 48-72h:** Start antibiotics

**2. Antibiotic Therapy:**

**Indications for Immediate Antibiotics:**
- **Age <6 months** (ALWAYS treat)
- **Bilateral AOM** (especially age <2 years)
- **Severe illness:**
  - Moderate-severe otalgia (>48h)
  - Fever ≥39°C
  - Toxic appearance
- **Otorrhea** (TM perforation)
- **Immunocompromised**
- **Concern for complications**

**First-Line:**
- **Amoxicillin 80-90 mg/kg/day PO divided BID × 5-10 days** (high-dose for drug-resistant *S. pneumoniae*)
  - **Duration:** 10 days (age <2y or severe), 5-7 days (age ≥2y, non-severe)

**Alternative (If Recent Amoxicillin Use <30 Days OR Concurrent Purulent Conjunctivitis):**
- **Amoxicillin-clavulanate 90 mg/kg/day (amoxicillin component) PO divided BID × 5-10 days**
- **Covers β-lactamase-producing *H. influenzae*, *M. catarrhalis***

**PCN Allergy (Non-Severe):**
- **Cefdinir 14 mg/kg/day PO divided BID**
- **Cefuroxime 30 mg/kg/day PO divided BID**
- **Cefpodoxime 10 mg/kg/day PO divided BID**

**PCN Allergy (Severe/IgE-Mediated):**
- **Azithromycin 10 mg/kg PO day 1, then 5 mg/kg days 2-5** (but increasing resistance)
- **Levofloxacin** (if older child, failed multiple therapies)

**Treatment Failure (No Improvement After 48-72h):**
- **Amoxicillin-clavulanate 90 mg/kg/day BID** (if initially amoxicillin)
- **Ceftriaxone 50 mg/kg IM/IV daily × 3 days** (if failed oral antibiotics)

**Duration:**
- **10 days:** Age <2 years OR severe disease
- **5-7 days:** Age ≥2 years, non-severe

### **Adults (Less Common):**

**Indications:**
- **Immunocompromised**
- **Barotrauma** (airplane, diving)
- **Structural abnormalities**

**Treatment:**
- **Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875mg PO BID × 5-7 days**
- **Levofloxacin 500mg PO daily × 5-7 days** (if PCN allergy)

### **Complications (Rare):**

**Intratemporal:**
- **TM perforation** (usually heals spontaneously)
- **Acute mastoiditis** (swelling, erythema behind ear, TM bulging laterally)
- **Facial nerve palsy** (rare)

**Intracranial:**
- **Meningitis, brain abscess, venous sinus thrombosis**
- **Urgent imaging, IV antibiotics, neurosurgery consult**

**Chronic:**
- **Chronic suppurative otitis media** (persistent otorrhea >6 weeks)
- **Cholesteatoma**
- **Hearing loss** (usually temporary, resolves with treatment)

### **Prevention:**

**Vaccines:**
- **PCV13 (pneumococcal conjugate vaccine)** - ↓AOM incidence by 20-30%
- **Influenza vaccine** - ↓viral AOM

**Risk Factor Modification:**
- **Breastfeeding** (↓risk)
- **Avoid secondhand smoke**
- **Avoid supine bottle feeding** (↑Eustachian tube dysfunction)

### **Recurrent AOM:**

**Definition:** ≥3 episodes in 6 months OR ≥4 episodes in 12 months

**Management:**
- **Consider tympanoplasty tubes** (if frequent infections, persistent MEE, hearing loss)
- **Antibiotic prophylaxis NOT routinely recommended** (↑resistance)

## Key Points

### **High-Dose Amoxicillin = First-Line:**
- **80-90 mg/kg/day divided BID × 5-10 days**
- **Covers drug-resistant *S. pneumoniae***

### **Observation if ≥2y + Non-Severe:**
- **Watchful waiting × 48-72h**
- **Start antibiotics if no improvement**

### **Always Treat if <6 Months:**
- **Age <6 months** → immediate antibiotics

### **Duration:**
- **10 days:** Age <2y or severe
- **5-7 days:** Age ≥2y, non-severe

### **Amoxicillin-Clavulanate if Recent Amoxicillin:**
- **Within 30 days** OR **concurrent purulent conjunctivitis**

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **High-dose amoxicillin 80-90 mg/kg/day BID** × 5-10 days (first-line)
- **Observation:** Age ≥2y + non-severe (mild pain, fever <39°C, certain follow-up)
- **Always treat:** Age <6 months, bilateral, severe, otorrhea
- **Duration:** 10 days (<2y or severe), 5-7 days (≥2y, non-severe)
- **Treatment failure:** Amoxicillin-clavulanate OR ceftriaxone IM × 3 days
- **Complications rare:** Mastoiditis, meningitis (urgent imaging, IV antibiotics)
