## Chronic Lymphadenitis: Differential Diagnosis

**Q:** What are the major causes of chronic cervical lymphadenitis, and how are nontuberculous mycobacteria (NTM) and cat scratch disease (CSD) differentiated?

**A:**

**Differential Diagnosis of Chronic Lymphadenitis:**

**Infectious:**
- **Nontuberculous mycobacteria (NTM)**: *M. avium* complex, *M. scrofulaceum*
- ***Bartonella henselae*** (cat scratch disease)
- **Tuberculosis**
- Fungi (*Histoplasma, Coccidioides*)
- Toxoplasmosis, tularemia

**Non-infectious:**
- Lymphoma, sarcoidosis

**Clinical Comparison:**

| Feature | NTM Lymphadenitis | Cat Scratch Disease (CSD) |
|---------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| **Age** | **Children <5 years** | Children/young adults |
| **Location** | **Unilateral cervical/submandibular** | Regional to inoculation site (axillary, epitrochlear common) |
| **Skin involvement** | **Violaceous overlying skin**, may ulcerate/"scrofuloderma" | Usually no overlying skin changes |
| **Systemic symptoms** | **Absent** (afebrile, well-appearing) | Low-grade fever, malaise |
| **History** | Soil/water exposure | **Cat scratch/bite** (kitten exposure) |
| **Diagnosis** | AFB culture (slow-growing), PCR | **Bartonella serology IgG >1:256**, PCR |
| **Treatment** | **Surgical excision** (preferred) ± clarithromycin + rifabutin × 12 weeks | **Azithromycin 500 mg × 1, then 250 mg daily × 4 days** (self-limited in immunocompetent) |

**Key Pearls:**
- NTM lymphadenitis: **Excision > antibiotics** (70-95% cure with surgery alone)
- CSD: Often **self-limited** (4-6 weeks); antibiotics shorten course but not required in immunocompetent
- TB must be ruled out in endemic areas (AFB smear, culture, IGRA/PPD)
- Histology: Both show **granulomatous inflammation** (difficult to distinguish)
