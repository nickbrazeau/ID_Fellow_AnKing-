## Question

How is septic arthritis managed? Use **"Urgent Arthrocentesis (WBC >50,000) → Drainage + Pathogen-Directed Antibiotics × 3-4 Weeks"** framework.

## Answer

### **Clinical Presentation:**

**Classic:**
- **Acute onset** (hours to days)
- **Joint pain, swelling, warmth, erythema**
- **Severely restricted range of motion**
- **Fever** (60%)
- **Usually monoarticular** (90%)

**Most Common Joints:**
- **Knee** (50%)
- **Hip** (20%)
- **Ankle, wrist, shoulder** (30% combined)

### **Diagnosis:**

**Synovial Fluid Analysis (ESSENTIAL):**

**Arthrocentesis (URGENT):**
- **Do NOT delay** for imaging (unless concern for prosthetic joint)
- **Send for:**
  - **Cell count with differential**
  - **Gram stain, aerobic/anaerobic culture**
  - **Crystal analysis** (r/o gout, pseudogout)

**Synovial Fluid Findings:**

| **Parameter** | **Normal** | **Non-Inflammatory** | **Inflammatory** | **Septic** |
|---------------|------------|----------------------|------------------|------------|
| **WBC** | <200 | 200-2,000 | 2,000-50,000 | **>50,000** (usually >100,000) |
| **PMNs** | <25% | <25% | 50-75% | **>90%** |
| **Gram stain** | Negative | Negative | Negative | **Positive 50-75%** |
| **Culture** | Negative | Negative | Negative | **Positive 70-90%** |
| **Glucose** | Near serum | Near serum | ↓ | **Very low (<40 mg/dL)** |

**Key:** **WBC >50,000 + PMNs >90% = septic arthritis until proven otherwise**

**Blood Cultures:**
- **Positive in 25-50%** (obtain before antibiotics)

**Imaging:**
- **X-ray:** Normal early, joint space widening (effusion), later erosions
- **Ultrasound:** Detects effusion (guides aspiration)
- **MRI:** If concern for osteomyelitis, abscess, hip involvement

### **Microbiology:**

**Non-Gonococcal (Most Common):**
- ***Staphylococcus aureus*** (40-60%, most common overall)
- **Streptococci** (20-30%): *S. pyogenes*, *S. pneumoniae*, *S. agalactiae*
- **Gram-negatives** (10-20%): *E. coli*, *Pseudomonas* (IVDU, elderly, immunocompromised)

**Gonococcal:**
- ***Neisseria gonorrhoeae*** (most common in sexually active young adults)
- **Disseminated gonococcal infection (DGI):**
  - **Triad:** Migratory polyarthritis/tenosynovitis, dermatitis (pustular rash), purulent arthritis
  - **Culture often negative** from joint (positive from cervix/urethra/pharynx/rectum)

**Special Populations:**
- **IVDU:** *S. aureus*, *Pseudomonas*, *Candida*, sternoclavicular/SI joint involvement
- **Immunocompromised:** Atypical organisms (*Mycobacteria*, *Nocardia*, fungi)

### **Treatment:**

**1. Joint Drainage (URGENT):**

**Methods:**
- **Serial arthrocentesis** (knee, shoulder, ankle) - daily drainage until fluid clear, WBC <20,000
- **Arthroscopic/open drainage:** Hip (surgical emergency), shoulder (if loculated), failed arthrocentesis

**2. Empiric Antibiotics (Start After Arthrocentesis):**

**Non-Gonococcal (Most Common):**
- **Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h** (covers MRSA)
- **If Gram-negative rods on Gram stain OR IVDU:** Add **ceftriaxone 2g IV daily** OR **cefepime 2g IV q8h**

**Gonococcal (Suspected DGI):**
- **Ceftriaxone 1-2g IV daily**
- **+ Azithromycin 1g PO × 1** (cover empiric *Chlamydia*)

**3. Pathogen-Directed Therapy:**

***S. aureus* (MSSA):**
- **Nafcillin 2g IV q4h** OR **cefazolin 2g IV q8h × 3-4 weeks**
- **Oral step-down** (after 1-2 weeks IV, if improving): Cephalexin 500mg QID, dicloxacillin 500mg QID

***S. aureus* (MRSA):**
- **Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h × 3-4 weeks**
- **Alternatives:** Daptomycin 6-8 mg/kg IV daily, linezolid 600mg PO/IV BID

**Streptococci:**
- **Penicillin G 4 million units IV q4h** OR **ceftriaxone 2g IV daily × 2-4 weeks**

**Gram-Negatives:**
- **Ceftriaxone 2g IV daily** OR **cefepime 2g IV q8h × 3-4 weeks**
- **Ciprofloxacin 750mg PO BID** (oral option if susceptible)

***N. gonorrhoeae*:**
- **Ceftriaxone 1-2g IV/IM daily × 7-14 days** (at least 24-48h after improvement, then switch to oral)
- **Oral step-down:** Cefixime 400mg PO BID OR ciprofloxacin 500mg PO BID (if susceptible) × total 7-14 days

**Duration:**
- **Non-gonococcal:** 3-4 weeks (IV 2-3 weeks, then oral step-down if improving)
- **Gonococcal:** 7-14 days (can switch to oral after 24-48h improvement)
- **Gram-negatives, S. aureus:** 3-4 weeks

### **Monitoring Response:**

**Clinical Improvement:**
- **Afebrile within 3-5 days**
- **Improved pain, range of motion** within 1 week
- **↓ESR, CRP** (may lag clinical improvement)

**Repeat Arthrocentesis:**
- **If no improvement** in 48-72h (reassess drainage, organism, antibiotic penetration)

### **Complications:**

**Acute:**
- **Joint destruction** (cartilage loss, subchondral bone erosion)
- **Osteomyelitis** (5-10%, adjacent bone involvement)

**Chronic:**
- **Degenerative arthritis** (30-50%, especially if delayed treatment)
- **Ankylosis, joint deformity**

### **Prognosis:**

**Mortality:** 5-15%

**Functional Outcome:**
- **Good outcome** (full recovery): 50-70%
- **Poor outcome** (residual pain, reduced ROM): 30-50%

**Risk Factors for Poor Outcome:**
- **Delayed diagnosis/treatment** (>7 days)
- **Hip or shoulder involvement**
- ***S. aureus*** infection
- **Age >60, comorbidities**

### **Special Situations:**

**Gonococcal Arthritis:**
- **Most common** in sexually active young adults
- **Often culture-negative** from joint (obtain cervical/urethral/pharyngeal/rectal cultures)
- **Shorter treatment** (7-14 days)

**IVDU:**
- **Unusual joints:** Sternoclavicular, sacroiliac, pubic symphysis
- **Atypical organisms:** *Pseudomonas*, *Candida*, polymicrobial

**Prosthetic Joint Infection:**
- **See separate card** (different management)

## Key Points

### **Urgent Arthrocentesis = Diagnostic:**
- **WBC >50,000 + PMNs >90%** = septic arthritis
- **Do NOT delay for imaging**

### **Drainage + Antibiotics:**
- **Serial arthrocentesis** OR **surgical drainage** (hip = surgical emergency)
- **Daily drainage** until fluid clear, WBC <20,000

### **Empiric = Vancomycin (Cover MRSA):**
- **Add ceftriaxone/cefepime** if Gram-negative rods or IVDU

### ***S. aureus* Most Common (40-60%):**
- **MSSA:** Nafcillin/cefazolin × 3-4 weeks
- **MRSA:** Vancomycin × 3-4 weeks

### **Gonococcal = Shorter Duration:**
- **Ceftriaxone × 7-14 days** (can switch to oral after 24-48h)
- **Culture often negative** from joint (test cervix/urethra/pharynx/rectum)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Urgent arthrocentesis** (WBC >50,000 + PMNs >90% = septic)
- **Drainage + antibiotics** (daily arthrocentesis until WBC <20,000)
- **Empiric:** Vancomycin (add ceftriaxone if Gram-negative/IVDU)
- ***S. aureus* most common** (nafcillin/vancomycin × 3-4 weeks)
- **Gonococcal:** Ceftriaxone × 7-14 days (shorter course)
- **Hip = surgical emergency** (arthroscopic/open drainage)
