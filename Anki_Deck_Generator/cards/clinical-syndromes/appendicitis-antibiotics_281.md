## Appendicitis: Microbiology and Antibiotic Management

**Q:** What is the microbiology of complicated appendicitis, and how do antibiotic recommendations differ for uncomplicated vs. perforated appendicitis?

**A:**

**Microbiology (perforated/complicated):**
- **Anaerobes**: *Bacteroides fragilis* (52%)
- **Gram-negatives**: *E. coli* (50%), other Enterobacterales
- **Polymicrobial** (mixed aerobic + anaerobic)

**Antibiotic Management:**

| Type | Definition | Antibiotic Approach | Duration |
|------|-----------|---------------------|----------|
| **Uncomplicated** | No perforation, gangrene, or abscess | **Single preoperative dose** → **no postoperative antibiotics** | Prophylaxis only |
| **Complicated/Perforated** | Perforation, gangrene, abscess | **Postoperative antibiotics** | **3-5 days** (or until afebrile + ↓ WBC + tolerating diet) |

**Antibiotic Regimens (complicated):**

**Preferred:**
- **Cefoxitin 2 g IV q6h** OR **cefotetan 2 g IV q12h** (single agent with anaerobic coverage)
- **Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q24h + metronidazole 500 mg IV q8h**
- **Piperacillin-tazobactam 3.375 g IV q6h**

**Avoid:**
- **Broad-spectrum agents** (meropenem, pip-tazo) as first-line → **↑ postoperative abscess rates**

**Non-Operative Management:**
- Antibiotics alone for uncomplicated appendicitis (controversial but increasingly studied)

**Pearl:** Shorter antibiotic courses (3-5 days) are **non-inferior** to traditional longer courses for perforated appendicitis.
