## Question

How is acute pharyngitis managed? Use **"Centor ≥3 → Test/Treat GAS (PCN VK × 10d), Avoid Antibiotics if Viral"** framework.

## Answer

### **Etiology:**

**Viral (80-90%):**
- **Rhinovirus, coronavirus, adenovirus** (common cold)
- **EBV** (mononucleosis) - 5-10% of adults
- **Influenza, parainfluenza**
- **HSV-1** (primary gingivostomatitis)

**Bacterial (5-15%):**
- ***Streptococcus pyogenes*** (Group A Strep, GAS) - **most important** (need treatment to prevent complications)
- ***Streptococcus dysgalactiae* (Group C/G Strep)**
- ***Fusobacterium necrophorum*** (Lemierre syndrome)
- ***Neisseria gonorrhoeae*** (pharyngeal gonorrhea)

### **Clinical Features:**

**Viral Pharyngitis:**
- **Gradual onset**
- **Cough, rhinorrhea, conjunctivitis** (viral URI symptoms)
- **Hoarseness**
- **Mild fever**

**Group A Strep (GAS) Pharyngitis:**
- **Sudden onset**
- **Severe sore throat**
- **Tonsillar exudates, palatal petechiae**
- **Tender anterior cervical lymphadenopathy**
- **Fever** (>38°C)
- **NO cough** (absence suggests bacterial)

### **Centor Criteria (Predict GAS):**

**1 point each:**
1. **Tonsillar exudates**
2. **Tender anterior cervical lymphadenopathy**
3. **Fever** (>38°C)
4. **Absence of cough**

**Modified Centor (McIsaac):** Add age
- **Age 3-14:** +1 point
- **Age 15-44:** 0 points
- **Age ≥45:** -1 point

**Score Interpretation:**
- **0-1:** 1-5% GAS → No test/treat
- **2:** 10-15% GAS → Rapid antigen detection test (RADT) OR culture
- **3-4:** 30-50% GAS → Test and treat (or empiric treatment)

### **Diagnostic Testing:**

**Rapid Antigen Detection Test (RADT):**
- **High specificity** (95-99%)
- **Lower sensitivity** (70-90%)
- **If negative in children/adolescents:** Confirm with throat culture (NOT needed in adults)

**Throat Culture (Gold Standard):**
- **Sensitivity 90-95%**
- **Takes 24-48h**

**Do NOT Test:**
- **Centor 0-1** (very low probability GAS)
- **Clear viral symptoms** (cough, rhinorrhea, conjunctivitis)

### **Treatment:**

**Group A Strep (GAS) Pharyngitis:**

**First-Line:**
- **Penicillin VK 500mg PO BID-TID × 10 days** (children: 250mg PO BID-TID)
- **Amoxicillin 500mg PO BID × 10 days** (better taste, once-daily option)
- **Benzathine penicillin G 1.2 million units IM × 1** (if adherence concern, single dose)

**PCN Allergy:**
- **Cephalexin 500mg PO BID × 10 days** (if NOT severe/IgE-mediated allergy)
- **Azithromycin 500mg PO daily × 5 days** (if severe PCN allergy, but 5-10% GAS resistance)
- **Clindamycin 300mg PO TID × 10 days**

**Duration:** **10 days** (MUST complete full course to prevent rheumatic fever)

**Benefits of Treatment:**
- **Prevents rheumatic fever** (if treated within 9 days of symptom onset)
- **Reduces suppurative complications** (peritonsillar abscess, cervical lymphadenitis)
- **Shortens symptom duration** (1-2 days)
- **Reduces transmission**

**Symptomatic Treatment (All Pharyngitis):**
- **NSAIDs, acetaminophen** (pain, fever)
- **Throat lozenges, salt water gargles**
- **Fluids, rest**

### **Special Situations:**

**Mononucleosis (EBV):**
- **Triad:** Pharyngitis, fever, lymphadenopathy (posterior cervical)
- **Splenomegaly** (50%), hepatomegaly
- **Avoid contact sports** (risk of splenic rupture × 3 weeks)
- **Do NOT give amoxicillin/ampicillin** (causes maculopapular rash in 90%)
- **Supportive care** (self-limited, 2-4 weeks)

**Pharyngeal Gonorrhea:**
- **Often asymptomatic** (90%)
- **If symptomatic:** Mild pharyngitis
- **Test if:** MSM, high-risk sexual behavior
- **Treatment:** Ceftriaxone 500mg IM × 1

**Lemierre Syndrome (*Fusobacterium necrophorum*):**
- **Thrombophlebitis of internal jugular vein** + septic emboli (lungs)
- **Young adults** (15-25 years)
- **Severe sore throat → sepsis** (within 7 days)
- **Treatment:** IV antibiotics (metronidazole + ceftriaxone OR carbapenem) × 3-6 weeks
- **High mortality** if untreated

**Peritonsillar Abscess:**
- **"Hot potato" voice, trismus, uvular deviation**
- **Needle aspiration or I&D** + antibiotics (amoxicillin-clavulanate)

### **Complications (Untreated GAS):**

**Suppurative:**
- **Peritonsillar abscess, retropharyngeal abscess**
- **Cervical lymphadenitis**
- **Otitis media, sinusitis**

**Non-Suppurative (Immune-Mediated):**
- **Acute rheumatic fever** (2-3 weeks post-infection)
  - **Prevented by antibiotics** (if given within 9 days)
- **Post-streptococcal glomerulonephritis** (1-3 weeks post-infection)
  - **NOT prevented by antibiotics**

## Key Points

### **Centor ≥3 → Test and Treat:**
- **High probability GAS** (30-50%)
- **Test with RADT** or empiric treatment

### **Penicillin VK × 10 Days:**
- **First-line** for GAS
- **MUST complete 10 days** (prevent rheumatic fever)

### **Do NOT Treat if Viral:**
- **Cough, rhinorrhea, conjunctivitis** = viral
- **Antibiotics NOT helpful** (avoid unnecessary use)

### **Benefits of Treating GAS:**
- **Prevents rheumatic fever** (if within 9 days)
- **Reduces suppurative complications**
- **Shortens symptoms** by 1-2 days

### **Mononucleosis:**
- **Do NOT give amoxicillin/ampicillin** (causes rash)
- **Supportive care**, avoid contact sports × 3 weeks

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Centor ≥3:** Test/treat GAS (penicillin VK 500mg BID × **10 days**)
- **Viral symptoms** (cough, rhinorrhea) → do NOT test/treat
- **Benefits:** Prevents rheumatic fever (if treated within 9 days)
- **Mononucleosis:** Avoid amoxicillin (rash), avoid contact sports × 3 weeks
- **Lemierre syndrome:** Severe pharyngitis → sepsis (internal jugular thrombophlebitis)
