## Question

How are CRBSIs managed? Use **"Remove Line + Antibiotics (*S. aureus* 14d, CoNS 7d, *Candida* Remove + Antifungal)"** framework.

## Answer

### **Definitions:**

**CRBSI (Catheter-Related Bloodstream Infection):**
- **Bacteremia/fungemia** originating from central venous catheter (CVC)
- **Diagnosis:** Blood culture from catheter + peripheral **simultaneously positive** (quantitative: catheter >3× CFU/mL than peripheral OR differential time-to-positivity ≥2h earlier from catheter)

**CLABSI (Central Line-Associated BSI - Surveillance Definition):**
- **Blood culture-positive** while CVC in place or within 48h of removal
- **Used for infection control metrics** (does NOT prove catheter source)

### **Microbiology:**

**Common Organisms:**
- **Coagulase-negative staphylococci (CoNS):** *S. epidermidis* (most common)
- ***S. aureus*** (MSSA/MRSA)
- **Enterococci** (*E. faecalis*, *E. faecium*)
- **Gram-negative rods:** *E. coli*, *Klebsiella*, *Pseudomonas*
- ***Candida* species** (especially with TPN, immunosuppression, broad-spectrum antibiotics)

### **Management:**

**General Principles:**

| Organism | Remove Catheter? | Duration (If Line Removed) |
|----------|------------------|----------------------------|
| ***S. aureus*** | **YES** | **14 days** (uncomplicated), 4-6 weeks (complicated) |
| **CoNS** | Attempt salvage if short-term OR remove | 7 days (if removed), 10-14 days (if retained + ALT) |
| **Enterococci** | Remove (unless salvageable) | 7-14 days |
| **Gram-negatives** | Remove | 7-14 days |
| ***Candida*** | **YES (always)** | **14 days after last positive culture** + ophthalmology exam |
| ***C. auris*** | **YES (always)** | ≥14 days |

**Indications to Remove Catheter:**

**Mandatory Removal:**
- ***S. aureus* bacteremia** (high rate of complications - endocarditis, osteomyelitis, epidural abscess)
- ***Candida* fungemia** (high mortality if line retained)
- **Tunnel/port pocket infection**
- **Septic thrombosis**
- **Septic shock, hemodynamic instability**
- **Persistent bacteremia >72h** despite antibiotics

**Consider Retention (With Antibiotic Lock Therapy):**
- **CoNS** (if short-term catheter, low virulence)
- **Tunneled catheter** (e.g., Hickman, port) in hemodynamically stable patient with long-term need

### **Antibiotic Management:**

**Empiric (While Awaiting Cultures):**
- **Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h** (MRSA, CoNS) + **cefepime 2g IV q8h** OR **piperacillin-tazobactam 3.375-4.5g IV q6-8h** (Gram-negatives)

**Organism-Specific:**

***S. aureus* (MSSA):**
- **Nafcillin 2g IV q4h** OR **cefazolin 2g IV q8h** × **14 days** (uncomplicated)
- **Complicated:** 4-6 weeks (endocarditis, osteomyelitis, epidural abscess)
- **TEE recommended** (rule out endocarditis)
- **Repeat blood cultures** 2-4 days after starting antibiotics (ensure clearance)

***S. aureus* (MRSA):**
- **Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h** OR **daptomycin 6-10 mg/kg IV daily** × 14 days (uncomplicated)

**CoNS:**
- **Vancomycin × 7 days** (if line removed)
- **Vancomycin × 10-14 days + antibiotic lock therapy** (if line retained)

***Candida*:**
- **Echinocandin** (caspofungin 70mg × 1, then 50mg daily OR micafungin 100mg daily) × **14 days after last positive culture**
- **Fluconazole 800mg × 1, then 400mg daily** (alternative if fluconazole-susceptible, hemodynamically stable)
- **Ophthalmology exam** (rule out endophthalmitis)
- **Repeat blood cultures** daily until negative

**Gram-Negatives:**
- **Cefepime, piperacillin-tazobactam, carbapenem** (based on susceptibilities) × 7-14 days

### **Antibiotic Lock Therapy (ALT):**

**Indications:**
- **Attempt to salvage** long-term catheter (tunneled line, port)
- **CoNS, enterococci** (NOT *S. aureus*, *Candida*, Gram-negatives)
- **Hemodynamically stable**

**Regimen:**
- **Vancomycin 2-5 mg/mL** + **heparin 100 units/mL** (dwell in catheter lumen × ≥12h daily × 10-14 days)
- **Systemic antibiotics** + antibiotic lock

**Success Rate:** 60-80% (lower for *S. aureus*, Gram-negatives)

### **Complications of *S. aureus* Bacteremia:**

**Must Evaluate For:**
- **Endocarditis:** TEE recommended (high sensitivity for vegetations)
- **Osteomyelitis/Vertebral discitis:** MRI if back pain, persistent fever/bacteremia
- **Epidural abscess:** MRI if neurologic symptoms, back pain
- **Septic arthritis:** Arthrocentesis if joint symptoms
- **Metastatic abscesses:** Lung, kidney, spleen (CT if persistent fever)

### **Prevention:**

**Bundle Approach:**
- **Hand hygiene**
- **Maximal sterile barrier** during insertion
- **Chlorhexidine skin prep** (>0.5%)
- **Optimal catheter site** (subclavian > internal jugular > femoral)
- **Daily assessment** of line necessity (remove if no longer needed)
- **Antimicrobial-impregnated catheters** (chlorhexidine/silver sulfadiazine) in high-risk settings

## Key Points

### ***S. aureus* = Always Remove + 14 Days:**
- **High complication rate** (endocarditis, osteomyelitis, epidural abscess)
- **TEE recommended** (rule out endocarditis)
- **Repeat cultures** 2-4 days post-antibiotics (ensure clearance)

### ***Candida* = Always Remove + 14 Days:**
- **High mortality if line retained**
- **Ophthalmology exam** (rule out endophthalmitis)
- **Repeat cultures** daily until negative

### **CoNS = Consider Salvage:**
- **Low virulence** (can attempt antibiotic lock therapy + systemic antibiotics)
- **Remove if:** Tunneled infection, septic thrombosis, persistent bacteremia >72h

### **Antibiotic Lock Therapy:**
- **Vancomycin 2-5 mg/mL** + heparin (dwell × ≥12h daily)
- **For CoNS, enterococci** in stable patient with long-term catheter need
- **NOT for *S. aureus*, *Candida*, Gram-negatives**

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- ***S. aureus*:** Remove line + nafcillin/cefazolin (MSSA) or vancomycin/daptomycin (MRSA) × 14 days, TEE
- ***Candida*:** Remove line + echinocandin × 14 days after last positive culture, ophthalmology exam
- **CoNS:** Remove (preferred) OR salvage with ALT (vancomycin lock + systemic) × 10-14 days
- **Persistent bacteremia >72h** → remove catheter
- **Prevention:** Chlorhexidine prep, hand hygiene, remove lines when no longer needed
