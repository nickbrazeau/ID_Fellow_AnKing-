## Question

How is infectious diarrhea managed? Use **"Supportive (Fluids), Antibiotics Only if Severe/Bloody/Immunocompromised, Avoid if STEC"** framework.

## Answer

### **Classification:**

**Non-Inflammatory (Watery):**
- **No fecal WBCs, no blood**
- **Pathogens:** *E. coli* (ETEC, EPEC), viruses (norovirus, rotavirus), *Giardia*, *Cryptosporidium*
- **Mechanism:** Enterotoxin-mediated

**Inflammatory (Bloody/Dysentery):**
- **Fecal WBCs, blood in stool**
- **Pathogens:** *Shigella*, *Salmonella*, *Campylobacter*, STEC (O157:H7), *C. difficile*, *Entamoeba histolytica*
- **Mechanism:** Mucosal invasion/cytotoxin

### **Common Pathogens:**

**Bacterial:**
- ***Campylobacter jejuni*** (most common bacterial cause in US)
- ***Salmonella* (non-typhoidal)**
- ***Shigella***
- **STEC (Shiga toxin-producing *E. coli*, O157:H7)**
- ***C. difficile***
- ***E. coli*** (ETEC = traveler's diarrhea)

**Viral:**
- **Norovirus** (most common overall, outbreaks)
- **Rotavirus** (children, vaccine preventable)

**Parasitic:**
- ***Giardia lamblia***
- ***Cryptosporidium***
- ***Entamoeba histolytica***

### **Indications for Stool Testing:**

**Test If:**
- **Severe illness** (fever, bloody diarrhea, dehydration)
- **Immunocompromised**
- **Healthcare-associated** (≥3 days hospitalization)
- **Outbreak setting**
- **Persistent diarrhea** (>7 days)

**Do NOT Test:**
- **Mild, self-limited diarrhea** (<3 days)
- **Asymptomatic patients**

### **Diagnostic Tests:**

**Stool Culture:**
- **Standard:** Detects *Salmonella*, *Shigella*, *Campylobacter*
- **STEC:** Request specific testing (O157:H7, Shiga toxin)

**Molecular Multiplex PCR Panels:**
- **Detects 20+ pathogens** (bacterial, viral, parasitic)
- **High sensitivity** (may detect colonization, not always pathogenic)

***C. difficile* Testing:**
- **Toxin EIA + GDH** OR **PCR** (if ≥3 unformed stools/24h + risk factors)

**Fecal Leukocytes/Lactoferrin:**
- **Suggests inflammatory diarrhea** (invasive pathogens)

### **Treatment:**

**Supportive Care (All Patients):**
- **Oral rehydration solution (ORS):** Preferred (WHO formula, Pedialyte)
- **IV fluids** if severe dehydration, unable to tolerate PO

**Antimotility Agents:**
- **Loperamide** (Imodium): Use ONLY if non-bloody, afebrile
- **Avoid if:** Bloody diarrhea, fever, suspected *C. difficile*/STEC (risk of toxic megacolon, HUS)

**Antibiotics (Selective Use):**

**Indications for Empiric Antibiotics:**
- **Severe illness:** High fever (>38.5°C), bloody diarrhea, >6 stools/day, signs of sepsis
- **Immunocompromised** (HIV CD4 <200, transplant, chemotherapy)
- **Traveler's diarrhea** (moderate-severe)
- **Suspected ***Shigella*** (dysentery, bloody diarrhea)

**Empiric Regimens:**
- **Ciprofloxacin 500mg PO BID × 3-5 days** OR **levofloxacin 500mg PO daily × 3 days**
- **Azithromycin 500mg PO daily × 3 days** (if fluoroquinolone resistance, pregnancy, children, or *Campylobacter* suspected)

**Pathogen-Specific Treatment:**

***Campylobacter jejuni*:**
- **Azithromycin 500mg PO daily × 3 days** (fluoroquinolone resistance common)
- **Treat if:** Severe illness, immunocompromised, diagnosed within 3-4 days of symptom onset

***Salmonella* (Non-Typhoidal):**
- **DO NOT treat if mild** (prolongs carriage)
- **Treat if:** Severe, immunocompromised, age <3 months or >50 years, bacteremia, extraintestinal infection
- **Regimen:** Ciprofloxacin 500mg PO BID × 5-7 days OR azithromycin

***Shigella*:**
- **ALWAYS treat** (shortens duration, reduces transmission)
- **Azithromycin 500mg PO daily × 3 days** (first-line, increasing fluoroquinolone resistance)
- **Ciprofloxacin 500mg PO BID × 3 days** (if susceptible)

**STEC (Shiga Toxin-Producing *E. coli*, O157:H7):**
- **DO NOT give antibiotics** (increases risk of hemolytic uremic syndrome [HUS])
- **Supportive care only**
- **Monitor for HUS** (triad: hemolytic anemia, thrombocytopenia, acute kidney injury)

***C. difficile*:**
- **See separate card**
- **Vancomycin 125mg PO QID × 10 days** OR **fidaxomicin 200mg PO BID × 10 days**

***Giardia lamblia*:**
- **Metronidazole 250mg PO TID × 5-7 days** OR **tinidazole 2g PO × 1**

***Cryptosporidium*:**
- **Nitazoxanide 500mg PO BID × 3 days** (immunocompetent)
- **HIV:** Optimize ART (immune reconstitution)

***Entamoeba histolytica*:**
- **Metronidazole 750mg PO TID × 7-10 days** + **paromomycin 25-35 mg/kg/day divided TID × 7 days** (luminal agent)

**Traveler's Diarrhea:**
- **Azithromycin 500mg PO daily × 3 days** (first-line, covers fluoroquinolone-resistant *Campylobacter*)
- **Rifaximin 200mg PO TID × 3 days** (if non-invasive, afebrile)

### **Complications:**

**Hemolytic Uremic Syndrome (HUS):**
- **STEC (O157:H7)** → Shiga toxin → HUS (5-10% of cases)
- **Triad:** Hemolytic anemia, thrombocytopenia, AKI
- **Do NOT give antibiotics** (increases risk)

**Reactive Arthritis:**
- ***Campylobacter*, *Salmonella*, *Shigella*, *Yersinia***
- **HLA-B27 associated**

**Guillain-Barré Syndrome:**
- ***Campylobacter jejuni*** (preceding infection in 30-40% of GBS cases)

**Post-Infectious IBS:**
- **10-15%** develop IBS symptoms after acute gastroenteritis

## Key Points

### **Supportive Care = Mainstay:**
- **ORS/IV fluids** (primary treatment)
- **Avoid loperamide** if bloody diarrhea, fever, *C. difficile*/STEC

### **Antibiotics Only if Severe/Immunocompromised:**
- **Indications:** High fever, bloody diarrhea, immunocompromised, traveler's diarrhea (moderate-severe)
- **Empiric:** Azithromycin 500mg × 3 days (first-line, covers *Campylobacter*)

### **DO NOT Treat STEC with Antibiotics:**
- **Increases HUS risk** (hemolytic anemia, thrombocytopenia, AKI)
- **Supportive care only**

### **Always Treat *Shigella*:**
- **Azithromycin 500mg × 3 days** (shortens duration, reduces transmission)

### **DO NOT Treat Mild *Salmonella*:**
- **Prolongs carriage**
- **Treat only if:** Severe, immunocompromised, age extremes, bacteremia

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Supportive care (ORS/fluids)** = primary treatment
- **Antibiotics:** Only if severe, bloody, immunocompromised
- **STEC:** DO NOT treat with antibiotics (↑HUS risk)
- ***Shigella*:** ALWAYS treat (azithromycin × 3 days)
- ***Campylobacter*:** Azithromycin (fluoroquinolone resistance)
- **Traveler's diarrhea:** Azithromycin 500mg × 3 days
