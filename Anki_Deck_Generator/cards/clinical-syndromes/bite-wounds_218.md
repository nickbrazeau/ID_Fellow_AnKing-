## Question

How are bite wounds managed? Use **"Clean + Irrigate, Amoxicillin-Clavulanate × 5d, Rabies/Tetanus Prophylaxis"** framework.

## Answer

### **Initial Management (ALL Bites):**

**1. Wound Care:**
- **Copious irrigation** (high-pressure saline, 15-20 minutes)
- **Debride devitalized tissue**
- **Do NOT close** (unless cosmetically necessary - face, low-risk)
- **Leave open** (high infection risk - hand, deep puncture, >12h old)

**2. Assess Depth, Location:**
- **High-risk wounds:** Deep, puncture, hand, foot, face, near joint/bone, immunocompromised
- **Low-risk:** Superficial, extremity (not hand/foot), <12h old

**3. Tetanus Prophylaxis:**
- **Update if >5 years** (or >10 years if clean minor wound)
- **Tdap preferred** (if not previously received)

**4. Rabies Assessment:**
- **See below**

### **Microbiology:**

**Animal Bites (Dog, Cat):**
- ***Pasteurella multocida*** (most common, 50-75% of cat/dog bites)
  - **Early infection** (<24h)
  - **Rapidly progressive cellulitis**
- ***Capnocytophaga canimorsus*** (dog bites, asplenia → sepsis, DIC)
- ***S. aureus*, Streptococci**
- **Anaerobes** (*Fusobacterium*, *Bacteroides*)

**Human Bites:**
- **Streptococci** (viridans group, *S. pyogenes*)
- ***Staphylococcus aureus***
- ***Eikenella corrodens*** (30-50%, human oral flora)
- **Anaerobes** (*Prevotella*, *Fusobacterium*, *Peptostreptococcus*)

**Rodent Bites:**
- ***Streptobacillus moniliformis*** (rat-bite fever)
- ***Spirillum minus*** (Asia)

### **Antibiotic Prophylaxis:**

**Indications for Prophylaxis:**
- **Cat bites** (50-80% infection rate, deep puncture)
- **Human bites**
- **Hand, foot, face, genital bites**
- **Deep punctures** (especially near bone, joint)
- **Moderate-severe wounds** (crushing injury, devascularized)
- **Immunocompromised** (diabetes, asplenia, cirrhosis, advanced age)
- **Delayed presentation** (>12h)

**Prophylactic Regimen (3-5 Days):**
- **Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875mg PO BID × 3-5 days** (first-line)
- **If PCN allergy:**
  - **Doxycycline 100mg PO BID + metronidazole 500mg PO TID**
  - OR **moxifloxacin 400mg PO daily**

**Treatment of Established Infection (5-10 Days):**

**Outpatient (Mild-Moderate):**
- **Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875mg PO BID × 5-10 days**
- **If PCN allergy:** Doxycycline + metronidazole OR moxifloxacin

**Inpatient (Severe, Hand Infection):**
- **Ampicillin-sulbactam 3g IV q6h**
- **Piperacillin-tazobactam 3.375g IV q6h**
- **If PCN allergy:** Moxifloxacin 400mg IV daily OR ciprofloxacin + metronidazole + vancomycin

**Duration:** 5-10 days (longer if osteomyelitis, septic arthritis)

### **Rabies Prophylaxis:**

**Assess Risk:**

**HIGH RISK (Prophylaxis Recommended):**
- **Bats** (ANY contact, even if no bite - saliva can transmit)
- **Unvaccinated dog/cat** (or unknown vaccination status, especially if stray/wild)
- **Wild carnivores** (raccoon, skunk, fox, coyote)

**LOW RISK (Observation/No Prophylaxis):**
- **Vaccinated dog/cat** (documented, up-to-date)
- **Rodents** (squirrels, hamsters, rats) - **very rare** rabies transmission
- **Lagomorphs** (rabbits) - extremely rare

**Rabies Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP):**

**If Previously Vaccinated:**
- **Vaccine only:** Days 0, 3 (2 doses)

**If Never Vaccinated:**
- **Rabies immune globulin (RIG):** 20 IU/kg (infiltrate wound + remainder IM distant site) × 1 (day 0)
- **+ Rabies vaccine (HDCV, PCEC):** Days 0, 3, 7, 14 (4 doses)

**IMPORTANT:** Rabies is **almost always fatal** once symptomatic → low threshold for prophylaxis

### **Special Situations:**

**Cat Bites:**
- **50-80% infection rate** (deep puncture wounds)
- **ALWAYS give prophylaxis** (amoxicillin-clavulanate)
- ***Pasteurella* rapid onset** (<24h)

**Human Bites (Clenched Fist Injury):**
- **"Fight bite"** - fist striking teeth
- **HIGH infection risk** (bone, joint involvement)
- **X-ray** (r/o fracture, foreign body)
- **Surgical consultation** (often needs washout, debridement)
- **Admit for IV antibiotics** (ampicillin-sulbactam)

**Dog Bites:**
- **Lower infection rate** (5-20%)
- **Prophylaxis** if high-risk wound (hand, deep, immunocompromised)
- ***Capnocytophaga*** sepsis (asplenia)

**Hand Infections:**
- **HIGH risk** for osteomyelitis, septic arthritis, tenosynovitis
- **Hospitalize** for IV antibiotics + surgical consultation
- **X-ray** (baseline, r/o fracture, foreign body)
- **Repeat X-ray** if no improvement (osteomyelitis)

***Capnocytophaga* Sepsis (Asplenia):**
- **Dog bite + asplenia** → fulminant sepsis, DIC, purpura fulminans
- **High mortality** (25-30%)
- **Early aggressive treatment** (ampicillin-sulbactam or carbapenem)

**Rat-Bite Fever (*Streptobacillus moniliformis*):**
- **Fever, rash (petechial), polyarthritis** (7-10 days post-bite)
- **Treatment:** Penicillin G 1.2 million units IV q4h OR doxycycline 100mg BID × 7-10 days

### **Complications:**

- **Cellulitis, abscess** (most common)
- **Osteomyelitis, septic arthritis** (especially hand bites)
- **Tenosynovitis** (hand bites)
- **Sepsis** (immunocompromised, asplenia)
- **Rabies** (if not prophylaxed)

## Key Points

### **Clean + Irrigate = PRIMARY:**
- **Copious high-pressure irrigation** (15-20 min)
- **Do NOT close** high-risk wounds (hand, deep puncture, >12h)

### **Amoxicillin-Clavulanate:**
- **First-line** for prophylaxis and treatment
- **Covers *Pasteurella*, *Eikenella*, *S. aureus*, anaerobes**

### **Prophylaxis if Cat, Human, Hand, Deep:**
- **Amox-clav 875mg BID × 3-5 days**

### **Rabies Prophylaxis:**
- **Bats, unvaccinated dogs/cats, wild carnivores** → RIG + vaccine
- **Vaccinated dogs/cats** → observe (no prophylaxis)

### **Hand Bites = High Risk:**
- **Hospitalize, IV antibiotics, surgical consultation**
- **X-ray** (r/o fracture, foreign body, osteomyelitis)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Irrigate + debride** (primary wound care)
- **Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875mg BID** (prophylaxis 3-5d, treatment 5-10d)
- **Prophylaxis:** Cat bites, human bites, hand/face, deep puncture, immunocompromised
- **Rabies:** Bats (any contact), unvaccinated dogs/cats, wild carnivores → RIG + vaccine
- **Hand bites:** High risk (hospitalize, IV antibiotics, surgery consult)
- **Asplenia + dog bite:** *Capnocytophaga* sepsis (fulminant, high mortality)
