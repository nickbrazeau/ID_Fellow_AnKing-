## Prevention of Infective Endocarditis: AHA Guidelines

**Q:** Which cardiac conditions and procedures require infective endocarditis prophylaxis per 2021 AHA guidelines, and what are the antibiotic regimens?

**A:**

**HIGH-RISK Cardiac Conditions (prophylaxis indicated):**
1. **Prosthetic valve or prosthetic material** for valve repair
2. **Prior infective endocarditis**
3. **Congenital heart disease (CHD)**:
   - Unrepaired cyanotic CHD (including palliative shunts/conduits)
   - Completely repaired CHD with prosthetic material/device (**first 6 months only**)
   - Repaired CHD with **residual defects** at/adjacent to prosthetic patch/device
4. **Cardiac transplant recipients** with valvulopathy

**Procedures Requiring Prophylaxis:**
- **Dental**: Manipulation of gingival tissue, periapical region, or perforation of oral mucosa
- **GI/GU procedures**: **NOT recommended** (2007 change)
- **Respiratory tract**: NOT routinely recommended

**Antibiotic Regimens (single dose 30-60 min before procedure):**

| Situation | Regimen |
|-----------|---------|
| **Oral/able to take PO** | **Amoxicillin 2 g PO** |
| **Unable to take PO** | Ampicillin 2 g IV/IM OR cefazolin/ceftriaxone 1 g IV/IM |
| **Penicillin allergy** | **Azithromycin 500 mg PO** OR clarithromycin 500 mg PO OR cephalexin 2 g PO (if no anaphylaxis) |
| **PCN allergy + unable to take PO** | Cefazolin 1 g IV/IM OR ceftriaxone 1 g IV/IM |

**Pearl:** Most patients do NOT need prophylaxis; limited to highest-risk cardiac conditions only.
