## Prostatitis: NIH Classification and Treatment

**Q:** What is the NIH classification system for prostatitis, and how does antibiotic treatment differ between acute and chronic bacterial prostatitis?

**A:**

**NIH Classification:**

| Category | Name | Features | Treatment Duration |
|----------|------|----------|-------------------|
| **I** | **Acute Bacterial Prostatitis (ABP)** | Severe symptoms, fever, systemic infection, acute UTI | **2-4 weeks** |
| **II** | **Chronic Bacterial Prostatitis (CBP)** | Chronic infection ± symptoms, **recurrent UTIs** (same strain) | **4-6 weeks** |
| **III** | Chronic Prostatitis/Chronic Pelvic Pain Syndrome (CP/CPPS) | Chronic pelvic pain, NO UTI | Trial antibiotics (often ineffective) |
| **IV** | Asymptomatic Inflammatory Prostatitis | Prostate inflammation, NO symptoms | NO treatment |

**Treatment:**

**Acute Bacterial Prostatitis (Category I):**

**Mild (outpatient):**
- **Fluoroquinolone** (ciprofloxacin 500 mg PO BID OR levofloxacin 750 mg PO daily) × **2-4 weeks**
- **TMP-SMX DS** (160/800 mg) PO BID × 2-4 weeks (if susceptible)

**Severe (inpatient):**
- **Ceftriaxone 1-2 g IV q24h** + **gentamicin 5 mg/kg IV q24h** OR
- **Ampicillin 2 g IV q6h + gentamicin**
- Transition to PO fluoroquinolone when clinically improved

**Chronic Bacterial Prostatitis (Category II):**
- **Fluoroquinolone** (preferred) × **4-6 weeks**
- **Cure rate ~70%** with fluoroquinolones
- Alternatives: Doxycycline, TMP-SMX (if susceptible)

**Rationale for Fluoroquinolones:**
- **Excellent prostate penetration** (high pKa, lipophilic)
- Broad spectrum (Gram-negative + Gram-positive)
- PO bioavailability

**Microbiology:**
- *E. coli* (most common), *Klebsiella, Proteus, Pseudomonas*
- *Enterococcus* (consider in catheterized patients)

**Pearl:** Chronic prostatitis requires prolonged therapy (4-6 weeks) for cure; shorter courses lead to relapse.
