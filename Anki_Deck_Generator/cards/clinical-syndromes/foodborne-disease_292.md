## Foodborne Disease: Incubation Periods and Etiology

**Q:** What are the incubation periods for toxin-mediated foodborne illnesses, and how do they differ from infectious causes?

**A:**

**Toxin-Mediated (Preformed Toxins - Rapid Onset):**

| Pathogen | Incubation | Symptoms | Duration | Food Sources |
|----------|-----------|----------|----------|--------------|
| ***S. aureus*** | **1-6 hours** (median 4h) | **Sudden vomiting** → explosive diarrhea | **12-24 hours** (self-limited) | Meats, potato salad, cream pastries (food handlers) |
| ***B. cereus* (emetic)** | **1-5 hours** (30 min-6h) | **Nausea + vomiting** (mimics *S. aureus*) | **6-24 hours** | **Fried rice** (reheated), pasta |
| ***B. cereus* (diarrheal)** | **8-16 hours** (6-15h) | **Diarrhea** + abdominal pain, minimal vomiting | **24 hours** | Meats, vegetables, sauces |
| ***C. perfringens*** | **8-16 hours** (median 10h) | **Diarrhea** + cramping (NO vomiting) | **24 hours** | Reheated meats, gravies |

**Infectious (Bacteria Multiply In Vivo - Longer Incubation):**

| Pathogen | Incubation | Key Features |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| *Salmonella* | **12-72 hours** | Fever, bloody diarrhea |
| *Campylobacter* | **2-5 days** | Bloody diarrhea, fever |
| *Shigella* | **1-3 days** | Dysentery (bloody diarrhea) |
| *E. coli* O157:H7 | **3-4 days** | Bloody diarrhea → HUS |
| Norovirus | **12-48 hours** | Vomiting > diarrhea, outbreaks |

**Key Principles:**
- **<6 hours incubation** → Think **preformed toxin** (*S. aureus*, *B. cereus* emetic)
- **Prominent vomiting + rapid onset** → Toxin-mediated
- **Fever + bloody diarrhea** → Invasive bacteria (requires antimicrobials)
- Toxin-mediated illnesses are **self-limited** (supportive care only)

**Pearl:** "Fried rice syndrome" = *B. cereus* emetic; reheating doesn't destroy heat-stable toxins.
