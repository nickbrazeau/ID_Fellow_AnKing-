## Acute Laryngitis: Etiology and Management

**Q:** What is the most common etiology of acute laryngitis, and when are antibiotics indicated?

**A:**

**Etiology:**
- **Viral (>90%)**: Rhinovirus, parainfluenza, RSV, coronavirus, adenovirus, influenza
- **Bacterial (rare)**: Secondary bacterial superinfection
  - *S. pneumoniae, H. influenzae, M. catarrhalis*
- **Non-infectious**: Voice overuse, GERD, smoking, inhalational injury

**Clinical Features:**
- **Hoarseness** ("raspy voice") - hallmark symptom
- Early vocal fatigue (esp. singers/professional voice users)
- Dry cough, sore throat, throat tickling
- **Short-term voice loss** possible
- **Duration:** 3-7 days (self-limited)

**Management:**
- **Antibiotics NOT routinely indicated** (viral etiology)
- **Voice rest** (most important)
- Hydration, humidified air/steam inhalation
- Cough suppressants (dextromethorphan)
- Avoid throat clearing, whispering (strains vocal cords)

**Antibiotic Indications (rare):**
- Clinical evidence of bacterial superinfection
- Immunocompromised host
- Persistent symptoms >2 weeks

**Pearl:** Voice rest is more important than any medication for recovery.
