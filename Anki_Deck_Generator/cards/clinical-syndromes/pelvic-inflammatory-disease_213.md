## Question

How is PID managed? Use **"Ceftriaxone 500mg IM × 1 + Doxycycline 100mg BID × 14d ± Metronidazole, Hospitalize if TOA/Severe"** framework.

## Answer

### **Definition:**

**Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID):**
- **Infection/inflammation** of upper female genital tract
- **Includes:** Endometritis, salpingitis, tubo-ovarian abscess (TOA), pelvic peritonitis

### **Clinical Presentation:**

**Minimum Criteria for Diagnosis:**
- **Lower abdominal/pelvic pain**
- **+ Cervical motion tenderness** OR **uterine tenderness** OR **adnexal tenderness**
- **+ No other cause identified**

**Additional Supportive Criteria:**
- **Fever** >38.3°C (101°F)
- **Mucopurulent cervical discharge**
- **Elevated ESR, CRP**
- **Laboratory evidence** of GC/CT infection

**Specific Criteria (Definitive Diagnosis):**
- **Endometrial biopsy:** Endometritis
- **Imaging:** Tubo-ovarian abscess, thickened fluid-filled tubes
- **Laparoscopy:** Purulent material from tubes, tubal erythema/edema

### **Microbiology:**

**Sexually Transmitted (Initiating Pathogens):**
- ***Neisseria gonorrhoeae*** (10-40%)
- ***Chlamydia trachomatis*** (10-30%)

**Polymicrobial (Secondary):**
- **Anaerobes:** *Bacteroides*, *Prevotella*
- **Facultative Gram-negatives:** *E. coli*, *Gardnerella vaginalis*
- **Streptococci**
- ***Mycoplasma genitalium***

### **Diagnostic Approach:**

**Labs:**
- **NAAT for GC/CT** (cervical, vaginal, or urine) - positive in only 10-40% with PID
- **Pregnancy test** (r/o ectopic pregnancy)
- **CBC, ESR, CRP** (non-specific, but elevated if PID)

**Imaging:**
- **Transvaginal ultrasound:** If concern for TOA, severe symptoms, unclear diagnosis
- **CT/MRI:** If ultrasound non-diagnostic

**Do NOT delay treatment** waiting for test results (empiric therapy if clinical suspicion)

### **Treatment:**

**Outpatient (Mild-Moderate PID):**

**Preferred:**
- **Ceftriaxone 500mg IM × 1** (1g if ≥150 kg)
- **+ Doxycycline 100mg PO BID × 14 days**
- **± Metronidazole 500mg PO BID × 14 days** (if BV, TOA, recent instrumentation)

**Alternative (If Ceftriaxone Not Available):**
- **Cefoxitin 2g IM × 1 + probenecid 1g PO × 1**
- **+ Doxycycline 100mg PO BID × 14 days ± metronidazole**

**Inpatient (Severe PID, TOA, Pregnancy):**

**Indications for Hospitalization:**
- **Tubo-ovarian abscess (TOA)**
- **Pregnancy**
- **Severe illness** (high fever, nausea/vomiting, unable to tolerate PO)
- **Surgical emergency** (r/o appendicitis, ectopic)
- **Failed outpatient therapy** (no improvement in 48-72h)
- **Immunocompromised** (HIV)

**Inpatient Regimens:**

**Regimen A (Preferred):**
- **Ceftriaxone 1g IV q24h** OR **cefotetan 2g IV q12h** OR **cefoxitin 2g IV q6h**
- **+ Doxycycline 100mg PO/IV q12h**
- **± Metronidazole 500mg IV/PO q8h** (if TOA, severe)

**Regimen B (Alternative):**
- **Clindamycin 900mg IV q8h**
- **+ Gentamicin 5-7 mg/kg IV q24h** (loading dose)
- **Once-daily gentamicin** (monitor levels if >48h)

**Transition to Oral:**
- **After 24-48h improvement** (afebrile, pain improving)
- **Complete 14 days total** with doxycycline 100mg PO BID ± metronidazole 500mg PO BID

### **Tubo-Ovarian Abscess (TOA):**

**Diagnosis:**
- **Imaging:** Transvaginal ultrasound (complex adnexal mass, fluid-filled tube)

**Treatment:**
- **Inpatient IV antibiotics** (regimen A or B above)
- **Drainage:** If no improvement in 48-72h OR abscess >9 cm
  - **Percutaneous drainage** (IR-guided)
  - **Surgical drainage** (if ruptured, peritonitis)

**Ruptured TOA:**
- **Surgical emergency** (high mortality if untreated)
- **Emergent laparotomy** + IV antibiotics

### **Follow-Up:**

**Outpatient:**
- **Re-evaluate in 48-72h** (in-person or phone)
- **Hospitalize if:** No improvement, worsening symptoms, unable to tolerate PO

**Test of Cure:**
- **NOT routinely recommended**
- **Retest for GC/CT in 3 months** (high reinfection rate)

### **Partner Management:**

**Treat All Partners:**
- **Within 60 days** (or most recent if >60 days)
- **Empiric treatment:** Ceftriaxone 500mg IM × 1 + doxycycline 100mg BID × 7 days
- **Expedited partner therapy (EPT)** if partner unable to come in

### **Complications:**

**Acute:**
- **Tubo-ovarian abscess** (15-30%)
- **Peritonitis** (if ruptured TOA)
- **Fitz-Hugh-Curtis syndrome** (perihepatitis, RUQ pain)

**Chronic (Long-Term):**
- **Infertility** (10-20% after 1st episode, 30-40% after 2nd, 50-60% after 3rd)
- **Ectopic pregnancy** (6-10× risk)
- **Chronic pelvic pain** (20-30%)

### **Prevention:**

**Screen High-Risk Women:**
- **Annual GC/CT screening** (sexually active women <25y, older women with risk factors)
- **Treat asymptomatic infections** (prevents progression to PID)

### **Special Situations:**

**Pregnancy:**
- **Hospitalize + IV antibiotics**
- **Avoid doxycycline** (use ceftriaxone + azithromycin instead)

**HIV-Positive:**
- **Same treatment**
- **Higher risk of TOA** (consider hospitalization)

**IUD:**
- **Do NOT routinely remove** (unless no improvement in 48-72h)
- **Remove if:** Actinomyces PID, unilateral TOA (concern for perforation)

## Key Points

### **Empiric Outpatient = Ceftriaxone + Doxycycline × 14d:**
- **Ceftriaxone 500mg IM × 1** + **doxycycline 100mg BID × 14 days**
- **Add metronidazole** if BV, TOA, recent instrumentation

### **Low Threshold to Treat:**
- **Clinical diagnosis:** Lower abdominal pain + cervical/uterine/adnexal tenderness
- **Do NOT wait for positive GC/CT** (only 10-40% positive in PID)

### **Hospitalize if TOA, Severe, Pregnancy:**
- **IV antibiotics** (ceftriaxone + doxycycline OR clindamycin + gentamicin)
- **Drainage** if no improvement in 48-72h or abscess >9 cm

### **14 Days Duration:**
- **Longer than GC/CT alone** (salpingitis requires extended therapy)

### **Complications = Infertility, Ectopic, Chronic Pain:**
- **10-20% infertility** after 1st episode
- **30-40%** after 2nd episode
- **50-60%** after 3rd episode

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Outpatient:** Ceftriaxone 500mg IM × 1 + doxycycline 100mg BID × **14 days** ± metronidazole
- **Hospitalize:** TOA, pregnancy, severe illness, failed outpatient
- **Low threshold to treat** (pelvic pain + CMT/uterine/adnexal tenderness)
- **GC/CT negative in 60-90%** of PID cases (polymicrobial)
- **Complications:** Infertility (10-20% after 1st episode), ectopic, chronic pain
- **Retest at 3 months** (high reinfection rate)
