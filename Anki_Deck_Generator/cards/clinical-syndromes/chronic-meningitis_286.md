## Chronic Meningitis: Differential Diagnosis and CSF Findings

**Q:** What are the major infectious causes of chronic meningitis (>4 weeks), and how do CSF findings help differentiate them?

**A:**

**Major Causes (by Population):**

**HIV-infected:**
- *Cryptococcus neoformans* (most common)
- *M. tuberculosis*
- *T. pallidum* (neurosyphilis)
- *Coccidioides immitis* (endemic areas)

**Immunocompetent:**
- TB, cryptococcus, endemic fungi (*Histoplasma, Coccidioides, Blastomyces*)
- *Brucella*, syphilis, Lyme disease

**CSF Findings:**

| Pathogen | Opening Pressure | WBC (cells/mm³) | Glucose | Protein | Specific Tests |
|----------|------------------|-----------------|---------|---------|----------------|
| **TB Meningitis** | ↑↑ | ~500 (lymphocyte predominant) | **Profound ↓** (CSF/serum <0.5) | ↑↑ (>100) | AFB smear (10-20% sens), PCR (50% sens), culture (takes 8 wks) |
| **Cryptococcus** | ↑↑ (often >250) | Variable (may be low in HIV) | ↓ | ↑ | **India ink 84%**, **CrAg (CSF/serum) >95%** |
| **Histoplasma** | ↑ | Lymphocytic | ↓ | ↑ | **Histoplasma Ag (CSF/urine)**, culture, antibody |
| **Coccidioides** | ↑ | Lymphocytic | ↓ | ↑ | **Coccidioidal Ab (CSF CF titer)**, Ag |

**Diagnostic Approach:**
1. **CSF studies**: Cell count/diff, glucose, protein, Gram stain, AFB smear, India ink
2. **Antigens**: CrAg, *Histoplasma* Ag (urine + CSF), *Coccidioides* Ab/Ag
3. **Cultures**: Bacterial, fungal (large volume), AFB
4. **Molecular**: TB PCR (sens 50%), fungal PCR
5. **Serum**: Coccidioidal Ab, Histoplasma Ag, HIV, RPR/VDRL

**Pearl:** Cryptococcal meningitis in HIV may have **minimal CSF pleocytosis**; TB requires high suspicion (AFB smear only 10-20% sensitive).
