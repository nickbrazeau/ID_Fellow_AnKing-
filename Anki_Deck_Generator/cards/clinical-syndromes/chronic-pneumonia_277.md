## Chronic Pneumonia: Differential Diagnosis

**Q:** What are the key causes of chronic pneumonia (symptoms >3-4 weeks), and what imaging/clinical features distinguish them?

**A:**

| Category | Pathogens | Imaging Features | Clinical Clues |
|----------|-----------|------------------|----------------|
| **Mycobacteria** | *M. tuberculosis*, MAC, *M. kansasii* | **Upper lobe cavities**, tree-in-bud, lymphadenopathy | Night sweats, weight loss, endemic areas, immunocompromised |
| **Endemic Fungi** | *Histoplasma, Coccidioides, Blastomyces* | Consolidation + **hilar/mediastinal LAD**, nodules, cavities | Geographic exposure (Ohio/Miss valleys, Southwest, Great Lakes) |
| **Cryptococcus** | *Cryptococcus neoformans/gattii* | Variable: nodules, masses, consolidation, cavities | Immunocompromised (HIV), pigeon exposure, ± CNS symptoms |
| **Nocardia** | *Nocardia asteroides* | **Thick-walled cavities**, nodules (may mimic malignancy) | Immunosuppressed, skin/CNS involvement |
| **Aspergillus** | *Aspergillus* (invasive) | **"Halo sign"** (early), air-crescent sign (late), cavities | Neutropenia, hematologic malignancy |

**Distinguishing Features:**
- **Erythema nodosum** → *Coccidioides* or TB
- **Solitary pulmonary nodule** → Consider endemic fungi (5-14%)
- **Hilar/mediastinal LAD** → *Histoplasma, Coccidioides* (frequent)
- **CNS involvement** → TB, *Cryptococcus*, *Coccidioides*, *Nocardia*

**Diagnostic Approach:**
- **History**: Travel, exposures, immunocompromised status
- **Imaging**: CT chest with contrast
- **Microbiology**: Cultures (bacterial, fungal, AFB), antigen testing (Histoplasma/Coccidioides urine Ag, serum β-D-glucan, Cryptococcal Ag)
- **Biopsy**: If diagnosis uncertain

**Pearl:** Endemic fungi frequently misdiagnosed as bacterial CAP → delayed antifungal therapy.
