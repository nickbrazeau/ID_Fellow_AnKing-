## Question

How do CSF profiles differentiate meningitis etiologies? Use **"Bacterial: ↓Glucose + ↑PMNs, Viral: Normal Glucose + Lymphs, TB/Fungal: ↓Glucose + Lymphs"** framework.

## Answer

### **Normal CSF:**
- **WBC:** <5 cells/μL (all mononuclear)
- **Protein:** 15-45 mg/dL
- **Glucose:** 50-75 mg/dL (CSF:serum ratio >0.6)
- **Opening pressure:** 10-20 cm H₂O

### **Bacterial Meningitis:**
- **WBC:** **100-10,000+ cells/μL** (usually >1000)
- **Differential:** **>80% PMNs** (neutrophils)
- **Protein:** **↑↑ (100-500+ mg/dL)**
- **Glucose:** **↓↓ (<40 mg/dL, CSF:serum ratio <0.4)**
- **Opening pressure:** Elevated (>25 cm H₂O)
- **Gram stain:** Positive in 60-90% (varies by organism)

**Organisms:** *S. pneumoniae*, *N. meningitidis*, *L. monocytogenes*, *H. influenzae*, *S. agalactiae*

### **Viral (Aseptic) Meningitis:**
- **WBC:** **10-1000 cells/μL** (usually 10-500)
- **Differential:** **>50% lymphocytes** (may be PMN-predominant early, shifts to lymphocytes by 12-24h)
- **Protein:** **Normal to mildly ↑ (50-100 mg/dL)**
- **Glucose:** **Normal (>45 mg/dL, ratio >0.6)**
- **Opening pressure:** Normal to mildly elevated

**Organisms:** Enteroviruses (most common), HSV-2, VZV, HIV, arboviruses

### **Tuberculous (TB) Meningitis:**
- **WBC:** **10-500 cells/μL** (usually 100-500)
- **Differential:** **Lymphocyte-predominant** (may be PMN-predominant early)
- **Protein:** **↑↑ (100-500+ mg/dL)**
- **Glucose:** **↓ (<45 mg/dL, ratio <0.5)**
- **Opening pressure:** Elevated
- **AFB smear:** Low sensitivity (10-20%), culture gold standard (takes weeks)

**Additional:** **Adenosine deaminase (ADA) >10 U/L** (supports TB diagnosis)

### **Fungal Meningitis (***Cryptococcus*, *Coccidioides***):**
- **WBC:** **20-500 cells/μL**
- **Differential:** **Lymphocyte-predominant**
- **Protein:** **↑ (50-200+ mg/dL)**
- **Glucose:** **↓ (<40 mg/dL)**
- **Opening pressure:** Often markedly elevated (*Cryptococcus* >25 cm H₂O common)

***Cryptococcus*:**
- **India ink:** Positive in 50-80% (especially AIDS)
- **Cryptococcal antigen (CrAg):** High sensitivity (>95%), gold standard

***Coccidioides*:**
- **Eosinophils in CSF** (20-40% of cases) - clue
- **Complement fixation titer ≥1:2** in CSF (diagnostic)

### **Partially Treated Bacterial Meningitis:**
- **Can mimic viral** (lymphocyte-predominant, normal glucose)
- **Elevated protein** (often >100 mg/dL, more than typical viral)
- **CSF culture negative** (if antibiotics given before LP)
- **Consider bacterial PCR** (if available)

### **Special Situations:**

**HSV Encephalitis (NOT Meningitis):**
- **RBCs present** (hemorrhagic, temporal lobe)
- **Lymphocytic pleocytosis**
- **Normal to ↑ protein**
- **Normal glucose**
- **HSV PCR:** Gold standard (high sensitivity)

**Lyme Meningitis:**
- **Lymphocytic pleocytosis**
- **↑ Protein**
- **Normal glucose**
- **CSF *Borrelia* antibodies** (with serum titers)

## Key Points

### **Glucose = Key Differentiator:**
- **Low (<40 mg/dL):** Bacterial, TB, fungal
- **Normal (>45 mg/dL):** Viral, Lyme

### **Cell Count/Type:**
- **PMN-predominant:** Bacterial (early viral/TB can be PMN-predominant, but shift to lymphs by 12-24h)
- **Lymphocyte-predominant:** Viral, TB, fungal, Lyme, partially treated bacterial

### **Protein:**
- **Very high (>200 mg/dL):** TB, fungal, bacterial
- **Mildly elevated (50-100 mg/dL):** Viral
- **Normal (<45 mg/dL):** Viral (some cases)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Bacterial:** ↓↓Glucose + ↑↑PMNs + ↑↑protein (WBC >1000)
- **Viral:** Normal glucose + lymphocytes + mildly ↑protein (WBC 10-500)
- **TB/Fungal:** ↓Glucose + lymphocytes + ↑↑protein (WBC 100-500)
- **Partially treated bacterial:** Can mimic viral (lymphs, normal glucose), but protein often >100 mg/dL
- **HSV encephalitis:** RBCs + lymphocytes + normal glucose → HSV PCR
