## Question

How do you distinguish true bacteremia from contaminants? Use **"Single Positive (CoNS, *Cutibacterium*) = Likely Contaminant, Multiple Positive + Clinical Syndrome = True"** framework.

## Answer

### **Common True Pathogens (Almost Always Significant if Isolated):**
- ***S. aureus*** (including MRSA)
- ***E. coli***, *Klebsiella*, other Enterobacterales
- ***Pseudomonas aeruginosa***
- ***Candida* species**
- ***Streptococcus pneumoniae*, *S. pyogenes*, *S. agalactiae***
- ***Enterococcus* species**
- **Anaerobes** (*Bacteroides*, *Clostridium*)

**Action:** **ALWAYS treat** (rarely contaminants)

### **Common Contaminants (Often Skin Flora):**
- **Coagulase-negative staphylococci (CoNS)** - *S. epidermidis*, *S. hominis*
- ***Cutibacterium* (formerly *Propionibacterium*) *acnes***
- ***Corynebacterium* species** (diphtheroids)
- ***Bacillus* species** (NOT *B. anthracis*)
- ***Micrococcus* species**
- **Viridans group streptococci** (unless endocarditis risk)

**Action:** Assess clinical significance

### **Criteria for True Bacteremia (vs. Contamination):**

**Favor True Bacteremia If:**
1. **≥2 positive blood cultures** (from separate venipunctures) with same organism
2. **Single positive culture** from patient with:
   - **Intravascular device** (central line, pacemaker, prosthetic valve)
   - **Clinical signs of infection** (fever, hypotension, elevated WBC)
   - **Source consistent** with organism (e.g., CoNS in patient with prosthetic valve)
   - **Time to positivity <15 hours** (earlier positive = higher organism burden)
3. **Organism known to cause endocarditis** (even if single bottle) - viridans strep, *S. lugdunensis*

**Favor Contamination If:**
1. **Single positive bottle** (out of ≥2 sets drawn)
2. **Typical skin flora** (CoNS, *Cutibacterium*, diphtheroids)
3. **No clinical signs of infection** (afebrile, stable, normal WBC)
4. **Time to positivity >24 hours** (late positive = low organism burden)
5. **Mixed flora** (polymicrobial with multiple skin organisms)

### **Special Cases:**

***S. lugdunensis* (CoNS Species):**
- **More virulent** than other CoNS
- **Can cause endocarditis** (acute, aggressive)
- **Treat as true pathogen** (similar to *S. aureus*)

**Viridans Streptococci:**
- **Usually contaminants** (oral flora)
- **BUT treat as true pathogen if:** Endocarditis risk factors (prosthetic valve, valvular disease, recent dental procedure)

***Bacillus* Species:**
- **Usually contaminant** (environmental, soil)
- ***B. cereus* can be true pathogen:** IVDU, immunocompromised, central lines, traumatic inoculation

**Anaerobes:**
- **Rarely contaminants** (difficult to grow)
- **Usually significant:** Intra-abdominal infection, aspiration pneumonia, necrotizing infections

### **Clinical Management:**

**If Unclear:**
- **Repeat blood cultures** (2-4 sets from separate sites)
- **Assess clinical context:** Fever? Hypotension? Source of infection?
- **Review time to positivity** (earlier = higher likelihood of true bacteremia)
- **Check for intravascular devices**

**If Likely Contamination:**
- **Do NOT treat** with antibiotics (avoid unnecessary antibiotic exposure, resistance, *C. difficile* risk)
- **Repeat cultures** if clinical suspicion remains

**If True Bacteremia:**
- **Source control:** Remove infected catheter, drain abscess
- **Appropriate antibiotics** based on organism/susceptibilities
- **Evaluate for complications:** Endocarditis (echo), metastatic foci (imaging)

## Key Points

### **CoNS = Most Common Contaminant:**
- **Single positive bottle** with CoNS (NOT *S. lugdunensis*) → likely contaminant
- **≥2 positive bottles** OR **intravascular device** → consider true bacteremia

### ***S. aureus* = ALWAYS Significant:**
- **Never** dismiss as contaminant
- **Evaluate for endocarditis**, metastatic foci (vertebral osteomyelitis, epidural abscess)
- **Repeat blood cultures** to document clearance

### **Time to Positivity:**
- **<15 hours:** Favor true bacteremia (high organism burden)
- **>24 hours:** Favor contamination (low organism burden, skin inoculation during draw)

### **Polymicrobial Bacteremia:**
- **True polymicrobial:** Intra-abdominal source (*E. coli* + *Bacteroides*), necrotizing fasciitis
- **Contamination:** Multiple skin flora organisms (CoNS + diphtheroids + *Cutibacterium*)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- ***S. aureus*, Gram-negatives, *Candida*** = ALWAYS treat
- **CoNS:** Single positive = likely contaminant, multiple positive + line = true bacteremia
- ***S. lugdunensis*:** Treat as *S. aureus* (virulent, causes endocarditis)
- **≥2 positive cultures (same organism)** = true bacteremia
- **Time to positivity <15h** = higher likelihood true bacteremia
