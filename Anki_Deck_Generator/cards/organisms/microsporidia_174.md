## Question

What are the key clinical syndromes and treatment of microsporidiosis? Use the **"AIDS Diarrhea (Enterocytozoon), Keratoconjunctivitis (Encephalitozoon) → Albendazole, Fumagillin (Eye), ART"** framework.

## Answer

### **Microsporidia Overview:**

**Organisms:** Obligate intracellular fungi (previously classified as protozoa)

**Common Species:**
- ***Enterocytozoon bieneusi*** (most common, 90% of GI infections)
- ***Encephalitozoon* species** (*E. intestinalis*, *E. cuniculi*, *E. hellem*) - 10% GI, also cause disseminated/ocular disease

**Transmission:** Ingestion/inhalation of spores (contaminated water, food, soil), possibly zoonotic

**Epidemiology:**
- **AIDS (CD4 <100)** - most common
- **Transplant recipients, immunosuppressed**

### **Clinical Syndromes:**

**Intestinal Microsporidiosis (Most Common):**

**Presentation:**
- **Chronic watery diarrhea** (non-bloody)
- **Weight loss, malabsorption**
- **Abdominal cramping**
- **Less severe than *Cryptosporidium*** (usually <1 L/day)

**Causative Organisms:**
- ***E. bieneusi*** (90%) - intestine-only, NO dissemination
- ***E. intestinalis*** (10%) - can disseminate

**Ocular Microsporidiosis (Keratoconjunctivitis):**

**Presentation:**
- **Chronic conjunctivitis:** Redness, tearing, photophobia, foreign body sensation
- **Keratitis:** Punctate epithelial lesions
- **Bilateral** (usually)

**Causative Organisms:**
- ***E. hellem*, *E. cuniculi*, *Vittaforma corneae***

**Disseminated Microsporidiosis:**

**Manifestations:**
- **Hepatitis, peritonitis, nephritis**
- **Sinusitis, bronchitis, pneumonia**
- **Myositis, encephalitis** (rare)

**Causative Organisms:**
- ***Encephalitozoon* species** (NOT *E. bieneusi*)

### **Diagnosis:**

**Stool (Intestinal):**
- **Modified trichrome stain (Weber chromotrope):** Spores (1-2 μm, pinkish-red, oval)
- **PCR:** Most sensitive (species identification)
- **Electron microscopy:** Gold standard (not routinely available)

**Corneal Scrapings (Ocular):**
- **Gram, Giemsa, modified trichrome stain**
- **Confocal microscopy**

**Tissue Biopsy:**
- **Small bowel biopsy:** Intraepithelial organisms (EM needed for definitive diagnosis)

**Key Microscopic Feature:**
- **Tiny spores (1-2 μm)** - smallest human parasite

### **Treatment:**

**Intestinal Microsporidiosis:**

***E. bieneusi*:**
- **Albendazole:** **NOT effective** (lacks drug target)
- **Fumagillin 20mg PO TID × 14 days:** Effective, but **NOT FDA-approved, difficult to obtain** (investigational in US, available in some countries)
- **ART (HIV):** **Most important** (immune reconstitution = cure)

***E. intestinalis*:**
- **Albendazole 400mg PO BID × 21 days** (highly effective)
- **ART:** Continue/optimize

**Ocular Microsporidiosis:**

***E. hellem*, *E. cuniculi*:**
- **Albendazole 400mg PO BID × 21 days**
- **+ Topical fumagillin 70 μg/mL (Fumidil B) eye drops:** Compounded (not commercially available in US)

**Disseminated Microsporidiosis:**
- **Albendazole 400mg PO BID × ≥4 weeks** (*Encephalitozoon* species)
- **ART:** Essential for cure

**Supportive Care:**
- **Hydration, nutritional support**

**Duration:**
- **Continue until CD4 >200 × 6 months on ART** (AIDS patients)

## Key Points

### **Smallest Human Parasite:**
- **Spores 1-2 μm** (tiny, can be missed on routine stool O&P)
- **Modified trichrome stain** (Weber chromotrope) required

### ***E. bieneusi* vs. *E. intestinalis*:**

| Feature | *E. bieneusi* | *E. intestinalis* |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Frequency (GI)** | 90% | 10% |
| **Dissemination** | NO | YES |
| **Albendazole** | **NOT effective** | **Effective** |
| **Treatment** | ART (fumagillin if available) | Albendazole + ART |

### **Fumagillin = Effective but Hard to Get:**
- **Only treatment for *E. bieneusi***
- **NOT FDA-approved** (investigational in US)
- **Available in some countries** (Europe)
- **Topical form** for ocular disease (compounded)

### **ART = Most Important:**
- **Immune reconstitution** = cure (especially for *E. bieneusi*, which lacks drug treatment)
- **CD4 >100** → symptoms resolve

### **Ocular Microsporidiosis:**
- **Chronic keratoconjunctivitis** (bilateral, redness, photophobia)
- **Treatment:** Albendazole (systemic) + topical fumagillin

### **Differentiate from *Cryptosporidium*:**
- ***Microsporidia*:** Smaller diarrhea volume, less severe
- ***Cryptosporidium*:** Profuse diarrhea (>10 L/day possible)
- **Both:** Chronic diarrhea in AIDS CD4 <100

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **AIDS CD4 <100** with chronic diarrhea → consider microsporidia
- **Tiny spores (1-2 μm)** on modified trichrome stain (Weber chromotrope)
- ***E. bieneusi* (90%):** ART only (fumagillin if available)
- ***E. intestinalis* (10%):** Albendazole 400mg BID × 21 days
- **Ocular:** Albendazole + topical fumagillin (compounded)
- **ART = most important** (immune reconstitution = cure)
