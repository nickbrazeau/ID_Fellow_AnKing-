## Question

How is cystoisosporiasis diagnosed and treated? Use the **"AIDS Diarrhea + Eosinophilia, Large Acid-Fast Oocysts → TMP-SMX"** framework.

## Answer

### ***Cystoisospora belli* (formerly *Isospora belli*) Overview:**

**Transmission:**
- **Fecal-oral:** Contaminated food/water
- **Endemic:** Tropical/subtropical regions (Latin America, Africa, Southeast Asia)

**Epidemiology:**
- **Immunocompetent:** Rare, self-limited
- **AIDS (CD4 <100):** Chronic diarrhea, common in endemic areas

### **Clinical Presentation:**

**AIDS / Immunocompromised:**
- **Chronic watery diarrhea** (weeks to months)
- **Weight loss, malabsorption**
- **Abdominal cramping**
- **Low-grade fever**
- **Eosinophilia** (20-40% of cases) - helpful clue

**Immunocompetent:**
- **Acute self-limited diarrhea** (1-2 weeks)
- **Rarely diagnosed** (mild illness)

### **Diagnosis:**

**Stool Testing:**
- **Modified acid-fast stain:** Oocysts (20-30 μm, **largest** of the acid-fast parasites), elliptical/oval, brightly acid-fast
- **Wet mount:** Oocysts (larger than *Cyclospora* 8-10 μm and *Cryptosporidium* 4-6 μm)
- **PCR:** Available in some labs (most sensitive)

**Microscopy Features:**
- **Oocysts:** 20-30 μm (elliptical/oval)
- **Larger than *Cyclospora* and *Cryptosporidium***
- **Sporulated oocysts:** 2 sporocysts (each with 4 sporozoites)

**Eosinophilia:**
- **Peripheral eosinophilia** (20-40% of cases)
- **Clue to diagnosis** in AIDS patient with diarrhea

### **Treatment:**

**TMP-SMX (Highly Effective):**
- **TMP-SMX DS (160/800mg) PO QID × 10 days** (immunocompetent)
- **AIDS:** **TMP-SMX DS QID × 10 days**
  - **Then maintenance:** **TMP-SMX DS 3×/week** (to prevent relapse)
  - **Continue until immune reconstitution** (CD4 >200 × 6 months on ART)

**Alternative (Sulfa Allergy):**
- **Pyrimethamine 50-75mg PO daily + leucovorin 10-25mg PO daily × 2-4 weeks**
- **Ciprofloxacin 500mg PO BID × 7 days** (less effective)

**Supportive Care:**
- **Hydration, electrolytes**

**ART:**
- **Start/optimize ART** (immune reconstitution = cure)

## Key Points

### **Largest Acid-Fast Oocyst:**
- ***Cystoisospora*:** 20-30 μm (largest, elliptical)
- ***Cyclospora*:** 8-10 μm
- ***Cryptosporidium*:** 4-6 μm
- **Size = key distinguishing feature**

### **Eosinophilia:**
- **20-40% of cases**
- **Clue to diagnosis** (uncommon in *Cryptosporidium*, *Cyclospora*)
- **Also seen with:** Helminth infections (but not other protozoa)

### **TMP-SMX QID:**
- **Higher dose than *Cyclospora*** (QID vs. BID)
- **Highly effective** (>90% cure)
- **Maintenance therapy essential** in AIDS (relapse common without)

### **AIDS-Defining Illness:**
- **Chronic diarrhea >1 month** in HIV = AIDS-defining
- **Common in endemic areas** (Africa, Latin America)

### **Relapse Common in AIDS:**
- **Without maintenance:** High relapse rate
- **TMP-SMX DS 3×/week** (same as *Pneumocystis* prophylaxis)
- **Stop when CD4 >200 × 6 months on ART**

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **AIDS CD4 <100** with chronic diarrhea + **eosinophilia** → suspect *Cystoisospora*
- **Large elliptical oocysts (20-30 μm)** on acid-fast stain
- **TMP-SMX DS QID × 10 days**, then **maintenance 3×/week** (AIDS)
- **Largest acid-fast oocyst** (vs. *Cyclospora* 8-10 μm, *Crypto* 4-6 μm)
- **Eosinophilia** (20-40%) = clue
