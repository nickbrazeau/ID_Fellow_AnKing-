## Uncommon and Subcutaneous Mycoses

**Q:** What are the key features and treatments for mucormycosis, chromoblastomycosis, mycetoma, dermatophytosis, and paracoccidioidomycosis?

**A:**

**Mucormycosis (Zygomycosis):**
- **Organisms**: Rhizopus, Mucor (Mucorales order)
- **Risk**: DKA, immunosuppressed, deferoxamine use
- **Syndromes**:
  - **Rhinocerebral** (most common): Black necrotic eschar on palate/nasal turbinates → orbital/brain invasion
  - Pulmonary, cutaneous, disseminated, GI
- **Hallmark**: **Angioinvasion** (tissue necrosis, thrombosis)
- **Treatment**: **Amphotericin B (liposomal) + surgical debridement**; isavuconazonium or posaconazole alternative

**Chromoblastomycosis:**
- **Organisms**: *Fonsecaea pedrosoi* (most common), *Cladosporium*
- **Route**: Traumatic inoculation (thorns, wood splinters)
- **Clinical**: **Verrucous (cauliflower-like) plaques** on extremities; limited to subcutaneous tissue
- **Diagnosis**: **Sclerotic bodies** (copper pennies) on histology
- **Treatment**: Itraconazole + terbinafine (combination); surgical excision for small lesions

**Mycetoma (Madura Foot):**
- **Organisms**: Fungi (eumycetoma) or bacteria (actinomycetoma - *Nocardia, Actinomadura*)
- **Route**: Traumatic inoculation
- **Clinical**: **Triad**: Tumor (swelling), sinuses (draining), grains (discharged granules - black/white/red depending on organism)
- **Treatment**:
  - **Eumycetoma**: Itraconazole or voriconazole × months-years; surgery
  - **Actinomycetoma**: TMP-SMX + amikacin/imipenem

**Dermatophytosis (Tinea):**
- **Organisms**: *Trichophyton, Microsporum, Epidermophyton*
- **Types**: Tinea capitis (scalp), corporis (body), cruris (groin), pedis (foot), unguium (nails)
- **Treatment**:
  - **Topical**: Azoles, terbinafine (most infections)
  - **Systemic**: Terbinafine or itraconazole (tinea capitis, unguium, extensive)

**Paracoccidioidomycosis:**
- **Organism**: *Paracoccidioides brasiliensis*
- **Geographic**: **Latin America** (endemic)
- **Clinical**: **"Mariner's wheel"** yeast on histology; oral ulcers, lymphadenopathy, pulmonary disease
- **Treatment**: **Itraconazole** (mild-moderate) or amphotericin B (severe) → itraconazole maintenance

**Key Points:**
- **Mucormycosis**: DKA risk; angioinvasion; amphotericin B + debridement
- **Chromoblastomycosis**: Verrucous plaques; sclerotic bodies
- **Mycetoma**: Tumor + sinuses + grains
- **Dermatophytosis**: Topical azoles/terbinafine (most); systemic for scalp/nails
- **Paracoccidioidomycosis**: Latin America; mariner's wheel yeast; itraconazole

**Mnemonic - "Mucor Munches (angioinvasion), Chrome Coins (sclerotic bodies), Mycetoma Makes grains"**

**Pearl:** Mucormycosis requires amphotericin B + aggressive surgical debridement (angioinvasive). Mycetoma triad: tumor, sinuses, grains. Chromoblastomycosis shows "copper penny" sclerotic bodies. Paracoccidioidomycosis endemic to Latin America with "mariner's wheel" yeast.
