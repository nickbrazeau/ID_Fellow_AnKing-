## Common Respiratory Viruses (RSV, Parainfluenza, Adenovirus)

**Q:** What are the clinical features and management of common respiratory viruses (RSV, parainfluenza, adenovirus, rhinovirus)?

**A:**

## 1. RESPIRATORY SYNCYTIAL VIRUS (RSV)

**Epidemiology:**
- **Most common cause of bronchiolitis** in infants (<1 year)
- **Seasonal** (fall/winter)

**Clinical Features:**

**Infants/Children:**
- **Bronchiolitis:** Wheezing, tachypnea, respiratory distress
- **Croup** (less common than parainfluenza)

**Adults (Immunocompromised, Elderly):**
- **Upper respiratory infection**
- **Pneumonia** (can be severe)

**Diagnosis:** **RSV PCR** (nasopharyngeal swab)

**Treatment:**

**Infants/Children:**
- **Supportive** (hydration, supplemental O2)
- **NO bronchodilators** (not effective)

**Immunocompromised Adults:**
- **Ribavirin** (inhaled or IV) - limited evidence

**Prevention:**
- **Palivizumab** (monoclonal antibody) - high-risk infants (monthly IM during RSV season)
- **Nirsevimab** (long-acting monoclonal) - all infants <8 months (single IM dose before RSV season)

**Key Point:** **RSV = #1 cause of bronchiolitis** in infants

---

## 2. PARAINFLUENZA VIRUS

**Epidemiology:**
- **Most common cause of croup** (laryngotracheobronchitis) in children

**Clinical Features:**
- **Croup:** Barking cough, stridor, hoarseness
- **Bronchiolitis, pneumonia**

**Diagnosis:** PCR (nasopharyngeal swab)

**Treatment:**

**Croup:**
- **Dexamethasone** 0.6 mg/kg PO/IM x 1 dose
- **Nebulized epinephrine** (if severe stridor/respiratory distress)

**Key Point:** **Parainfluenza = #1 cause of croup** (barking cough, stridor)

---

## 3. ADENOVIRUS

**Clinical Syndromes:**
- **Pharyngoconjunctival fever** (pharyngitis + conjunctivitis)
- **Acute respiratory disease** (military recruits)
- **Gastroenteritis** (serotypes 40, 41)
- **Hemorrhagic cystitis**
- **Severe pneumonia** (immunocompromised - HSCT recipients)

**Diagnosis:** PCR, viral culture

**Treatment:**
- **Supportive** (immunocompetent)
- **Cidofovir** (severe disease in immunocompromised)

**Key Point:** **Pharyngoconjunctival fever** = adenovirus

---

## 4. RHINOVIRUS / ENTEROVIRUS

**Epidemiology:**
- **Most common cause of common cold** (rhinovirus)
- **Enterovirus D68:** Acute flaccid myelitis (rare)

**Clinical Features:**
- **Common cold:** Rhinorrhea, nasal congestion, sore throat
- **Exacerbation of asthma/COPD**

**Treatment:** Supportive

**Key Point:** **Rhinovirus = #1 cause of common cold**

---

## COMPARISON TABLE

| **Virus** | **Key Clinical Syndrome** | **Treatment** |
|-----------|---------------------------|---------------|
| **RSV** | **Bronchiolitis** (infants) | Supportive (nirsevimab prophylaxis) |
| **Parainfluenza** | **Croup** (barking cough, stridor) | **Dexamethasone** (croup) |
| **Adenovirus** | **Pharyngoconjunctival fever** | Supportive (cidofovir if severe) |
| **Rhinovirus** | **Common cold** | Supportive |

---

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **RSV = #1 cause of bronchiolitis** in infants
- **Parainfluenza = #1 cause of croup** (treat with dexamethasone)
- **Adenovirus = pharyngoconjunctival fever**
- **Rhinovirus = #1 cause of common cold**
- **Nirsevimab (long-acting monoclonal)** for RSV prophylaxis (all infants <8 months)
