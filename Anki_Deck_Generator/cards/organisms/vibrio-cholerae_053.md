## Vibrio cholerae (Cholera)

**Q:** What is the pathophysiology, clinical presentation, and treatment of cholera (Vibrio cholerae)?

**A:**

**Microbiology:**
- **Gram-negative, comma-shaped rod** (curved bacillus)
- Motile (single polar flagellum)
- Serogroups: **O1** (classic and El Tor biotypes) and **O139** cause epidemic cholera
- Natural habitat: Brackish water, estuaries

**Pathophysiology:**

**Toxin-Mediated Diarrhea:**
1. **Cholera toxin (CTX):** A-B subunit toxin
2. Binds to GM1 ganglioside receptors on intestinal epithelial cells
3. A subunit activates adenylate cyclase → ↑ cAMP
4. **Result:** Massive secretion of Cl⁻, HCO₃⁻, and water into intestinal lumen
5. **Net effect:** Secretory diarrhea (up to 1 L/hour) without epithelial damage

**Transmission:**
- Fecal-oral route (contaminated water or food)
- Endemic in areas with poor sanitation
- Pandemic potential (7th pandemic ongoing since 1961)

---

**Clinical Presentation:**

**Incubation:** 12 hours to 5 days (typically 2-3 days)

**Spectrum:**
- **75%:** Asymptomatic or mild diarrhea
- **25%:** Severe cholera (cholera gravis)

**Classic "Cholera Gravis":**
- **"Rice-water stools":** Voluminous, watery, odorless diarrhea with mucus flecks
- **Massive fluid loss:** 10-20 L/day possible
- **Rapid dehydration:** Within hours
- **Hypokalemia, metabolic acidosis** (HCO₃⁻ loss)
- **Hypotension, shock** (if untreated)
- **"Washerwoman's hands"** (skin wrinkling from dehydration)
- **No fever** (afebrile)

**Complications:**
- Acute renal failure (pre-renal)
- Hypovolemic shock
- Hypokalemia-induced arrhythmias
- Death (if untreated mortality ~50%, treated <1%)

---

**Diagnosis:**

**Clinical:**
- Acute watery diarrhea + severe dehydration in endemic area

**Laboratory:**
- **Stool culture:** TCBS agar (thiosulfate-citrate-bile salts-sucrose)
- Yellow colonies (V. cholerae ferments sucrose)
- Serotyping (O1 or O139)
- Rapid diagnostic tests (RDTs) available

---

**Treatment:**

**1. Rehydration (CORNERSTONE OF THERAPY):**

**Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS):**
- **>99% survival with prompt ORS**
- WHO low-osmolarity ORS (≤270 mOsm/L):
  - Sodium chloride 2.6 g
  - Glucose 13.5 g
  - Potassium chloride 1.5 g
  - Trisodium citrate 2.9 g
  - Water to 1 L
- **Target:** Replace losses (match output)
- Effective for 80-90% of cases

**IV Fluids (Severe Dehydration/Shock):**
- **Ringer's lactate** (preferred) or normal saline
- Rapid bolus: 100 mL/kg over 3-4 hours (adults)
- Switch to ORS once able to drink

**2. Antibiotics (Adjunctive):**

**Indications:**
- Severe cholera (reduce duration, volume of diarrhea by ~50%)
- Decreases shedding

**First-Line Options:**

| **Antibiotic** | **Dosing** | **Duration** |
|----------------|------------|--------------|
| **Azithromycin** (preferred) | 1g PO x1 | Single dose |
| **Doxycycline** | 300 mg PO x1 | Single dose |
| **Ciprofloxacin** | 1g PO x1 or 500 mg PO BID | 1 or 3 days |
| **Tetracycline** | 500 mg PO QID | 3 days |

**Resistance Concerns:**
- Increasing resistance to tetracycline, ciprofloxacin in some regions
- Azithromycin often preferred

**3. Zinc Supplementation (Pediatrics):**
- **Children 6 months - 5 years:** Zinc 20 mg PO daily x 10-14 days
- Reduces duration and severity

---

**Prevention:**

**Water Safety:**
- Boil or chlorinate drinking water
- Avoid raw seafood in endemic areas

**Vaccination:**
- **Oral vaccines:** Dukoral (WC-rBS), Shanchol, Euvichol (killed whole-cell)
- ~60-80% protection for 2-3 years
- Used in outbreak settings and high-risk travelers

**Public Health:**
- Sanitation, safe water supply
- Case isolation, contact tracing

---

**Mnemonic: "Cholera = WATER TOXIN"**
- **W**atery diarrhea (rice-water)
- **A**febrile
- **T**oxin-mediated (CTX)
- **E**pidemic potential
- **R**ehydration saves lives

- **T**CBS agar (culture)
- **O**RS is treatment
- **X**tra (zinc in kids)
- **I**ncubation 2-3 days
- **N**o epithelial damage

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **Rehydration = 99% survival** (ORS is curative in most cases)
- Antibiotics are adjunctive (not primary treatment)
- **Rice-water stools + rapid dehydration** = think cholera
- Unlike invasive diarrhea (Shigella, Salmonella), cholera is afebrile and non-bloody
- **Most important public health intervention:** Clean water + sanitation
- Cholera toxin causes secretory (not inflammatory) diarrhea → no WBCs in stool
