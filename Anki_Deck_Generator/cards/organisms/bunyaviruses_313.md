## Bunyaviruses (Hantavirus, CCHF, Rift Valley Fever, La Crosse)

**Q:** What are the major bunyavirus syndromes, their target organs, and mortality rates?

**A:**

**Overview:**
- **Order**: Bunyavirales (formerly family Bunyaviridae)
- Most infections: **Self-limited febrile illness** (1-4 days) with headache, myalgia, nausea
- Many **asymptomatic**
- Some cause **severe disease** (hemorrhagic fever, encephalitis)

**Major Bunyavirus Diseases:**

| Virus | Syndrome | Target Organ(s) | Mortality | Key Features |
|-------|----------|-----------------|-----------|--------------|
| **Hantavirus** | **HFRS** (hemorrhagic fever with renal syndrome) | **Kidneys** | **15%** | Fever, hemorrhage, **acute renal failure**; rodent reservoir (urine) |
| **Hantavirus** | **HCPS** (hantavirus cardiopulmonary syndrome) | **Lungs, heart** | **50%** | Fever, **acute respiratory distress**; New World hantaviruses (Americas) |
| **Crimean-Congo HF (CCHFV)** | Hemorrhagic fever | **Vascular endothelium, liver** | **Up to 30%** | Headache, limb pain, **bleeding from multiple orifices**; tick-borne |
| **Rift Valley Fever (RVFV)** | Hemorrhagic hepatitis, encephalitis, retinitis | **Liver, brain, eyes** | **Up to 60%** (severe outbreaks) | Blurred vision/blindness, encephalitis, hemorrhagic hepatitis; mosquito-borne |
| **La Crosse Virus (LACV)** | Encephalitis | **Brain** | Low (but severe morbidity) | Fever, **seizures**, drowsiness, focal neurologic signs; children; mosquito-borne (California serogroup) |

**Key Clinical Syndromes:**

**1. Hemorrhagic Fever with Renal Syndrome (HFRS):**
- Hantavirus (Old World: *Hantaan, Dobrava, Puumala, Seoul* viruses)
- **Rodent reservoir** (aerosolized urine)
- Fever → hemorrhage → **acute kidney injury**

**2. Hantavirus Cardiopulmonary Syndrome (HCPS):**
- Hantavirus (New World: *Sin Nombre, Andes* viruses)
- **Rodent reservoir** (deer mice in Americas)
- Fever → **ARDS** → shock; **50% mortality**

**3. Crimean-Congo Hemorrhagic Fever (CCHF):**
- **Tick-borne** (*Hyalomma* ticks)
- Sudden fever, headache → **hemorrhagic manifestations**
- **Up to 30% mortality**

**4. Rift Valley Fever (RVF):**
- **Mosquito-borne** (livestock amplifying hosts)
- Hepatitis, **encephalitis**, **retinitis** (blindness)
- Up to 60% mortality in severe outbreaks

**5. La Crosse Encephalitis:**
- **Mosquito-borne** (*Aedes triseriatus*)
- **Pediatric encephalitis** with seizures
- Most common cause of pediatric arboviral encephalitis in US

**Pearl:** Bunyaviruses have distinct target organs: **kidneys (HFRS), lungs (HCPS), brain (La Crosse), liver/eyes (RVF), vascular endothelium (CCHF)**. HCPS has 50% mortality; CCHF up to 30%.

**Mnemonic - "Hanta-RENAL and Hanta-LUNG"**: HFRS (renal 15%), HCPS (pulmonary 50%)
