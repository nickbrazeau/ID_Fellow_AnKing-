## Arboviruses (Dengue, Zika, Chikungunya, West Nile)

**Q:** What are the key clinical features, diagnosis, and management of major arboviral infections (dengue, Zika, chikungunya, West Nile virus)?

**A:**

## OVERVIEW

**Transmission:** **Arthropod vectors** (mosquitoes, ticks)

**Key Arboviruses:**
- **Dengue** (Aedes mosquito)
- **Zika** (Aedes mosquito)
- **Chikungunya** (Aedes mosquito)
- **West Nile virus** (Culex mosquito)

---

## 1. DENGUE

**Vector:** *Aedes aegypti*, *A. albopictus*

**Geographic:** Tropical/subtropical (Southeast Asia, Latin America, Africa)

**Clinical Features:**
- **Fever, severe headache** ("breakbone fever")
- **Retro-orbital pain**
- **Myalgias, arthralgias**
- **Rash** (maculopapular, after fever defervescence)

**Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever/Dengue Shock Syndrome:**
- **Thrombocytopenia, hemorrhage**
- **Plasma leakage** (pleural effusion, ascites)
- **Shock**

**Diagnosis:** NS1 antigen (early), IgM/IgG serology, PCR

**Treatment:** **Supportive** (IV fluids, avoid NSAIDs/aspirin)

**Key Point:** **Avoid NSAIDs** (increase bleeding risk)

---

## 2. ZIKA

**Vector:** *Aedes aegypti*

**Geographic:** Americas, Africa, Asia

**Clinical Features:**
- **Mild:** Fever, rash, conjunctivitis, arthralgias (20% symptomatic)
- **Congenital Zika syndrome:**
  - **Microcephaly**
  - **Brain abnormalities** (calcifications)

**Complications:**
- **Guillain-Barr√© syndrome** (adults)

**Diagnosis:** Zika PCR (serum, urine), IgM serology

**Treatment:** Supportive

**Prevention:** **Avoid pregnancy** in endemic areas (congenital defects)

**Key Point:** **Microcephaly** = congenital Zika syndrome

---

## 3. CHIKUNGUNYA

**Vector:** *Aedes aegypti*, *A. albopictus*

**Geographic:** Africa, Asia, Americas

**Clinical Features:**
- **Fever, severe polyarthralgias** (bilateral, symmetric)
- **Rash** (maculopapular)
- **Chronic arthritis** (months to years - 30-60%)

**Diagnosis:** PCR, IgM serology

**Treatment:** **Supportive** (NSAIDs for arthralgias)

**Key Point:** **Severe, prolonged arthralgias** = chikungunya

---

## 4. WEST NILE VIRUS

**Vector:** *Culex* mosquitoes

**Geographic:** North America, Europe, Middle East

**Clinical Features:**
- **Asymptomatic** (80%)
- **West Nile fever:** Fever, headache, myalgias
- **Neuroinvasive disease** (<1%):
  - **Meningitis, encephalitis**
  - **Acute flaccid paralysis** (polio-like)

**Risk Factors for Severe Disease:** Age >60, immunocompromised

**Diagnosis:** **West Nile IgM (CSF or serum)**

**Treatment:** Supportive

**Key Point:** **Acute flaccid paralysis** = West Nile neuroinvasive disease

---

## COMPARISON TABLE

| **Virus** | **Vector** | **Key Clinical Feature** | **Diagnosis** |
|-----------|------------|--------------------------|---------------|
| **Dengue** | *Aedes* | **Hemorrhagic fever** (severe thrombocytopenia) | NS1 antigen, IgM |
| **Zika** | *Aedes* | **Microcephaly** (congenital) | PCR, IgM |
| **Chikungunya** | *Aedes* | **Severe, prolonged arthralgias** | PCR, IgM |
| **West Nile** | *Culex* | **Acute flaccid paralysis** (neuroinvasive) | **CSF/serum IgM** |

---

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **Dengue:** Avoid NSAIDs/aspirin (bleeding risk)
- **Zika:** Avoid pregnancy in endemic areas (microcephaly risk)
- **Chikungunya:** Severe, prolonged arthralgias (NSAIDs for symptom relief)
- **West Nile:** Acute flaccid paralysis (neuroinvasive disease)
