## Question

What is the classic presentation and treatment of sporotrichosis? Use the **"Rose Thorn, Ascending Lymphangitis (Sporotrichoid Spread) → Itraconazole × 3-6 Months"** framework.

## Answer

### ***Sporothrix schenckii* Overview:**

**Geography:** Worldwide (especially tropical/subtropical)

**Transmission:**
- **Traumatic inoculation** (thorns, splinters, moss, hay, soil)
- **Classic:** Rose thorn prick (gardeners, florists, farmers)

### **Clinical Syndromes:**

**Lymphocutaneous Sporotrichosis (Most Common, 75%):**

**Presentation:**
- **Primary lesion:** Papule/nodule at inoculation site (finger, hand, arm) → ulcerates
- **Ascending lymphangitis:** Chain of subcutaneous nodules along lymphatic drainage (hallmark)
- **"Sporotrichoid spread":** Pattern of ascending nodules along lymphatics
- **Painless** (usually)
- **NO systemic symptoms** (fever, constitutional symptoms rare)

**Differential Diagnosis of Sporotrichoid Spread:**
- ***S. schenckii*** (most common)
- ***Mycobacterium marinum*** (fish tank granuloma)
- ***Nocardia brasiliensis***
- ***Leishmania braziliensis***

**Fixed Cutaneous Sporotrichosis:**
- **Single lesion** (no lymphatic spread)
- **Papule, nodule, plaque, or verrucous lesion**

**Pulmonary Sporotrichosis:**
- **Chronic pneumonia** (alcoholics, COPD)
- **Cavitary disease** (mimics TB)
- **Rare**

**Disseminated Sporotrichosis:**
- **Immunocompromised** (HIV, alcoholism)
- **Multiple skin lesions, bone/joint involvement, meningitis** (rare)

**Osteoarticular Sporotrichosis:**
- **Monoarthritis** (knee, wrist, ankle, elbow)
- **Osteomyelitis** (often from direct inoculation)

### **Diagnosis:**

**Culture (Gold Standard):**
- **Skin biopsy, pus, synovial fluid**
- **Grows in 3-5 days** (25-30°C = mold, 35-37°C = yeast)

**Histopathology:**
- **Granulomatous inflammation**
- **Organisms often difficult to see** (small, sparse)
- **Asteroid bodies** (yeast surrounded by eosinophilic material, Splendore-Hoeppli phenomenon) - rare, but pathognomonic

**Serology/PCR:**
- **Not routinely available**

### **Treatment:**

**Lymphocutaneous / Fixed Cutaneous:**
- **Itraconazole 200mg PO BID × 3-6 months** (continue ≥2-4 weeks after lesions resolve)
- **Alternative:** Terbinafine 250mg PO BID × 3-6 months (effective, less expensive)

**Osteoarticular:**
- **Itraconazole 200mg PO BID × 12 months**

**Pulmonary:**
- **Itraconazole 200mg PO BID × 12 months**
- **Severe:** Liposomal amphotericin B (initial) → itraconazole

**Disseminated / Meningitis:**
- **Liposomal amphotericin B 3-5 mg/kg IV daily** (until clinically improved)
- **Then:** **Itraconazole 200mg PO BID × ≥12 months**

**Pregnancy:**
- **Liposomal amphotericin B** (azoles contraindicated)

**Itraconazole Monitoring:**
- **Check levels** (trough 1-2 mcg/mL)

## Key Points

### **Sporotrichoid Spread:**
- **Ascending chain of nodules** along lymphatics
- **Hallmark** of lymphocutaneous sporotrichosis
- **Differential:** *M. marinum*, *Nocardia*, *Leishmania*

### **Rose Thorn:**
- **Classic exposure** (rose gardener)
- **Traumatic inoculation** from thorns, splinters, moss, hay

### **Itraconazole = First-Line:**
- **200mg PO BID × 3-6 months** (lymphocutaneous)
- **Alternative:** Terbinafine (effective, less expensive)

### **Painless Ascending Nodules:**
- **Usually painless** (helps distinguish from bacterial lymphangitis)
- **Subacute progression** (weeks to months)

### **Asteroid Bodies:**
- **Rare, but pathognomonic**
- **Yeast with eosinophilic "star-burst"** (Splendore-Hoeppli phenomenon)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Rose thorn prick** → painless nodule (hand/arm) → **ascending chain of nodules** along lymphatics
- **"Sporotrichoid spread"** = ascending lymphangitis pattern
- **Itraconazole 200mg BID × 3-6 months** (lymphocutaneous)
- **Differential:** *M. marinum* (fish tank), *Nocardia*, *Leishmania*
- **Dimorphic fungus** (mold at 25°C, yeast at 37°C)
