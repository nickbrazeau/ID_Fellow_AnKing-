## Question

How is diphtheria diagnosed and managed? Use the **"Pseudomembrane → Antitoxin + PCN/Erythro, Prevent with Vaccine"** framework.

## Answer

### ***Corynebacterium diphtheriae* (Diphtheria) Overview:**

**Transmission:** Respiratory droplets, direct contact with skin lesions

**Epidemiology:**
- **Rare in US** (due to vaccination)
- **Endemic** in developing countries (Asia, Africa, Eastern Europe)
- **Toxin-mediated disease** (requires toxigenic strains carrying *tox* gene from bacteriophage)

### **Clinical Presentation:**

**Respiratory Diphtheria:**

**Pharyngeal/Tonsillar:**
- **Pseudomembrane** (thick, gray, adherent membrane on tonsils/pharynx/uvula) - HALLMARK
- **Attempts to remove → bleeding**
- **"Bull neck":** Marked cervical lymphadenopathy, edema
- **Fever, sore throat, dysphagia**
- **Stridor** (laryngeal involvement → airway obstruction)

**Laryngeal/Tracheobronchial:**
- **Stridor, hoarseness, croupy cough**
- **Can cause complete airway obstruction**

**Cutaneous Diphtheria:**
- **Non-healing ulcers** with gray membrane (tropical countries)
- **Less likely to cause systemic toxicity** (but can occur)

### **Complications (Toxin-Mediated):**

**Myocarditis:**
- **Most common cause of death** (10-25% of respiratory diphtheria)
- **Timing:** 1-2 weeks after onset
- **Manifestations:** Arrhythmias (heart block, ventricular arrhythmias), heart failure
- **ECG monitoring essential**

**Neuropathy:**
- **Cranial nerve palsies:** Palatal paralysis (nasal voice, regurgitation), diplopia, facial weakness
- **Peripheral neuropathy:** Ascending paralysis (Guillain-Barré-like) - weeks to months after infection
- **Usually reversible**

**Airway Obstruction:**
- **Pseudomembrane detachment → aspiration/obstruction**

### **Diagnosis:**

**Clinical:** **Suspect with pseudomembrane** + appropriate exposure history

**Laboratory:**
- **Culture:** Loeffler medium or tellurite agar (cystine-tellurite blood agar)
- **Gram stain:** Gram-positive rods, **"Chinese characters"** arrangement
- **Toxin detection:** Elek test or PCR for *tox* gene (confirms toxigenic strain)

**DO NOT delay treatment** for culture confirmation (high mortality without antitoxin)

### **Treatment:**

**Diphtheria Antitoxin (DAT):**
- **MUST give immediately** if diphtheria suspected (before culture confirmation)
- **Neutralizes circulating toxin** (does NOT reverse tissue-bound toxin)
- **Dose:** 20,000-120,000 units IV (based on site/severity)
- **Obtain from CDC** (no longer commercially available in US)
- **Check for horse serum allergy** before administration

**Antibiotics:**
- **Penicillin G 100,000-150,000 units/kg/day IV divided q6h × 14 days**
- OR **erythromycin 500mg IV/PO QID × 14 days**
- **Eliminates organism, prevents transmission** (does NOT treat toxin effects)

**Supportive Care:**
- **Airway management:** Intubation/tracheostomy if laryngeal involvement
- **Cardiac monitoring:** Telemetry for myocarditis (can develop complete heart block)
- **Isolation:** Droplet precautions until 2 negative cultures (24h apart after antibiotics complete)

**Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (Close Contacts):**
- **Antibiotics:** Erythromycin 500mg PO QID × 7 days OR penicillin G benzathine 1.2 million units IM × 1
- **Vaccination:** Booster dose (if >5 years since last dose)
- **Monitor for symptoms** × 7 days

### **Prevention:**

**Vaccination:**
- **DTaP (children <7 years):** 2, 4, 6, 15-18 months, 4-6 years
- **Tdap (adolescents/adults):** 1 dose at 11-12 years, then Td boosters every 10 years
- **Vaccine contains diphtheria toxoid** (inactivated toxin)

## Key Points

### **Pseudomembrane:**
- **Thick, gray, adherent** membrane on tonsils/pharynx
- **Bleeds when removed** (vs. strep throat exudate, which wipes off easily)
- **Pathognomonic** for diphtheria

### **Antitoxin Critical:**
- **MUST give immediately** if diphtheria suspected (don't wait for culture)
- **Only neutralizes circulating toxin** (tissue-bound toxin → irreversible damage)
- **Available from CDC** (no longer commercial)

### **Toxin-Mediated Complications:**
- **Myocarditis:** Most common cause of death (arrhythmias, heart block)
- **Neuropathy:** Cranial nerve palsies (palatal, diplopia), ascending paralysis (weeks-months)
- **Airway obstruction:** Pseudomembrane detachment

### **Antibiotics = Secondary:**
- **Penicillin or erythromycin × 14 days**
- **Eliminates organism** (prevents transmission)
- **Does NOT treat toxin** (antitoxin required)

### **"Bull Neck":**
- **Marked cervical lymphadenopathy + edema**
- **Indicates severe disease**

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Pseudomembrane + bull neck** = diphtheria until proven otherwise
- **Antitoxin FIRST** (don't wait for culture), then antibiotics
- **Myocarditis** = most common cause of death (ECG monitoring essential)
- **Vaccination:** DTaP (kids), Tdap (adolescents/adults), Td boosters q10y
- **Rare in US** (high vaccination coverage), but consider in immigrants/travelers
