## Vibrio parahaemolyticus: Gastroenteritis

**Q:** What is the clinical presentation and treatment of Vibrio parahaemolyticus gastroenteritis?

**A:**

**Microbiology:**
- **Gram-negative, curved rod** (halophilic)
- Natural habitat: Marine/estuarine waters
- Thermostable direct hemolysin (TDH) and TDH-related hemolysin (TRH) - virulence factors

**Epidemiology:**
- **Most common Vibrio species causing gastroenteritis** in the US
- Warm coastal waters (summer months)
- Associated with raw/undercooked shellfish (especially oysters, shrimp)

---

**Clinical Presentation:**

**Incubation:** 4-96 hours (typically 12-24 hours)

**Acute Gastroenteritis (Most Common):**
- **Watery diarrhea** (may be bloody in 25%)
- **Abdominal cramps, nausea, vomiting**
- **Fever** (50-70%)
- **Headache**
- Self-limited: 2-5 days (usually <7 days)

**Mild-Moderate Illness:**
- Most cases are mild
- Less severe than cholera or V. vulnificus

**Rare Complications (Immunocompromised):**
- Wound infections (if seawater exposure)
- Septicemia (rare, but seen in cirrhosis/immunosuppression)
- Mortality low (<1%) except in high-risk patients

---

**Diagnosis:**

**Clinical:**
- Acute gastroenteritis after seafood consumption (especially oysters)
- Summer months, coastal area

**Laboratory:**
- **Stool culture:** TCBS agar (green colonies - sucrose non-fermenter)
- **Serotyping:** Many serotypes exist

**Not Routinely Needed:**
- Most cases diagnosed clinically
- Culture if severe, bloody diarrhea, or immunocompromised

---

**Treatment:**

**Most Cases (Mild Gastroenteritis):**

**1. Supportive Care ONLY:**
- **Oral rehydration** (ORS or sports drinks)
- Rest
- No antibiotics needed (self-limited)

**Antibiotics NOT Recommended for:**
- Uncomplicated gastroenteritis
- Immunocompetent patients
- Short duration (<3 days)

**2. Antibiotics (Severe Cases Only):**

**Indications:**
- Severe or prolonged diarrhea (>3 days)
- Bloody diarrhea
- Immunocompromised (liver disease, HIV, etc.)
- Systemic symptoms (sepsis)

**Antibiotic Options:**

| **Antibiotic** | **Dosing** | **Duration** |
|----------------|------------|--------------|
| **Doxycycline** (preferred) | 100 mg PO BID | 3 days |
| **Fluoroquinolones** | Ciprofloxacin 500 mg PO BID | 3 days |
| **Tetracycline** | 500 mg PO QID | 3 days |

**Resistance:**
- Tetracyclines and fluoroquinolones remain highly effective
- Increasing ampicillin resistance (avoid)

**3. Wound Infections:**
- If seawater exposure + wound → treat as V. vulnificus (doxycycline + ceftriaxone)

---

**Prevention:**

**Food Safety:**
- **Avoid raw or undercooked shellfish** (oysters, clams)
- Cook seafood thoroughly:
  - Internal temperature ≥145°F (63°C) for 15 seconds
  - Oysters: Boil 3-5 minutes after shells open
- Refrigerate seafood promptly

**High-Risk Patients:**
- Liver disease, immunosuppressed → avoid ALL raw shellfish

**Seawater Exposure:**
- Avoid warm seawater if open wounds
- Rinse wounds immediately after ocean exposure

---

**Comparison: V. parahaemolyticus vs V. vulnificus vs V. cholerae**

| **Feature** | **V. parahaemolyticus** | **V. vulnificus** | **V. cholerae** |
|-------------|-------------------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Source** | Raw shellfish | Raw oysters | Contaminated water |
| **Syndrome** | Gastroenteritis (mild) | Sepsis, necrotizing fasciitis | Secretory diarrhea |
| **Severity** | Mild, self-limited | **Lethal (>50% mortality)** | Severe dehydration |
| **Risk Factors** | Healthy hosts | **Liver disease** | Poor sanitation |
| **Treatment** | Supportive (no abx) | **Urgent debridement + abx** | ORS ± antibiotics |
| **Fever** | ✅ Common | ✅ Common | ❌ Afebrile |
| **TCBS Agar** | Green (sucrose -) | Green (sucrose -) | Yellow (sucrose +) |

---

**Mnemonic: "V. para = Mild Gastro, No Panic"**
- **P**arahaemolyticus = **P**retty mild
- **A**cute gastroenteritis
- **R**aw seafood (oysters)
- **A**ntibiotics rarely needed

**Contrast:**
- V. **v**ulnificus = **V**ery lethal (liver disease, oysters → sepsis)
- V. **c**holerae = **C**opious diarrhea (cholera, rice-water)

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **V. parahaemolyticus = most common Vibrio gastroenteritis in US** (but least severe)
- Self-limited in most cases → supportive care only
- Antibiotics NOT routinely needed (unlike cholera or V. vulnificus)
- If bloody diarrhea → consider antibiotics (doxycycline 3 days)
- Immunocompromised with V. parahaemolyticus → treat like V. vulnificus (can cause sepsis)
- **Tetracyclines/fluoroquinolones remain effective** (first-line if antibiotics needed)
