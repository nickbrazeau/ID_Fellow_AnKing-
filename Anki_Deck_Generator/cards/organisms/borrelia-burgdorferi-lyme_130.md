## Question

How is Lyme disease diagnosed and treated by stage? Use the **"Doxycycline for Early, IV Ceftriaxone for Neuro/Cardiac"** framework.

## Answer

### **Lyme Disease Stages:**

| Stage | Timing | Clinical Features | Treatment |
|-------|--------|------------------|-----------|
| **Early Localized** | **3-30 days** post-tick bite | ***Erythema migrans* (EM)** (bull's-eye rash), fever, fatigue, headache, arthralgia | **Doxycycline 100mg PO BID × 10-21 days** (preferred) |
| **Early Disseminated** | **Weeks-months** | **Multiple EM lesions**, **facial palsy** (bilateral common), **carditis** (AV block), **meningitis**, arthralgia | **Doxycycline** (if no neuro/cardiac) OR **IV ceftriaxone** (if neuro/cardiac) |
| **Late** | **Months-years** | **Lyme arthritis** (knee most common, oligoarticular), **chronic encephalopathy**, **polyneuropathy** | **Doxycycline 100mg BID × 28 days** (arthritis) OR **IV ceftriaxone** (neurologic) |

### **Treatment by Manifestation:**

**Early Localized (Erythema Migrans):**
- **Doxycycline 100mg PO BID** × 10-21 days (preferred, most data for 10 days)
- **Alternatives:**
  - **Amoxicillin 500mg PO TID** × 14-21 days (pregnancy, age <8)
  - **Cefuroxime 500mg PO BID** × 14-21 days
  - **Azithromycin 500mg daily** × 7-10 days (less effective, only if β-lactam/doxy contraindicated)

**Facial Palsy (Isolated, No Other Neurologic Findings):**
- **Doxycycline 100mg PO BID** × 14-21 days
- **Consider LP** if other neurologic symptoms present

**Meningitis/Encephalitis:**
- **Ceftriaxone 2g IV daily** × 14-28 days
- **Alternatives:** Cefotaxime 2g IV q8h, penicillin G 18-24 MU IV daily

**Carditis (AV Block):**
- **1st degree (PR >200ms):** **Doxycycline 100mg PO BID** × 14-21 days
- **2nd/3rd degree (high-grade AV block):** **Ceftriaxone 2g IV daily** × 14-21 days (+ telemetry, consider temporary pacemaker)

**Lyme Arthritis:**
- **Doxycycline 100mg PO BID** × 28 days OR **amoxicillin 500mg PO TID** × 28 days
- **If persistent:** Repeat 28-day course
- **If persistent after 2 courses:** Consider arthroscopic synovectomy (PCR-negative synovium = post-infectious inflammatory arthritis, not active infection)

## Key Points

### **Erythema Migrans (EM):**
- **"Bull's-eye" rash:** Expanding red rash with central clearing (classic, but only 20-30%)
- **More commonly:** Uniformly erythematous, homogeneous
- **Size:** ≥5 cm diameter (by definition)
- **Location:** Site of tick bite (thigh, groin, axilla common)
- **Diagnosis:** **Clinical** (do NOT need serology if EM present + endemic area + tick exposure)

### **Lyme Serology:**

**Two-Tier Testing:**
1. **EIA/IFA (enzyme immunoassay)** - screening
2. **If positive/equivocal → Western blot** (IgM and IgG) - confirmatory

**Interpretation:**
- **Early EM (<30 days):** Often **seronegative** (antibodies not yet developed) → **treat based on clinical diagnosis**
- **Early disseminated/late:** Serology usually positive
- **IgM without IgG after 4-6 weeks:** **Likely false positive** (IgG should appear if true infection)
- **Serology remains positive for years** (cannot use to assess treatment response or reinfection)

**When to Test:**
- **Do NOT test** if EM present in endemic area (clinical diagnosis)
- **Test** if: Atypical presentation, non-endemic area, later manifestations (arthritis, neurologic, cardiac)

### **Post-Treatment Lyme Disease Syndrome (PTLDS):**
- **Persistent symptoms** (fatigue, arthralgia, cognitive difficulties) **after appropriate treatment**
- **Pathophysiology:** Unclear (likely post-infectious, immune-mediated, NOT active infection)
- **Serology:** Remains positive (does NOT indicate treatment failure)
- **Management:** Supportive care, **do NOT retreat with prolonged antibiotics** (no benefit, risk of harm)

### **Prophylaxis:**

**Single-Dose Doxycycline (Post-Tick Bite):**
- **Indication:** *Ixodes* tick attached **≥36 hours** (or engorged) + **endemic area** + **tick removed within 72 hours**
- **Regimen:** **Doxycycline 200mg PO × 1 dose**
- **Efficacy:** ~85% reduction in Lyme disease risk
- **Not routinely recommended** (low absolute risk, most Lyme easily treated)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **EM rash:** Doxycycline 100mg BID × 10-21 days (clinical diagnosis, no serology needed)
- **Facial palsy (isolated):** Doxycycline PO × 14-21 days
- **Meningitis/carditis:** IV ceftriaxone × 14-28 days
- **Lyme arthritis:** Doxycycline PO × 28 days
- **Early EM often seronegative** (treat clinically, don't wait for serology)
- **Post-treatment symptoms:** Do NOT retreat with prolonged antibiotics
- **Prophylaxis:** Doxycycline 200mg × 1 (only if *Ixodes* attached ≥36h in endemic area)
