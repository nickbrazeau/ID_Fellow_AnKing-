## Arenaviruses (Lassa, Junin, Machupo, LCMV)

**Q:** What are the major arenavirus diseases, their geographic distributions, and mortality rates?

**A:**

**Overview:**
- **Reservoir**: Rodents (urine, feces, saliva)
- **Transmission**: Inhalation of aerosolized excreta or direct contact
- Two groups: **Old World** (Africa) and **New World** (Americas)

**Major Arenaviral Diseases:**

| Virus | Disease | Geography | Mortality | Key Features |
|-------|---------|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| **Lassa virus** | **Lassa fever** | **West Africa** (Nigeria, Sierra Leone, Liberia, Guinea) | **15-50%** hospitalized patients | Most common hemorrhagic fever in West Africa; endemic; chronic infection of *Mastomys natalensis* (rat) |
| **Junin virus** | **Argentine hemorrhagic fever (AHF)** | **Argentina** (pampas) | **15-30%** untreated; **<1%** with immune plasma | Seasonal (harvest season); vaccine available |
| **Machupo virus** | **Bolivian hemorrhagic fever (BHF)** | **Bolivia** | **25-30%** | 1963-1964 outbreak: 637 cases |
| **Guanarito virus** | **Venezuelan hemorrhagic fever (VHF)** | **Venezuela** | **~15%** | Less common |
| **Lymphocytic choriomeningitis virus (LCMV)** | **LCMV infection** | **Worldwide** | **Rarely fatal** | *Mus musculus* (house mouse) reservoir; **70% asymptomatic/mild**; meningitis/encephalitis in severe cases; **teratogenic** (congenital infection) |

**Clinical Syndromes:**

**Viral Hemorrhagic Fever (Lassa, Junin, Machupo, Guanarito):**
- Fever, myalgia, hemorrhagic manifestations
- Multi-organ failure (capillary leak, shock)
- **Lassa**: Most common; endemic in West Africa

**LCMV (Unique Presentation):**
- **70% asymptomatic** or mild (flu-like/GI illness)
- **CNS disease**: Aseptic meningitis, encephalitis (rarely fatal, usually complete recovery)
- **Congenital LCMV**: **Hydrocephalus, chorioretinitis**, neurologic deficits (pet rodent exposure during pregnancy)

**Treatment:**

**Ribavirin:**
- Effective for **Lassa fever** (reduces mortality)
- May benefit other arenaviral hemorrhagic fevers
- **IV ribavirin** for severe disease

**Immune Plasma:**
- **Argentine hemorrhagic fever**: Reduces mortality to <1% (from 15-30%)

**Key Points:**
- **Rodent reservoir** (species-specific)
- **Old World**: Lassa (West Africa, 15-50% mortality)
- **New World**: Junin/Machupo/Guanarito (South America, 15-30% mortality)
- **LCMV**: Worldwide, usually mild, **teratogenic**
- Ribavirin effective for Lassa; immune plasma for Junin

**Mnemonic - "Lassa Lacks America"**: Lassa (Old World/Africa); Junin/Machupo/Guanarito (New World/Americas)
