## Coxsackievirus, Echovirus, and Numbered Enteroviruses

**Q:** What are the major enterovirus syndromes and which viruses cause them?

**A:**

**Overview - Non-Polio Enteroviruses:**
- **Picornaviridae family**
- **Groups**: Coxsackievirus A (23 serotypes), Coxsackievirus B (6 serotypes), Echoviruses (29 serotypes), Numbered enteroviruses (EV-D68, EV-A71, etc.)
- **Transmission**: Fecal-oral, respiratory droplets
- **Seasonality**: **Summer/fall** peak

**Major Clinical Syndromes:**

| Syndrome | Primary Viruses | Clinical Features |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Hand-Foot-Mouth Disease (HFMD)** | **Coxsackie A16** (most common), **EV-A71** | **Vesicles** on palms, soles, oral mucosa; fever; usually mild; EV-A71 can cause **severe CNS disease** (encephalitis, AFM) |
| **Herpangina** | Coxsackie A viruses, **Coxsackie A2** (re-emerging), Coxsackie B, EV-A71 | **Vesicles/ulcers** on posterior oropharynx (soft palate, uvula); fever, sore throat; self-limited |
| **Viral myocarditis/pericarditis** | **Coxsackie B** (most common), Coxsackie A, Echoviruses | Chest pain, heart failure, arrhythmias; can be fatal or cause dilated cardiomyopathy |
| **Aseptic meningitis** | **Coxsackie B**, Echoviruses, EV-A71 | Fever, headache, nuchal rigidity; CSF pleocytosis (lymphocytic); usually benign, self-limited |
| **Acute Flaccid Myelitis (AFM)** | **Enterovirus D68 (EV-D68)**, EV-A71 | **Asymmetric flaccid paralysis** (polio-like); limb weakness; respiratory illness prodrome; associated with EV-D68 outbreaks |
| **Pleurodynia (Bornholm disease)** | **Coxsackie B** | **Sudden pleuritic chest/abdominal pain** (intercostal muscle inflammation); fever; self-limited |

**Enterovirus D68 (EV-D68):**
- Causes **respiratory illness** in children (cold-like, wheezing, asthma exacerbation)
- Associated with **Acute Flaccid Myelitis (AFM)** outbreaks (polio-like paralysis)
- **Sporadic outbreaks** in US (2014, 2018, 2020s)

**Enterovirus A71 (EV-A71):**
- Causes HFMD (like Cox A16) but **more severe**
- Complications: **Encephalitis, AFM**, cardiopulmonary collapse
- Endemic in Asia-Pacific region

**Key Points:**
- **Coxsackie A**: HFMD, herpangina
- **Coxsackie B**: Myocarditis, pleurodynia, aseptic meningitis
- **EV-D68**: Respiratory illness + **AFM** (polio-like paralysis)
- **EV-A71**: Severe HFMD with CNS complications

**Mnemonic - "Cox-A Mouth, Cox-B Heart"**: Coxsackie A (HFMD/herpangina oral lesions), Coxsackie B (myocarditis)

**Pearl:** Coxsackie B is the most common cause of viral myocarditis. EV-D68 causes outbreaks of acute flaccid myelitis (AFM), a polio-like paralysis. EV-A71 HFMD can cause severe CNS complications (encephalitis, AFM).
