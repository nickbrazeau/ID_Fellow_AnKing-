## Question

What infections does *M. catarrhalis* cause? Use the **"Otitis Media (Kids), COPD Exacerbations (Adults), >90% β-Lactamase+"** framework.

## Answer

### ***Moraxella catarrhalis* Overview:**

**Normal Flora:** Nasopharynx (commensal)

**Clinical Infections:**
- **Otitis media** (children) - 3rd most common after *S. pneumoniae*, *H. influenzae*
- **Sinusitis** (children and adults)
- **COPD exacerbations** (adults) - 10-15% of bacterial exacerbations
- **Pneumonia** (rare, elderly with lung disease)

### **Treatment:**

**Key:** **>90% produce β-lactamase** (resistant to ampicillin/amoxicillin)

**Effective Agents:**
- **Amoxicillin-clavulanate** 875/125mg PO BID
- **Cephalosporins:** Cefuroxime, cefdinir, cefpodoxime
- **Macrolides:** Azithromycin, clarithromycin
- **Fluoroquinolones:** Levofloxacin, moxifloxacin
- **TMP-SMX** DS BID

**Avoid:** Ampicillin, amoxicillin (β-lactamase resistance)

### **Otitis Media:**
- Usually part of **polymicrobial infection** (*S. pneumoniae*, *H. influenzae*, *M. catarrhalis*)
- **Treatment:** Amoxicillin-clavulanate (covers β-lactamase producers)

### **COPD Exacerbations:**
- **Common bacterial causes:** *H. influenzae*, *S. pneumoniae*, *M. catarrhalis*
- **Treatment:** Amoxicillin-clavulanate, doxycycline, or fluoroquinolone × 5-7 days

## Key Points

### **Resistance:**
- **>90% β-lactamase positive** (BRO-1, BRO-2 enzymes)
- **Intrinsically resistant:** Ampicillin, amoxicillin

### **Microbiology:**
- **Gram-negative diplococci** (resembles *Neisseria*)
- **Oxidase positive** (differentiates from *Acinetobacter*)
- **Distinguishing from *Neisseria*:** Does NOT ferment glucose/maltose

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **>90% β-lactamase positive** → avoid ampicillin/amoxicillin
- **Otitis media + sinusitis** (kids), **COPD exacerbations** (adults)
- **Amoxicillin-clavulanate** first-line for otitis/sinusitis
- **Rarely causes invasive disease** (usually mucosal infections)
