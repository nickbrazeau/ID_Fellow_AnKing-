## Malaria: Diagnosis & Species Identification

**Q:** What are the diagnostic methods, species characteristics, and clinical features of the five human Plasmodium species causing malaria?

**A:**

## EPIDEMIOLOGY

**Global Burden:**
- **247 million cases/year** (2021 WHO)
- **619,000 deaths/year** (mostly children <5 years in sub-Saharan Africa)
- **Endemic regions:** Sub-Saharan Africa (95% of cases), South Asia, Southeast Asia, Latin America

**US Cases:**
- **~2,000 imported cases/year**
- **Rare autochthonous transmission** (mosquito-borne within US)

**Key Point:** **Sub-Saharan Africa = 95% of global malaria cases**

---

## FIVE HUMAN PLASMODIUM SPECIES

| **Species** | **Frequency** | **Geographic Distribution** | **Severity** | **Key Features** |
|-------------|---------------|----------------------------|--------------|------------------|
| ***P. falciparum*** | **#1 (75%)** | **Sub-Saharan Africa**, Southeast Asia | **Most severe** (causes cerebral malaria, death) | High parasitemia (>5%), cytoadherence, sequestration |
| ***P. vivax*** | **#2 (20%)** | Latin America, Asia, Middle East, Horn of Africa | **Relapsing** (hypnozoites in liver) | Duffy antigen required (protective in West Africa) |
| ***P. ovale*** | **<5%** | West Africa | **Relapsing** (hypnozoites) | Two subspecies (*P. o. curtisi*, *P. o. wallikeri*) |
| ***P. malariae*** | **<5%** | Sub-Saharan Africa, Southeast Asia | **Chronic** (can persist decades) | Quartan fever (72h cycle), nephrotic syndrome |
| ***P. knowlesi*** | **<1%** | Southeast Asia (Malaysia, Thailand) | **Severe** (can mimic falciparum) | Zoonotic (monkey malaria), 24h cycle (quotidian fever) |

**Key Point:** ***P. falciparum* = #1 (75%), most severe, causes cerebral malaria**

---

## CLINICAL PRESENTATION

**Classic Features:**
- **Fever** (paroxysms - cyclic)
- **Chills, rigors**
- **Sweating** (after fever breaks)
- **Headache**
- **Myalgias**
- **Nausea, vomiting**
- **Splenomegaly** (if chronic/repeated infection)

**Fever Patterns (Classic, But Often Absent):**
- ***P. falciparum*:** Irregular (no specific pattern)
- ***P. vivax*, *P. ovale*:** Tertian fever (48h cycle - fever every other day)
- ***P. malariae*:** Quartan fever (72h cycle - fever every 3rd day)
- ***P. knowlesi*:** Quotidian fever (24h cycle - fever daily)

**Key Point:** **Fever patterns often NOT reliable** (irregular in many cases)

---

## DIAGNOSIS

**Key Principle:** **Thick + thin blood smears = gold standard**

---

### **1. Thick and Thin Blood Smears (Gold Standard):**

**Thick Smear:**
- **Purpose:** **Detection** (high sensitivity - concentrates parasites)
- **RBCs lysed** → Parasites concentrated
- **Sensitivity:** Can detect as low as 5-10 parasites/μL

**Thin Smear:**
- **Purpose:** **Species identification, parasitemia calculation**
- **RBCs intact** → Allows morphologic identification
- **Parasitemia (%):** (# infected RBCs / Total RBCs) × 100

**Key Point:** **Thick smear = detection**, **Thin smear = species ID + parasitemia**

---

**Morphologic Features for Species Identification:**

| **Species** | **Rings** | **Trophozoites** | **Schizonts** | **Gametocytes** | **Unique Features** |
|-------------|-----------|------------------|---------------|------------------|---------------------|
| ***P. falciparum*** | **Multiple rings per RBC** (2-5)<br>**Appliqué forms** (rings on RBC edge)<br>**Double chromatin dots** | **NOT seen** (sequestered in capillaries) | **NOT seen** (sequestered) | **Banana-shaped** (crescent) | **Ring forms only** on peripheral smear |
| ***P. vivax*** | Large rings | **Ameboid trophozoites** | 12-24 merozoites | Round | **Schüffner dots** (stippling)<br>**Enlarged RBCs** |
| ***P. ovale*** | Large rings | Ameboid | 8-12 merozoites | Round | **Schüffner dots**<br>**Oval/fimbriated RBCs** |
| ***P. malariae*** | Compact rings | **Band forms** (rectangular) | 6-12 merozoites (rosette) | Round | **Band forms**<br>Normal-sized RBCs |
| ***P. knowlesi*** | Multiple rings (mimics *P. falciparum*) | Band forms (mimics *P. malariae*) | 16 merozoites | Round | **Cannot distinguish from *P. malariae/vivax* by microscopy** (need PCR) |

**Key Point:** ***P. falciparum* = ring forms only** (trophozoites/schizonts sequestered in capillaries)

---

### **2. Rapid Diagnostic Tests (RDTs):**

**Antigens Detected:**
- **HRP2 (Histidine-Rich Protein 2):** ***P. falciparum* specific**
- **pLDH (Plasmodium Lactate Dehydrogenase):** Pan-Plasmodium OR species-specific
- **Aldolase:** Pan-Plasmodium

**Common RDT Formats:**
- **HRP2 + Pan-pLDH:** Detects *P. falciparum* + other species
- **HRP2 + pLDH (Pf + Pv):** Detects *P. falciparum* + *P. vivax*

**Advantages:**
- **Rapid** (15-20 minutes)
- **No microscopy expertise needed**
- **Point-of-care**

**Limitations:**
- **Cannot quantify parasitemia** (cannot assess severity)
- **HRP2 persists weeks after treatment** (false positive if recent infection)
- **HRP2-deleted *P. falciparum*** strains (emerging in Africa, South America)
- **Cannot reliably distinguish *P. knowlesi***

**Key Point:** **RDTs useful for rapid diagnosis, but CANNOT quantify parasitemia** (need smear for severity)

---

### **3. PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction):**

**Advantages:**
- **Most sensitive** (can detect <1 parasite/μL)
- **Species identification** (including *P. knowlesi*, mixed infections)

**Limitations:**
- **Not widely available** (reference labs)
- **Slow turnaround** (hours to days)
- **Expensive**

**Indication:**
- **Low-level parasitemia** (smear negative, high suspicion)
- ***P. knowlesi* confirmation** (cannot distinguish from *P. malariae* on smear)
- **Mixed infections**

---

### **4. When to Test for Malaria:**

**Indications:**
- **Fever + travel to endemic area** (within 1 year, especially <3 months)
- **Fever of unknown origin** with travel history
- **Sepsis with travel history**

**Key Point:** **Consider malaria in ANY febrile traveler from endemic area** (especially sub-Saharan Africa)

---

## PARASITEMIA & SEVERITY

**Parasitemia Calculation:**
- **Parasitemia (%) = (# Infected RBCs / Total RBCs counted) × 100** (on thin smear)

**Severity Classification:**

| **Parasitemia** | **Severity** |
|-----------------|--------------|
| **<2%** | **Uncomplicated** |
| **2-5%** | **Moderate** |
| **>5%** | **Severe malaria** (high risk of complications) |
| **>10%** | **Very severe** (mortality ↑↑) |

**Key Point:** **Parasitemia >5% = severe malaria** (high risk of cerebral malaria, death)

---

## SPECIES-SPECIFIC CLINICAL FEATURES

---

### ***Plasmodium falciparum***

**Epidemiology:**
- **#1 species (75%)**
- **Sub-Saharan Africa** (>95% of cases)

**Clinical Features:**
- **Most severe**
- **High parasitemia** (>5% common)
- **Cytoadherence** (infected RBCs stick to endothelium) → **Sequestration** → Organ ischemia

**Complications (Severe Malaria):**
- **Cerebral malaria** (altered mental status, seizures, coma)
- **Severe anemia** (Hgb <7 g/dL)
- **Acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS)**
- **Acute kidney injury**
- **Hypoglycemia** (quinine/quinidine side effect)
- **Lactic acidosis**
- **Hyperparasitemia** (>5%)

**Mortality:**
- **Uncomplicated:** <1% (with treatment)
- **Severe:** 10-20% (with treatment), **>50% without treatment**

**Key Point:** ***P. falciparum* = cerebral malaria, death** (most dangerous species)

---

### ***Plasmodium vivax***

**Epidemiology:**
- **#2 species (20%)**
- **Latin America, Asia, Middle East**
- **Duffy antigen required** for RBC invasion → Duffy-negative individuals (most West Africans) are **resistant**

**Clinical Features:**
- **Relapsing malaria** (hypnozoites in liver → reactivate weeks to years later)
- **Milder than falciparum** (but can still cause severe disease)
- **Lower parasitemia** (<2%)

**Complications:**
- **Severe anemia** (chronic/repeated relapses)
- **Splenic rupture** (rare)

**Key Point:** ***P. vivax* = relapsing** (hypnozoites) → **Need primaquine** for radical cure

---

### ***Plasmodium ovale***

**Epidemiology:**
- **<5%**
- **West Africa**

**Clinical Features:**
- **Relapsing malaria** (hypnozoites)
- **Milder than falciparum**

**Key Point:** ***P. ovale* = relapsing** → **Need primaquine** for radical cure

---

### ***Plasmodium malariae***

**Epidemiology:**
- **<5%**
- **Sub-Saharan Africa, Southeast Asia**

**Clinical Features:**
- **Quartan fever** (72h cycle)
- **Chronic, low-level parasitemia** (can persist for decades without treatment)
- **Nephrotic syndrome** (immune complex deposition)

**Key Point:** ***P. malariae* = chronic** (can persist decades) + nephrotic syndrome

---

### ***Plasmodium knowlesi***

**Epidemiology:**
- **<1%**
- **Southeast Asia** (Malaysia, Thailand, Philippines)
- **Zoonotic** (monkey malaria, *Macaca fascicularis*)

**Clinical Features:**
- **24-hour cycle** (quotidian fever - daily)
- **Can mimic *P. falciparum*** (high parasitemia, severe disease)
- **Rapidly increasing parasitemia** (can double every 24h)

**Diagnosis:**
- **Cannot distinguish from *P. malariae* on smear** (need PCR)

**Key Point:** ***P. knowlesi* = zoonotic, daily fever, severe** (mimic falciparum)

---

## MIXED INFECTIONS

**Frequency:**
- **5-10%** of infections (especially *P. falciparum* + *P. vivax*)

**Diagnosis:**
- **Microscopy** (may miss minor species)
- **PCR** (more sensitive for mixed infections)

---

**Mnemonic: "FVOM-K for 5 Species"**
- **F**alciparum (75%, most severe)
- **V**ivax (20%, relapsing)
- **O**vale (relapsing)
- **M**alariae (chronic)
- **K**nowlesi (zoonotic, Southeast Asia)

**Mnemonic: "Falciparum = Rings Only (Sequester Trophozoites/Schizonts)"**
- **F**alciparum
- **R**ings only on peripheral smear
- **S**equestration (trophozoites/schizonts in capillaries)

**Mnemonic: "Vivax/Ovale = Hypnozoites (Primaquine Needed)"**
- **V**ivax and **O**vale
- **H**ypnozoites (dormant liver stage)
- **P**rimaquine needed for radical cure (prevent relapses)

**Clinical Pearls:**
- ***P. falciparum* = #1 (75%), most severe, cerebral malaria, >5% parasitemia = severe**
- **Thick smear = detection (high sensitivity), Thin smear = species ID + parasitemia**
- ***P. falciparum* = rings only** on peripheral smear (trophozoites/schizonts sequestered)
- **Multiple rings per RBC** = *P. falciparum* or *P. knowlesi*
- **Banana-shaped gametocytes** = *P. falciparum*
- ***P. vivax* and *P. ovale* = relapsing** (hypnozoites) → Need primaquine for radical cure
- ***P. malariae* = chronic** (persist decades), quartan fever (72h), nephrotic syndrome
- ***P. knowlesi* = zoonotic, daily fever (24h), mimics falciparum severity**
- **RDTs rapid but CANNOT quantify parasitemia** (need smear for severity assessment)
- **HRP2 persists weeks after treatment** (false positive if recent infection)
- **Parasitemia >5% = severe malaria** (high risk of cerebral malaria, death)
- **Consider malaria in ANY febrile traveler from endemic area**
