## Miscellaneous Gram-Neg Bacilli & Endemic Treponematoses

**Q:** What are the clinical features of donovanosis, endemic treponematoses, leptospirosis, and relapsing fever?

**A:**

**Klebsiella granulomatis (Donovanosis/Granuloma Inguinale):**
- **STI**: Painless, progressive genital ulcers ("beefy red" granulomatous)
- **Diagnosis**: Donovan bodies (bipolar staining in macrophages)
- **Treatment**: Azithromycin 1 g PO weekly OR doxycycline 100 mg PO BID × ≥3 weeks (until lesions heal)

**Endemic Treponematoses (Non-Venereal):**
- **Yaws** (*T. pallidum pertenue*): Tropics; **framboesia** (raspberry-like skin lesions); bone involvement
- **Bejel** (endemic syphilis, *T. pallidum endemicum*): Arid regions; oral/skin lesions
- **Pinta** (*T. carateum*): Latin America; **dyschromic skin lesions** only (no systemic)
- **Treatment**: **Benzathine penicillin G 2.4 million units IM single dose** (or azithromycin 30 mg/kg PO × 1)

**Leptospira Species (Leptospirosis):**
- **Transmission**: Animal urine (rats, livestock) in water; occupational/recreational water exposure
- **Clinical**: Biphasic illness
  - **Phase 1**: Fever, headache, myalgias, conjunctival suffusion
  - **Phase 2**: **Weil disease** (severe): Jaundice, renal failure, pulmonary hemorrhage (10% mortality)
- **Diagnosis**: PCR, serology (MAT)
- **Treatment**: **Doxycycline 100 mg PO BID** (mild) OR **penicillin G/ceftriaxone IV** (severe) × 7 days
- **Prophylaxis**: Doxycycline 200 mg PO weekly (high-risk exposure)

**Relapsing Fever (Borrelia recurrentis/hermsii):**
- **Transmission**: Louse-borne (epidemic) or tick-borne (endemic)
- **Clinical**: **Relapsing fevers** (3-7 days fever, afebrile 7 days, recurs); Jarisch-Herxheimer reaction common
- **Diagnosis**: Blood smear (spirochetes visible)
- **Treatment**: **Doxycycline or erythromycin** × 7-10 days

**Key Points:**
- **Donovanosis**: "Beefy red" ulcers; azithromycin or doxycycline ≥3 weeks
- **Endemic treponematoses**: Single-dose benzathine penicillin G
- **Leptospirosis**: Weil disease = jaundice + renal failure + pulmonary hemorrhage; doxycycline prophylaxis available
- **Relapsing fever**: Visible spirochetes on smear; Jarisch-Herxheimer reaction

**Mnemonic - "Leptospirosis = Lepto in Water, Weil = Worsening (jaundice, renal, pulmonary)"**

**Pearl:** Leptospirosis Weil disease triad: jaundice, renal failure, pulmonary hemorrhage (10% mortality). Endemic treponematoses treated with single IM dose benzathine penicillin (same as syphilis). Relapsing fever spirochetes visible on blood smear.
