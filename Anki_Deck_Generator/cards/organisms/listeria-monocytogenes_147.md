## Question

Who gets *Listeria* and how is it treated? Use the **"Neonates, Pregnant, Elderly, Immunocompromised → Ampicillin + Gentamicin"** framework.

## Answer

### ***Listeria monocytogenes* Overview:**

**Transmission:**
- **Foodborne:** Unpasteurized dairy, deli meats, hot dogs, soft cheeses, smoked seafood
- **Intracellular pathogen** (crosses blood-brain barrier, placenta)

**High-Risk Groups:**
- **Pregnant women** (20× increased risk)
- **Neonates**
- **Elderly (>65 years)**
- **Immunocompromised:** Transplant, steroids, biologics, HIV, malignancy

### **Clinical Syndromes:**

**Meningitis/Meningoencephalitis:**
- **Most common presentation** in non-pregnant adults
- **High-risk groups:** Elderly, immunocompromised
- **Presentation:** Fever, headache, altered mental status, seizures
- **CSF:** Lymphocytic or neutrophilic pleocytosis, ↑protein, ↓glucose
- **Mortality:** 15-30%

**Bacteremia Without Meningitis:**
- **Febrile illness** without CNS involvement
- **Common in pregnancy** (can lead to fetal infection)

**Neonatal Listeriosis:**
- **Early-onset (<7 days):** Sepsis, pneumonia, granulomatosis infantiseptica (multisystem abscesses)
- **Late-onset (>7 days):** Meningitis
- **Vertical transmission:** Transplacental or during delivery

**Pregnancy:**
- **Mild febrile illness** in mother (flu-like)
- **Fetal complications:** Miscarriage, stillbirth, preterm labor, neonatal infection

**Rhombencephalitis (Brainstem Encephalitis):**
- **Cranial nerve palsies**, ataxia, altered mental status
- **MRI:** Brainstem lesions
- **Poor prognosis**

### **Treatment:**

**Meningitis:**
- **Ampicillin 2g IV q4h** + **gentamicin 5-7 mg/kg IV daily** × 21 days
- **Gentamicin:** First 7-14 days (synergy with ampicillin)

**Bacteremia (No CNS Involvement):**
- **Ampicillin 2g IV q4h** × 14 days

**Penicillin Allergy:**
- **TMP-SMX 5 mg/kg (TMP component) IV q8h**
- **Avoid cephalosporins** (poor activity against *Listeria*)

**Pregnancy:**
- **Ampicillin 2g IV q4h** × 14 days (if bacteremia) or longer if CNS involvement

**Prophylaxis:** None recommended (even after outbreak exposure)

## Key Points

### **Why NOT Cephalosporins:**
- ***Listeria* intrinsically resistant** to cephalosporins
- **Always add ampicillin** to empiric meningitis regimen in high-risk groups
- **Standard meningitis regimen (ceftriaxone + vancomycin) misses *Listeria***

### **Empiric Meningitis Coverage:**
**Add ampicillin if:**
- Age >50 years
- Immunocompromised
- Chronic illness (diabetes, alcohol use, liver disease)

**Regimen:** **Ceftriaxone + vancomycin + ampicillin**

### **Food Safety (Prevention):**
- **Avoid high-risk foods** (pregnant, elderly, immunocompromised):
  - Unpasteurized milk/cheese
  - Deli meats, hot dogs (unless heated to steaming)
  - Smoked seafood (lox, kippered fish)
  - Refrigerated pâtés/meat spreads
- ***Listeria* grows at refrigerator temperatures** (4°C)

### **Microbiology:**
- **Gram-positive rods** (short, coccobacilli)
- **Motile at 20-25°C** ("tumbling motility")
- **β-hemolytic** on blood agar
- **Catalase positive** (distinguishes from *Streptococcus*)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **High-risk groups:** Pregnant, neonates, elderly, immunocompromised
- **Ampicillin 2g q4h + gentamicin** (meningitis × 21 days)
- **Cephalosporins ineffective** → always add ampicillin to empiric meningitis regimen if high-risk
- **Foodborne:** Unpasteurized dairy, deli meats, soft cheeses
- **Grows at refrigerator temperatures** (4°C)
- **TMP-SMX** alternative if penicillin allergy
