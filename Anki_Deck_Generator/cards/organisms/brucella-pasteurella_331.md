## Brucella and Pasteurella Species

**Q:** What are the treatments for brucellosis and Pasteurella bite infections?

**A:**

**Brucellosis (Brucella spp.):**

**Overview:**
- **Zoonotic**: Unpasteurized dairy (goats, sheep, cattle), occupational (veterinarians, slaughterhouse workers)
- **Also called**: Malta fever, undulant fever, Mediterranean fever

**Clinical Features:**
- **Undulant fever** (relapsing), night sweats
- Arthralgias, malaise, weight loss, hepatosplenomegaly
- **Complications**: Spondylitis (10-20%), endocarditis (rare, most lethal), neurobrucellosis, orchitis

**Treatment (WHO Guidelines):**

**Uncomplicated:**
- **Doxycycline 100 mg PO BID + rifampin 600-900 mg PO daily × 6 weeks** (standard)
- Alternative: **Doxycycline × 6 weeks + streptomycin/gentamicin × 2-3 weeks** (lower relapse)

**Complicated (Spondylitis, CNS, Endocarditis):**
- **Triple therapy**: Doxycycline + rifampin + gentamicin × 2-3 weeks, then doxycycline + rifampin × 6 weeks total
- **2024 meta-analysis**: Triple therapy > dual therapy (lower treatment failure)

**Key Points:**
- **Always combination therapy** (monotherapy high relapse)
- **Prolonged treatment** (6 weeks minimum)
- **Gentamicin** can substitute for streptomycin

---

**Pasteurella Species:**

**Overview:**
- **Normal oral flora** of dogs, cats, other animals
- **Most common**: *P. multocida*

**Clinical Syndromes:**

**Dog/Cat Bite Wound Infection:**
- **Rapid onset** (24 hours)
- **Cellulitis, purulent discharge**, localized pain/swelling
- Can cause **osteomyelitis, septic arthritis, tenosynovitis** (deep puncture wounds)

**Respiratory Infections:**
- COPD exacerbation, pneumonia (rare)

**Treatment:**

**Bite Wound:**
- **Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg PO BID** (preferred; covers Pasteurella + anaerobes)
- Alternatives: Doxycycline, fluoroquinolones, ceftriaxone

**Penicillin Allergy:**
- Doxycycline 100 mg PO BID OR fluoroquinolone (levofloxacin, moxifloxacin)

**Duration:** 5-14 days (depends on severity)

**Key Points:**
- **Brucellosis**: Doxycycline + rifampin × 6 weeks (standard); triple therapy for complicated
- **Pasteurella**: Amoxicillin-clavulanate for dog/cat bite wounds; rapid onset (<24h)

**Mnemonic - "Brucella = BRUtal fever, needs COMbination RX (Doxy + Rifampin)"**

**Pearl:** Brucellosis requires prolonged combination therapy (doxycycline + rifampin × 6 weeks minimum); triple therapy recommended for complicated cases per 2024 meta-analysis. Pasteurella bite infections have rapid onset (<24h) and respond to amoxicillin-clavulanate.
