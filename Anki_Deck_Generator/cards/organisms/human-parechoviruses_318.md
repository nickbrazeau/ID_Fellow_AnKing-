## Human Parechoviruses

**Q:** What are the clinical manifestations and key diagnostic features of human parechovirus infections in neonates?

**A:**

**Overview:**
- **Picornaviridae family** (related to enteroviruses)
- **18+ serotypes**; **HPeV3** most common/severe
- **Second most common cause** of viral meningitis in children (after enteroviruses)
- **Age**: Primarily affects **infants <90 days old**

**Clinical Manifestations:**

**Neonates/Young Infants (<90 days):**

| Syndrome | Features |
|----------|----------|
| **Sepsis-like illness** | Fever, irritability, poor feeding, lethargy; mimics bacterial sepsis |
| **Meningitis/Encephalitis** | Fever, irritability, seizures; **CSF often normal** (NO pleocytosis) |
| **Hepatitis** | Elevated transaminases |
| **Myocarditis** | Heart failure, arrhythmias |
| **Severe systemic illness** | Multi-organ involvement, pulmonary infiltrates |

**Older Children:**
- **Mild respiratory/GI illness** (self-limited)

**Key Diagnostic Features:**

**HPeV3 Meningoencephalitis:**
- **Most common cause** of aseptic meningitis in infants <90 days (North America)
- **CSF typically NORMAL** (no pleocytosis) - distinguishes from enteroviral meningitis
- MRI: White matter changes

**Seasonality:**
- **Biannual summer-fall peaks** (similar to enteroviruses)

**Diagnosis:**
- **RT-PCR** (blood, CSF, stool, respiratory secretions)
- Parechovirus testing NOT included in standard enterovirus PCR panels

**Recent Outbreaks:**
- **2022**: Unexpected peaks in US (severe/fatal cases)
- **2022-2023**: Europe (echovirus 11 + parechovirus) - neonatal sepsis/meningoencephalitis outbreaks (some fatal)

**Treatment:**
- **NO antiviral therapy**
- **Supportive care** only

**Key Points:**
- **HPeV3**: Most common serotype causing severe disease in neonates
- **Neonatal sepsis-like illness** + meningoencephalitis (often **normal CSF**)
- **Must specifically test** for parechovirus (not included in standard enterovirus PCR)
- 2022-2023 outbreaks in US/Europe with severe neonatal disease
