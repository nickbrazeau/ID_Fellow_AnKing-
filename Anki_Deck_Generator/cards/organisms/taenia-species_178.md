## Question

How do taeniasis and cysticercosis differ in transmission and treatment? Use the **"Taeniasis (Undercooked Pork/Beef) = Praziquantel × 1, Cysticercosis (Fecal-Oral Eggs) = Albendazole + Steroids"** framework.

## Answer

### **Taenia Species Overview:**

***T. solium*** (pork tapeworm), ***T. saginata*** (beef tapeworm), ***T. asiatica*** (Asian tapeworm)

### **Taeniasis (Intestinal Tapeworm Infection):**

**Transmission:** Ingestion of **undercooked meat** containing cysticerci (larval form)
- ***T. solium*:** Pork
- ***T. saginata*:** Beef

**Presentation:**
- **Usually asymptomatic** or mild GI symptoms
- **Proglottids in stool** (motile segments) - patients may notice
- **Can persist for years**

**Diagnosis:**
- **Stool:** Ova or proglottids
- **Scotch tape test** (perianal)

**Treatment:**
- **Praziquantel 5-10 mg/kg PO × 1** (single dose)
- OR **niclosamide 2g PO × 1**

### **Cysticercosis (***T. solium*** ONLY):**

**Transmission:** Ingestion of ***T. solium* eggs** (fecal-oral from human tapeworm carrier)
- **NOT from eating pork** (eggs, not cysts, cause cysticercosis)
- **Autoinfection possible** (if patient has intestinal tapeworm)

**Pathophysiology:** Eggs hatch → larvae penetrate intestine → disseminate hematogenously → encyst in tissues (CNS, muscles, eyes)

**Neurocysticercosis (Most Serious):**

**Presentation:**
- **Seizures** (most common, 70-90%) - focal or generalized
- **Headache, increased ICP** (hydrocephalus, cysticercotic encephalitis)
- **Cognitive impairment, psychiatric symptoms**
- **Focal neurologic deficits** (rare)

**Imaging (MRI > CT):**
- **Viable cysts:** Rounded, fluid-filled, scolex (parasite head) visible
- **Degenerating cysts:** Ring-enhancing lesions, edema
- **Calcified cysts:** Chronic, inactive (no longer viable)
- **Ventricular/subarachnoid:** Hydrocephalus, vasculitis

**Diagnosis:**
- **MRI brain:** Cystic lesions with scolex (pathognomonic), ring-enhancing, or calcifications
- **Serology:** Enzyme-linked immunoelectrotransfer blot (EITB) - high sensitivity/specificity
- **CSF:** Eosinophilia (if present, suggestive), EITB

**Treatment (Depends on Stage/Location):**

**Viable Parenchymal Cysts (<2 lesions):**
- **Albendazole 15 mg/kg/day (max 800mg) divided BID × 10-14 days**
- OR **praziquantel 50 mg/kg/day divided TID × 10-14 days**
- **+ Prednisone 60mg PO daily** OR **dexamethasone 6-12mg IV/PO daily** (taper over 2-4 weeks)
- **+ Antiepileptic drug** (if seizures)

**Rationale for steroids:** Reduce inflammation from dying parasites (can worsen edema, seizures)

**Multiple Viable Cysts (>2):**
- **Albendazole + praziquantel** (combination more effective)
- **Longer duration** (up to 28 days)
- **Steroids**

**Calcified Cysts (Inactive):**
- **NO antihelminthic** (cysts already dead)
- **Antiepileptic drug** (if seizures)

**Ventricular/Subarachnoid:**
- **Neurosurgical intervention** (shunt for hydrocephalus, cyst removal)
- **Controversial role** for antihelminthics (can worsen inflammation)

**Ocular Cysticercosis:**
- **Surgical removal** (antihelminthics contraindicated - can cause blindness from inflammation)

## Key Points

### **Taeniasis vs. Cysticercosis:**

| Feature | Taeniasis | Cysticercosis |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| **Organism** | *T. solium*, *T. saginata* | ***T. solium* ONLY** |
| **Transmission** | **Undercooked meat** (cysticerci) | **Fecal-oral** (eggs) |
| **Location** | **Intestine** (adult worm) | **Tissues** (CNS, muscle, eye) - cysts |
| **Symptoms** | Asymptomatic/mild GI | **Seizures** (neurocysticercosis) |
| **Treatment** | Praziquantel 5-10 mg/kg × 1 | Albendazole 15 mg/kg/day × 10-14d + steroids |

### **Neurocysticercosis = Leading Cause of Acquired Epilepsy Worldwide:**
- **Most common helminth infection of CNS**
- **Endemic:** Latin America, Sub-Saharan Africa, Asia

### **Scolex on MRI:**
- **Pathognomonic** for cysticercosis
- **"Dot within a cyst"** (scolex = parasite head)

### **Steroids Essential:**
- **Prevent inflammation** from dying parasites
- **Start with or before antihelminthics**
- **Taper over 2-4 weeks**

### **Calcified Cysts = NO Treatment:**
- **Already dead** (chronic, inactive)
- **Antihelminthics NOT indicated**
- **Treat seizures** with antiepileptics

### **Ocular = Surgery ONLY:**
- **Antihelminthics contraindicated** (inflammation can cause blindness)
- **Surgical removal** required

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Taeniasis:** Undercooked pork/beef → praziquantel × 1
- **Cysticercosis:** Fecal-oral (*T. solium* eggs) → albendazole 15 mg/kg/day × 10-14d + steroids
- **Neurocysticercosis:** Seizures + cystic brain lesions with scolex
- **Calcified cysts:** NO antihelminthics (already dead), treat seizures only
- **Ocular:** Surgery (NOT antihelminthics)
