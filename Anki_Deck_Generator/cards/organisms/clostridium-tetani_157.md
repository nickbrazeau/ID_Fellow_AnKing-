## Question

How is tetanus clinically recognized and managed? Use the **"Lockjaw + Risus Sardonicus → Tetanus Toxoid/TIG + Metronidazole, Prevent with Vaccine"** framework.

## Answer

### ***Clostridium tetani* (Tetanus) Overview:**

**Organism:** Anaerobic, spore-forming, Gram-positive rod

**Transmission:**
- **Wound contamination** with spores (soil, feces, rusty objects)
- **Spores germinate** in anaerobic conditions (puncture wounds, crush injuries, burns, IVDU, neonatal umbilical stump)

**Pathophysiology:**
- **Tetanus toxin (tetanospasmin):** Blocks inhibitory neurotransmitters (GABA, glycine) → **uninhibited muscle contraction**

### **Clinical Presentation:**

**Generalized Tetanus (Most Common, 80%):**

**Triad:**
1. **Trismus ("lockjaw"):** Masseter muscle spasm (difficulty opening mouth)
2. **Risus sardonicus:** Fixed smile (facial muscle spasm)
3. **Opisthotonus:** Arched back (extensor muscle spasm)

**Progression:**
- **Descending pattern:** Jaw → face → neck → trunk → extremities
- **Generalized muscle spasms** (painful, sudden, triggered by stimuli like noise, light, touch)
- **Autonomic instability:** Labile BP/HR, diaphoresis, arrhythmias
- **Respiratory failure:** Laryngeal/respiratory muscle spasm

**Consciousness:** **ALWAYS intact** (no altered mental status)

**Localized Tetanus:**
- **Muscle spasm near wound** (painful rigidity)
- **Can progress to generalized**

**Cephalic Tetanus (Rare):**
- **Head/neck wound** (ear infections)
- **Cranial nerve palsies**

**Neonatal Tetanus:**
- **Umbilical stump infection** (unsterile delivery, unvaccinated mother)
- **Difficulty feeding** (trismus), **generalized rigidity**, **seizures**
- **High mortality** (>50% in developing countries)

### **Diagnosis:**

**Clinical:** Diagnosis based on **clinical presentation** (no lab test)

**Key Features:**
- **History of wound** (even minor, often forgotten)
- **Trismus, risus sardonicus, opisthotonus**
- **Painful muscle spasms triggered by stimuli**
- **Normal mental status**
- **NO fever** (unless secondary infection)

**Lab:** No specific test (*C. tetani* rarely cultured from wound)

### **Treatment:**

**Human Tetanus Immune Globulin (TIG):**
- **Dose:** **500 units IM × 1** (neutralizes unbound toxin)
- **Give immediately** (does NOT reverse toxin already bound to neurons)
- **Infiltrate around wound** if accessible

**Antibiotics:**
- **Metronidazole 500mg IV q6-8h × 7-10 days** (preferred)
- OR **penicillin G 2-4 million units IV q4-6h × 7-10 days**
- **Eliminates *C. tetani*** (prevents further toxin production)

**Wound Care:**
- **Surgical debridement** (remove necrotic tissue, foreign bodies → create aerobic environment)

**Supportive Care (Critical):**

**Muscle Spasm Control:**
- **Benzodiazepines:** Diazepam 5-10mg IV q4-6h PRN (high doses often needed, 100-300mg/day)
- **If inadequate:** Neuromuscular blockade + mechanical ventilation

**Autonomic Instability:**
- **Magnesium sulfate** (stabilizes cardiovascular lability)
- **β-blockers, clonidine** (if needed)

**Environment:**
- **Quiet, dark room** (minimize stimuli that trigger spasms)

**Airway/Respiratory:**
- **Early intubation/tracheostomy** (laryngospasm, respiratory failure common)

**Tetanus Toxoid Vaccination:**
- **Give during illness** (immune globulin does NOT provide immunity)
- **Start/complete vaccine series** (natural infection does NOT provide immunity)

**Prognosis:**
- **Mortality 10-20% in developed countries** (higher in elderly, neonates)
- **Recovery slow** (weeks to months as new neuromuscular junctions form)

### **Prevention:**

**Vaccination:**

**Primary Series:**
- **DTaP (children <7 years):** 2, 4, 6, 15-18 months, 4-6 years
- **Tdap (≥7 years):** 1 dose, then **Td boosters every 10 years**

**Wound Management:**

| Wound Type | Vaccination History | Tetanus Toxoid | TIG |
|------------|---------------------|----------------|-----|
| **Clean, minor** | <3 doses or unknown | **Yes** | No |
| **Clean, minor** | ≥3 doses, last >10 years | **Yes** | No |
| **Dirty/deep/puncture** | <3 doses or unknown | **Yes** | **Yes** (250 units IM) |
| **Dirty/deep/puncture** | ≥3 doses, last >5 years | **Yes** | No |

**Pregnancy:**
- **Tdap during each pregnancy** (27-36 weeks) → passive immunity to newborn

## Key Points

### **Trismus + Risus Sardonicus + Opisthotonus:**
- **Triad diagnostic** of generalized tetanus
- **Descending pattern** (jaw → face → trunk)
- **Consciousness intact** (distinguishes from meningoencephalitis)

### **Autonomic Instability:**
- **Labile BP/HR** (life-threatening arrhythmias)
- **Treat with magnesium sulfate** (preferred)

### **TIG vs. Tetanus Toxoid:**
- **TIG (immune globulin):** Neutralizes unbound toxin (immediate protection)
- **Tetanus toxoid (vaccine):** Induces immunity (takes weeks, give anyway since natural infection does NOT immunize)

### **Natural Infection Does NOT Immunize:**
- **Toxin dose too low** to induce immunity
- **MUST vaccinate** during/after illness

### **Wound Management:**
- **Dirty wound + incomplete vaccination** → give **toxoid + TIG**
- **Clean wound + complete vaccination (>10 years ago)** → give **toxoid only**

### **Why Metronidazole > Penicillin:**
- **Penicillin** may worsen spasms (GABA antagonist properties)
- **Metronidazole preferred**

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Trismus + risus sardonicus + opisthotonus** + normal mental status = tetanus
- **TIG 500 units IM** + **metronidazole 500mg IV q6h** + **wound debridement** + **supportive care**
- **High-dose benzodiazepines** for muscle spasms (often need ventilation)
- **Vaccinate during illness** (natural infection does NOT provide immunity)
- **Wound prophylaxis:** Dirty wound + incomplete vaccination → toxoid + TIG
