## Psittacosis (Chlamydia psittaci)

**Q:** What are the epidemiology, clinical features, and treatment of psittacosis?

**A:**

**Overview:**
- **Causative agent**: *Chlamydia psittaci*
- **Also known as**: Parrot fever, ornithosis, avian chlamydiosis
- **Zoonotic**: Transmitted from birds to humans

**Epidemiology:**

**Bird Reservoirs:**
- **Pet birds**: Parrots, parakeets, cockatiels, macaws
- **Poultry**: Turkeys, ducks
- **Wild birds**: Pigeons

**Transmission:**
- **Inhalation** of aerosolized dried bird droppings, respiratory secretions
- Occupational risk: Pet store workers, poultry farmers, veterinarians

**Clinical Features:**

**Incubation:** **5-19 days**

**Presentation:**
- **Flu-like prodrome**: High fever, chills, severe headache, myalgia
- **Atypical pneumonia**: Dry cough, dyspnea
- **Relative bradycardia** (pulse-temperature dissociation)
- Hepatosplenomegaly (20-70%)
- **Diarrhea** (uncommon in other atypical pneumonias)
- Conjunctivitis, epistaxis (nosebleeds)

**Laboratory:**
- **Leukopenia** or normal WBC (NOT leukocytosis)
- Elevated transaminases
- CXR: Patchy infiltrates (can be severe)

**Complications (Rare but Serious):**
- Endocarditis, myocarditis
- Hepatitis
- Encephalitis, meningoencephalitis
- Respiratory failure

**Diagnosis:**
- **PCR** (respiratory specimens)
- **Serology**: Fourfold rise in antibody titer or single high titer (IgM + IgG)
- Culture (rarely done, requires biosafety level 3)

**Treatment:**

**First-line:**
- **Doxycycline 100 mg PO BID × 10-21 days** (preferred; **2-3 weeks** to prevent relapse)

**Alternatives:**
- **Macrolides**: Azithromycin, clarithromycin (less effective; **macrolide failures reported**)
- Fluoroquinolones (levofloxacin, moxifloxacin)

**Key Treatment Points:**
- **Tetracyclines preferred** due to macrolide failures
- **Treat for 2-3 weeks** (shorter courses associated with relapse)
- Avoid macrolides as first-line

**2024 Outbreak:**
- **Argentina (April 2024)**: 20 suspected cases, 10 confirmed psittacosis cases

**Key Points:**
- **Bird exposure history** critical for diagnosis
- **Atypical pneumonia** + GI symptoms + relative bradycardia
- **Doxycycline × 2-3 weeks** (macrolide failures reported)
- Can cause severe systemic disease (endocarditis, encephalitis)

**Mnemonic - "PSITTacosis = Pet Store Ill with Temperature-pulse Trouble, Atypical Cough"**: Pet birds, relative bradycardia, atypical pneumonia

**Pearl:** Psittacosis causes atypical pneumonia with relative bradycardia and GI symptoms after bird exposure. Treat with doxycycline × 2-3 weeks (NOT macrolides as first-line due to treatment failures). Argentina had outbreak in 2024.
