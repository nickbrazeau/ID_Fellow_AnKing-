## Question

How is amebiasis treated? Use the **"Metronidazole (Tissue) + Paromomycin (Luminal)"** two-step framework.

## Answer

### ***Entamoeba histolytica* Overview:**

**Transmission:**
- **Fecal-oral** route (contaminated food/water)
- **Endemic:** Developing countries (Latin America, Africa, Asia)
- **High-risk:** Travelers, immigrants, MSM, institutionalized

**Forms:**
- **Trophozoites** (motile, tissue-invasive)
- **Cysts** (infectious form, environmentally resistant)

### **Clinical Syndromes:**

**Intestinal Amebiasis (Amebic Colitis):**
- **Gradual onset** (days to weeks)
- **Bloody diarrhea** ("anchovy paste" or "raspberry jelly" stool)
- **Abdominal pain**, tenesmus
- **Subacute to chronic** (vs acute bacterial dysentery)
- **Low-grade fever** (or afebrile)
- **Complications:** **Toxic megacolon**, **perforation** (rare, high mortality)

**Amebic Liver Abscess:**
- **Right upper quadrant pain** (referred to right shoulder)
- **Fever** (high-grade, persistent)
- **Hepatomegaly**, tender liver
- **History of diarrhea:** May or may not be present (50% have no GI symptoms)
- **Imaging:** Single abscess (right lobe most common), **"anchovy paste" pus** if aspirated
- **Serology:** Positive in >90%
- **Complications:** Rupture into pleura, peritoneum, pericardium

**Asymptomatic Colonization:**
- Cyst passage without symptoms
- Still requires treatment (prevent transmission + invasive disease)

### **Diagnosis:**

**Intestinal Amebiasis:**
- **Stool antigen (EIA):** **Best test**, detects *E. histolytica* antigen (distinguishes from non-pathogenic *E. dispar*)
- **Stool O&P:** Trophozoites with **ingested RBCs** (pathognomonic for *E. histolytica*)
- **Serology (antibody):** Positive in ~70% (less sensitive for intestinal, more for liver abscess)
- **Colonoscopy:** **Flask-shaped ulcers** (classic finding)

**Amebic Liver Abscess:**
- **Imaging:** Ultrasound or CT (single abscess, right lobe)
- **Serology:** **>90% sensitive** (IgG antibody), test of choice
- **Aspiration:** **"Anchovy paste" pus** (sterile, no bacteria) - rarely needed for diagnosis

### **Treatment:**

**Two-Step Therapy (Tissue + Luminal):**

**Step 1: Tissue Amebicide (Treat Invasive Disease):**
- **Metronidazole** 750mg PO/IV TID × 7-10 days OR
- **Tinidazole** 2g PO daily × 3 days (intestinal) or 5 days (liver abscess)

**Step 2: Luminal Amebicide (Eradicate Cysts, Prevent Relapse):**
- **Paromomycin** 25-35 mg/kg/day PO divided TID × 7 days OR
- **Iodoquinol** 650mg PO TID × 20 days

**Amebic Liver Abscess:**
- **Metronidazole** 750mg PO/IV TID × 7-10 days (or tinidazole 2g daily × 5 days)
- **+**
- **Paromomycin** 25-35 mg/kg/day divided TID × 7 days
- **Aspiration:** Usually NOT needed (unless rupture risk, very large abscess >5cm, or no response to antibiotics)

**Asymptomatic Cyst Passers:**
- **Paromomycin alone** 25-35 mg/kg/day divided TID × 7 days OR
- **Iodoquinol** 650mg PO TID × 20 days

## Key Points

### **Why Two-Step Therapy:**
- **Metronidazole/tinidazole** (tissue amebicides) kill trophozoites in tissue but **NOT cysts in gut lumen**
- **Paromomycin/iodoquinol** (luminal amebicides) kill cysts in gut lumen
- **Without luminal agent:** 40-60% relapse rate

### ***E. histolytica* vs *E. dispar*:**
- ***E. histolytica*:** **Pathogenic** (causes invasive disease)
- ***E. dispar*:** **Non-pathogenic** (10× more common, no treatment needed)
- **Stool O&P cannot distinguish** (look identical)
- **Stool antigen (EIA) distinguishes** (detects only *E. histolytica*)

### **Amebic vs Bacterial Dysentery:**

| Feature | Amebic | Bacterial (Shigella, Campylobacter) |
|---------|--------|-------------------------------------|
| **Onset** | **Gradual** (days-weeks) | **Acute** (hours-days) |
| **Fever** | Low-grade or absent | High-grade |
| **Stool** | Bloody, mucoid | Bloody, mucoid |
| **Fecal WBCs** | **Few or absent** | **Many** |
| **Colonoscopy** | **Flask-shaped ulcers** | Diffuse inflammation, shallow ulcers |

### **Aspiration of Liver Abscess:**
**Indications (rarely needed):**
- **Imminent rupture** (large abscess >5cm, left lobe)
- **No response** to antibiotics after 3-5 days
- **Rule out pyogenic abscess** (if uncertain diagnosis)

**"Anchovy Paste" Pus:**
- **Sterile** (no bacteria, negative Gram stain/culture)
- **Chocolate/anchovy** color (necrotic hepatocytes)

### **High-Risk Groups:**
- **MSM** (especially HIV+)
- **Institutionalized** (mental health facilities)
- **Immigrants/travelers** from endemic areas

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Metronidazole 750mg TID × 7-10 days + paromomycin 25-35 mg/kg/day TID × 7 days**
- **Two-step therapy mandatory** (tissue + luminal amebicide)
- **Stool antigen (EIA)** distinguishes *E. histolytica* (pathogenic) from *E. dispar* (non-pathogenic)
- **Flask-shaped ulcers** on colonoscopy = classic for amebiasis
- **Liver abscess:** Serology >90% sensitive, aspiration rarely needed
- **"Anchovy paste" pus** = amebic liver abscess
- **Gradual onset** (vs acute bacterial dysentery)
