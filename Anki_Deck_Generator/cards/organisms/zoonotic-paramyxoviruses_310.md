## Zoonotic Paramyxoviruses: Nipah and Hendra Viruses

**Q:** What are the reservoirs, geographic distributions, mortality rates, and transmission patterns of Nipah and Hendra viruses?

**A:**

**Overview - Henipaviruses:**
- Genus Henipavirus (family Paramyxoviridae)
- **Natural reservoir**: *Pteropus* fruit bats (flying foxes)
- Two human-pathogenic species: Nipah virus (NiV), Hendra virus (HeV)
- **WHO priority pathogens** (pandemic potential)

**Comparison:**

| Feature | **Nipah Virus (NiV)** | **Hendra Virus (HeV)** |
|---------|----------------------|------------------------|
| **Geographic distribution** | **Bangladesh, India, Malaysia**, Southeast Asia (Cambodia, Indonesia, Thailand, Philippines) | **Australia** only |
| **Outbreaks** | Malaysia 1998-1999 (initial); **Bangladesh/India ongoing** (annual outbreaks); latest 2024 (Bangladesh, India) | Australia (sporadic equine cases) |
| **Amplifying host** | **Pigs** (Malaysia/Philippines); **direct bat-to-human** (Bangladesh/India via date palm sap) | **Horses** â†’ humans |
| **Mortality (CFR)** | **40-75%** (Malaysia ~40%; Bangladesh/India **50-100%**) | **57%** |

**Clinical Features:**

**Incubation:** **4-14 days** (can be up to 45 days)

**Presentation:**
- Initial: Fever, headache, myalgia, vomiting, sore throat
- Progression: **Acute encephalitis** (dizziness, drowsiness, altered consciousness, neurologic signs)
- **Severe respiratory disease** (ARDS)
- **Delayed/relapsing encephalitis** (months to years later)

**Person-to-Person Transmission:**

**Nipah Virus:**
- **Significant human-to-human transmission** documented
- **Bangladesh (2001-2008)**: ~**50% of cases** due to human-to-human spread
- **Siliguri outbreak (2001)**: **75% of cases** among hospital staff/visitors
- Occurs among family members and healthcare workers

**Hendra Virus:**
- Rare human-to-human transmission

**Treatment:**
- **NO approved antivirals or vaccines**
- **Intensive supportive care** for respiratory/neurologic complications

**Outcomes:**
- CFR 40-75%
- **20% of survivors**: Persistent neurologic complications

**Key Points:**
- Nipah has **pandemic potential** (high mortality + human-to-human transmission)
- **Bangladesh/India**: Annual outbreaks via contaminated date palm sap
- **Malaysia/Philippines**: Pig amplifying host
- **Australia**: Hendra via horses (rare human cases)

**Mnemonic - "Nipah = Nearby Hospital spread"**: Significant nosocomial transmission (50% Bangladesh cases, 75% Siliguri outbreak)

**Pearl:** Nipah virus has 40-75% mortality and documented human-to-human transmission (50% of Bangladesh cases). Contaminated date palm sap is primary route in South Asia. No specific treatment exists.
