## Question

How is *S. aureus* bacteremia managed? Use the **"TEE + Source Control + 14 Days (Uncomplicated) or 4-6 Weeks (Complicated)"** framework.

## Answer

### **Management Framework:**

**1. Source Control:**
- Remove infected devices (central lines, pacemakers, prosthetic joints if infected)
- Drain abscesses
- Debride infected tissues

**2. Repeat Blood Cultures:**
- **Daily until clearance** (critical for determining duration)
- Persistent bacteremia = complicated

**3. Echocardiography (TEE):**
- **TEE preferred** over TTE (higher sensitivity: 90-100% vs 30-60%)
- **Recommended for ALL patients** with SAB
- **Mandatory if:** Healthcare-associated, persistent fever >72h, persistent bacteremia, murmur, prosthetic valve

**4. Antimicrobial Therapy:**

| Susceptibility | Treatment |
|----------------|-----------|
| **MSSA** | **Nafcillin or oxacillin 2g IV q4h** OR **cefazolin 2g IV q8h** (preferred if no infective endocarditis) |
| **MRSA** | **Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h** (target trough 15-20) OR **daptomycin 6-10 mg/kg IV daily** |

### **Duration:**

**Uncomplicated SAB (all must be met):**
- Exclude endocarditis (TEE negative or low clinical suspicion + not performed)
- No implanted prosthetics
- Repeat blood cultures negative within 2-4 days
- Defervescence within 72 hours
- No metastatic sites (bone, joint, epidural abscess)
- **Duration: 14 days** from first negative blood culture

**Complicated SAB (any of above not met):**
- **Duration: 4-6 weeks** from first negative blood culture
- Endocarditis: 6 weeks (native valve), 6-8 weeks (prosthetic valve)

## Key Points

### **Why TEE for All SAB:**
- 10-30% of SAB cases have endocarditis
- TTE misses many cases (low sensitivity)
- Detection of endocarditis changes duration (4-6 weeks vs 14 days)
- Identifies valvular complications (abscess, perforation)

### **MRSA vs MSSA Treatment:**
- **MSSA:** Nafcillin/oxacillin preferred (better outcomes than vancomycin)
- **Cefazolin:** Alternative for MSSA (non-inferior, easier dosing) - but avoid in endocarditis (inoculum effect)
- **MRSA:** Vancomycin OR daptomycin (daptomycin preferred if vancomycin MIC >1.5, persistent bacteremia, or pneumonia)

### **Adjunctive Rifampin:**
- **Generally NOT recommended** for SAB alone
- Consider for prosthetic joint infections (biofilm penetration)
- Risk: Antagonism with some antibiotics, drug interactions

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **TEE for all SAB** (detects endocarditis in 10-30%)
- **Repeat cultures daily** until negative (determines duration)
- **Uncomplicated:** 14 days, **Complicated:** 4-6 weeks
- **MSSA:** Nafcillin/oxacillin (better than vancomycin)
- **Remove all infected devices** (lines, hardware)
- **Persistent bacteremia >72h:** Consider endocarditis, undrained source, resistant organism
