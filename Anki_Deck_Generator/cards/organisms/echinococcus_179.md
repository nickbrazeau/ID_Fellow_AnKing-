## Question

How is hydatid cyst disease managed? Use the **"Dog Tapeworm, Hepatic/Pulmonary Cysts, Anaphylaxis Risk → Albendazole + Surgery/PAIR"** framework.

## Answer

### ***Echinococcus* Species Overview:**

***E. granulosus*** (cystic echinococcosis, CE), ***E. multilocularis*** (alveolar echinococcosis, AE)

**Transmission:** Ingestion of eggs from dog/fox feces (contaminated food, water, direct contact)

**Geography:** Worldwide (sheep-raising areas - Mediterranean, Middle East, South America, Central Asia, China)

### **Cystic Echinococcosis (***E. granulosus***):**

**Presentation:**
- **Asymptomatic for years** (slow-growing cysts, 1-5 cm/year)
- **Liver (60-70%):** RUQ pain, hepatomegaly, biliary obstruction (if ruptured into biliary tree)
- **Lung (20-30%):** Cough, chest pain, hemoptysis (if ruptures into bronchus)
- **Other:** Brain, bone, kidney (rare)

**Complications:**
- **Cyst rupture → anaphylaxis** (protoscoleces release → severe allergic reaction, potentially fatal)
- **Secondary echinococcosis** (spillage → daughter cysts)

**Diagnosis:**
- **Imaging (US, CT, MRI):** Cystic lesions with:
  - **Daughter cysts** (cysts within cysts)
  - **"Water lily sign"** (detached membranes floating in cyst)
  - **Calcification** (rim, indicates old/inactive cyst)
- **Serology:** ELISA, indirect hemagglutination (IHA) - confirms diagnosis (sensitivity 60-90%)
- **NEVER aspirate without surgical backup** (risk of anaphylaxis, spillage)

**Treatment:**

**Surgery:**
- **Indicated:** Large cysts (>5 cm), complicated cysts (rupture, infection, biliary communication)
- **Pre-op albendazole:** 4 days before + 1-3 months after (reduce viability, prevent recurrence)
- **Intraoperative:** Inject hypertonic saline (kills protoscoleces), careful removal to avoid spillage

**PAIR (Puncture, Aspiration, Injection, Re-aspiration):**
- **Percutaneous treatment** under imaging guidance
- **Indications:** Uncomplicated cysts >5 cm (WHO CE1, CE3)
- **Albendazole:** 4 hours before + 1-3 months after
- **Inject hypertonic saline/ethanol** → aspirate → repeat
- **Contraindicated:** Superficial cysts (risk of spillage), inactive/calcified cysts

**Albendazole Alone (Watch-and-Wait):**
- **Albendazole 15 mg/kg/day divided BID (max 800mg/day) × 3-6 months** (cycles: 28 days on, 14 days off)
- **Indications:** Small (<5 cm), asymptomatic, inoperable, or patient refusal
- **Monitor:** LFTs, CBC (can cause hepatotoxicity, bone marrow suppression)
- **Cure rate:** 30-40% (lower than surgery/PAIR)

**Inactive/Calcified Cysts:**
- **Observation** (no treatment needed)

### **Alveolar Echinococcosis (***E. multilocularis***):**

**Presentation:**
- **Hepatic mass** (tumor-like, infiltrative, "malignant" behavior)
- **Cholestatic jaundice, hepatomegaly**
- **Can metastasize** (lungs, brain)

**Treatment:**
- **Surgical resection** (if possible) + **albendazole ≥2 years**
- **Lifelong albendazole** if unresectable (palliative)
- **Poor prognosis** without treatment

## Key Points

### **Anaphylaxis Risk:**
- **Cyst rupture** → massive antigen release → anaphylaxis (potentially fatal)
- **NEVER aspirate** without surgical backup, antihistamines, epinephrine available
- **Pre-treat with albendazole** (reduces viability)

### **"Water Lily Sign":**
- **Detached membranes** floating in cyst (US/CT)
- **Pathognomonic** for hydatid cyst

### **Daughter Cysts:**
- **Cysts within cysts**
- **Distinguishes from simple hepatic cysts, amoebic liver abscess**

### **Albendazole = Adjunct:**
- **NOT curative alone** (30-40% cure)
- **Use with surgery/PAIR** (before + after)
- **Cycles:** 28 days on, 14 days off × 3-6 months

### **PAIR Advantages:**
- **Less invasive** than surgery
- **Lower morbidity**, faster recovery
- **Similar efficacy** to surgery for uncomplicated cysts

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Shepherd, dog contact** + hepatic/pulmonary cyst with daughter cysts = echinococcosis
- **"Water lily sign"** = pathognomonic
- **NEVER aspirate** (anaphylaxis risk)
- **Treatment:** Albendazole + surgery/PAIR (cysts >5 cm), albendazole alone (small, inoperable)
- **Alveolar (*E. multilocularis*):** Aggressive, tumor-like, needs resection + lifelong albendazole
