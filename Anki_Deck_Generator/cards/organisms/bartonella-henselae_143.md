## Question

How is *Bartonella henselae* infection managed? Use the **"CSD = Self-Limited (Immunocompetent), Azithromycin Shortens Course"** framework.

## Answer

### ***Bartonella henselae* Overview:**

**Transmission:**
- **Cat scratch or bite** (especially kittens <1 year)
- **Cat flea feces** on cat claws (flea-borne)
- **Cats = reservoir** (asymptomatic)

### **Clinical Syndromes:**

**Cat Scratch Disease (CSD) - Immunocompetent:**

**Classic Presentation:**
- **Primary lesion:** Papule/pustule at inoculation site (appears 3-10 days after scratch)
- **Regional lymphadenopathy** (develops 1-3 weeks later)
  - **Proximal to scratch site** (axillary most common, then epitrochlear, cervical)
  - **Tender, enlarged nodes** (1-5 cm), may suppurate (10-40%)
- **Systemic symptoms:** Low-grade fever (30%), malaise, headache
- **Self-limited:** Nodes resolve over 2-4 months

**Atypical CSD:**
- **Parinaud oculoglandular syndrome:** Granulomatous conjunctivitis + preauricular lymphadenopathy (direct inoculation to eye)
- **Neuroretinitis:** Vision loss, optic disc swelling + macular star exudate
- **Encephalopathy:** Altered mental status, seizures (rare, children)
- **Hepatosplenic:** Granulomas in liver/spleen, fever, abdominal pain
- **Osteomyelitis:** Rare

**Bacillary Angiomatosis (BA) - Immunocompromised (HIV):**
- **Cutaneous:** Red/purple papules/nodules (resemble Kaposi sarcoma or pyogenic granuloma)
- **Visceral:** Liver (peliosis hepatis), spleen, bone, lymph nodes
- **Bacteremia** (*Bartonella quintana* more common than *B. henselae*)

**Endocarditis:**
- ***Bartonella* = common cause** of culture-negative endocarditis
- **Subacute presentation**, large vegetations
- ***B. quintana* (body louse-borne) > *B. henselae***
- **Serology** for diagnosis

### **Diagnosis:**

**Cat Scratch Disease:**
- **Clinical diagnosis** (cat exposure + regional lymphadenopathy)
- **Serology:** IgG/IgM antibodies (>90% sensitive) - supports diagnosis but not always needed
- **PCR:** Lymph node aspirate or biopsy (if atypical presentation)
- **Histopathology:** Granulomas with stellate necrosis (suggestive)
- **Culture:** Very difficult (requires prolonged incubation, specialized media)

**Bacillary Angiomatosis:**
- **Biopsy:** Warthin-Starry silver stain (shows clumps of bacilli)
- **PCR/culture:** From tissue
- **Serology:** Less useful (HIV patients may not mount antibody response)

**Endocarditis:**
- **Blood cultures:** Typically negative (fastidious organism)
- **Serology:** High titers (>1:800) suggestive
- **PCR:** Excised valve tissue

### **Treatment:**

**Uncomplicated CSD (Immunocompetent):**
- **Observation** (self-limited, resolves in 2-4 months)
- **Azithromycin 500mg × 1 → 250mg daily × 4 days** (or 10 mg/kg × 1 → 5 mg/kg daily × 4 days in children) - **shortens duration** of lymphadenopathy by ~50%
- **Indications for antibiotics:** Severe symptoms, cosmetic concerns, atypical manifestations

**Atypical CSD (Neuroretinitis, Encephalopathy, Hepatosplenic):**
- **Doxycycline 100mg PO BID + rifampin 300mg PO BID** × 4-6 weeks OR
- **Azithromycin** + **rifampin**

**Bacillary Angiomatosis (HIV/Immunocompromised):**
- **Erythromycin 500mg PO QID** OR **doxycycline 100mg PO BID** × 3-4 months (minimum)
- **Longer duration** if bone/visceral involvement or slow response
- **Treat until CD4 >200** (or immune reconstitution)

**Endocarditis:**
- **Doxycycline 100mg PO/IV BID + gentamicin 1 mg/kg IV q8h** × 6 weeks
- **Alternative:** Azithromycin or rifampin instead of gentamicin
- **Often requires valve replacement** (large vegetations)

**Aspiration of Lymph Node:**
- **If large/fluctuant:** Needle aspiration (NOT incision/drainage - can lead to chronic draining sinus)

## Key Points

### **Differentiation:**

| Feature | CSD (Immunocompetent) | Bacillary Angiomatosis (HIV) |
|---------|----------------------|------------------------------|
| **Host** | Immunocompetent | **HIV, CD4 <100** |
| **Organism** | ***B. henselae*** | ***B. henselae*** or ***B. quintana*** |
| **Lesions** | Lymphadenopathy | **Vascular proliferative lesions** (papules/nodules) |
| **Course** | **Self-limited** | **Progressive** if untreated |
| **Treatment** | Observation or azithromycin × 5 days | **Doxycycline or erythromycin × 3-4 months** |

### **Bacillary Angiomatosis Mimics:**
- **Kaposi sarcoma** (HIV, HHV-8)
- **Pyogenic granuloma**
- **Hemangioma**
- **Histopathology distinguishes:** BA shows vascular proliferation + clumps of bacilli (Warthin-Starry stain)

### **Culture-Negative Endocarditis:**
- ***Bartonella* = common cause** (along with *Coxiella burnetii*, *Brucella*, HACEK, nutritionally variant streptococci)
- **High-titer serology** (>1:800) + clinical/echocardiographic findings → presumptive diagnosis

### **Prevention:**
- **Avoid rough play with cats** (especially kittens)
- **Wash scratches/bites** immediately
- **Flea control** in cats
- **Immunocompromised:** Avoid cat scratches, consider avoiding cat ownership

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **CSD:** Cat scratch/bite → papule → regional lymphadenopathy (self-limited)
- **Azithromycin 500mg × 1 → 250mg × 4 days** shortens lymphadenopathy duration by ~50%
- **Bacillary angiomatosis (HIV):** Red/purple vascular nodules → doxycycline × 3-4 months
- **Parinaud syndrome:** Granulomatous conjunctivitis + preauricular adenopathy
- **Endocarditis:** Culture-negative, high serology titers, doxycycline + gentamicin × 6 weeks
- **Do NOT incise lymph nodes** (risk of chronic draining sinus)
