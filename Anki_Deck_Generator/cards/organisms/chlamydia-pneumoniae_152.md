## Question

What is the clinical presentation and treatment of *C. pneumoniae* pneumonia? Use the **"Persistent Cough, Young Adult, Treat as Atypical CAP (Macrolide/Doxy × 7-10d)"** framework.

## Answer

### ***Chlamydia pneumoniae* Overview:**

**Transmission:** Respiratory droplets, **person-to-person**

**Epidemiology:**
- **Common** in school-age children, young adults, military recruits
- **Outbreaks** in closed settings (schools, nursing homes)
- **Accounts for 5-10%** of community-acquired pneumonia

### **Clinical Presentation:**

**Community-Acquired Pneumonia:**
- **Biphasic illness:** Pharyngitis → pneumonia (1-2 weeks later)
- **Persistent dry cough** (can last weeks)
- **Hoarseness** (pharyngitis, laryngitis)
- **Gradual onset** (similar to *Mycoplasma*)
- **Minimal systemic symptoms:** Low-grade fever, malaise
- **CXR:** Patchy infiltrates (often mild)

**Other Syndromes:**
- **Bronchitis** (more common than pneumonia)
- **Pharyngitis, sinusitis**

### **Diagnosis:**

**Clinical:** Typically diagnosed clinically (atypical CAP syndrome)

**Laboratory:**
- **PCR** (respiratory panel) - preferred if available
- **Serology:** IgM (acute), 4-fold IgG rise (not useful for acute management)

### **Treatment:**

**Preferred:**
- **Azithromycin 500mg PO × 1, then 250mg daily × 4 days** (total 5 days)
- **Doxycycline 100mg PO BID × 7-10 days**

**Alternative:**
- **Levofloxacin 750mg PO daily × 5 days**
- **Clarithromycin 500mg PO BID × 7 days**

**Duration:** 7-10 days (doxycycline/clarithromycin), 5 days (azithromycin/levofloxacin)

## Key Points

### **Biphasic Illness:**
- **Phase 1:** Pharyngitis, hoarseness (1-2 weeks)
- **Phase 2:** Pneumonia, persistent cough
- **Helps differentiate** from *Mycoplasma* (no pharyngitis)

### **Persistent Cough:**
- **Hallmark:** Dry, persistent cough lasting weeks
- **Similar to *Mycoplasma*** but with hoarseness

### **β-Lactams Ineffective:**
- **Intracellular organism** (like *Mycoplasma*, *Legionella*)
- **Always cover atypical pathogens** in CAP empiric regimen

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Biphasic illness:** Pharyngitis → pneumonia (1-2 weeks later)
- **Persistent dry cough + hoarseness**
- **Young adult, gradual onset**
- **Macrolide or doxycycline × 7-10 days**
- **β-lactams ineffective** (intracellular)
