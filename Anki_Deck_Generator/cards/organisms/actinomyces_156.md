## Question

What are the key clinical presentations of *Actinomyces* and treatment? Use the **"Cervicofacial, Pelvic (IUD), Pulmonary → Sulfur Granules → High-Dose Penicillin × Months"** framework.

## Answer

### ***Actinomyces* Species Overview:**

**Organism:**
- **Anaerobic/microaerophilic, Gram-positive, branching filamentous bacteria**
- **Normal oral flora** (gingival crevices, tonsils, GI tract, female genital tract)

**Transmission:** Endogenous (disruption of mucosal barrier)

**Risk Factors:**
- **Poor dental hygiene, dental procedures** (cervicofacial)
- **IUD use** (pelvic)
- **Aspiration risk** (pulmonary)
- **Abdominal surgery, trauma** (intra-abdominal)

### **Clinical Syndromes:**

**Cervicofacial Actinomycosis ("Lumpy Jaw") - 50% of Cases:**

**Presentation:**
- **Slowly progressive, painless swelling** of jaw/neck (weeks to months)
- **"Woody" induration** (firm, non-fluctuant mass)
- **Draining sinuses** (fistulas to skin) with **sulfur granules**
- **Crosses tissue planes** (does not respect fascial boundaries)
- **No lymphadenopathy** (distinguishes from malignancy/TB)

**Trigger:** Dental procedure, trauma, poor oral hygiene

**Thoracic (Pulmonary) Actinomycosis - 15-20%:**

**Presentation:**
- **Chronic pneumonia** (weeks to months)
- **Fever, cough, weight loss** (mimics TB, malignancy)
- **Chest wall involvement** (crosses pleura → empyema, chest wall abscess, draining sinuses)

**Imaging:**
- **Consolidation, cavitation, pleural effusion**
- **Chest wall mass** (late complication)

**Trigger:** Aspiration (alcoholism, poor dentition)

**Abdominopelvic Actinomycosis - 20%:**

**Intra-abdominal:**
- **Abdominal mass, fistulas, abscess**
- **Mimics malignancy, IBD, appendicitis**
- **Trigger:** Abdominal surgery, perforated viscus, appendicitis

**Pelvic (Women):**
- **IUD-associated** (chronic IUD use >1 year)
- **Pelvic mass, abscess, tubo-ovarian abscess**
- **Mimics pelvic malignancy, PID**

### **Diagnosis:**

**Histopathology (Gold Standard):**
- **Sulfur granules:** Yellow aggregates of organisms (NOT actually sulfur)
- **Gram stain:** Gram-positive, branching filaments with PMNs
- **Culture:** Slow-growing, requires anaerobic conditions (alert lab if suspected)

**Imaging:**
- **CT/MRI:** Mass lesion, abscess, fistula formation

**Clinical Clue:** Chronic mass + draining sinuses + sulfur granules

### **Treatment:**

**High-Dose Penicillin (Preferred):**
- **Penicillin G 18-24 million units IV daily (divided q4h) × 2-6 weeks**
- **Then:** **Penicillin VK 500mg PO QID** OR **amoxicillin 500mg PO TID × 6-12 months**

**Alternative (Penicillin Allergy):**
- **Ceftriaxone 2g IV daily × 2-6 weeks**
- **Then:** **Doxycycline 100mg PO BID** OR **clindamycin 300mg PO QID × 6-12 months**

**Duration:**
- **Total 6-12 months** (prolonged to prevent relapse)
- **Longer for extensive disease** (thoracic, CNS)

**Adjunctive:**
- **Surgical debridement/drainage** often needed (extensive disease, abscess, foreign body removal [IUD])

**Key Points for IUD-Associated:**
- **Remove IUD**
- **Antibiotics + removal** usually curative (surgery rarely needed)

## Key Points

### **Sulfur Granules:**
- **Yellow aggregates** (colonies of *Actinomyces*)
- **NOT sulfur** (bacterial aggregates with calcium phosphate)
- **Seen in drainage/pus** (macroscopic)
- **Pathognomonic** for actinomycosis

### **"Lumpy Jaw" (Cervicofacial):**
- **Painless, woody induration** of jaw/neck
- **Draining sinuses** to skin
- **No lymphadenopathy** (helps exclude malignancy/TB)
- **After dental procedure** or trauma

### **Crosses Tissue Planes:**
- **Does NOT respect fascial boundaries** (unlike most infections)
- **Forms fistulas** to adjacent structures (skin, chest wall, pleura)
- **Mimics malignancy** (chronic mass, invasive)

### **IUD-Associated Pelvic Actinomycosis:**
- **Chronic IUD use** (>1 year)
- **Pelvic mass, TOA** (mimics malignancy, PID)
- **Treatment:** Remove IUD + prolonged antibiotics (usually no surgery needed)

### **Long Duration Treatment:**
- **6-12 months** (prevents relapse)
- **High-dose penicillin initially** (IV × 2-6 weeks)
- **Then oral penicillin/amoxicillin** (months)

### **Anaerobic Culture Required:**
- **Alert lab** if *Actinomyces* suspected (requires anaerobic incubation)
- **Slow-growing** (days to weeks)
- **Often mixed infection** (with other oral anaerobes)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Cervicofacial ("lumpy jaw")**, **pelvic (IUD)**, **pulmonary** (aspiration)
- **Sulfur granules** in drainage = diagnostic clue
- **High-dose penicillin × 2-6 weeks IV, then oral × 6-12 months**
- **Crosses tissue planes** (does NOT respect fascia)
- **IUD:** Remove + antibiotics (surgery rarely needed)
- **Mimics malignancy** (chronic mass, invasive growth)
