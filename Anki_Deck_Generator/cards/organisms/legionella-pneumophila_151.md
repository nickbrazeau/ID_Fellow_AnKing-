## Question

How is *Legionella* pneumonia diagnosed and treated? Use the **"Water Source, Hyponatremia, Urinary Antigen → Levofloxacin/Azithro × 5-10d"** framework.

## Answer

### ***Legionella pneumophila* Overview:**

**Transmission:**
- **Inhalation** of aerosolized water containing *Legionella*
- **Sources:** Cooling towers, hot tubs, showers, decorative fountains, mist machines
- **NOT person-to-person**

**Risk Factors:**
- **Age >50 years, smoking**
- **COPD, chronic lung disease**
- **Immunosuppression** (transplant, steroids, TNF-α inhibitors)
- **Diabetes, chronic kidney/liver disease**

### **Clinical Syndromes:**

**Legionnaires' Disease (Pneumonia):**

**Presentation:**
- **Acute onset** high fever (>40°C), chills
- **Productive cough** (can be dry initially)
- **GI symptoms:** Diarrhea (25-50%), nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain
- **Neurologic:** Confusion, headache
- **Relative bradycardia** (pulse-temperature dissociation)

**Laboratory:**
- **Hyponatremia** (SIADH) - present in ~50%
- **Elevated transaminases, CRP**
- **Lymphopenia** (can mimic viral infection)

**CXR:**
- **Rapidly progressive infiltrates** (unilateral → bilateral)
- **Consolidation, cavitation** (especially immunocompromised)
- **Pleural effusion** (30-50%)

**Pontiac Fever:**
- **Self-limited febrile illness** (flu-like, NO pneumonia)
- **Incubation:** 1-2 days (vs 2-10 days for Legionnaires')
- **Resolves in 2-5 days** without treatment

### **Diagnosis:**

**Urinary Antigen Test (Preferred):**
- **Detects L. pneumophila serogroup 1** (70% of cases)
- **Sensitivity 70-90%, specificity >95%**
- **Positive within 24h**, remains positive for weeks
- **Advantage:** Rapid, non-invasive, unaffected by antibiotics

**Culture:**
- **Buffered charcoal yeast extract (BCYE) agar** with antibiotics
- **Gold standard** (detects all species/serogroups)
- **Slow:** 3-5 days
- **Advantage:** Detects non-serogroup 1, allows susceptibility testing

**PCR:**
- **Respiratory panel** (sputum, BAL)
- **Rapid, detects all *Legionella* species**

**Serology:**
- **Not useful for acute diagnosis** (requires convalescent sample 3-6 weeks later)

### **Treatment:**

**Preferred Agents:**

**Community-Acquired:**
- **Levofloxacin 750mg IV/PO daily × 5-7 days** (preferred, high lung penetration)
- **Azithromycin 500mg IV/PO daily × 3 days, then 250mg daily × 5 days** (total 7-10 days)

**Severe/Nosocomial:**
- **Levofloxacin 750mg IV daily × 7-10 days** (preferred)
- OR **azithromycin 500mg IV daily × 10-14 days**

**Alternative:**
- **Moxifloxacin 400mg IV/PO daily × 5-10 days**

**Duration:**
- **Immunocompetent:** 5-7 days (fluoroquinolone), 10 days (macrolide)
- **Immunocompromised:** 14-21 days

**Note:**
- **β-lactams ineffective** (intracellular organism, poor penetration)
- **Always cover empirically** in severe CAP (fluoroquinolone or macrolide)

## Key Points

### **Hyponatremia (SIADH):**
- **Present in ~50%** of *Legionella* pneumonia
- **Due to SIADH** (syndrome of inappropriate ADH secretion)
- **Useful clinical clue:** Pneumonia + hyponatremia (Na+ <130) → consider *Legionella*

### **Relative Bradycardia:**
- **Pulse-temperature dissociation** (fever without expected tachycardia)
- **Also seen in:** Typhoid fever, drug fever, atypical pathogens
- **Not specific**, but suggestive in appropriate context

### **Why Urinary Antigen Preferred:**
- **Rapid** (results in hours)
- **Non-invasive** (urine, not sputum)
- **Remains positive** for weeks (can diagnose even after antibiotics started)
- **Limitation:** Only detects serogroup 1 (misses 30% of cases)

### **Water Source Outbreaks:**
- **Common sources:** Cooling towers (HVAC systems), hot tubs, hotel/hospital water systems
- **Investigate outbreaks:** Culture water systems, implement disinfection protocols

### **Immunocompromised Patients:**
- **Higher risk:** Transplant patients, TNF-α inhibitors, high-dose steroids
- **More severe disease:** Cavitation, extrapulmonary involvement (rare)
- **Longer treatment:** 14-21 days

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Severe CAP + hyponatremia + GI symptoms** → suspect *Legionella*
- **Urinary antigen** (rapid, preferred) + culture (gold standard, slow)
- **Levofloxacin 750mg daily × 5-7d** (preferred) OR **azithromycin × 7-10d**
- **β-lactams ineffective** (intracellular)
- **Water source exposure** (cooling towers, hot tubs, showers)
- **Relative bradycardia** (pulse-temp dissociation)
