## Question

What are the key clinical syndromes caused by adenovirus? Use the **"Pharyngoconjunctival Fever, Hemorrhagic Cystitis (BMT), Severe in Immunocompromised → Cidofovir"** framework.

## Answer

### **Adenovirus Overview:**

**Transmission:** Respiratory droplets, fecal-oral, contaminated water, fomites

**Epidemiology:**
- **>50 serotypes** (cause different syndromes)
- **Common in children** (URI, gastroenteritis)
- **Outbreaks:** Schools, military barracks, swimming pools

### **Clinical Syndromes:**

**Pharyngoconjunctival Fever:**
- **Fever + pharyngitis + conjunctivitis** (bilateral, non-purulent)
- **Common in children** (summer camps, pools)
- **Self-limited** (5-7 days)

**Epidemic Keratoconjunctivitis:**
- **Severe conjunctivitis** with **corneal involvement** (photophobia, foreign body sensation)
- **Highly contagious** (contaminated ophthalmology equipment, healthcare workers)
- **Can last weeks**, vision impairment possible

**Respiratory Infections:**
- **URI:** Rhinorrhea, pharyngitis (common in children)
- **Pneumonia:** Can be severe (especially serotypes 3, 4, 7, 14)
  - **Military recruits** at risk (crowding)
  - **Bilateral infiltrates**, prolonged illness

**Gastroenteritis:**
- **Watery diarrhea** (serotypes 40, 41)
- **Common in children <2 years**
- **Second most common viral gastroenteritis** (after rotavirus/norovirus)

**Hemorrhagic Cystitis:**
- **Common in stem cell transplant recipients** (post-engraftment, day 10-40)
- **Gross hematuria, dysuria, urgency**
- **Can be severe** (bladder outlet obstruction from clots)

**Hepatitis (Immunocompromised):**
- **Transplant recipients** (liver, stem cell)
- **Elevated transaminases**, hepatomegaly
- **Can be fulminant**

**Disseminated Disease (Severe Immunocompromised):**
- **Transplant (especially stem cell), T-cell deficiencies**
- **Pneumonia, hepatitis, colitis, nephritis**
- **High mortality** (30-60%)

### **Diagnosis:**

**PCR:** Respiratory panel, stool, blood (quantitative viral load in transplant), urine

**Antigen Detection:** Rapid tests (conjunctival swabs, stool)

**Culture:** Slow (not routinely used)

### **Treatment:**

**Immunocompetent:**
- **Supportive care ONLY**
- **Self-limited** in most cases

**Immunocompromised (Severe Disease):**

**Cidofovir:**
- **1 mg/kg IV weekly** (with probenecid + saline hydration to reduce nephrotoxicity)
- **OR:** **5 mg/kg IV every 2 weeks**
- **Used for:** Disseminated disease, severe pneumonia, hepatitis in transplant
- **Nephrotoxic** (dose-related)
- **Limited evidence** (no RCTs, case series/registry data)

**Reduce Immunosuppression:**
- **Taper IS** if possible (especially in transplant recipients)

**Brincidofovir (Experimental):**
- **Oral lipid conjugate** of cidofovir (less nephrotoxic)
- **Not FDA-approved** for adenovirus (available for smallpox only)
- **Use via compassionate use/clinical trial**

**IVIG:**
- **Consider** in severe cases (no strong evidence)

### **Prevention:**

**Live Oral Vaccine:**
- **Military recruits only** (serotypes 4, 7)
- **Not available** for general public

**Infection Control:**
- **Hand hygiene** (soap and water, alcohol-based gel less effective)
- **Disinfection:** Bleach-based (adenovirus resistant to many disinfectants)
- **Isolation:** Contact precautions (healthcare settings)

## Key Points

### **Pharyngoconjunctival Fever:**
- **Triad:** Fever + pharyngitis + bilateral conjunctivitis
- **Summer camps, pools** (waterborne transmission)
- **Self-limited**

### **Hemorrhagic Cystitis in Transplant:**
- **Stem cell transplant** (post-engraftment, day 10-40)
- **Gross hematuria** (can be severe)
- **Usually self-limited**, supportive care

### **Disseminated Disease = High Mortality:**
- **Transplant recipients** (stem cell > solid organ)
- **Pneumonia + hepatitis + colitis**
- **Mortality 30-60%**
- **Consider cidofovir + reduce IS**

### **Cidofovir Nephrotoxic:**
- **Requires probenecid + saline hydration**
- **Monitor renal function** closely
- **Dose-dependent nephrotoxicity**

### **Infection Control:**
- **Soap and water** (alcohol gel less effective)
- **Bleach-based disinfectants** (resistant to many cleaners)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Pharyngoconjunctival fever** = fever + pharyngitis + bilateral conjunctivitis (summer camps)
- **Hemorrhagic cystitis** = common in stem cell transplant (post-engraftment)
- **Disseminated disease** in immunocompromised (transplant) → cidofovir ± reduce IS
- **Supportive care** for immunocompetent
- **Cidofovir nephrotoxic** (probenecid + hydration required)
