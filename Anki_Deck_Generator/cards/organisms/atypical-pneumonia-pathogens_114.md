## Question

What are the atypical pneumonia pathogens and treatment? Use the **"Mycoplasma, Legionella, Chlamydia → Macrolides or Fluoroquinolones"** framework.

## Answer

### **Atypical Pneumonia Pathogens:**

| Organism | Clinical Features | Diagnosis | Treatment |
|----------|------------------|-----------|-----------|
| ***Mycoplasma pneumoniae*** | **Young adults**, **gradual onset**, dry cough, bullous myringitis, **extrapulmonary** (hemolytic anemia, erythema multiforme) | **Serology** (IgM), PCR | **Azithromycin 500mg × 1 → 250mg daily × 4 days** OR **doxycycline 100mg BID × 7-10 days** |
| ***Legionella pneumophila*** | **Severe pneumonia**, GI symptoms (diarrhea), **hyponatremia**, ↑LFTs, confusion, relative bradycardia | **Urinary antigen** (L. pneumophila serogroup 1), sputum culture on BCYE agar | **Levofloxacin 750mg daily × 7-10 days** (preferred) OR **azithromycin 500mg daily** |
| ***Chlamydia (Chlamydophila) pneumoniae*** | **Mild pneumonia**, pharyngitis, hoarseness, young adults | **Serology** (retrospective), PCR | **Azithromycin** OR **doxycycline × 10-14 days** |
| ***Chlamydia psittaci*** (Psittacosis) | **Bird exposure** (parrots), pneumonia, hepatosplenomegaly, relative bradycardia | **Serology**, PCR | **Doxycycline 100mg BID × 10-14 days** |

### **Key Clinical Features:**

**Mycoplasma pneumoniae:**
- **"Walking pneumonia"** (mild, outpatient)
- **Extrapulmonary manifestations:**
  - **Cold agglutinin hemolytic anemia** (IgM against RBCs at cold temps)
  - **Erythema multiforme**, **Stevens-Johnson syndrome**
  - **Bullous myringitis** (blisters on tympanic membrane)
  - **Encephalitis**, Guillain-Barré syndrome
- **X-ray:** Worse than clinical exam

**Legionella pneumophila:**
- **Severe pneumonia** (often ICU admission)
- **Risk factors:** Immunosuppressed, elderly, smokers, chronic lung disease
- **Extrapulmonary:**
  - **Hyponatremia** (SIADH) - classic finding
  - **GI symptoms** (diarrhea, nausea)
  - **Elevated LFTs**, **elevated CPK**
  - **Confusion**, **relative bradycardia** (fever-pulse dissociation)
- **Source:** Water systems (cooling towers, hot tubs, fountains)

**Chlamydia pneumoniae:**
- **Mild pneumonia**, often **pharyngitis + hoarseness**
- **Young adults**, community outbreaks

**Psittacosis (*C. psittaci*):**
- **Bird exposure** (parrots, pigeons, poultry)
- Pneumonia + **hepatosplenomegaly**
- **Relative bradycardia**

## Key Points

### **Diagnostic Clues:**

***Mycoplasma*:**
- **Cold agglutinins** (50% of cases) - agglutination of RBCs at 4°C
- **Erythema multiforme** or **bullous myringitis**
- **Younger patients**, gradual onset

***Legionella*:**
- **Hyponatremia** (most specific finding)
- **GI symptoms** (diarrhea)
- **Urinary antigen** (only detects serogroup 1, 70-80% of cases)

### **Treatment:**

**First-Line:**
- ***Mycoplasma/Chlamydia*:** **Azithromycin** OR **doxycycline**
- ***Legionella*:** **Fluoroquinolone** (levofloxacin, moxifloxacin) preferred over macrolides

**Why Fluoroquinolones for *Legionella*:**
- **Better intracellular penetration** (Legionella is intracellular)
- **Lower mortality** vs macrolides in severe cases

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- ***Mycoplasma*:** Young adults, cold agglutinins, erythema multiforme, macrolides/doxycycline
- ***Legionella*:** Hyponatremia, GI symptoms, urinary antigen, fluoroquinolones preferred
- ***Chlamydia pneumoniae*:** Pharyngitis + pneumonia, young adults
- **Psittacosis:** Bird exposure, doxycycline
- **All atypicals:** Resistant to β-lactams (no cell wall)
- **Empiric CAP therapy:** Often includes atypical coverage (azithromycin or fluoroquinolone)
