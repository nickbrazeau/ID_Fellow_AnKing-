## Question

How is cryptosporidiosis diagnosed and managed? Use the **"Watery Diarrhea (AIDS CD4 <100), Acid-Fast Oocysts → Restore Immunity (ART), ± Nitazoxanide"** framework.

## Answer

### ***Cryptosporidium* Species Overview:**

**Transmission:**
- **Fecal-oral:** Contaminated water (swimming pools, lakes, municipal water), food, person-to-person
- **Highly infectious:** Low inoculum (10-100 oocysts)
- **Chlorine-resistant** (oocysts survive routine water chlorination)

**Epidemiology:**
- **Immunocompetent:** Self-limited diarrhea (1-2 weeks)
- **Immunocompromised (AIDS CD4 <100):** Chronic, severe, life-threatening

### **Clinical Presentation:**

**Immunocompetent:**
- **Watery diarrhea** (profuse, non-bloody)
- **Abdominal cramping, nausea**
- **Low-grade fever**
- **Self-limited** (1-2 weeks)

**AIDS (CD4 <100):**
- **Chronic watery diarrhea** (>1 month, can be >10 L/day)
- **Severe dehydration, weight loss, wasting**
- **Malabsorption**
- **Cholangitis:** Sclerosing cholangitis (RUQ pain, ↑Alk phos, biliary strictures on ERCP)

**Other Immunocompromised:**
- **Transplant, chemotherapy:** Can have chronic diarrhea (less severe than AIDS)

### **Diagnosis:**

**Stool Testing:**
- **Modified acid-fast stain:** Oocysts (4-6 μm, pink/red, round)
- **DFA (direct fluorescent antibody):** Sensitive, specific
- **PCR (GI pathogen panel):** Most sensitive (preferred)
- **Antigen EIA:** Moderate sensitivity

**Note:** May require **multiple stool samples** (3 specimens) - oocyst shedding can be intermittent

**Imaging (Biliary Disease):**
- **ERCP:** Sclerosing cholangitis (biliary strictures, papillary stenosis)

### **Treatment:**

**Immunocompetent:**
- **Supportive care ONLY** (hydration, electrolytes)
- **Self-limited** (1-2 weeks)
- **Nitazoxanide optional** (may shorten duration, but not routinely needed)

**AIDS / Immunocompromised:**

**Primary Strategy: Restore Immune Function:**
- **Start/optimize ART** (for HIV)
- **Immune reconstitution = MOST effective treatment**
- **Symptoms resolve** as CD4 rises >100

**Nitazoxanide:**
- **500mg PO BID × 3 days** (immunocompetent)
- **500mg PO BID × 14 days** (AIDS/immunocompromised, may need longer)
- **Modest efficacy** in AIDS (better if CD4 >50)
- **NOT curative** in severe immunosuppression (need immune recovery)

**Supportive Care:**
- **Aggressive hydration** (IV fluids if severe)
- **Nutritional support**
- **Antimotility agents:** **Avoid** (can worsen symptoms, prolong infection)

**Biliary Cryptosporidiosis:**
- **ERCP with sphincterotomy** (relieve obstruction)
- **ART** (immune restoration)

**Alternative / Adjunctive (Limited Evidence):**
- **Paromomycin 500-750mg PO QID** (aminoglycoside, not absorbed)
- **Azithromycin 600mg PO daily** (may have modest benefit)
- **Often used in combination** with nitazoxanide in severe cases

## Key Points

### **Acid-Fast Oocysts:**
- **Modified acid-fast stain:** Pink/red oocysts (4-6 μm)
- **Diagnostic clue** (differentiates from *Giardia*, *Cyclospora*)

### **Chlorine-Resistant:**
- **Oocysts survive** routine water chlorination
- **Outbreaks** from swimming pools, municipal water
- **Prevention:** Boil water OR filters (<1 μm pore size)

### **CD4 <100 = Chronic Diarrhea:**
- **Profuse watery diarrhea** (>1 month, can be >10 L/day)
- **Wasting, severe dehydration**
- **ART = MOST effective treatment** (restore immunity)

### **Sclerosing Cholangitis:**
- **AIDS patients:** RUQ pain, ↑Alk phos
- **ERCP:** Biliary strictures, papillary stenosis
- **Treatment:** ERCP sphincterotomy + ART

### **Nitazoxanide Limited in AIDS:**
- **Modest efficacy** (works better if CD4 >50)
- **NOT curative** in severe immunosuppression
- **ART essential** for cure

### **Outbreaks:**
- **Swimming pools, water parks** (chlorine-resistant)
- **Daycare centers** (person-to-person)
- **Contaminated municipal water** (Milwaukee 1993: 400,000 cases)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Watery diarrhea** (immunocompetent = self-limited, AIDS CD4 <100 = chronic/severe)
- **Acid-fast oocysts** (4-6 μm) on modified acid-fast stain or PCR
- **Chlorine-resistant** (outbreaks from pools, water)
- **AIDS:** ART = most effective (restore immunity), nitazoxanide modest benefit
- **Sclerosing cholangitis** in AIDS (RUQ pain, ↑Alk phos) → ERCP + ART
