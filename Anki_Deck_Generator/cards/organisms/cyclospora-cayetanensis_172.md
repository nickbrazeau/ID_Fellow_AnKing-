## Question

How is cyclosporiasis diagnosed and treated? Use the **"Watery Diarrhea (Travelers, Raspberry Outbreaks), Acid-Fast Oocysts (Larger than Crypto) → TMP-SMX"** framework.

## Answer

### ***Cyclospora cayetanensis* Overview:**

**Transmission:**
- **Fecal-oral:** Contaminated food (fresh produce, berries, basil, lettuce), water
- **NOT directly person-to-person** (oocysts require days-weeks in environment to sporulate and become infectious)

**Epidemiology:**
- **Endemic:** Tropical/subtropical regions (Latin America, Southeast Asia, Nepal, Haiti)
- **Travelers' diarrhea** (common in travelers to endemic areas)
- **Outbreaks:** Fresh produce (raspberries, basil, salad greens)

### **Clinical Presentation:**

**Immunocompetent:**
- **Watery diarrhea** (explosive, non-bloody)
- **Prolonged, relapsing** (average 7 weeks if untreated, can wax/wane)
- **Fatigue, anorexia, weight loss** (prominent)
- **Abdominal cramping, bloating, nausea**
- **Low-grade fever**

**Immunocompromised (AIDS, transplant):**
- **More severe, prolonged**
- **Chronic diarrhea, wasting**
- **Relapse common** (may require prolonged/maintenance therapy)

### **Diagnosis:**

**Stool Testing:**
- **Modified acid-fast stain:** Oocysts (8-10 μm, **larger than *Cryptosporidium* 4-6 μm**), variably acid-fast (some pink, some clear)
- **Autofluorescence:** Oocysts fluoresce blue-green under UV (254-365 nm) - diagnostic clue
- **PCR (GI pathogen panel):** Most sensitive (preferred)

**Microscopy Features:**
- **Oocysts:** 8-10 μm (twice size of *Cryptosporidium*)
- **Sporulated oocysts:** 2 sporocysts (each with 2 sporozoites) - seen after maturation in environment

**Note:** May require **multiple stool samples** (3 specimens)

### **Treatment:**

**TMP-SMX (Highly Effective):**
- **TMP-SMX DS (160/800mg) PO BID × 7-10 days** (immunocompetent)
- **AIDS/immunocompromised:** **TMP-SMX DS BID × 10 days**
  - **Higher relapse rate** → consider longer therapy (14 days) or maintenance (TMP-SMX SS or DS 3×/week)

**Sulfa Allergy (NO Good Alternative):**
- **Ciprofloxacin 500mg PO BID × 7 days** (less effective, ~60% response vs. >90% for TMP-SMX)
- **Nitazoxanide 500mg PO BID × 7-14 days** (limited data, variable efficacy)

**Supportive Care:**
- **Hydration, electrolytes**

**Prevention:**
- **Wash produce** (though oocysts resistant to routine washing)
- **Avoid raw produce** in endemic areas (travelers)

## Key Points

### **Larger Oocysts than *Cryptosporidium*:**
- ***Cyclospora*:** 8-10 μm
- ***Cryptosporidium*:** 4-6 μm
- **Key distinction** on microscopy

### **Autofluorescence:**
- **Blue-green fluorescence** under UV (254-365 nm)
- **Diagnostic clue** (distinguishes from *Cryptosporidium*)

### **Prolonged, Relapsing Diarrhea:**
- **Average 7 weeks** if untreated (vs. 1-2 weeks for *Cryptosporidium* in immunocompetent)
- **Wax and wane** (relapsing/remitting pattern)
- **Prominent fatigue, weight loss**

### **TMP-SMX = Highly Effective:**
- **>90% cure rate** (unlike *Cryptosporidium*, which lacks effective treatment in AIDS)
- **TMP-SMX DS BID × 7-10 days**
- **NO good alternative** for sulfa allergy (ciprofloxacin less effective)

### **Raspberry Outbreaks:**
- **Classic association:** Fresh raspberries (imported from endemic areas)
- **Also:** Basil, lettuce, salad greens

### **NOT Directly Contagious:**
- **Oocysts NOT immediately infectious** (require days-weeks to sporulate in environment)
- **Foodborne/waterborne** transmission

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Prolonged watery diarrhea** (travelers to endemic areas, fresh produce outbreaks)
- **Acid-fast oocysts (8-10 μm)** larger than *Crypto* (4-6 μm)
- **Autofluorescence** (blue-green under UV) = diagnostic clue
- **TMP-SMX DS BID × 7-10 days** (highly effective, >90% cure)
- **Raspberries, basil** (classic outbreak sources)
