## Question

How is giardiasis diagnosed and treated? Use the **"Stool Antigen (Best Test), Metronidazole or Tinidazole"** framework.

## Answer

### ***Giardia lamblia* (Giardia intestinalis) Overview:**

**Transmission:**
- **Fecal-oral** route
- **Contaminated water** (streams, lakes, poorly treated water) - "hiker's/backpacker's diarrhea"
- **Daycare centers** (person-to-person)
- **Food-borne** (contaminated food)
- **Sexual transmission** (oral-anal contact)

**Geographic:**
- **Worldwide** (most common intestinal parasite in US)
- **Endemic:** Developing countries, wilderness areas

### **Clinical Presentation:**

**Acute Giardiasis:**
- **Incubation:** 1-2 weeks
- **Diarrhea:** **Watery, greasy, foul-smelling**, frothy
- **Flatulence** (bloating, excessive gas)
- **Abdominal cramps**, nausea
- **Weight loss** (malabsorption)
- **No blood or mucus** in stool (non-invasive)
- **No eosinophilia**

**Chronic Giardiasis:**
- **Persistent diarrhea** (weeks to months)
- **Malabsorption** (fat, lactose, vitamin B12)
- **Weight loss**, **fatigue**
- **Lactose intolerance** (can persist after treatment)

**Asymptomatic Carriers:**
- Common (25-50% of infected individuals)
- Can shed cysts, transmit infection

### **Diagnosis:**

| Test | Sensitivity | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **Stool antigen (EIA/immunofluorescence)** | **>90%** | **Best test** (single specimen), detects *Giardia* antigen |
| **Stool O&P** | **50-70%** (single sample), **>90%** (3 samples) | Look for **trophozoites** or **cysts**, requires multiple samples |
| **Duodenal aspirate/biopsy** | High | **Gold standard** (rarely needed), for refractory cases |
| **PCR (molecular)** | **>95%** | Most sensitive, not widely available |

**Best Approach:**
- **Stool antigen** (EIA) - **single specimen**, high sensitivity, test of choice

### **Treatment:**

**First-Line:**
- **Metronidazole** 250-500mg PO TID × 5-7 days OR
- **Tinidazole** 2g PO × **1 dose** (single-dose, preferred if available)

**Alternatives:**
- **Nitazoxanide** 500mg PO BID × 3 days
- **Paromomycin** 25-35 mg/kg/day PO divided TID × 5-10 days (pregnancy-safe, poorly absorbed)

**Refractory Giardiasis:**
- **Longer course:** Metronidazole × 10-14 days OR
- **Combination:** Metronidazole + quinacrine OR albendazole
- **Consider:** Immune deficiency (HIV, IgA deficiency, CVID), re-infection

### **Special Populations:**

**Pregnancy:**
- **Defer treatment** if asymptomatic (wait until 2nd/3rd trimester or postpartum)
- **If treatment needed:** **Paromomycin** 25-35 mg/kg/day divided TID × 5-10 days (category B, poorly absorbed)

**Children:**
- **Metronidazole** 15 mg/kg/day divided TID × 5-7 days OR
- **Nitazoxanide** (approved for children ≥1 year)

## Key Points

### **No Eosinophilia:**
- *Giardia* is a **protozoan** (NOT helminth)
- **Does NOT cause eosinophilia** (unlike tissue-invasive helminths)

### **Lactose Intolerance Post-Giardiasis:**
- **Transient lactose intolerance** common after treatment (damage to brush border)
- **May persist weeks to months**
- **Recommend:** Avoid dairy temporarily, lactase supplements

### **Immunodeficiency and Giardia:**
- **Increased risk:** **IgA deficiency**, **CVID** (common variable immunodeficiency), **HIV** (especially untreated)
- **Chronic, severe, refractory** infection
- **Treatment:** Same regimens, but often require prolonged therapy

### **Prevention:**
- **Boil water** (1 minute), **filter** (0.1-1 micron), **iodine/chlorine** (less reliable for *Giardia* cysts)
- **Avoid untreated water** in wilderness
- **Hand hygiene** (daycare, food handlers)

### **Microscopy Findings:**
- **Trophozoites:** "Falling leaf" motility, **2 nuclei** (resembles "face" with two eyes)
- **Cysts:** Oval, 4 nuclei, resistant to chlorine

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Stool antigen (EIA)** = best test (single specimen, >90% sensitive)
- **Metronidazole 250-500mg TID × 5-7 days** OR **tinidazole 2g × 1 dose**
- **Greasy, foul-smelling, frothy diarrhea** + bloating = *Giardia*
- **No eosinophilia** (protozoan, not helminth)
- **Lactose intolerance** can persist after treatment
- **Pregnancy:** Paromomycin (poorly absorbed, safe)
- **IgA deficiency/CVID:** Chronic, refractory giardiasis
