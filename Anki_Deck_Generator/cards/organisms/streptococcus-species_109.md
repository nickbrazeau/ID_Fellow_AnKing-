## Question

What are the major *Streptococcus* species and syndromes? Use the **"GAS (Pyogenes), GBS (Agalactiae), Pneumo, Viridans"** framework.

## Answer

### **Major Streptococcal Groups:**

| Species | Hemolysis | Key Syndromes | Treatment |
|---------|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| ***S. pyogenes*** **(Group A Strep, GAS)** | **β-hemolytic** | **Pharyngitis**, cellulitis, **necrotizing fasciitis**, scarlet fever, **post-strep sequelae** (ARF, PSGN) | **Penicillin G 2-4 MU IV q4h** + **clindamycin 900mg IV q8h** (toxin-mediated) |
| ***S. agalactiae*** **(Group B Strep, GBS)** | **β-hemolytic** | **Neonatal sepsis/meningitis**, **maternal bacteremia** (post-partum endometritis), diabetic foot infections | **Penicillin G** OR **ampicillin + gentamicin** (meningitis/endocarditis) |
| ***S. pneumoniae*** **(Pneumococcus)** | **α-hemolytic** | **Pneumonia**, **meningitis**, otitis media, sinusitis | **Ceftriaxone 2g IV daily** (meningitis: + vancomycin) |
| **Viridans Streptococci** | **α-hemolytic** | **Subacute bacterial endocarditis** (SBE), dental abscess | **Penicillin G 2-3 MU IV q4h** OR **ceftriaxone 2g daily** |

### **Group A Strep (GAS) Syndromes:**

**Non-Invasive:**
- **Pharyngitis** (strep throat)
- **Impetigo** (superficial skin infection)
- **Scarlet fever** (pharyngitis + sandpaper rash)

**Invasive:**
- **Cellulitis/erysipelas**
- **Necrotizing fasciitis** (Type II, monomicrobial)
- **Toxic shock syndrome** (streptococcal TSS)

**Post-Streptococcal Sequelae:**
- **Acute rheumatic fever (ARF):** Carditis, arthritis, chorea, subcutaneous nodules, erythema marginatum (2-4 weeks post-pharyngitis)
- **Post-streptococcal glomerulonephritis (PSGN):** Hematuria, HTN, edema (1-3 weeks post-pharyngitis or impetigo)

**Treatment:**
- **Pharyngitis:** Penicillin V 500mg PO TID × 10 days OR amoxicillin 500mg PO TID × 10 days
- **Necrotizing fasciitis/TSS:** **Penicillin G + clindamycin** (clindamycin inhibits toxin production)

### **Group B Strep (GBS):**

**Neonatal Disease:**
- **Early-onset (<7 days):** Sepsis, pneumonia, meningitis (vertical transmission)
- **Late-onset (7-90 days):** Meningitis (community-acquired)

**Adults:**
- Bacteremia, endocarditis, pneumonia (elderly, diabetics)
- Post-partum endometritis

**Intrapartum Prophylaxis:**
- **Screen** at 36-37 weeks (vaginal-rectal culture)
- **Give penicillin G** IV during labor if GBS+ or risk factors

## Key Points

### **GAS Necrotizing Fasciitis:**
- **Penicillin + clindamycin** (clindamycin critical for toxin suppression)
- **Urgent surgical debridement** (within 6 hours)
- High mortality (20-30%)

### **Viridans Strep Endocarditis:**
- **Subacute presentation** (indolent, weeks to months)
- **Risk factors:** Dental procedures, poor dentition
- **Treatment:** Penicillin G 2-3 MU IV q4h × 4 weeks (or ceftriaxone 2g daily)
- **Prophylaxis:** For high-risk cardiac conditions (prosthetic valve, prior endocarditis)

### ***S. pneumoniae* Meningitis:**
- **Ceftriaxone 2g IV q12h + vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h**
- **Dexamethasone 10mg IV q6h × 4 days** (before or with first antibiotic dose)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **GAS:** Penicillin + clindamycin for necrotizing fasciitis/TSS
- **GBS:** Intrapartum prophylaxis (penicillin G) prevents neonatal disease
- **Pneumococcus:** Ceftriaxone + vancomycin for meningitis (until susceptibilities known)
- **Viridans strep:** Subacute endocarditis (dental procedures)
- **Clindamycin** critical for GAS toxin-mediated disease
