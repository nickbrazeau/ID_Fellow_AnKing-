## Free-Living Amebae, Toxocara, and Harmful Algal Blooms

**Q:** What are the clinical features and treatments for PAM, GAE, VLM, and cyanobacterial illness?

**A:**

**Free-Living Amebae:**

| Organism | Disease | Route | Clinical | Mortality | Treatment |
|----------|---------|-------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| ***Naegleria fowleri*** | **PAM** (Primary Amebic Meningoencephalitis) | **Warm freshwater** (swimming, nasal irrigation) → olfactory nerve → brain | **Acute fulminant meningoencephalitis** (3-7 days): Severe frontal headache, fever, N/V, altered mental status; rapid death | **>97%** (4 survivors of 167 US cases 1962-2024) | **Amphotericin B IV/intrathecal + miltefosine + azithromycin + fluconazole + rifampin** |
| ***Acanthamoeba*** | **GAE** (Granulomatous Amebic Encephalitis) + keratitis | Hematogenous spread (skin/lungs) → brain; **keratitis** (contact lens wearers) | **Subacute/chronic** encephalitis (weeks-months): Focal neuro deficits, seizures; **keratitis**: painful red eye, corneal ulcer | **94%** (GAE); keratitis treatable if caught early | GAE: Pentamidine + azoles + miltefosine; **Keratitis**: Chlorhexidine + polyhexamethylene biguanide + propamidine |
| ***Balamuthia mandrillaris*** | **GAE** | Soil/dust inhalation or skin inoculation → brain | **Subacute/chronic** encephalitis; skin lesions common | **90%** | Miltefosine + pentamidine + azoles + sulfadiazine |

**Key Diagnostic Clues:**
- **PAM**: Warm freshwater exposure; **fulminant course** (death <7 days); motile trophozoites in CSF
- **GAE**: **Chronic** course; immunocompromised (Acanthamoeba); soil exposure (Balamuthia)
- **Acanthamoeba keratitis**: Contact lens use + tap water/hot tub exposure

---

**Visceral Larva Migrans (VLM) - Toxocara:**
- **Organisms**: *Toxocara canis* (dogs), *T. cati* (cats)
- **Transmission**: Ingestion of eggs (pica, contaminated soil - playgrounds, sandboxes)
- **Syndromes**:
  - **VLM**: Hepatosplenomegaly, pneumonitis, fever, eosinophilia (children <5 years)
  - **Ocular larva migrans**: Unilateral vision loss, retinal granuloma (older children)
- **Diagnosis**: **Serology** (ELISA); **eosinophilia**
- **Treatment**: **Albendazole 400 mg PO BID × 5 days** + corticosteroids (for severe inflammation)

---

**Cyanobacteria & Harmful Algal Blooms (HABs):**
- **Organisms**: Blue-green algae (cyanobacteria); dinoflagellates, diatoms
- **Toxins**: Microcystins (cyanobacteria), saxitoxin (paralytic shellfish poisoning), brevetoxin (neurotoxic shellfish poisoning)
- **Exposure**: Contaminated water (swimming, drinking); shellfish consumption
- **Clinical**:
  - **Skin**: Rash, blisters
  - **GI**: N/V/D, abdominal pain (microcystins)
  - **Neurologic**: Paresthesias, paralysis (saxitoxin - PSP)
  - **Respiratory**: Cough, asthma exacerbation (aerosol exposure)
- **Treatment**: **Supportive care** (no antidote)

**Key Points:**
- **Naegleria PAM**: >97% mortality; warm freshwater; fulminant (<7 days)
- **Acanthamoeba**: Keratitis (contact lenses); GAE (immunocompromised)
- **Balamuthia**: Soil exposure; skin lesions + GAE
- **Toxocara VLM**: Eosinophilia + hepatosplenomegaly; pica in children
- **HABs**: Microcystins (hepatotoxic), saxitoxin (paralytic shellfish poisoning)

**Mnemonic - "Naegleria = Nasal entry, Nearly always fatal"**

**Pearl:** Naegleria fowleri causes PAM with >97% mortality (4 survivors of 167 US cases). Warm freshwater exposure + fulminant meningoencephalitis (<7 days). Acanthamoeba keratitis in contact lens wearers. Toxocara VLM causes eosinophilia + hepatosplenomegaly in children with pica.
