## Yersinia enterocolitica, Bordetella pertussis, Rat-Bite Fever, Capnocytophaga

**Q:** What are the key features and treatments for these miscellaneous bacterial pathogens?

**A:**

**Yersinia enterocolitica/pseudotuberculosis:**
- **Transmission**: Contaminated food (esp. pork), water
- **Clinical**: Enterocolitis, **pseudoappendicitis** (mesenteric adenitis mimicking appendicitis), reactive arthritis
- **Treatment**: Self-limited in most; **fluoroquinolones or TMP-SMX** for severe cases; ceftriaxone for bacteremia

**Bordetella pertussis (Whooping Cough):**
- **Clinical Stages**: 1) Catarrhal (cold-like), 2) Paroxysmal (**spasmodic cough with inspiratory "whoop"**), 3) Convalescent
- **Complications**: Apnea (infants), pneumonia, encephalopathy
- **Treatment**: **Azithromycin 500 mg × 1 day, then 250 mg daily × 4 days** (or 5-day course)
  - Alternatives: Clarithromycin, TMP-SMX
  - **Most effective in catarrhal stage** (before paroxysms); later treatment prevents transmission but doesn't shorten course
- **2024**: 16,000 US cases (4× increase from 2023)
- **Post-exposure prophylaxis**: Azithromycin for close contacts

**Rat-Bite Fever:**
- **Organisms**: *Streptobacillus moniliformis* (US) or *Spirillum minus* (Asia)
- **Transmission**: Rat bite or contact with rat urine/secretions
- **Clinical**: Fever, rash (petechial/maculopapular on extremities including palms/soles), **polyarthralgia/arthritis** ("Haverhill fever" if foodborne)
- **Treatment**: **Penicillin G 1.2 million units IV q4-6h** or amoxicillin; doxycycline alternative
- **Duration**: 14 days

**Capnocytophaga:**
- **Source**: Dog/cat oral flora
- **Risk**: **Asplenic patients, immunocompromised**
- **Clinical**: Sepsis, DIC, purpura fulminans (rapidly fatal in asplenic)
- **Treatment**: Amoxicillin-clavulanate, ceftriaxone, or fluoroquinolones

**Key Points:**
- **Pertussis**: Azithromycin × 5 days; 16,000 US cases in 2024
- **Rat-bite fever**: Penicillin × 14 days; arthritis common
- **Capnocytophaga**: High mortality in asplenic patients; dog/cat bites

**Mnemonic - "Pertussis = Paroxysmal cough, Prophylaxis contacts, azithromycin Preferred"**

**Pearl:** Pertussis treatment (azithromycin) most effective in catarrhal stage but given later to prevent transmission. Capnocytophaga causes sepsis/DIC in asplenic patients after dog/cat bites. Rat-bite fever includes polyarthritis and requires penicillin × 14 days.
