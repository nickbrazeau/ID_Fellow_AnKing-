## Alphaviruses

**Q:** How do arthritogenic and encephalitic alphaviruses differ in clinical manifestations and mortality?

**A:**

**Two Clinical Syndromes:**

**1. Arthritogenic Alphaviruses (Fever, Rash, Arthralgia):**

| Virus | Geographic Distribution | Clinical Features | Chronic Arthritis |
|-------|-------------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Chikungunya (CHIKV)** | Worldwide (Africa, Asia, Americas) | **Fever, severe polyarthralgia** (hands/feet), rash (40-50%), myalgia | **Months to years** |
| **Mayaro (MAYV)** | Central/South America | Fever, rash, polyarthralgia, myalgia, myositis | Similar to CHIKV |
| **Ross River (RRV)** | Australia, South Pacific | Polyarthralgia, rash, fever | Months |
| **O'nyong'nyong (ONNV)** | Africa | Polyarthralgia, rash, fever | Variable |

**2. Encephalitic Alphaviruses (CNS Disease):**

| Virus | Geographic Distribution | Vector | Human Mortality | Neurologic Sequelae |
|-------|-------------------------|--------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **Eastern Equine Encephalitis (EEEV)** | North America, Caribbean | *Culiseta melanura* | **30-70%** | Severe (survivors often have permanent deficits) |
| **Western Equine Encephalitis (WEEV)** | North America | *Culex tarsalis* | **3-15%** | Moderate |
| **Venezuelan Equine Encephalitis (VEEV)** | Americas (North, Central, South) | *Culex (Melanoconion)* | **<1%** adults | ~15% develop serious neurologic complications |

**Key Clinical Distinctions:**
- **Arthritogenic**: >90% have fever + polyarthralgia (CHIKV incubation 3-6 days); chronic arthritis common
- **Encephalitic**: Meningitis/encephalitis; **EEEV has highest mortality** (30-70%)
- **Overlap**: Encephalitic alphaviruses can cause arthralgia/myalgia (especially VEEV)

**Treatment:**
- **NO approved antivirals** for human alphavirus infections
- **Supportive care** only (NSAIDs for arthralgia, ICU for encephalitis)
- Vaccines available for equine use only

**Pearl:** EEEV has the highest mortality (30-70%) of any arboviral encephalitis in the US. Chikungunya causes debilitating chronic polyarthralgia lasting months to years in many patients.

**Mnemonic - "EEE = Extremely Elevated Encephalitis mortality"** (30-70%)
