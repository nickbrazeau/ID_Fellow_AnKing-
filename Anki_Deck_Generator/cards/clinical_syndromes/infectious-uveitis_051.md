## Infectious Uveitis: Causes and Treatment

**Q:** What are the major infectious causes of uveitis, and how should they be treated?

**A:**

**Definition:**
Inflammation of the uveal tract (iris, ciliary body, choroid) → can be anterior, intermediate, posterior, or panuveitis

**Major Infectious Causes:**

**1. Viral Anterior Uveitis:**

| **Virus** | **Features** |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Herpes Simplex Virus (HSV)** | Unilateral, may have dendritic keratitis, IOP ↑ |
| **Varicella-Zoster Virus (VZV)** | Associated with zoster ophthalmicus, sectoral iris atrophy |
| **Cytomegalovirus (CMV)** | Coin-shaped keratic precipitates (KP), IOP ↑, immunocompromised |
| **Rubella** | Fuchs heterochromic iridocyclitis |

**2. Toxoplasma gondii:**
- **Most common infectious posterior uveitis**
- Retinochoroiditis with "headlight in the fog" appearance
- Old chorioretinal scar + new active lesion
- Treatment: Pyrimethamine + sulfadiazine + folinic acid (or TMP-SMX)

**3. Syphilis (Treponema pallidum):**
- **"Great mimicker"** - can cause any uveitis pattern
- Always check **RPR/VDRL + treponemal-specific test** in uveitis workup
- Treatment: IV penicillin G 18-24 million units/day x 10-14 days

**4. Tuberculosis (Mycobacterium tuberculosis):**
- Chronic granulomatous uveitis
- Rule out in endemic areas or high-risk patients
- Treatment: Standard TB therapy (RIPE)

**5. Fungal:**
- **Candida:** Endogenous endophthalmitis (IVDU, TPN, immunosuppressed)
- **Histoplasma:** "Histo spots" chorioretinal scars
- Treatment: Systemic + intravitreal antifungals

---

**Treatment Principles:**

**1. Treat Underlying Infection FIRST:**
- Aqueous or vitreous tap before steroids (especially if suspected infection)
- Appropriate antimicrobial therapy (see above)

**2. Once Infection Controlled:**
- Corticosteroids (topical ± systemic) to limit tissue damage
- Goal: Control inflammation, prevent vision loss

**3. Immunosuppression (If Needed):**
- Methotrexate, azathioprine, mycophenolate (noninfectious uveitis)
- **NEVER use immunosuppression before ruling out infection**

---

**Workup for Uveitis:**

**Infectious Screening:**
- **Syphilis:** RPR/VDRL + treponemal test
- **TB:** CXR, IGRA/PPD
- **Toxoplasma:** IgG/IgM (if posterior uveitis)
- **HSV/VZV:** PCR from aqueous if suspected
- **CMV:** PCR from aqueous (especially immunocompromised)

**Non-Infectious:**
- HLA-B27 (ankylosing spondylitis, reactive arthritis)
- ANA, RF (autoimmune)
- ACE, lysozyme (sarcoidosis)

---

**Mnemonic: "TUSH CHARMS" for infectious uveitis causes**
- **T**oxoplasma (#1 posterior)
- **U**shering in herpes viruses (HSV, VZV, CMV)
- **S**yphilis (great mimicker)
- **H**istoplasma

- **C**andida (endogenous)
- **H**IV-related opportunistic infections
- **A**RN (acute retinal necrosis - HSV/VZV)
- **R**ubella
- **M**ycobacterium tuberculosis
- **S**arcoidosis (technically non-infectious)

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **Always rule out syphilis and TB** in uveitis workup (treatable!)
- Posterior uveitis → think Toxoplasma first
- CMV anterior uveitis → check HIV status
- **NEVER give steroids without ruling out infection** (can worsen HSV, VZV, TB, fungal)
- Unilateral anterior uveitis with elevated IOP → think viral (HSV/VZV/CMV)
