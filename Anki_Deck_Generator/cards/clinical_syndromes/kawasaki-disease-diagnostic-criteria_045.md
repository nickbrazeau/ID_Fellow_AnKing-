## Kawasaki Disease: Diagnostic Criteria

**Q:** What are the diagnostic criteria for classic Kawasaki disease?

**A:**

**Definition:**
Kawasaki disease is an acute, self-limited vasculitis of unknown etiology affecting medium-sized arteries, predominantly in children <5 years old.

**Classic Kawasaki Disease Criteria (AHA):**

**Required:** Fever ≥5 days (≥38°C / 100.4°F)

**PLUS ≥4 of 5 clinical features:**

| **Feature** | **Details** |
|-------------|-------------|
| **1. Bilateral conjunctival injection** | Non-exudative, bulbar (NO discharge, NO uveitis) |
| **2. Oral mucosal changes** | • Erythema and cracking of lips<br>• **Strawberry tongue** (erythematous, papillae prominent)<br>• Diffuse oral/pharyngeal erythema<br>• NO ulcers or exudates |
| **3. Polymorphous rash** | Usually on trunk/extremities, NOT vesicular |
| **4. Extremity changes** | • **Acute:** Erythema of palms/soles, edema of hands/feet<br>• **Subacute (2-3 weeks):** Desquamation starting at fingertips/toes |
| **5. Cervical lymphadenopathy** | ≥1.5 cm diameter, usually unilateral, firm, non-fluctuant |

**Mnemonic: "CRASH and BURN"**
- **C**onjunctivitis (bilateral, non-exudative)
- **R**ash (polymorphous)
- **A**denopathy (cervical, ≥1.5 cm)
- **S**trawberry tongue
- **H**ands and feet (erythema, edema, later desquamation)
- **BURN** = Fever ≥5 days (the "burn")

**Incomplete Kawasaki Disease:**
- Fever ≥5 days + <4 criteria
- More common in infants <6 months and children >5 years
- Higher risk of coronary complications (diagnostic delay)
- Requires high suspicion + supportive lab findings (↑ CRP/ESR, ↓ albumin, anemia, thrombocytosis)

**Key Differential Features:**

| **Kawasaki** | **NOT Kawasaki** |
|--------------|------------------|
| Non-exudative conjunctivitis | Exudative conjunctivitis (bacterial) |
| No oral ulcers | Oral ulcers (Stevens-Johnson, viral stomatitis) |
| Strawberry tongue | Coated tongue |
| Desquamation starts at fingertips | Generalized peeling |

**Labs (Supportive):**
- ↑ ESR, CRP (acute phase reactants)
- ↑ WBC with left shift
- **Thrombocytosis** (peaks week 2-3, can be >1 million)
- ↓ Albumin
- Normocytic anemia
- Sterile pyuria

**Clinical Pearl:**
- **Think Kawasaki if:** Child with prolonged fever (>5 days) + rash + red eyes + irritability (disproportionate to fever)
- **Don't wait for all criteria:** Incomplete KD needs treatment too (check echo)
- Fever typically high (39-40°C) and **refractory to antipyretics**
