## Question

How are healthcare-associated infections prevented? Use the **"Hand Hygiene + Bundles (CLABSI, CAUTI, VAP, SSI)"** framework.

## Answer

### **Major HAIs and Prevention:**

**1. Central Line-Associated BSI (CLABSI):**

**CLABSI Bundle:**
- **Hand hygiene** before insertion/access
- **Maximal barrier precautions** (cap, mask, sterile gown, gloves, large drape)
- **Chlorhexidine skin prep** (preferred over povidone-iodine)
- **Optimal site selection** (subclavian > internal jugular > femoral)
- **Daily review** of line necessity (remove if not needed)

**2. Catheter-Associated UTI (CAUTI):**

**CAUTI Bundle:**
- **Avoid unnecessary catheterization** (only if indicated)
- **Aseptic insertion** technique
- **Maintain closed drainage system**
- **Remove catheter ASAP** (daily necessity assessment)

**Indications for Foley:**
- Acute urinary retention/obstruction
- Accurate urine output monitoring (ICU, critically ill)
- Perioperative (select surgeries)
- Sacral wounds with incontinence

**3. Ventilator-Associated Pneumonia (VAP):**

**VAP Bundle:**
- **Head of bed elevated 30-45°**
- **Daily sedation vacation** + spontaneous breathing trial
- **Oral care with chlorhexidine**
- **Peptic ulcer prophylaxis**
- **DVT prophylaxis**
- **Early extubation** when possible

**4. Surgical Site Infections (SSI):**

**SSI Bundle:**
- **Appropriate antibiotic prophylaxis** (within 60 min before incision)
- **Chlorhexidine-alcohol skin prep**
- **Maintain normothermia** during surgery
- **Glycemic control** (glucose <200 mg/dL)
- **No hair removal** (or clippers, not razors)

## Key Points

### **Hand Hygiene:**
- **Most important** HAI prevention measure
- **Alcohol-based hand rub** preferred (unless *C. difficile* → soap + water)
- **5 moments:** Before patient contact, before aseptic task, after body fluid exposure, after patient contact, after contact with patient surroundings

### **Isolation Precautions:**

| Precaution | When | Pathogen Examples |
|------------|------|------------------|
| **Contact** | Direct contact transmission | MRSA, VRE, *C. difficile*, CRE |
| **Droplet** | Large droplets (>5 μm) | Influenza, *Neisseria meningitidis*, pertussis |
| **Airborne** | Small particles (<5 μm) | TB, measles, varicella (chickenpox) |

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Hand hygiene** = #1 HAI prevention measure
- **CLABSI:** Maximal barrier precautions + chlorhexidine skin prep + daily necessity review
- **CAUTI:** Avoid unnecessary catheterization, remove ASAP
- **VAP:** HOB elevated 30-45°, daily sedation vacation, early extubation
- **SSI:** Antibiotic prophylaxis within 60 min, chlorhexidine-alcohol prep
- **Remove devices ASAP** (lines, catheters, tubes)
