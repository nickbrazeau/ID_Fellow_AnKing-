## Question

What are the key OIs by CD4 threshold? Use the **"<200 = PCP, <100 = Toxo, <50 = MAC/CMV"** framework.

## Answer

### **Opportunistic Infections by CD4 Count:**

| CD4 Count | Opportunistic Infections | Prophylaxis |
|-----------|-------------------------|-------------|
| **<200** | ***Pneumocystis jirovecii* pneumonia (PCP)** | **TMP-SMX DS daily** (or 3×/week) |
| **<100** | ***Toxoplasma* encephalitis**, **Cryptococcal meningitis** | **TMP-SMX DS daily** (covers both PCP + Toxo) |
| **<50** | ***Mycobacterium avium* complex (MAC)**, **CMV retinitis/colitis** | **Azithromycin 1200mg weekly** (MAC prophylaxis) |

### **Key OI Presentations:**

**PCP (CD4 <200):**
- **Presentation:** Subacute dyspnea, dry cough, fever, hypoxemia
- **Diagnosis:** BAL with silver stain or DFA, ↑ LDH, bilateral ground-glass infiltrates
- **Treatment:** TMP-SMX 15-20 mg/kg/day (TMP component) × 21 days + prednisone if PaO2 <70

**Toxoplasma (CD4 <100):**
- **Presentation:** Headache, confusion, focal neurologic deficits, seizures
- **Imaging:** Multiple ring-enhancing lesions (basal ganglia common)
- **Treatment:** Pyrimethamine + sulfadiazine + leucovorin × ≥6 weeks

**MAC (CD4 <50):**
- **Presentation:** Fever, night sweats, diarrhea, abdominal pain, elevated alk phos
- **Diagnosis:** Blood cultures (AFB), colonoscopy with biopsy
- **Treatment:** Clarithromycin + ethambutol (± rifabutin)

**CMV (CD4 <50):**
- **Retinitis:** "Pizza pie" retina, vision loss
- **Colitis:** Bloody diarrhea, abdominal pain
- **Treatment:** Valganciclovir 900mg PO BID (induction) → daily (maintenance)

**Cryptococcal Meningitis (CD4 <100):**
- **Presentation:** Headache, fever, meningismus, ↑ ICP (can be subtle)
- **Diagnosis:** CSF cryptococcal antigen (CrAg), India ink (50-80% sensitive)
- **Treatment:** Amphotericin B + flucytosine × 2 weeks → fluconazole consolidation

## Key Points

### **Prophylaxis Regimens:**

**Primary Prophylaxis Indications:**
- **PCP:** CD4 <200 or CD4 <14% or oropharyngeal candidiasis
- **Toxoplasmosis:** CD4 <100 AND positive Toxoplasma IgG
- **MAC:** CD4 <50

**Stopping Prophylaxis (after ART):**
- **PCP:** Can stop if CD4 >200 × 3 months on ART
- **Toxo:** Can stop if CD4 >200 × 3 months on ART
- **MAC:** Can stop if CD4 >100 × 3 months on ART

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **TMP-SMX** covers both PCP and Toxo (dual prophylaxis)
- **PCP** most common OI (CD4 <200)
- **MAC** and **CMV** occur at CD4 <50 (late-stage AIDS)
- **Start ART** in all patients with OIs (timing varies by OI)
- **IRIS** (immune reconstitution inflammatory syndrome) can occur 2-12 weeks after starting ART
