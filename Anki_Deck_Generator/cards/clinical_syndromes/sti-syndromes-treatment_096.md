## Question

How are common STI syndromes treated? Use the **"Dual Therapy for GC, Doxy for CT, Penicillin for Syphilis"** framework.

## Answer

### **STI Syndromes and Treatment:**

**Urethritis/Cervicitis (GC/CT):**
- **Gonorrhea:** **Ceftriaxone 500mg IM × 1** (1g if weight >150kg)
- **Chlamydia:** **Doxycycline 100mg PO BID × 7 days**
- **Dual therapy:** Treat for both GC + CT if either detected (often co-infected)

**Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID):**
- **Outpatient:** Ceftriaxone 500mg IM × 1 + **doxycycline 100mg BID × 14 days** + **metronidazole 500mg BID × 14 days**
- **Inpatient:** Cefoxitin IV + doxycycline OR ceftriaxone + doxycycline + metronidazole

**Genital Ulcers:**

| Cause | Clinical Features | Treatment |
|-------|------------------|-----------|
| **Syphilis** (*T. pallidum*) | Painless ulcer (chancre), regional adenopathy | **Benzathine PCN G 2.4 MU IM × 1** (primary/secondary) |
| **Herpes** (HSV) | Painful vesicles → ulcers, recurrent | **Acyclovir 400mg TID × 7-10 days** (or valacyclovir 1g BID) |
| **Chancroid** (*H. ducreyi*) | Painful ulcer + tender adenopathy | Azithromycin 1g × 1 OR ceftriaxone 250mg IM × 1 |
| **LGV** (*C. trachomatis* L1-L3) | Ulcer → painful inguinal adenopathy (buboes) | **Doxycycline 100mg BID × 21 days** |

**Genital Warts (HPV 6/11):**
- **Patient-applied:** Imiquimod, podofilox
- **Provider-applied:** Cryotherapy, TCA, surgical excision

## Key Points

### **Gonorrhea Resistance:**
- **Ceftriaxone** now only agent with reliable activity
- **Fluoroquinolones** no longer recommended (resistance)
- **Azithromycin monotherapy** no longer recommended (resistance)
- **Always test for cure** if pharyngeal GC (higher treatment failure)

### **Syphilis Staging:**
- **Primary:** Painless chancre (3 weeks post-exposure), heals in 3-6 weeks
- **Secondary:** Rash (palms/soles), condyloma lata, lymphadenopathy (6-12 weeks post-exposure)
- **Latent:** No symptoms, positive serology (early <1 year, late >1 year)
- **Tertiary:** Gummas, aortitis, neurosyphilis (years later)

**Syphilis Treatment:**
- **Primary/secondary/early latent (<1 year):** Benzathine PCN G 2.4 MU IM × 1
- **Late latent (>1 year) or unknown duration:** Benzathine PCN G 2.4 MU IM × 3 doses (weekly)
- **Neurosyphilis:** IV penicillin G 18-24 MU/day × 10-14 days

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Always treat GC + CT together** (high co-infection rate)
- **Ceftriaxone 500mg IM** for gonorrhea (only reliable agent)
- **Doxycycline 7 days** for chlamydia (not azithromycin 1g × 1 anymore)
- **Syphilis = penicillin** (benzathine PCN G)
- **Test for cure** if pharyngeal GC or pregnant
- **Screen all STI patients** for HIV, syphilis, HCV
