## Question

How are bloodstream infections managed? Use the **"Source Control + Remove Line + Targeted Therapy + Duration"** framework.

## Answer

### **Key Principles:**

**1. Source Control:**
- **Identify and control source** (drain abscess, remove infected device, debride tissue)
- Most critical intervention

**2. Repeat Blood Cultures:**
- **Repeat daily** until clearance documented (especially *S. aureus*, *Candida*)
- Guides duration of therapy

**3. Line Management:**
- **Remove catheter** if CLABSI (central line-associated BSI)
- **Exceptions:** Short-term catheter salvage for low-virulence organisms (CoNS) + antibiotic lock therapy

**4. Targeted Therapy:**
- **De-escalate** from empiric to pathogen-directed therapy based on cultures/susceptibilities

### **Complicated vs Uncomplicated SAB:**

**Uncomplicated SAB (all must be met):**
- Exclude endocarditis (TEE negative or not performed if very low risk)
- No implanted prosthetic devices
- Repeat blood cultures negative within 2-4 days
- Defervescence within 72 hours
- No metastatic sites (bone, joint, epidural)
- **Duration: 14 days**

**Complicated SAB:**
- Any of above not met
- **Duration: 4-6 weeks** (IV therapy)

## Key Points

### **Central Line-Associated BSI (CLABSI):**

**Remove catheter if:**
- *S. aureus*, *Candida*, or GNB bacteremia
- Septic thrombophlebitis
- Endocarditis
- Tunnel infection, port abscess

**Consider salvage (with antibiotic lock therapy) if:**
- CoNS (coagulase-negative *Staph*)
- Short-term catheter (<2 weeks)
- No complications, source controlled

### **When to Perform TEE in SAB:**
- **Always** in complicated SAB or high-risk (healthcare-associated, persistent fever, prolonged bacteremia)
- **Consider** in all SAB cases (yields diagnosis in 10-30%)
- TTE has low sensitivity (~30-60%), TEE preferred

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Remove line** if *S. aureus*, *Candida*, or GNB
- **TEE required** for all *S. aureus* bacteremia (high endocarditis risk)
- **Repeat cultures daily** until clearance (*S. aureus*, *Candida*)
- **Uncomplicated SAB:** 14 days, **complicated:** 4-6 weeks
- **Source control** most important intervention
- ***Candida* bacteremia:** Ophthalmology exam (endophthalmitis), remove all lines
