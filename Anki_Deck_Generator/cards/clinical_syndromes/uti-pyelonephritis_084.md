## Question

What is the empiric therapy for uncomplicated cystitis vs pyelonephritis? Use the **"CYSTITIS = Nitro/TMP vs PYELO = Ceftriaxone/FQ"** mnemonic.

## Answer

### **Uncomplicated Cystitis (Women):**

**First-Line Options (IDSA):**
1. **Nitrofurantoin** 100mg PO BID × 5 days
2. **TMP-SMX** DS BID × 3 days (if local resistance <20%)
3. **Fosfomycin** 3g PO × 1 dose

**Avoid:** Fluoroquinolones (reserve for complicated/pyelonephritis)

### **Complicated UTI / Pyelonephritis:**

**Outpatient (Mild):**
- **Ciprofloxacin** 500mg PO BID × 7 days OR
- **Levofloxacin** 750mg PO daily × 5 days OR
- **Ceftriaxone** 1g IM/IV × 1 dose, then oral step-down

**Inpatient (Severe):**
- **Ceftriaxone** 1-2g IV daily OR
- **Fluoroquinolone** IV (ciprofloxacin 400mg IV q12h or levofloxacin 750mg IV daily) OR
- **Pip-tazo/cefepime** if sepsis or risk for resistant organisms

**Duration:**
- Pyelonephritis: **7-14 days** (10-14 days if men)
- Cystitis: **3-5 days**

## Key Points

### **Uncomplicated vs Complicated UTI:**

| Feature | Uncomplicated | Complicated |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| **Population** | Healthy non-pregnant women | Men, pregnant, catheter, anatomic abnormality, immunosuppressed |
| **First-line** | Nitrofurantoin, TMP-SMX, fosfomycin | Fluoroquinolone, ceftriaxone |
| **Duration** | 3-5 days | 7-14 days |

### **Special Populations:**

**Pregnant Women:**
- **Avoid:** Fluoroquinolones, TMP-SMX (1st trimester)
- **Use:** β-lactams (amoxicillin-clavulanate, cephalexin)
- **Asymptomatic bacteriuria:** MUST treat (risk of pyelonephritis)

**Men:**
- All UTIs considered "complicated"
- Duration: **7-14 days**
- Consider prostatitis if recurrent

**Catheter-Associated UTI:**
- Remove/replace catheter if possible
- Treat only if **symptomatic** (not asymptomatic bacteriuria)

### **When to Use Fluoroquinolones:**
- **Pyelonephritis** (severe)
- **Complicated UTI**
- **Resistant organisms** (ESBL *E. coli*)
- **Avoid for simple cystitis** (FDA warning - reserve for serious infections)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Uncomplicated cystitis:** Nitrofurantoin or TMP-SMX (avoid FQ)
- **Pyelonephritis:** Fluoroquinolone or ceftriaxone
- **Pregnant:** Treat asymptomatic bacteriuria (prevents pyelonephritis)
- **Men:** All UTIs = complicated (7-14 days)
- **Catheter-associated:** Only treat if symptomatic
