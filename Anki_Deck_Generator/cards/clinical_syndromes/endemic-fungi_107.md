## Question

What are the endemic fungi by geography? Use the **"Histo = Ohio/Mississippi, Cocci = Southwest, Blasto = Great Lakes"** framework.

## Answer

### **Endemic Fungi by Geography:**

| Fungus | Endemic Region | Presentation | Diagnosis | Treatment |
|--------|---------------|--------------|-----------|-----------|
| ***Histoplasma capsulatum*** | **Ohio/Mississippi River valleys** | **Acute pulmonary** (flu-like), **disseminated** (pancytopenia, hepatosplenomegaly, adrenal insufficiency in HIV) | **Urine/serum Histoplasma antigen**, culture, biopsy (intracellular yeast) | **Itraconazole 200mg BID** (mild-moderate) OR **liposomal amphotericin B** (severe/disseminated) |
| ***Coccidioides immitis/posadasii*** | **Southwestern US** (Arizona, California), Mexico | **Valley fever:** Pneumonia, erythema nodosum, arthralgia; **Disseminated:** Meningitis, osteomyelitis (especially in Filipinos, African Americans) | **Serology** (IgM/IgG, complement fixation), culture | **Fluconazole 400mg daily** (mild-moderate) OR **amphotericin B** (severe, meningitis) |
| ***Blastomyces dermatitidis*** | **Great Lakes, Ohio/Mississippi valleys** | **Pulmonary:** Acute pneumonia; **Extrapulmonary:** Skin (verrucous lesions), bone, CNS | **Culture**, biopsy (broad-based budding yeast), **urine antigen** (cross-reacts with *Histoplasma*) | **Itraconazole 200mg BID** (mild-moderate) OR **liposomal amphotericin B** (severe, CNS) |

### **Key Clinical Features:**

**Histoplasmosis:**
- **Exposure:** Bird/bat droppings (caves, construction, demolition)
- **Acute:** Self-limited flu-like illness (most common)
- **Disseminated (HIV):** Fever, pancytopenia, hepatosplenomegaly, adrenal insufficiency
- **Chronic pulmonary:** Mimics TB (cavitary disease, upper lobes)
- **Diagnosis:** **Urine/serum Histoplasma antigen** (best test for disseminated)

**Coccidioidomycosis (Valley Fever):**
- **Exposure:** Dust inhalation (construction, farming, earthquakes)
- **Primary:** Flu-like illness, erythema nodosum (desert rheumatism)
- **Disseminated risk:** Filipinos, African Americans, immunosuppressed, pregnant (3rd trimester)
- **Meningitis:** Chronic meningitis (requires lifelong fluconazole)
- **Diagnosis:** **Serology** (IgM = acute, IgG/CF = chronic/disseminated)

**Blastomycosis:**
- **Exposure:** Soil, decaying wood (near waterways)
- **Pulmonary:** Acute or chronic pneumonia
- **Skin:** Verrucous (wart-like) lesions
- **Diagnosis:** Culture (gold standard), biopsy (broad-based budding yeast)

## Key Points

### **Treatment Summary:**

**Mild-Moderate Disease:**
- **Histoplasmosis:** Itraconazole 200mg BID × 6-12 weeks
- **Coccidioidomycosis:** Fluconazole 400mg daily × 3-6 months
- **Blastomycosis:** Itraconazole 200mg BID × 6-12 months

**Severe/Disseminated:**
- **All:** **Liposomal amphotericin B** 3-5 mg/kg daily × 1-2 weeks → step down to azole

**CNS Disease:**
- **Coccidioidal meningitis:** **Fluconazole 400-800mg daily × lifelong**
- **Blastomycosis:** Liposomal amphotericin B → itraconazole (prolonged)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Histoplasmosis:** Ohio/Mississippi valleys, urine antigen (disseminated)
- **Coccidioidomycosis:** Southwest US, erythema nodosum, high-risk groups (Filipinos, African Americans)
- **Blastomycosis:** Great Lakes, broad-based budding yeast, skin lesions
- **All dimorphic fungi:** Mold in environment (25°C), yeast in body (37°C)
- **Itraconazole** for mild-moderate, **amphotericin B** for severe
