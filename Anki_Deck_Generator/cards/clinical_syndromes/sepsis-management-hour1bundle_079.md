## Question

What is the Surviving Sepsis Campaign Hour-1 Bundle? Use the **"LACTATE-CULTURES-ANTIBIOTICS-FLUIDS-PRESSORS"** mnemonic.

## Answer

### **"LACTATE-CULTURES-ANTIBIOTICS-FLUIDS-PRESSORS" Mnemonic:**

**LACTATE:**
- **L**actate measurement
- **A**ll sepsis patients
- **C**heck initially
- **T**rend if elevated
- **A**ssess tissue perfusion
- **T**arget: normalize (<2 mmol/L)
- **E**levated = worse prognosis

**CULTURES:**
- **C**ultures before antibiotics (if no delay >45 min)
- **U**rgently obtain
- **L**ikely source (blood, urine, sputum, wounds)
- **T**wo sets blood cultures
- **U**nder 45 min delay acceptable
- **R**apid ID critical
- **E**arly cultures improve yield
- **S**ource control if indicated

**ANTIBIOTICS:**
- **A**ntibiotics within 1 hour
- **N**o delay (broad-spectrum empiric)
- **T**ime-dependent mortality benefit
- **I**V, appropriate dose
- **B**road coverage initially
- **I**dentify source
- **O**ptimal: <1 hour from recognition
- **T**arget likely pathogens
- **I**nitiate immediately
- **C**ritical for survival
- **S**ource-appropriate empiric regimen

**FLUIDS:**
- **F**luid bolus 30 mL/kg crystalloid
- **L**actated Ringer's or NS
- **U**rgent (within 3 hours)
- **I**nitial resuscitation
- **D**ocument administration
- **S**eptic shock or lactate ≥4

**PRESSORS:**
- **P**ressors if hypotensive despite fluids
- **R**eassess after fluids
- **E**arly vasopressors (norepinephrine 1st-line)
- **S**ystemic perfusion goal (MAP ≥65)
- **S**tart if MAP <65 after fluids
- **O**utcome improves with early use
- **R**efactory hypotension
- **S**tart during/after 30mL/kg fluids

### **Hour-1 Bundle Components (SSC 2021):**

| Step | Action | Timing |
|------|--------|--------|
| **1. Measure lactate** | Initial lactate level | Immediately |
| **2. Obtain blood cultures** | Before antibiotics (if no delay >45 min) | Before or with antibiotics |
| **3. Administer broad-spectrum antibiotics** | IV empiric therapy | **Within 1 hour** |
| **4. Fluid resuscitation** | 30 mL/kg crystalloid bolus | Within 3 hours (if hypotension or lactate ≥4) |
| **5. Vasopressors** | Norepinephrine if MAP <65 | If hypotensive during/after fluids |

**Key Change:** Previously "3-hour" and "6-hour" bundles → Now unified **"Hour-1 Bundle"** (emphasizes urgency)

## Key Points

### **Antibiotic Selection:**

**Empiric Regimens (Broad-Spectrum):**
- **Sepsis (unknown source):** Vancomycin + piperacillin-tazobactam (or cefepime + metronidazole)
- **Septic shock:** Consider adding aminoglycoside or fluoroquinolone for synergy
- **Immunocompromised:** Add antifungal (echinocandin) and consider antiviral
- **Healthcare-associated:** Cover MRSA + *Pseudomonas* + resistant Gram-negatives

**Timing:** Every hour delay in antibiotics → **↑ mortality by 4-9%**

### **Fluid Resuscitation:**

**Initial:** 30 mL/kg crystalloid within 3 hours (if hypotensive or lactate ≥4)
- **Example:** 70 kg patient → **2,100 mL** (~2 liters)
- **Preferred fluid:** Lactated Ringer's or balanced crystalloids (vs NS)

**Reassess:** After initial bolus:
- Clinical exam (perfusion, UOP, mental status)
- Repeat lactate if initially elevated
- Dynamic assessment (passive leg raise, fluid responsiveness)

### **Vasopressors:**

**First-Line:** **Norepinephrine**
- Target MAP ≥65 mmHg
- Start if hypotensive despite fluids or during fluid resuscitation

**Second-Line:** Vasopressin (0.03 U/min) or epinephrine

### **Source Control:**

**Urgent (<6-12 hours) if:**
- Necrotizing soft tissue infection
- Intra-abdominal source (abscess, perforation)
- Obstructed biliary tract/urinary tract
- Device-related infection (remove device)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Hour-1 Bundle** = time-zero when sepsis first recognized
- **Antibiotics within 1 hour** (most critical intervention)
- **30 mL/kg fluids** for hypotension or lactate ≥4
- **Norepinephrine** = first-line vasopressor
- **Source control** essential (drain, debride, remove devices)
- De-escalate antibiotics once culture results available
