## Question

How do you assess CAP severity using CURB-65 and PSI? Use the **"CURB-65 = Confusion, Urea, RR, BP, 65+"** mnemonic.

## Answer

### **CURB-65 Score (1 point each, max 5):**

- **C**onfusion (new onset, altered mental status)
- **U**rea >7 mmol/L (BUN >19 mg/dL)
- **R**espiratory rate ≥30/min
- **B**lood pressure: SBP <90 or DBP ≤60 mmHg
- **65+** years of age

**Interpretation:**

| Score | Severity | Mortality | Disposition |
|-------|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **0-1** | Low | <3% | **Outpatient** |
| **2** | Moderate | 9% | **Consider admission** |
| **≥3** | Severe | 15-40% | **Hospitalize** (consider ICU) |

### **Expanded CURB-65 Criteria:**

**IDSA/ATS Major Criteria (≥1 → ICU):**
- **Septic shock** requiring vasopressors
- **Respiratory failure** requiring mechanical ventilation

**IDSA/ATS Minor Criteria (≥3 → ICU):**
- **R**espiratory rate ≥30/min
- **PaO2/FiO2 ratio ≤250**
- **M**ultilobar infiltrates
- **C**onfusion
- **U**remia (BUN ≥20 mg/dL)
- **L**eukopenia (WBC <4000)
- **T**hrombocytopenia (platelets <100,000)
- **H**ypothermia (<36°C)
- **H**ypotension requiring aggressive fluids

## Key Points

### **PSI (Pneumonia Severity Index) - Alternative:**

**More complex** (20 variables including age, comorbidities, vitals, labs)

**PSI Classes:**

| Class | Mortality | Disposition |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **I-II** | <1% | Outpatient |
| **III** | 2-3% | Brief observation or outpatient |
| **IV** | 9% | Hospital |
| **V** | 27% | Hospital (consider ICU) |

**CURB-65 simpler** → more commonly used at bedside

### **ICU Admission Criteria (IDSA/ATS):**

**≥1 Major Criterion:**
1. Septic shock (vasopressors needed)
2. Respiratory failure (intubation needed)

**OR ≥3 Minor Criteria** (listed above)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **CURB-65 ≥2** → consider hospital admission
- **CURB-65 ≥3** → high risk, hospitalize, consider ICU
- **Major criteria** (shock or intubation) → **ICU**
- **≥3 minor criteria** → **ICU**
- CURB-65 **simpler than PSI** for bedside use
