## Question

What are the major tick-borne infections? Use the **"Lyme, RMSF, Ehrlichia/Anaplasma, Babesia"** framework with **"Doxycycline for Most"**.

## Answer

### **Major Tick-Borne Infections:**

| Disease | Pathogen | Vector | Presentation | Treatment |
|---------|----------|--------|--------------|-----------|
| **Lyme Disease** | *Borrelia burgdorferi* | ***Ixodes* (deer tick)** | **Erythema migrans** (bull's-eye rash), early disseminated (cardiac, neuro), late (arthritis) | **Doxycycline 100mg BID × 10-21 days** (early); IV ceftriaxone (neuro, cardiac) |
| **RMSF** | *Rickettsia rickettsii* | ***Dermacentor* (dog tick, wood tick)** | **Fever, headache, rash** (wrists/ankles → centripetal), severe illness | **Doxycycline 100mg BID × 5-7 days** (start empirically if suspected) |
| **Ehrlichiosis** | *Ehrlichia chaffeensis* | ***Amblyomma* (Lone Star tick)** | Fever, headache, **no rash**, leukopenia, thrombocytopenia, ↑LFTs | **Doxycycline 100mg BID × 5-10 days** |
| **Anaplasmosis** | *Anaplasma phagocytophilum* | ***Ixodes* (deer tick)** | Fever, headache, **no rash**, leukopenia, thrombocytopenia, ↑LFTs | **Doxycycline 100mg BID × 5-10 days** |
| **Babesiosis** | *Babesia microti* | ***Ixodes* (deer tick)** | **Hemolytic anemia**, fever, sweats (malaria-like) | **Atovaquone 750mg BID + azithromycin 500mg × 1 → 250mg daily × 7-10 days** |

### **Key Clinical Features:**

**Lyme Disease (3 Stages):**
1. **Early localized (3-30 days):** Erythema migrans (bull's-eye rash), fever
2. **Early disseminated (weeks-months):** Carditis (AV block), facial palsy, meningitis
3. **Late (months-years):** Arthritis (knee), chronic encephalopathy

**RMSF (Most Severe):**
- **Triad:** Fever, headache, rash (but only 60% have rash in first 3 days)
- **Rash:** Starts wrists/ankles (centripetal spread), petechial
- **High mortality** if untreated → **Start doxycycline empirically** if suspected
- **Lab:** Thrombocytopenia, hyponatremia

**Ehrlichiosis/Anaplasmosis:**
- **Similar presentation:** Fever, headache, malaise, NO RASH
- **Lab:** Leukopenia, thrombocytopenia, ↑LFTs
- **Morulae** on peripheral smear (intracellular inclusions in WBCs)

**Babesiosis:**
- **Hemolytic anemia** (jaundice, dark urine, splenomegaly)
- **"Maltese cross"** on blood smear (pathognomonic)
- **Severe in asplenic** patients

## Key Points

### **When to Suspect Tick-Borne Illness:**
- **Recent tick exposure** (especially endemic areas: Northeast, Upper Midwest, Mid-Atlantic)
- **Summer months** (May-September)
- **Fever + headache ± rash** within days-weeks of tick bite

### **Treatment Principles:**
- **Doxycycline** first-line for Lyme, RMSF, ehrlichiosis, anaplasmosis
- **RMSF:** Don't wait for confirmatory testing (high mortality if delayed)
- **Babesiosis:** Atovaquone + azithromycin (NOT doxycycline)
- **Lyme prophylaxis:** Single dose doxycycline 200mg if *Ixodes* tick attached >36h (endemic area)

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **RMSF:** Start doxycycline empirically if suspected (don't wait for rash or confirmation)
- **Lyme:** Doxycycline 100mg BID × 10-21 days (early), IV ceftriaxone (neuro/cardiac)
- **Ehrlichia/Anaplasma:** Fever + no rash + cytopenias
- **Babesiosis:** Hemolytic anemia, "Maltese cross" on smear
- **Doxycycline safe in children** for RMSF (short course, life-threatening illness)
