## Infective Endocarditis: Duke Criteria (2023 Duke-ISCVID Update)

**Q:** What are the modified Duke criteria (2023 Duke-ISCVID update) for diagnosing infective endocarditis, and how do they differ from the original Duke criteria?

**A:**

**Overview:**
- **Duke criteria:** Clinical scoring system for diagnosing infective endocarditis (IE)
- **Original Duke criteria (1994):** Major and minor criteria
- **Modified Duke criteria (2000):** Added Q fever, Bartonella; revised echo criteria
- **2023 Duke-ISCVID Criteria (Latest Update):** Expanded imaging beyond echocardiography (PET/CT, cardiac CT), updated microbiology, refined clinical criteria

---

**2023 Duke-ISCVID Criteria Categories:**

**Definite IE:**
- **2 major criteria** OR
- **1 major + 3 minor criteria** OR
- **5 minor criteria**

**Possible IE:**
- **1 major + 1 minor criterion** OR
- **3 minor criteria**

**Rejected IE:**
- Firm alternative diagnosis OR
- Resolution of IE syndrome with ≤4 days of antibiotics OR
- No pathologic evidence at surgery/autopsy after ≤4 days of antibiotics

---

## MAJOR CRITERIA

---

### **Major Criterion 1: Positive Blood Cultures**

**A. Typical Microorganisms (From ≥2 Separate Blood Cultures):**

**Viridans Streptococci:**
- *Streptococcus gallolyticus* (formerly S. bovis - associated with colon cancer)
- Other viridans strep (S. mitis, S. sanguinis, S. mutans, S. salivarius)

**HACEK Group:**
- *Haemophilus*, *Aggregatibacter*, *Cardiobacterium*, *Eikenella*, *Kingella*

**Staphylococcus aureus:**
- **In absence of primary focus** (i.e., not from obvious SSTI, pneumonia, or line infection)
- If S. aureus from obvious primary focus → Consider "minor criterion" instead

**Enterococcus:**
- Enterococcus faecalis, E. faecium

---

**B. Microorganisms Consistent with IE (From Persistently Positive Blood Cultures):**

**Persistently Positive Blood Cultures:**
- ≥2 positive cultures drawn >12 hours apart OR
- All of 3 OR majority of ≥4 separate cultures (first and last drawn ≥1 hour apart)

---

**C. Single Positive Blood Culture for *Coxiella burnetii* OR:**
- **IgG antibody titer >1:800** (Q fever)

---

**D. Single Positive Blood Culture or Positive Molecular Assay (PCR) for:**
- **Tropheryma whipplei** (Whipple disease)
- **Bartonella species** (culture-negative IE)
- **Mycoplasma hominis** (prosthetic valve endocarditis)

---

### **Major Criterion 2: Imaging Evidence of Endocardial Involvement**

**2023 Duke-ISCVID Expansion:** Added **PET/CT**, **cardiac CT**, and **cardiac MRI** (beyond echocardiography)

---

**A. Echocardiography (TTE or TEE):**

**Vegetation:**
- Oscillating intracardiac mass on valve, supporting structures, or implanted material
- In path of regurgitant jet
- **No alternative anatomic explanation**

**Abscess:**
- Perivalvular abscess
- **Intracardiac abscess**
- Pseudoaneurysm

**New Partial Dehiscence of Prosthetic Valve:**
- New prosthetic valve dehiscence (not old/stable)

**New Valvular Regurgitation:**
- **New or worsening valvular regurgitation**
- (Baseline regurgitation does NOT count)

**Key Point:** **TEE >> TTE** for sensitivity (90-100% vs 50-70%)

---

**B. FDG-PET/CT or Radiolabeled Leukocyte SPECT/CT (NEW in 2023):**

**Indication:**
- **Prosthetic valve endocarditis (PVE)** or **cardiac device IE**
- **>3 months post-implantation** (to avoid false positives from post-surgical inflammation)

**Positive Finding:**
- **Abnormal FDG uptake** at site of prosthetic valve or cardiac device

**Key Point:** **PET/CT improves sensitivity for PVE** (echo often limited by shadowing/artifacts)

---

**C. Cardiac CT (NEW in 2023):**

**Findings:**
- **Perivalvular lesions** (abscess, pseudoaneurysm, fistula, perforation)
- **Coronary artery embolism** (septic emboli to coronaries)

**Key Point:** **Cardiac CT superior for detecting paravalvular abscesses** (compared to echo)

---

## MINOR CRITERIA

---

### **Minor Criterion 1: Predisposition**

**Predisposing Heart Condition:**
- **Prosthetic valve**
- **Prior endocarditis**
- **Congenital heart disease** (unrepaired or repaired with residual defect)
- **Rheumatic heart disease**
- **Degenerative valve disease** (calcific aortic stenosis, mitral regurgitation)
- **Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy**

**OR**

**Predisposing Risk Factor:**
- **Injection drug use (IDU)**

---

### **Minor Criterion 2: Fever**

**Temperature >38°C (>100.4°F)**

---

### **Minor Criterion 3: Vascular Phenomena**

**Major Arterial Emboli:**
- **Septic pulmonary emboli** (right-sided endocarditis)
- **Mycotic aneurysm**
- **Intracranial hemorrhage** (mycotic aneurysm rupture)
- **Conjunctival hemorrhages**
- **Janeway lesions** (painless erythematous macules on palms/soles)

**2023 Update:**
- **Recent embolic stroke** or **asymptomatic cerebral/systemic emboli** on imaging (CT/MRI)

---

### **Minor Criterion 4: Immunologic Phenomena**

**Glomerulonephritis:**
- Immune complex deposition

**Osler Nodes:**
- Painful, violaceous nodules on finger/toe pads

**Roth Spots:**
- Retinal hemorrhages with pale center

**Rheumatoid Factor Positivity:**
- (Non-specific)

---

### **Minor Criterion 5: Microbiologic Evidence**

**Positive Blood Culture NOT Meeting Major Criterion:**
- Organisms atypical for IE (e.g., coagulase-negative Staph from 1 culture)
- OR typical organisms from single blood culture

**OR**

**Serologic Evidence of Active Infection:**
- With organism consistent with IE (e.g., Bartonella, Brucella, Legionella, Chlamydia psittaci)

---

## 2023 DUKE-ISCVID KEY UPDATES

**What's New in 2023?**

| **Category** | **2000 Modified Duke** | **2023 Duke-ISCVID** |
|--------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| **Imaging** | Echocardiography only | ✅ **Added:** PET/CT, cardiac CT, cardiac MRI for PVE/device IE |
| **S. aureus** | Always "major" if bacteremic | ✅ **Refined:** Only "major" if **no primary focus** (exclude catheter/SSTI) |
| **Molecular Diagnostics** | Not included | ✅ **Added:** PCR for Bartonella, T. whipplei, Mycoplasma hominis |
| **Embolic Events** | Imaging emboli not specified | ✅ **Added:** Asymptomatic emboli on CT/MRI (stroke, systemic) |
| **Prosthetic Valve IE** | Echo often inadequate | ✅ **Added:** PET/CT (>3 months post-op) improves sensitivity |

## CLINICAL APPLICATION

---

**Example 1: Definite IE (2 Major Criteria)**

**Case:** 65-year-old with known aortic stenosis presents with fever and S. aureus bacteremia (no obvious primary focus). TEE shows vegetation on aortic valve.

**Duke Criteria:**
- **Major #1:** Positive blood cultures for S. aureus (typical organism, no primary focus)
- **Major #2:** TEE shows vegetation

**Diagnosis:** **Definite IE (2 major criteria)**

---

**Example 2: Definite IE (1 Major + 3 Minor)**

**Case:** 40-year-old IVDU with fever, new murmur, septic pulmonary emboli on CT, and multiple blood cultures growing S. aureus.

**Duke Criteria:**
- **Major #1:** Positive blood cultures for S. aureus (no primary focus)
- **Minor #1:** Predisposing risk factor (IVDU)
- **Minor #2:** Fever >38°C
- **Minor #3:** Vascular phenomena (septic pulmonary emboli)

**Diagnosis:** **Definite IE (1 major + 3 minor)**

---

**Example 3: Possible IE (1 Major + 1 Minor)**

**Case:** 70-year-old with prosthetic aortic valve, fever, and single blood culture growing coagulase-negative Staph (CoNS).

**Duke Criteria:**
- **Major #1:** None (single CoNS culture does NOT meet major criterion)
- **Minor #1:** Predisposing heart condition (prosthetic valve)
- **Minor #2:** Fever >38°C
- **Minor #5:** Microbiologic evidence (positive culture not meeting major criterion)

**Diagnosis:** **Possible IE (1 major [if echo shows vegetation] + 1-2 minor)** OR **Possible IE (0 major + 3 minor)**

**Next Steps:**
- **TEE** (TTE may miss PVE)
- **PET/CT** (if TEE negative and >3 months post-op)
- **Repeat blood cultures**

---

**Example 4: Culture-Negative Endocarditis (Q Fever)**

**Case:** 55-year-old farmer with prosthetic valve, fever, new vegetation on TEE, negative routine blood cultures, *Coxiella burnetii* IgG titer 1:1600.

**Duke Criteria:**
- **Major #1:** Positive serology for *C. burnetii* (IgG >1:800)
- **Major #2:** TEE shows vegetation

**Diagnosis:** **Definite IE (2 major criteria)**

---

## DIAGNOSTIC APPROACH

**Step 1: Suspect IE if:**
- Bacteremia with typical organism (S. aureus, viridans strep, enterococcus)
- Fever + new murmur
- Fever + predisposing heart condition (prosthetic valve, prior IE, IDU)
- Embolic phenomena (stroke, septic emboli)
- Fever of unknown origin + risk factors

---

**Step 2: Obtain ≥3 Sets of Blood Cultures (Before Antibiotics):**
- Draw from **separate venipunctures**
- **≥1 hour apart** (first and last)
- **≥20 mL per set** (10 mL aerobic + 10 mL anaerobic)

**Hold antibiotics until cultures obtained** (unless unstable)

---

**Step 3: Echocardiography:**

**TTE First:**
- Good for initial screening
- Sensitivity 50-70% (misses small vegetations, PVE)

**TEE if:**
- **TTE negative** but high suspicion (esp. PVE, device IE)
- **Prosthetic valve**
- **Complications suspected** (abscess, fistula)
- **Poor TTE windows**

**TEE Sensitivity:** 90-100%

---

**Step 4: Advanced Imaging (If TEE Negative and High Suspicion):**

**PET/CT or Cardiac CT:**
- **PVE or device IE** (>3 months post-op)
- Detects paravalvular lesions, device lead vegetations

---

**Step 5: Workup for Culture-Negative IE (If Negative Routine Cultures):**

**Serologies:**
- **Coxiella burnetii** (Q fever) - IgG phase I >1:800
- **Bartonella** (cat-scratch disease) - serology, PCR
- **Brucella** - serology
- **Mycoplasma hominis** (PVE)
- **Tropheryma whipplei** (Whipple) - PCR

**Extended Incubation:**
- Hold blood cultures **≥3 weeks** for HACEK (though modern systems detect earlier)

---

## SENSITIVITY & SPECIFICITY

**Duke Criteria Sensitivity:**
- **80-90%** for native valve endocarditis (NVE)
- **60-70%** for prosthetic valve endocarditis (PVE) - improved to ~85% with PET/CT

**Common Causes of "Possible IE" (Not Definite):**
- **Prior antibiotics** → Negative cultures
- **Right-sided IE** → Echo may miss tricuspid vegetations
- **Culture-negative organisms** (Coxiella, Bartonella, etc.) → Need serologies
- **PVE** → Echo limited by prosthesis shadowing → Need PET/CT

---

## PITFALLS

**1. S. aureus Bacteremia From CLABSI:**
- **Not automatically "major criterion"** if obvious catheter source
- **Remove catheter, repeat cultures** → If persistent bacteremia, consider IE

**2. Single Positive CoNS Culture:**
- Likely contaminant
- **Repeat cultures** before attributing to IE

**3. TTE Negative in PVE:**
- **TEE or PET/CT mandatory** (TTE sensitivity only ~20-30% for PVE)

**4. Prior Antibiotics:**
- **Culture-negative IE** → Consider serologies for fastidious organisms

**5. "Possible IE" = High-Risk Diagnosis:**
- Treat as **presumptive IE** until alternative diagnosis confirmed
- Repeat imaging (TEE, PET/CT)

---

**Mnemonic: "FROM JANE" (Major Criteria)**
- **F**ever (minor, but key symptom)
- **R**egurgitation (new valvular regurgitation - major)
- **O**scillating mass (vegetation - major)
- **M**icro organisms (typical from blood cultures - major)
- **J**aneway lesions (vascular - minor)
- **A**bscess (perivalvular - major)
- **N**ew dehiscence (prosthetic valve - major)
- **E**chocardio findings (vegetation, abscess, regurgitation - major)

**Mnemonic: "I'M FEVERISH" (Minor Criteria)**
- **I**njection drug use (predisposition)
- **M**urmur (predisposition - valve disease)
- **F**ever (>38°C)
- **E**mboli (vascular phenomena)
- **V**alve abnormality (predisposition)
- **E**ye findings (Roth spots - immunologic)
- **R**heumatoid factor (immunologic)
- **I**mmune complex GN (immunologic)
- **S**erologies positive (microbiologic)
- **H**eart condition (predisposition)

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **Duke criteria = 80-90% sensitive** for NVE, 60-70% for PVE (improved to ~85% with PET/CT)
- **TEE >> TTE** for sensitivity (90-100% vs 50-70%)
- **PET/CT essential for PVE** (if >3 months post-op and TEE negative)
- **S. aureus bacteremia = IE until proven otherwise** (unless obvious primary focus)
- **≥3 sets of blood cultures** before antibiotics (separate venipunctures, ≥1h apart)
- **"Possible IE" = treat as presumptive IE** until alternative diagnosis
- **Culture-negative IE:** Check *Coxiella*, Bartonella, Brucella serologies
- **2023 Duke-ISCVID updates:** Added PET/CT, cardiac CT; refined S. aureus criteria; added PCR diagnostics
- **Cardiac CT best for paravalvular abscess detection** (better than echo)
- **Janeway lesions = painless** (vascular), **Osler nodes = painful** (immunologic)
