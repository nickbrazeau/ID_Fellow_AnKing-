## Question

Which parasites cause eosinophilia? Use the **"Tissue-Invasive Helminths = Eosinophilia"** framework.

## Answer

### **Parasites and Eosinophilia:**

**Causes of Eosinophilia:**
- **Tissue-invasive helminths** (worms that migrate through tissues)
- **NOT** protozoa (no eosinophilia)
- **NOT** intestinal helminths (unless tissue invasion)

### **Common Parasites Causing Eosinophilia:**

| Parasite | Transmission | Presentation | Diagnosis | Treatment |
|----------|--------------|--------------|-----------|-----------|
| ***Strongyloides*** | **Soil (bare feet)** | Asymptomatic OR **hyperinfection syndrome** (if immunosuppressed) | **Serology**, stool O&P (low sensitivity) | **Ivermectin 200 mcg/kg daily × 2 days** |
| ***Schistosoma*** | **Freshwater exposure** (Africa, Middle East, South America) | **Katayama fever** (acute), chronic: hematuria (*S. haematobium*), hepatosplenomegaly, portal HTN | **Serology**, urine/stool for eggs | **Praziquantel 40-60 mg/kg** (single day, divided doses) |
| ***Toxocara*** | **Dog/cat feces** (pica, children) | **Visceral larva migrans** (hepatosplenomegaly, eosinophilia), **ocular larva migrans** | **Serology** | **Albendazole 400mg BID × 5 days** |
| **Hookworm** | **Soil (bare feet)** | Iron-deficiency anemia, GI symptoms | **Stool O&P** | **Albendazole 400mg × 1** OR **mebendazole** |
| ***Trichinella*** | **Undercooked pork/game** | Fever, myalgia, periorbital edema, eosinophilia | **Serology**, muscle biopsy | **Albendazole 400mg BID × 8-14 days** |
| **Ascaris** | **Fecal-oral** | **Loeffler syndrome** (pneumonitis during larval migration), intestinal obstruction | **Stool O&P** | **Albendazole 400mg × 1** |

### **Special Consideration: *Strongyloides*:**

**Hyperinfection Syndrome:**
- **Risk factors:** Corticosteroids, immunosuppression (HTLV-1, transplant, chemotherapy)
- **Presentation:** Severe diarrhea, GI hemorrhage, sepsis (Gram-negative bacteremia), respiratory failure
- **High mortality** (>50% if untreated)
- **Screen before immunosuppression** if endemic exposure (Southeast Asia, Africa, Latin America)

**Treatment:**
- **Uncomplicated:** Ivermectin 200 mcg/kg daily × 2 days
- **Hyperinfection:** Ivermectin 200 mcg/kg daily (continue until stool/sputum negative × 2 weeks)

## Key Points

### **Eosinophilia Algorithm:**

**Peripheral Eosinophilia (AEC >500 cells/μL):**
1. **History:** Travel, soil exposure, raw food, immunosuppression
2. **Stool O&P × 3** (low sensitivity for *Strongyloides*)
3. ***Strongyloides* serology** (if endemic exposure, especially before immunosuppression)
4. ***Schistosoma* serology** (if freshwater exposure in endemic areas)
5. Consider *Toxocara*, *Trichinella*, hookworm, *Ascaris* based on exposures

### **Protozoa vs Helminths:**
- **Protozoa (single-celled):** ***Giardia*, *Entamoeba*, *Cryptosporidium*** → **NO eosinophilia**
- **Helminths (worms):** Tissue-invasive helminths → **Eosinophilia**

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Eosinophilia = Helminths** (tissue-invasive), NOT protozoa
- ***Strongyloides*:** Screen before immunosuppression (ivermectin prophylaxis)
- **Hyperinfection syndrome:** High mortality, occurs with corticosteroids/immunosuppression
- ***Schistosoma*:** Freshwater exposure (Africa, Middle East, South America), praziquantel
- **Stool O&P × 3** (multiple samples improve sensitivity)
- ***Strongyloides* serology** more sensitive than stool O&P
