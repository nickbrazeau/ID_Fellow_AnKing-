## Question

How do you interpret CSF findings in meningitis? Use the **"Bacterial = LOW-glucose HIGH-protein HIGH-WBC (PMNs)"** mnemonic.

## Answer

### **CSF Interpretation Table:**

| Parameter | Normal | Bacterial | Viral | Fungal/TB |
|-----------|--------|-----------|-------|-----------|
| **Opening pressure** | 10-18 cmH2O | ↑↑ (>25) | ↑ (18-25) | ↑↑ (>25) |
| **Appearance** | Clear | **Cloudy/purulent** | Clear | **Cloudy** |
| **WBC count** | <5 cells/µL | **>1000** (often >10,000) | 10-1000 | 25-500 |
| **WBC differential** | - | **>80% PMNs** | **>50% lymphs** | **Lymphs** |
| **Protein** | 15-45 mg/dL | **↑↑ (>200)** | ↑ (50-100) | **↑↑ (100-500)** |
| **Glucose** | >40 mg/dL (CSF:serum >0.6) | **↓↓ (<40, ratio <0.4)** | Normal | **↓ (<45)** |
| **Gram stain** | Negative | **Positive 60-90%** | Negative | Negative (AFB for TB) |

### **"Bacterial = LOW-glucose HIGH-protein HIGH-WBC (PMNs)" Mnemonic:**

**Bacterial:**
- **B**rick-wall HIGH: WBC >1000, protein >200
- **A**cid-fast for TB (fungal/TB similar)
- **C**loudiness, purulent
- **T**eens-hundreds for glucose (LOW <40)
- **E**mergency (treat immediately)
- **R**atio CSF:serum glucose <0.4
- **I**ncreased opening pressure
- **A**cute presentation (<24 hrs)
- **L**ow glucose critical finding

**PMNs:**
- **P**olymorphonuclear cells >80% (neutrophils)
- **M**assive WBC elevation (>1000)
- **N**eed urgent antibiotics
- **S**tat Gram stain/culture

### **Viral (Aseptic) Meningitis:**
- **Lymphocytic predominance** (>50%)
- **Normal glucose** (key differentiator)
- **Moderate WBC** (10-1000)
- **Mildly elevated protein** (50-100)

### **Fungal/TB Meningitis:**
- **Lymphocytic** predominance
- **Low glucose** (like bacterial)
- **Very high protein** (100-500)
- **Moderate WBC** (25-500)
- **Subacute/chronic** presentation (weeks)

## Key Points

### **Critical CSF Findings:**

**Send for:**
- Cell count with differential
- Protein, glucose (with simultaneous serum glucose)
- Gram stain, culture
- Consider: Cryptococcal antigen, HSV PCR, enterovirus PCR, TB AFB/PCR

**Red Flags (Bacterial):**
- **Glucose <40 mg/dL** or ratio <0.4
- **WBC >1000** with PMN predominance
- **Cloudy/purulent** appearance
- **Positive Gram stain**

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Bacterial:** HIGH WBC (PMNs), LOW glucose, HIGH protein
- **Viral:** Lymphs, NORMAL glucose, mild protein elevation
- **Fungal/TB:** Lymphs, LOW glucose, VERY HIGH protein, subacute
- **Never delay antibiotics** for LP if suspect bacterial meningitis
- CSF glucose <40 or CSF:serum ratio <0.4 = **pathologic**
