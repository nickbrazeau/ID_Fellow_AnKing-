## Viral Meningitis & Encephalitis Summary

**Q:** What are the common etiologies, CSF findings, and management of viral meningitis and encephalitis?

**A:**

## VIRAL MENINGITIS

**Most Common Causes:**
- **Enteroviruses** (coxsackie, echovirus) - **#1 cause** (85-90%)
- **HSV-2** (Mollaret meningitis - recurrent)
- **VZV, HIV, mumps**

---

### **Clinical Features:**

- **Fever, headache, photophobia**
- **Neck stiffness** (may be mild)
- **No altered mental status** (vs encephalitis)

**Key Point:** **Meningitis = meningeal signs WITHOUT focal neuro deficits/altered MS**

---

### **CSF Findings:**

| **Parameter** | **Viral Meningitis** | **Bacterial Meningitis** |
|---------------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| **Opening pressure** | Normal or mildly ↑ | Elevated |
| **WBC** | 10-500 cells/μL | >1000 cells/μL |
| **Differential** | **Lymphocyte predominance** | **PMN predominance** |
| **Protein** | Normal or mildly ↑ (<100 mg/dL) | Elevated (>100 mg/dL) |
| **Glucose** | **Normal** (>45 mg/dL) | **Low** (<45 mg/dL) |

**Key Point:** **Lymphocytic pleocytosis + normal glucose** = viral meningitis

---

### **Diagnosis:**

- **Enterovirus PCR** (CSF) - most common cause
- **HSV-1/2 PCR** (CSF)
- **VZV PCR** (CSF)

---

### **Treatment:**

- **Supportive care** (hydration, analgesia)
- **Acyclovir** 10 mg/kg IV q8h (if HSV/VZV suspected - start empirically)

**Key Point:** **Start empiric acyclovir** if encephalitis features (altered MS) until HSV PCR back

---

## VIRAL ENCEPHALITIS

**Most Common Causes:**
- **HSV-1** (60% of cases with identified cause)
- **Enteroviruses, VZV, West Nile virus, arboviruses**

---

### **Clinical Features:**

- **Fever, headache**
- **Altered mental status** (confusion, disorientation)
- **Focal neurologic deficits** (aphasia, hemiparesis)
- **Seizures**

**Key Point:** **Encephalitis = altered MS + focal neuro findings**

---

### **Diagnosis:**

**CSF:**
- **Lymphocytic pleocytosis**
- **RBCs** (if HSV encephalitis - hemorrhagic)
- **HSV PCR** (sensitivity >95%)

**MRI:**
- **Temporal lobe hyperintensity** (T2/FLAIR) - HSV encephalitis

**EEG:**
- **PLEDs** (periodic lateralizing epileptiform discharges) - HSV encephalitis

---

### **Treatment:**

**Empiric (Start Immediately):**
- **Acyclovir** 10 mg/kg IV q8h x 14-21 days

**Key Point:** **DO NOT wait for MRI/PCR** - start acyclovir immediately if encephalitis suspected

---

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **Enteroviruses = #1 cause of viral meningitis** (85-90%)
- **Lymphocytic pleocytosis + normal glucose** = viral meningitis
- **HSV-1 = #1 cause of sporadic encephalitis** (temporal lobe)
- **Start acyclovir empirically** if altered MS/focal neuro (encephalitis)
- **Meningitis = meningeal signs, NO altered MS**
- **Encephalitis = altered MS + focal neuro deficits**

**Media:** None
