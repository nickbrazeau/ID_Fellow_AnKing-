## Question

What are the major antiparasitic drug classes? Use the **"MET-AZOLE-BENZ-QUINE"** mnemonic.

## Answer

### **"MET-AZOLE-BENZ-QUINE" Mnemonic:**

**MET (Metronidazole):**
- **M**etronidazole, tinidazole
- **E**ntamoeba, Giardia, Trichomonas
- **T**rophozoites killed

**AZOLE (Azoles for Parasites):**
- Albendazole, mebendazole
- **Z**one: helminths (roundworms, tapeworms)
- **O**ral therapy
- **L**arvae and adult worms
- **E**xcellent for most helminths

**BENZ (Benzimidazoles = Albendazole/Mebendazole):**
- **B**lock microtubule polymerization
- **E**ctop arasites (scabies, lice) use ivermectin/permethrin
- **N**ematodes (roundworms)
- **Z**oonotic infections (Echinococcus, Toxocara)

**QUINE (Antimalarials):**
- **Q**uinine, chloroquine
- **U**sed for malaria
- **I**nhibit heme polymerization
- **N**ewer: artemisinin-based combination therapy (ACT)
- **E**xcellent for *Plasmodium*

### **Major Classes:**

| Class | Examples | Primary Uses |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| **Nitroimidazoles** | Metronidazole, tinidazole | *Giardia*, *Entamoeba*, *Trichomonas* |
| **Benzimidazoles** | **Albendazole**, mebendazole | Helminths (roundworms, tapeworms) |
| **Antimalarials** | Artemisinin, chloroquine, atovaquone-proguanil | *Plasmodium* (malaria) |
| **Pyrimethamine** | Pyrimethamine + sulfadiazine | *Toxoplasma* |
| **Ivermectin** | Ivermectin | *Strongyloides*, onchocerciasis, scabies |
| **Praziquantel** | Praziquantel | Flukes, tapeworms |

## Key Points

### **Common Parasitic Infections & First-Line Treatments:**

| Parasite | Disease | First-Line Drug |
|----------|---------|-----------------|
| ***Giardia lamblia*** | Giardiasis | Metronidazole or tinidazole |
| ***Entamoeba histolytica*** | Amebiasis | Metronidazole + paromomycin (for cysts) |
| ***Plasmodium*** spp. | Malaria | Artemisinin-based combination therapy (ACT) |
| ***Toxoplasma gondii*** | Toxoplasmosis | Pyrimethamine + sulfadiazine + leucovorin |
| **Roundworms** (*Ascaris*, *hookworm*) | Intestinal nematodes | **Albendazole** or mebendazole |
| ***Strongyloides*** | Strongyloidiasis | **Ivermectin** |
| **Tapeworms** (*Taenia*) | Cestodiasis | **Praziquantel** or albendazole |
| **Schistosomiasis** | Flukes | **Praziquantel** |

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Albendazole** = workhorse for most helminths (broad spectrum)
- **Ivermectin** = *Strongyloides* (critical in immunosuppressed)
- **Praziquantel** = flukes and tapeworms
- **Artemisinin-based ACT** = gold standard for malaria
- **Metronidazole** = protozoa (Giardia, Entamoeba, Trichomonas)
