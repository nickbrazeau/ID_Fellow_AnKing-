## Levofloxacin vs Moxifloxacin: Comparison

**Q:** What are the key differences between levofloxacin and moxifloxacin, and when should each be used?

**A:**

**Comparative Spectrum:**

| **Feature** | **Levofloxacin** | **Moxifloxacin** |
|-------------|------------------|------------------|
| **Generation** | 2nd/3rd (classification varies) | 3rd/4th |
| **Gram-negatives** | Excellent (incl. Pseudomonas) | Good (NO Pseudomonas) |
| **Gram-positives** | Good (Strep pneumo) | Better (↑ Strep pneumo) |
| **Anaerobes** | Poor | Good (Bacteroides) |
| **Atypicals** | Excellent | Excellent |
| **Pseudomonas** | ✅ Moderate | ❌ Inadequate |

**Clinical Indications:**

**Levofloxacin:**
- **CAP** (750 mg daily x 5 days for outpatient)
- **HAP/VAP** (if Pseudomonas risk is LOW)
- **UTI/pyelonephritis** (good option, but cipro preferred for Pseudomonas)
- **Skin/soft tissue** (MRSA coverage)
- Balanced respiratory fluoroquinolone

**Moxifloxacin:**
- **CAP** (especially aspiration risk due to anaerobic coverage)
- **Intra-abdominal infections** (with anaerobic coverage)
- **Diabetic foot infections** (MRSA + anaerobes)
- **NOT for Pseudomonas** (inadequate activity)
- **NOT for UTI** (poor urinary concentration)

**Dosing:**
- **Levofloxacin:** 750 mg PO/IV daily (CAP), 500 mg daily (UTI)
- **Moxifloxacin:** 400 mg PO/IV daily

**Mnemonic: "Levo Leaves Pseudomonas, Moxi Makes anaerobes disappear"**
- **Levo** = Good Pseudomonas coverage
- **Moxi** = Good anaerobic coverage (but NO Pseudomonas)

**Clinical Pearl:**
- **Levofloxacin** = Balanced (use when Pseudomonas possible)
- **Moxifloxacin** = Enhanced Gram-positive + anaerobes (use for aspiration pneumonia, avoid if Pseudomonas risk)
