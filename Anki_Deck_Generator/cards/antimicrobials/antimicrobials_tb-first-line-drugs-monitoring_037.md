## First-Line TB Drugs: Mechanisms and Monitoring

**Q:** What are the mechanisms and major adverse effects of first-line TB drugs, and what monitoring is required?

**A:**

**Mechanisms and Toxicities:**

| **Drug** | **Mechanism** | **Major Adverse Effects** | **Monitoring** |
|----------|---------------|---------------------------|----------------|
| **Rifampin (R)** | Inhibits RNA polymerase | Hepatotoxicity, drug interactions (CYP450 inducer), orange discoloration (body fluids) | LFTs monthly, drug interaction assessment |
| **Isoniazid (I)** | Inhibits mycolic acid synthesis (InhA enzyme) | Hepatotoxicity, peripheral neuropathy, drug-induced lupus | LFTs monthly, **pyridoxine (B6) prophylaxis**, neurologic assessment |
| **Pyrazinamide (P)** | Unknown mechanism (requires acidic pH) | Hepatotoxicity, hyperuricemia/gout, arthralgias | LFTs monthly, uric acid (if symptomatic) |
| **Ethambutol (E)** | Inhibits arabinosyl transferase (cell wall) | **Optic neuritis** (dose-dependent, reversible if caught early) | **Baseline + monthly: visual acuity, color vision** (Ishihara plates) |

**Hepatotoxicity Monitoring:**
- **Baseline LFTs** (AST, ALT, bilirubin)
- **Monthly LFTs** during treatment
- **Hold therapy if:**
  - Symptomatic (N/V, jaundice, RUQ pain) + ALT/AST >3x ULN
  - Asymptomatic but ALT/AST >5x ULN
- **Risk factors:** Alcohol use, pre-existing liver disease, HIV, older age

**Peripheral Neuropathy Prevention (Isoniazid):**
- **Pyridoxine (vitamin B6) 25-50 mg daily** for all patients
- Especially: DM, HIV, alcohol use disorder, malnutrition, pregnancy, renal failure

**Ethambutol Optic Neuritis:**
- **Dose-dependent:** 15 mg/kg = 0.8% risk, 25 mg/kg = 5-6% risk
- **Symptoms:** Decreased visual acuity, red-green color blindness
- **Reversible** if detected early (stop drug)
- **Check monthly:** "Can you see red? Can you see green?"

**Mnemonic: "RIPE Harms"**
- **R**IF = **R**NA polymerase, **R**ugs (drug interactions, orange fluids)
- **I**NH = **I**njures nerves (peripheral neuropathy), **I**njures liver
- **P**ZA = **P**ain (gout/arthralgias), **P**oor liver function
- **E**MB = **E**yes (optic neuritis)

**Clinical Pearls:**
- All 3 (RIF, INH, PZA) cause hepatotoxicity → monitor LFTs monthly
- Rifampin is a **potent CYP450 inducer** → check all drug interactions
- ALWAYS give pyridoxine with isoniazid
- Ethambutol can usually be stopped after 2 months if drug-susceptible TB confirmed
