## Question

Summarize the five generations of cephalosporins using the **"G+ → G-"** concept. Which generation covers MRSA?

## Answer

**Mnemonic: "G+ PLUS to G- NEG" - As generations increase, Gram-positive coverage decreases, Gram-negative coverage increases**

| Generation | Key Agents | Gram-Positive | Gram-Negative | Special Features |
|------------|-----------|---------------|---------------|------------------|
| **1st** | Cefazolin, cephalexin | +++++ (MSSA, Strep) | + (*E. coli*, *Proteus*, *Klebsiella*) | Best MSSA coverage |
| **2nd** | Cefuroxime, cefoxitin | ++++ | ++ (+ *H. flu*, *M. cat*) | *H. flu*, some anaerobes (cefoxitin) |
| **3rd** | Ceftriaxone, ceftazidime | +++ | ++++ (Enterobacterales ± *Pseudomonas*) | **CNS penetration**, *Pseudomonas* (ceftaz only) |
| **4th** | Cefepime | +++ | +++++ (best *Pseudomonas*) | **AmpC stable**, CNS penetration |
| **5th** | Ceftaroline | +++++ (**MRSA**) | +++ (NO *Pseudomonas*) | **ONLY β-lactam with MRSA activity** |

## Key Points

### **General Rules:**
- **NO generation** covers: MRSA (except 5th), Enterococcus, Listeria, atypicals
- **3rd-5th generations** penetrate CSF (meningitis treatment)
- **Ceftazidime & cefepime** = anti-*Pseudomonas* activity
- **Ceftaroline (5th gen)** = ONLY cephalosporin active vs MRSA

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **1st gen (cefazolin):** Perioperative prophylaxis, MSSA infections
- **3rd gen (ceftriaxone):** Community-acquired pneumonia, meningitis, gonorrhea
- **3rd gen (ceftazidime):** *Pseudomonas* coverage (but weak Gram-pos)
- **4th gen (cefepime):** Nosocomial infections, febrile neutropenia (broad spectrum)
- **5th gen (ceftaroline):** MRSA pneumonia, skin infections

### **Increasing Generation = Spectrum Shift:**
- ↑ Gram-negative coverage
- ↑ β-lactamase stability
- ↓ Gram-positive coverage (EXCEPT 5th gen)
