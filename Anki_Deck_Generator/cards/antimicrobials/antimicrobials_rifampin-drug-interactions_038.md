## Rifampin Drug-Drug Interactions

**Q:** Why does rifampin cause extensive drug interactions, and what are the most clinically significant interactions?

**A:**

**Mechanism:**
- Rifampin is a **potent inducer** of:
  - **CYP450 enzymes** (3A4, 1A2, 2C9, 2C19, 2D6)
  - **Glucuronidation**
  - **P-glycoprotein** transport system
- Result: ↓ serum concentrations of many drugs (↓ efficacy, treatment failure)

**Critical Drug Interactions:**

| **Drug Class** | **Effect** | **Management** |
|----------------|------------|----------------|
| **HIV Protease Inhibitors (PIs)** | ↓ PI levels by >80% | **Avoid rifampin** → Use rifabutin (less potent inducer) |
| **HIV Integrase Inhibitors** (raltegravir) | ↓ AUC by 40% | ↑ Dose to 800 mg BID (from 400 mg BID) |
| **Warfarin** | ↓ Warfarin effect (↑ clearance via CYP2C9) | ↑ Warfarin dose significantly, monitor INR closely |
| **Oral contraceptives** | ↓ Efficacy → contraceptive failure | Use alternative contraception (barrier, IUD) |
| **Immunosuppressants** (tacrolimus, cyclosporine) | ↓ Levels → rejection risk | Monitor levels closely, may need 2-3x dose increase |
| **Azoles** (itraconazole, voriconazole) | ↓ Azole levels | Avoid or use posaconazole/isavuconazole (less affected) |
| **Statins** | ↓ Statin levels | May need dose adjustment |
| **Corticosteroids** | ↓ Steroid levels | May need higher doses |

**Rifabutin as Alternative:**
- **Less potent CYP450 inducer** than rifampin
- Preferred in patients on:
  - HIV protease inhibitors
  - Complex drug regimens where interactions are prohibitive
- Still causes interactions, but manageable

**Onset and Offset:**
- Enzyme induction onset: 5-7 days
- **Effects persist 2-4 weeks after stopping rifampin** (enzyme levels normalize slowly)

**Mnemonic: "RIFAMPIN WIPES drugs away"**
- **W**arfarin (↓ effect)
- **I**mmunosuppressants (↓ levels)
- **P**rotease inhibitors (↓ by >80%)
- **E**strogen/oral contraceptives (↓ efficacy)
- **S**tatins, Steroids, etc.

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **ALWAYS review medication list** before starting rifampin
- Consider rifabutin if patient on complex regimen (especially HIV)
- Warn patients on oral contraceptives to use alternative methods
- Rifampin interaction is **bidirectional** (other drugs also affect rifampin levels)
