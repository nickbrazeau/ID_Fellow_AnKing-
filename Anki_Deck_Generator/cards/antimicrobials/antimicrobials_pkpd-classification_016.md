## Question

Compare **time-dependent** vs **concentration-dependent** antibiotic killing. Which PK/PD parameter predicts efficacy for each, and which antibiotic classes fall into each category?

## Answer

| Feature | Time-Dependent | Concentration-Dependent |
|---------|----------------|------------------------|
| **Key PK/PD parameter** | **T>MIC** (time above MIC) | **AUC/MIC** or **Cmax/MIC** |
| **Efficacy driver** | Duration of exposure | Magnitude of peak concentration |
| **Target T>MIC** | 40-70% of dosing interval | N/A |
| **Target AUC/MIC** | N/A | ≥100-125 (Gram-neg); ≥25-35 (Gram-pos) |
| **Target Cmax/MIC** | N/A | >10 |
| **Dosing strategy** | Frequent dosing or continuous infusion | High-dose, extended interval |
| **Post-antibiotic effect (PAE)** | Minimal to moderate | Prolonged |

### **Antibiotic Classes:**

**Time-Dependent:**
- β-lactams (penicillins, cephalosporins, carbapenems)
- Vancomycin
- Linezolid

**Concentration-Dependent:**
- Aminoglycosides
- Fluoroquinolones
- Daptomycin
- Metronidazole

**Intermediate (time-dependent with prolonged PAE):**
- Azithromycin, tetracyclines

## Key Points

- **β-lactams:** Infuse over 3-4 hours (or continuous infusion) to maximize T>MIC
- **Aminoglycosides:** Once-daily high-dose dosing exploits concentration-dependent killing and PAE
- **Vancomycin:** AUC/MIC now preferred target (400-600) over trough monitoring
