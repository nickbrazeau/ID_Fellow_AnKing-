## Question

What are the three main functional groups in the **Bush-Jacoby classification** of β-lactamases, and what are their molecular class correlates?

## Answer

| Bush-Jacoby Group | Molecular Class | Key Characteristics | Examples |
|-------------------|-----------------|---------------------|----------|
| **Group 1** | Class C | Cephalosporinases (not inhibited by clavulanate) | *E. coli* AmpC, CMY variants |
| **Group 2** | Classes A & D | Broad-spectrum, ESBLs, serine carbapenemases | TEM, SHV, CTX-M (ESBLs); KPC (carbapenemase) |
| **Group 3** | Class B | Metallo-β-lactamases (require Zn²⁺) | NDM, VIM, IMP |

### Key Subgroups:
- **Group 2be:** Extended-spectrum β-lactamases (ESBLs)
- **Group 2f:** Serine carbapenemases (KPC, OXA-48)
- **Group 3a:** Broad-spectrum MBLs (IMP, VIM, NDM)

## Key Points

- By 2009, >890 unique β-lactamase sequences identified
- **Group 2** is the largest with 7 subgroups (2a-2f)
- **Inhibitor profiles** distinguish subgroups: Group 1 & 3 resistant to clavulanate; Group 2 variably inhibited
