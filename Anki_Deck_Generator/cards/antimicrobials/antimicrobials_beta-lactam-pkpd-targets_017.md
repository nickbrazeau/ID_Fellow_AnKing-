## Question

What is the **T>MIC target** for β-lactam antibiotics, and how does it vary by drug class? What dosing strategies optimize target attainment?

## Answer

### **T>MIC Targets (% of dosing interval drug concentration > MIC):**

| β-Lactam Class | Bacteriostasis | Bactericidal | Near-Maximal Killing |
|----------------|---------------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Penicillins** | 30-40% | 50% | 60-70% |
| **Cephalosporins** | 35-40% | 50-60% | 65-70% |
| **Carbapenems** | 20-30% | 40% | 40-50% |

**General clinical target:** Free drug concentration >MIC for **≥40-50%** of dosing interval

### **Dosing Strategies to Optimize T>MIC:**

1. **Extended infusion (3-4 hours):**
   - Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5g IV over 4 hours q8h
   - Meropenem 2g IV over 3 hours q8h
   - Cefepime 2g IV over 3-4 hours q8h

2. **Continuous infusion:**
   - After loading dose, constant infusion maintains steady-state >MIC
   - Best for critically ill with augmented renal clearance

3. **Shorter dosing intervals:**
   - Q6h instead of q8h dosing

## Key Points

- **Carbapenems** require less T>MIC than other β-lactams due to prolonged PAE
- **Augmented renal clearance** (critically ill): Consider higher doses or extended/continuous infusion
- **T>4×MIC** may be required for immunocompromised or severe infections
