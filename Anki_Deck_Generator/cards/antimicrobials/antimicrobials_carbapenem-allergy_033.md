## Question

What is the risk of **cross-reactivity** between carbapenems and penicillins in patients with documented penicillin allergy?

## Answer

### **Cross-Reactivity Risk:**

| Allergy Type | Cross-Reactivity Risk | Clinical Approach |
|--------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| **IgE-mediated penicillin allergy** (positive skin test) | **<1%** (0.87%) | Generally safe to use |
| **History of penicillin allergy** (no testing) | 1-3% | Consider skin testing or cautious use |
| **Penicillin anaphylaxis** | <1% but higher caution | Skin testing recommended |

### **Evidence:**
- **Systematic review:** 1,127 penicillin-allergic patients → 0.87% cross-reactivity to carbapenems (95% CI 0.32-2.32%)
- **Prospective study:** 212 patients with IgE-mediated penicillin reactions + positive skin tests → **ALL tolerated carbapenems**
- **Overall carbapenem allergy incidence:** 0.3-3.7% in general population

### **Structural Differences:**

| Feature | Penicillins | Carbapenems |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **Core structure** | 4:5 fused β-lactam/thiazolidine ring | 4:5 fused β-lactam/hydroxyethyl ring |
| **Key difference** | Sulfur atom at C-1 | **Carbon atom at C-1** (NOT sulfur) |
| **Side chains** | Variable R groups | Different R groups |

**Structural dissimilarity explains low cross-reactivity**

## Key Points

### **Clinical Recommendations (2024):**

1. **Low-risk penicillin allergy (rash only, remote history):**
   - Carbapenems are safe - use without testing

2. **IgE-mediated allergy (urticaria, angioedema, anaphylaxis):**
   - Cross-reactivity <1%
   - Can use with caution OR perform skin testing first
   - Most guidelines: carbapenem use is acceptable

3. **Recent anaphylaxis to penicillin:**
   - Consider skin testing (negative predictive value ~100%)
   - Graded challenge if needed
   - Alternative: aztreonam (NO cross-reactivity with β-lactams except ceftazidime)

### **Safest β-Lactam for Penicillin Allergy:**
- **Aztreonam:** Monobactam structure - NO cross-reactivity with penicillins (except ceftazidime due to shared side chain)
- **Carbapenems:** <1% cross-reactivity - generally safe

### **Comparative Cross-Reactivity:**
- **Cephalosporins:** 2-3% (higher than carbapenems)
- **Carbapenems:** <1%
- **Aztreonam:** 0% (except ceftazidime)
