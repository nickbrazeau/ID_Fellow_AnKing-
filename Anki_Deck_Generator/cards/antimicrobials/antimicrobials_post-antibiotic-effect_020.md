## Question

What is the **post-antibiotic effect (PAE)**, and how does it differ among major antibiotic classes? Use the **"Long PAE = AG-FQ-Carbs"** mnemonic.

## Answer

### **Definition:**
PAE = persistent suppression of bacterial growth **after antibiotic removal**, even without host defenses present

### **PAE Duration by Class (Against Gram-Negatives):**

| Antibiotic Class | PAE Duration | Clinical Implication |
|------------------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Aminoglycosides (AG)** | **1-5 hours** | Extended-interval dosing possible |
| **Fluoroquinolones (FQ)** | **2-7.5 hours** | Once-daily dosing effective |
| **Carbapenems** | **2-4 hours** | Longer than other β-lactams |
| **Penicillins/Cephalosporins** | **<1 hour** | Require frequent/continuous dosing |
| **Vancomycin** | **2-5 hours** (vs Gram-pos) | Allows q12h dosing |

### **Mnemonic: "Long PAE = AG-FQ-Carbs"**
- **A**minoglycosides
- **G**lycopeptides (vancomycin vs Gram-positives)
- **F**luoro**q**uinolones
- **Carb**apenems

### **All β-lactams have SHORT PAE vs Gram-negatives, EXCEPT carbapenems**

## Key Points

- PAE allows **extended dosing intervals** despite short serum half-life (aminoglycosides, fluoroquinolones)
- **β-lactams** (except carbapenems) have minimal PAE → need T>MIC optimization
- PAE is **concentration-dependent** (higher peak → longer PAE)
- Against **Gram-positives**, most antibiotics have PAE (including β-lactams ~1-2 hours)
