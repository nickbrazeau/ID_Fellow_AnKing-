## Anthelmintic Drug Classes and Mechanisms

**Q:** What are the mechanisms of action for the 4 major anthelmintic drug classes? (Use mnemonic **BAPI**)

**A:**

| Class | Drugs | Mechanism | Target Helminths |
|-------|-------|-----------|------------------|
| **B**enzimidazoles | Albendazole, mebendazole | Bind **β-tubulin** → inhibit microtubule polymerization → ↓ glucose uptake → ATP depletion → immobilization | **Nematodes** (roundworms): Ascaris, hookworm, pinworm, Strongyloides |
| **A**vermectins | Ivermectin | ↑ **Glutamate-gated Cl⁻ channels** → hyperpolarization → pharyngeal/muscle paralysis | **Nematodes** (Strongyloides, onchocerciasis, lymphatic filariasis) |
| **P**raziquantel | Praziquantel | ↑ **Ca²⁺ permeability** → uncontrolled muscle contraction → paralysis & tegument disruption | **Cestodes** (tapeworms) + **Trematodes** (flukes/schistosomes) |
| **I**sospora/broad | Nitazoxanide | Inhibits **PFOR-dependent electron transfer** | Broad: nematodes, cestodes, protozoa |

**Key Pearls:**
- **Benzimidazoles require fat** for absorption (take with meals)
- **Ivermectin**: Empty stomach; avoid in Loa loa (encephalopathy risk)
- **Praziquantel**: CYP3A4 substrate (drug interactions)
