## Ciprofloxacin: Specific Uses and Spectrum

**Q:** What are the key clinical indications for ciprofloxacin, and what organisms does it cover best?

**A:**

**Spectrum Highlights:**
- **Best Gram-negative coverage** of all fluoroquinolones
- **Best Pseudomonas aeruginosa** activity (MIC 2-8x lower than levo/moxi)
- E. coli, Klebsiella, Enterobacter, Proteus, Salmonella, Shigella, Campylobacter
- **POOR Strep pneumoniae** coverage (not for CAP monotherapy)
- **POOR anaerobic** coverage

**Key Clinical Indications:**

| **Indication** | **Dosing** | **Notes** |
|----------------|------------|-----------|
| **Complicated UTI/pyelonephritis** | 400 mg IV q8-12h or 500-750 mg PO BID | First-line for resistant GNR, Pseudomonas |
| **Prostatitis (chronic bacterial)** | 500 mg PO BID x 4-6 weeks | Good prostatic penetration |
| **Infectious diarrhea** | 500 mg PO BID x 3-5 days | Salmonella, Shigella, Campylobacter (↑resistance) |
| **Typhoid fever** | 500 mg PO BID x 7-14 days | Alternative if susceptible (resistance common in South Asia) |
| **Anthrax post-exposure prophylaxis** | 500 mg PO BID x 60 days | Bioterrorism agent |
| **Pseudomonas infections** | 400 mg IV q8h (± aminoglycoside) | Always combine for serious infections |

**DO NOT Use For:**
- ❌ Community-acquired pneumonia (poor Strep pneumo coverage)
- ❌ Anaerobic infections (no Bacteroides coverage)
- ❌ MRSA or Strep pyogenes skin infections

**Mnemonic: "Cipro for Pseudomonas, Pee, and Poop"**
- **P**seudomonas (best coverage)
- **P**ee (UTI, prostatitis)
- **P**oop (GI infections: Salmonella, Shigella, Campylobacter)

**Clinical Pearl:**
- Increasing fluoroquinolone resistance in Campylobacter and Salmonella worldwide (especially Asia/Africa)
- Always check local resistance patterns before empiric use
