## Nitroimidazoles: Metronidazole and Tinidazole

**Q:** What is the mechanism of action of nitroimidazoles (metronidazole/tinidazole), and what is their antimicrobial spectrum? (Use mnemonic **DRAG**)

**A:**

**Mechanism (4-step process):**
1. **D**iffusion into anaerobic organisms
2. **R**eductive activation by pyruvate-ferredoxin oxidoreductase (PFOR)
3. **A**ctivated cytotoxic free radicals formed
4. **G**enomic DNA strand breakage â†’ cell death

**Protozoal Spectrum (both drugs):**
- *Trichomonas vaginalis*
- *Entamoeba histolytica* (amoebiasis)
- *Giardia lamblia/intestinalis*

**Bacterial Spectrum:**
- Obligate **anaerobes** (Bacteroides, *C. difficile*, anaerobic cocci)

**Tinidazole vs Metronidazole:**
- **Longer half-life** (single-dose option for trichomoniasis)
- **Higher cure rates** for protozoal infections
- **Better toxicity profile**
- Same mechanism and spectrum

**Resistance Note:** Treatment failure in giardiasis occurs in 5-40% of cases.
