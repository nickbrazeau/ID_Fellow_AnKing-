## First-Line Anthelmintic Treatments by Helminth Class

**Q:** What are the first-line treatments and cure rates for common helminthic infections?

**A:**

**NEMATODES (Roundworms):**
- **Ascaris lumbricoides**: Albendazole 400 mg × 1 dose (88% cure rate)
- **Hookworm**: Albendazole 400 mg × 1 dose (72% cure) > pyrantel (32%)
- ***Strongyloides stercoralis***: **Ivermectin 200 μg/kg daily × 2 days** (97% cure) >> albendazole (63%)
- **Pinworm** (*Enterobius*): Albendazole 400 mg × 1, repeat in 2 weeks (94% cure)
- **Whipworm** (*Trichuris*): **Ivermectin + albendazole** combo (38-80% cure) > monotherapy

**CESTODES (Tapeworms):**
- ***Taenia* spp., *Diphyllobothrium***: **Praziquantel 5-10 mg/kg × 1** (75-85% cure)
- **Neurocysticercosis**: Albendazole 15 mg/kg/day × 8-30 days ± steroids
- **Echinococcosis** (hydatid): Albendazole long-term ± surgery

**TREMATODES (Flukes):**
- ***Schistosoma* spp.**: **Praziquantel 40-60 mg/kg** (divided doses × 1 day) (97-100% cure)
- ***Clonorchis, Paragonimus***: Praziquantel 75 mg/kg/day ÷ 3 doses × 1-2 days

**Key Pearl:** Ivermectin is **contraindicated** in *Loa loa* coinfection (encephalopathy risk).
