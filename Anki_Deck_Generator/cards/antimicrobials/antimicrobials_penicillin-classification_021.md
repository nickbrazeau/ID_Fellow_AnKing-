## Question

What are the five classes of penicillins, and what is their spectrum of activity? Use the **NAACU** mnemonic.

## Answer

**NAACU mnemonic for penicillin classes:**

| Class | Agents | Spectrum | Key Features |
|-------|--------|----------|--------------|
| **N**atural | Pen G, Pen VK | Streptococci, oral anaerobes, *Treponema*, *Neisseria* | No Gram-neg rods; destroyed by Î²-lactamase |
| **A**ntistaphylococcal | Nafcillin, oxacillin, dicloxacillin | MSSA (penicillinase-resistant) | NO activity vs MRSA or Gram-negatives |
| **A**minopenicillins | Ampicillin, amoxicillin | Natural PCN + *Listeria*, *Enterococcus*, some Gram-neg (*E. coli*, *Proteus*, *H. flu*) | "HELPS kill Enterococci": *H. flu*, *E. coli*, *Listeria*, *Proteus*, *Salmonella* |
| **C**arboxypenicillins | Ticarcillin | Aminopenicillin + *Pseudomonas* | Largely replaced by ureidopenicillins |
| **U**reidopenicillins | Piperacillin | Broadest spectrum; best anti-*Pseudomonas* activity | Usually combined with tazobactam |

## Key Points

### **Antipseudomonal Penicillins:**
- Carboxy- and ureidopenicillins = "extended-spectrum penicillins"
- **Piperacillin** is most potent vs *Pseudomonas*

### **Clinical Pearls:**
- **Natural PCN:** *Strep pyogenes*, syphilis, *Leptospira*
- **Aminopenicillins alone:** NO reliable coverage for *S. aureus* (most produce penicillinase)
- **Ampicillin > amoxicillin** for *Enterococcus* (use amp IV, not amox)
- **All penicillins:** Active vs *Listeria* EXCEPT antistaphylococcal PCNs
