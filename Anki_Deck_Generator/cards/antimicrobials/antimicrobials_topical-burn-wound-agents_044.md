## Topical Agents for Burns and Wounds

**Q:** What are the main topical antibacterial agents for burns and wounds, and how do they differ?

**A:**

**Comparison Table:**

| **Agent** | **Spectrum** | **Penetration** | **Main Use** | **Adverse Effects** |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Silver sulfadiazine (Silvadene)** | Broad-spectrum (Gram+, Gram-, some fungi) | Poor eschar penetration | **Most common for partial-thickness burns** | Delays eschar separation, leukopenia (transient), sulfa allergy |
| **Mafenide acetate (Sulfamylon)** | Broad-spectrum, **excellent Pseudomonas** | **Penetrates eschar** | Deep burns, infected burns, **cartilage** (ear, nose) | **Painful on application**, metabolic acidosis (carbonic anhydrase inhibitor), sulfa allergy |
| **Bacitracin** | Gram-positive ONLY (Staph, Strep) | Superficial | Minor wounds, abrasions, superficial burns | Allergic contact dermatitis (common), minimal systemic absorption |

**Silver Sulfadiazine:**
- **Mechanism:** Silver ions â†’ bacterial cell wall/membrane damage
- **Advantages:** Well-tolerated, painless, broad spectrum
- **Disadvantages:**
  - Delays wound healing (eschar separation)
  - Transient leukopenia (monitor CBC)
  - **Avoid in:** Sulfa allergy, pregnancy (near term), G6PD deficiency
- **Application:** Apply 1/16-inch thickness BID

**Mafenide Acetate:**
- **Mechanism:** Sulfonamide (inhibits folic acid synthesis)
- **Unique advantage:** **Penetrates eschar** (only topical agent that does)
- **Key indication:** Cartilaginous areas (ear burns - prevents chondritis)
- **Disadvantages:**
  - **Painful** (burning sensation for 20-30 min after application)
  - **Metabolic acidosis** (carbonic anhydrase inhibitor)
  - Monitor ABG/electrolytes if large surface area
- **Application:** Apply BID

**Bacitracin:**
- **Mechanism:** Inhibits bacterial cell wall synthesis (peptidoglycan)
- **Spectrum:** Gram-positive ONLY (NOT Gram-negatives)
- **Use:** Minor wounds, cuts, abrasions, post-procedure
- **Advantage:** Over-the-counter, safe, minimal systemic absorption
- **Disadvantage:** Allergic contact dermatitis common (10-15%)

**Mnemonic: "Silver Stops Microbes, Mafenide Makes it through eschar, Bacitracin for Basic wounds"**
- **Silver** = Broad, but stops eschar separation
- **Mafenide** = Makes it through eschar (penetrates)
- **Bacitracin** = Basic (minor wounds, Gram-positive only)

**Clinical Pearls:**
- **Silver sulfadiazine:** First-line for most partial-thickness burns
- **Mafenide:** Reserve for deep burns, cartilage involvement, or when eschar penetration needed
- **Bacitracin:** Acceptable for minor wounds but high allergy rate (consider alternatives like petroleum jelly for clean wounds)

**Modern Alternatives:**
- Silver-impregnated dressings (less messy, sustained release)
- Honey-based products (antimicrobial, promotes healing)
