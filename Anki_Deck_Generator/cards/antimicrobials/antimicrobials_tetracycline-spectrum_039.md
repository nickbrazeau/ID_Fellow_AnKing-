## Question

What is the spectrum of tetracyclines (especially doxycycline), and what are the key clinical uses? Use **CHARM-RAT** mnemonic for atypicals.

## Answer

### **CHARM-RAT mnemonic for tetracycline coverage of atypicals:**

- **C**hlamydia (*C. trachomatis*, *C. pneumoniae*, *C. psittaci*)
- **H**elicobacter (*H. pylori* - with other agents)
- **A**naplasma and **E**hrlichia
- **R**ickettsia (RMSF, typhus, scrub typhus, Q fever)
- **M**ycoplasma pneumoniae
- **R**elapsing fever (Borrelia)
- **A**nthrax (*Bacillus anthracis*)
- **T**reponemal (syphilis - alternative to PCN)

### **Broad Spectrum Coverage:**

| Organism Type | Activity |
|---------------|----------|
| **Gram-positives** | MSSA, Streptococci (NOT reliable vs MRSA) |
| **Gram-negatives** | Variable (*H. flu*, *Moraxella*, *E. coli*, *Vibrio*) |
| **Atypicals** | **EXCELLENT** (see CHARM-RAT above) |
| **Spirochetes** | *Borrelia* (Lyme), *Leptospira*, *Treponema* |
| **Parasites** | *Plasmodium* (malaria prophylaxis) |
| **Anaerobes** | Tigecycline only (NOT doxycycline) |

### **Key Clinical Uses of Doxycycline:**

| Indication | Dose | Duration |
|------------|------|----------|
| **RMSF** (Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever) | 100mg PO/IV BID | Until afebrile 24-48h |
| **Lyme disease** (early localized) | 100mg PO BID | 10-21 days |
| **Community-acquired pneumonia** | 100mg PO/IV BID | 5-7 days |
| **Malaria prophylaxis** | 100mg PO daily | Start 1-2d before, continue 4wks after |
| **Chlamydia** | 100mg PO BID | 7 days |
| **Acne vulgaris** | 50-100mg PO daily | Months |

## Key Points

### **Doxycycline = Drug of Choice:**
- **All rickettsial diseases** (RMSF, ehrlichiosis, anaplasmosis) - empiric therapy for ALL ages including children <8 years
- **Lyme disease** (early)
- **Atypical pneumonia** (*Mycoplasma*, *Chlamydia*)

### **Contraindications/Cautions:**
- **Pregnancy:** Avoid (tooth discoloration in fetus)
- **Children <8 years:** Risk of permanent tooth staining (BUT use for RMSF - benefits outweigh risks)
- **Take with food:** â†“ GI upset
- **Photosensitivity:** Common side effect

### **Resistance:**
- *Pseudomonas*, *Proteus*, *Enterococcus* - intrinsically resistant
- Variable activity vs Enterobacterales (efflux pumps)
