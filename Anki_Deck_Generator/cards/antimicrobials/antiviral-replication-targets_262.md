## Antiviral Drug Targets: Viral Replication Cycle

**Q:** What are the 6 stages of the viral replication cycle, and which antivirals target each stage? (Use mnemonic **AEU-RAR**)

**A:**

| Stage | Drug Examples | Mechanism |
|-------|---------------|-----------|
| **A**ttachment | Maraviroc (HIV), palivizumab (RSV mAb) | Block viral receptor binding |
| **E**ntry/Fusion | Enfuvirtide (HIV fusion inhibitor) | Prevent membrane fusion |
| **U**ncoating | Amantadine, rimantadine | Block M2 proton channel (influenza A) |
| **R**eplication (DNA) | Acyclovir, foscarnet, cidofovir | Inhibit viral DNA polymerase |
| **R**eplication (RNA) | Remdesivir, favipiravir, sofosbuvir | Inhibit RNA-dependent RNA polymerase (RdRp) |
| **A**ssembly/Maturation | Ritonavir, lopinavir, atazanavir | Inhibit viral protease → immature virions |
| **R**elease | Oseltamivir, zanamivir | Inhibit neuraminidase (influenza) |

**Key Principle:** **Selective toxicity** = Target viral enzymes without harming host cells (major challenge in antiviral development).

**Host-Targeted Therapy:** Interferons (IFN-α, IFN-β) amplify innate immunity pathways.
