## Antimalarial Drug Classes and Mechanisms

**Q:** What are the 4 major antimalarial drug classes and their mechanisms of action? (Use mnemonic **QAAA**)

**A:**

| Class | Drugs | Mechanism | Life Cycle Stage |
|-------|-------|-----------|------------------|
| **Q**uinolines | Chloroquine, quinine, mefloquine, primaquine | ↑ Toxic heme accumulation (blocks hemozoin polymerization in food vacuole) | Blood schizonts (primaquine: hypnozoites) |
| **A**rtemisinin derivatives | Artesunate, artemether, arteether | Free radical production via peroxide bridge → parasite damage | Early + late blood stages |
| **A**ntifolates | Pyrimethamine, proguanil, sulfadoxine | Inhibit DHFR + DHPS → block folate metabolism & DNA synthesis | Blood schizonts |
| **A**tovaquone | Atovaquone (+ proguanil = Malarone) | Inhibits mitochondrial cytochrome bc1 → blocks ATP production | Blood schizonts |

**Key Pearl:** Primaquine uniquely targets **hypnozoites** (*P. vivax/ovale*) and **gametocytes** (transmission-blocking).
