## Corticosteroids in Infectious Diseases: Evidence-Based Indications

**Q:** What are the evidence-based indications for corticosteroids in infectious diseases, including doses and mortality benefit?

**A:**

| Indication | Agent & Dose | Timing | Mortality Benefit | Evidence Quality |
|------------|--------------|--------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Septic shock** (refractory) | Hydrocortisone 200 mg/day (50 mg IV q6h or infusion) | After adequate fluid + vasopressors | Modest benefit | Weak/Low (SCCM) |
| **Bacterial meningitis** | Dexamethasone 0.15 mg/kg IV q6h × 2-4 days | **10-20 min BEFORE or with first antibiotic dose** | ↓ Mortality & neurologic sequelae (*S. pneumoniae*) | Strong |
| **Tuberculous meningitis** | Dexamethasone 0.3-0.4 mg/kg/day tapered over 6-8 weeks | With TB therapy | **↓ Deaths by ~25%** (3-18 mo) | High (Cochrane) |
| **PCP** (HIV + hypoxia) | Prednisone 40 mg PO BID × 5d → 40 mg daily × 5d → 20 mg daily × 11d | **Within 72 hrs** of PCP therapy start | ↓ Mortality & respiratory failure (PaO₂ <70 or A-a >35) | High |
| **Severe CAP** (septic shock) | Hydrocortisone 200 mg/day | With septic shock | Modest benefit | Moderate (SCCM) |

**AVOID:** High-dose/short-duration (>400 mg/day hydrocortisone equivalent <3 days) in septic shock

**Pearl:** Timing critical in meningitis (must give before/with antibiotics); PCP benefit only if hypoxemic.
