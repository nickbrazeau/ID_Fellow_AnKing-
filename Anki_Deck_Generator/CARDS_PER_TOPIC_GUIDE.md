## üìè How Many Cards? By Topic Complexity

### **Simple Topics (3-5 cards)**

**Examples:**
- Single organism basics
- Single drug mechanism
- Single clinical sign

**Card breakdown:**
1. Microbiology/mechanism
2. Clinical presentation
3. Diagnosis/testing
4. Treatment (first-line)
5. Key pearl/complication

**Example: Listeria monocytogenes**
- Card 1: Microbiology (gram-positive, motile, cold growth)
- Card 2: High-risk populations (pregnancy, elderly, immunocompromised)
- Card 3: Clinical syndromes (bacteremia, meningitis, rhombencephalitis)
- Card 4: Treatment (ampicillin ¬± gentamicin)
- Card 5: Food sources and prevention

---

### **Moderate Topics (5-7 cards)**

**Examples:**
- Clinical syndromes with multiple etiologies
- Drug classes with multiple agents
- Organisms with multiple clinical presentations

**Card breakdown:**
1. Overview/definition
2-3. Major subtypes/etiologies
4. Diagnosis/workup
5. Treatment approach
6. Complications/pearls
7. Contrasting learning (vs similar conditions)

**Example: Community-Acquired Pneumonia**
- Card 1: Definition and risk stratification (PORT, CURB-65)
- Card 2: Common organisms (S. pneumoniae, H. influenzae, atypicals)
- Card 3: Diagnostic approach (blood cultures, sputum, procalcitonin)
- Card 4: Outpatient treatment (amox-clav + macrolide vs respiratory FQ)
- Card 5: Inpatient treatment (ceftriaxone + azithro)
- Card 6: ICU treatment (Œ≤-lactam + azithro or FQ)
- Card 7: Complications (empyema, parapneumonic effusion)

---

### **Complex Topics (7-10 cards)**

**Examples:**
- Multi-faceted syndromes
- Broad drug classes
- Major organisms with diverse manifestations

**Card breakdown:**
1. Overview with mnemonic
2-4. Major clinical presentations (separate cards)
5-6. Diagnostic approaches for each
7-8. Treatment strategies
9. Resistance patterns/stewardship
10. Special populations/pearls

**Example: Staphylococcus aureus**
- Card 1: Microbiology and virulence factors (PVL, toxins, biofilm)
- Card 2: MRSA vs MSSA epidemiology and risk factors
- Card 3: Bacteremia definitions (uncomplicated vs complicated)
- Card 4: Bacteremia workup (echo indications, imaging)
- Card 5: Bacteremia treatment duration (2 weeks vs 4-6 weeks)
- Card 6: Endocarditis specific management
- Card 7: Skin/soft tissue infections (cellulitis, abscesses)
- Card 8: Pneumonia and necrotizing pneumonia
- Card 9: Osteomyelitis and prosthetic joint infections
- Card 10: MRSA treatment options (vancomycin, daptomycin, linezolid, ceftaroline)

## üéì Category-Specific Guidance

### **Antimicrobials (Chapters 1-40)**

**Average: 5-6 cards per antibiotic/class**

Essential cards:
- Mechanism of action (with mnemonic if complex)
- Spectrum of activity (table format)
- Pharmacokinetics (PK/PD, dosing pearls)
- Key drug interactions
- Resistance mechanisms
- Toxicities/monitoring

**Example counts:**
- Carbapenems: 6 cards
- Vancomycin: 7 cards (includes monitoring)
- Fluoroquinolones: 6 cards
- Antibiotic allergy: 8 cards (multiple approaches)

### **Clinical Syndromes (Chapters 40-89)**

**Average: 5-7 cards per syndrome**

Essential cards:
- Definition/clinical presentation
- Common etiologies (table if >3)
- Diagnostic approach (algorithm if available)
- Empiric treatment
- Definitive treatment
- Complications
- Contrasts with similar syndromes

**Example counts:**
- Fever of unknown origin: 8 cards
- Urinary tract infections: 7 cards
- Endocarditis: 10 cards (Duke criteria, management, complications)
- Sepsis: 8 cards (definitions, bundles, vasopressors)

- Microbiology and identification

### **Organisms - Viral/Fungal/Parasitic**

### **Special Populations (Chapters 274-296)**

**Average: 6-8 cards per population**

**Example:**
- Solid organ transplant: 8 cards (timeline, CMV, PJP, aspergillus, prophylaxis)

## üìã Decision Framework

### **For Each Topic, Ask:**

1. **How many distinct clinical entities?**
   - Single presentation: 3-5 cards
   - Multiple presentations: 5-8 cards
   - Highly diverse: 8-10 cards

2. **How complex is diagnosis?**
   - Simple test: 1 card
   - Algorithm needed: 2-3 cards
   - Multiple approaches: 3-4 cards

3. **How many treatment options?**
   - Single drug: 1 card
   - Multiple regimens: 2-3 cards
   - Complex decision tree: 3-4 cards

4. **How important clinically?**
   - Rare/low-yield: Minimum cards
   - Common/board-relevant: More cards
   - High-stakes (e.g., meningitis): Maximum cards

## ‚öñÔ∏è Quality > Quantity

**Better to have:**
- 5 excellent cards with mnemonics and images
- Clear, focused questions
- Evidence-based, cited answers

**Than:**
- 10 mediocre cards
- Overlapping content
- Vague or unsourced information

## üéØ Practical Workflow

### **Starting a New Topic:**

1. **Survey the topic** (PubMed, guidelines, repository PDFs)
2. **Identify key concepts** (5-10 distinct learnable items)
3. **Group into cards:**
   - Microbiology/basics
   - Clinical presentation
   - Diagnosis
   - Treatment
   - Complications/pearls
4. **Generate cards** with mnemonics and images
5. **Mark topic complete** when adequately covered

### **When to Move On:**

‚úÖ **Move to next topic when:**
- Core concepts covered (microbiology ‚Üí diagnosis ‚Üí treatment)
- Evidence-based content with citations
- Mnemonics/images included
- No major gaps in clinical reasoning flow

‚ùå **Don't:**
- Force exactly 5 cards if topic needs 3 or 8
- Create redundant cards to hit a number
- Skip important aspects to move faster
